Full text

Gunhild is an Old Norse female name composed of the words "gunnr" and "hild," both meaning battle. The name has been used in Sweden since at least the 10th century.

The name appears on runestones, for example, on U 652 in Kumla, Övergrans parish, Uppland: "Gunhild had these markings made for Tjälve, her son, and for herself. Arbjörn carved this stone."

The name was relatively popular in the 1940s and 1950s, but its usage has significantly decreased since then. The average age of people named Gunhild is 79 years (as of 2019).

A Latin variant of the name is Gunilla.

As of December 31, 2014, there were a total of 11,537 women registered in Sweden with the name Gunhild, of whom 4,037 used it as their given name. The corresponding figures for Gunhilda were 17 and 1, respectively.

Name day in Sweden: January 30th. In the Swedish-speaking Finnish calendar of names, Gunhild's name day is November 1st, since 1950.
