TL;DRA court has ruled in favor of the Malmö municipality, which had previously denied a request from an employee who wanted to work additional hours as a firefighter. The decision was based on the fact that the employee already had too much workload in their regular job to take on an extra assignment.
In plain language

A person working as a project manager in the Malmö municipality wanted to work part-time as a firefighter in addition to their regular job. This is known as a secondary occupation. The municipality refused the request because the person had very important responsibilities and could not prioritize anything else.

The union, Naturvetarna, took the case to court, arguing that the municipality was not allowed to refuse in this way. They demanded compensation, claiming that the municipality violated the rules of the collective bargaining agreement, which is an agreement between employers and trade unions.

The Labor Court, which resolves disputes regarding working conditions, ruled that the Malmö municipality had acted correctly. The court stated that the municipality had valid reasons for refusing the request because the project manager had a high workload and important deadlines to meet.

This decision highlights the importance of employers documenting why they deny requests for secondary occupations. If an employer can demonstrate that the decision is based on real circumstances, such as the employee having too much work in their regular job, then they are acting within the rules.

Kommentar Med bisyssla förstås varje syssla, tillfällig eller stadigvarande, som utövas vid sidan av anställningen och som inte är hänförlig till privatlivet (AD 1985 nr 69). Arbetsgivaren bör ha rutiner för hur bisysslan ska anmälas. Arbetsgivaren ska minst en gång per år i samband med utvecklings/medarbetarsamtal informera om skyldigheten och därutöver informera på lämpligt vis. Sedan 2017 ska arbetstagaren på eget initiativ anmäla bisysslan och lämna de uppgifter som arbetsgivaren anser behövs för bedömning av bisysslan. Arbetsgivaren kan förbjuda bisysslan med stöd av AB § 8. För anställda i kommuner, regioner och kommunalförbund gäller endast bestämmelserna om arbetshindrande eller konkurrerande bisyssla. Förtroendeskadliga bisysslor regleras för dessa arbetstagare i 7 § lagen om offentlig anställning.
