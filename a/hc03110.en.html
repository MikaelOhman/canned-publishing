TL;DRThe government is proposing new regulations for the trading of stocks and other securities to comply with EU laws. The aim is to make it easier for certain companies to operate and to clarify the rules.
In plain language

The government intends to amend several laws governing securities trading in Sweden. These proposals are being introduced to ensure that Swedish regulations are aligned with the new rules established within the European Union. The changes include measures to simplify the licensing requirements for certain companies involved in securities trading.

The proposals also introduce new regulations for the trading venues themselves. These regulations address issues such as the conditions under which trading can be paused and the permissible range of price fluctuations. Furthermore, the regulations governing the Swedish Financial Supervisory Authority (Finansinspektionen), the agency responsible for ensuring compliance with the laws, will be amended. The criteria under which the Financial Supervisory Authority has the authority to prohibit trading will be clarified.

The proposals also affect other laws related to the recording of financial instruments and the governance of limited liability companies. There are also changes concerning the handling of the settlement of purchases and sales in the market.

The new regulations regarding when the Financial Supervisory Authority can prohibit trading are proposed to come into effect on June 1, 2025. The remaining amendments to the Securities Market Act are proposed to take effect on September 29, 2025.

Full text

The proposal includes legislative measures necessary due to changes in the EU's regulations and directives concerning financial instruments markets (MiFIR and MiFID II). These changes primarily involve regulatory relaxations for investment firms. The proposal suggests amendments to the Securities Market Act, concerning, among other things:

  • Exemptions from the requirement to obtain a license to operate a securities business.
  • Operational requirements for companies operating trading platforms.
  • Provisions regarding the suspension of trading.
  • Rules on the minimum permissible price change.
  • The Swedish Financial Supervisory Authority's (Finansinspektionen) powers to intervene.

The proposal also suggests an amendment to the Securities Market Act regarding the Swedish Financial Supervisory Authority's decisions to prohibit trading in certain cases. This amendment is proposed in response to the European Commission's concerns about whether the EU's transparency directive has been implemented in Swedish law in a sufficiently clear and precise manner.

The proposal also includes certain amendments to supplementary provisions and references to the EU regulation on securities depositories.

The amendment concerning the Swedish Financial Supervisory Authority's decisions to prohibit trading in certain cases is proposed to come into force on June 1, 2025. The same applies to the amendments to the supplementary provisions and references to the EU regulation on securities depositories. The remaining amendments are proposed to come into force on September 29, 2025.
