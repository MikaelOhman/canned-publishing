Full text

Erik Trolle, who held properties in Fånö, Ekholmen, and Lagnö, was born around 1460 and died in 1529 or 1530. He was a Swedish clergyman, district judge (lagman), member of the Council of the Realm in 1487, and Regent of Sweden in 1512. He was the son of Arvid Trolle and Kristina Jönsdotter Gädda (died 1465), and the father of Archbishop Gustaf Trolle.

== Biography == Trolle was enrolled at the University of Rostock in 1480, mentioned as a knight in 1485, switched from his ecclesiastical career, and became a member of the Council of the Realm in 1487. Initially, he sympathized with King Hans of the Kalmar Union, but after Hans's downfall, he joined Svante Nilsson (Sture), the Regent of Sweden. On May 18, 1512, he was elected by the Council of the Realm as Regent, but this election was annulled by Sten Sture the Younger at a meeting in Stockholm in July 1512. Trolle subsequently did not actively participate in national politics but became a district judge (lagman) in Närke and Uppland. After the uprising against Christian II in 1521, he fled to Denmark in 1523, but returned in 1527. He was alive on August 27, 1529, but died shortly thereafter. He was buried in the Nynäs family vault at Bälinge Church in Södermanland.
