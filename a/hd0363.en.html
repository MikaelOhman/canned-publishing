Certain privacy concerns related to arms transactions and the Schengen Information System.

TL;DRThe government wants to amend the law to protect important information. This involves keeping confidential details about arms deals and information shared between police forces in Europe.
In plain language

The government wants to amend the law regarding public access to information and confidentiality. This means that certain information should be kept secret and not be disclosed to just anyone. The first proposal concerns firearms. The government wants to protect information that the police have regarding the purchase and sale of firearms. This applies to the lists and notifications that those dealing in firearms must submit to the police.

The second proposal concerns cooperation between police forces in Europe. In a system called the Schengen Information System, police share information with each other. The government wants to better protect the information that is shared within this system. This is particularly relevant for information about criminal investigations in other countries and information about people's private lives.

The purpose of these changes is to protect sensitive information so that it does not fall into the wrong hands. The initial changes regarding firearms are proposed to come into effect on April 1, 2026. For the other changes, the government will determine a date later.

Full text

The government proposes amendments to the law on freedom of information and secrecy, which would expand the protection of confidential information regarding transactions involving firearms, relating to the activities of the Police Authority, specifically concerning the records and notifications that firearms dealers and professional intermediaries are required to maintain or submit to the authority.

The government also proposes amendments to the law on freedom of information and secrecy, which would extend the existing confidentiality regulations to protect new information that will be exchanged within the framework of police cooperation in the Schengen Information System, and which relates to foreign criminal investigations and the personal circumstances of individuals.

The proposed amendments are scheduled to come into force on April 1, 2026, and on a date determined by the government.
