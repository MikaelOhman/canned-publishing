The principle of public access, with simplified rules for individual, smaller entities in the school system.

TL;DRThe principle of public access – the right to access public documents – should also apply to private schools (operated by individual entities), which should be treated as government agencies. However, smaller school operators may be granted certain exemptions in the handling of these documents.
In plain language

The principle of public access grants everyone the right to access public documents held by government agencies. Currently, this principle does not fully apply to independent schools.

The government proposes that the right to access public documents should also apply to legal entities that are private operators in the education system. These entities should be treated as government agencies when the public access and confidentiality act is applied.

To avoid placing an undue burden, smaller operators may be allowed to apply certain simplified procedures in handling these requests.

Full text

The proposal includes suggested amendments to the Swedish Act on Access to Public Records and Official Secrets (2009:400), the Archives Act (1990:782), and the Education Act (2010:800), which would introduce the principle of public access for private entities acting as governing bodies within the education system. The proposals essentially entail the following:

  • The right to access public documents should apply to legal entities that are private governing bodies within the education system.
  • Such governing bodies should be treated as authorities when applying the Act on Access to Public Records and Official Secrets. Certain confidentiality provisions that would then apply to such governing bodies should be adjusted to ensure that confidentiality does not hinder access to information about the education system.
  • Smaller governing bodies should be allowed to apply regulations that provide certain simplifications in the handling of public documents. This applies to governing bodies with a maximum of 450 children and students, or, if they only operate independent preschools, a maximum of 100 children, or those that are part of a group of companies where the total number of children and students in the group companies is that high.
  • The provisions of the Archives Act that apply to municipal authorities' archives should apply to the relevant private governing bodies, with the exception of smaller governing bodies, which should preserve public documents for a minimum of seven years.
  • During the first two years after the implementation, all legal entities that are private governing bodies within the education system should be allowed to apply simplified regulations.

The proposed amendments to the law should come into effect on January 1, 2027.
