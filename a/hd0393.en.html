In plain language

The government has proposed a change to the law governing social services, which is the part of the government responsible for care and support. The proposal states that those operating facilities for elderly individuals must ensure that staff possess a level of Swedish proficiency sufficient to perform their job duties effectively.

This means that it is not enough to simply have staff present; they must also have the necessary language skills to understand and communicate effectively in their work. The government will also be granted the authority to determine the specific level of Swedish proficiency required for this particular type of employment.

If the parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new regulations will come into effect on July 1, 2026. The purpose is to increase the safety and quality of care for elderly people by ensuring that language is not a barrier in their interactions with staff.

Full text

The proposal suggests an amendment to the Social Services Act (2025:400) that introduces a requirement for those operating businesses within the social services sector providing care for elderly individuals to ensure that the staff carrying out these services possess the necessary knowledge of Swedish to effectively perform their duties. Furthermore, the proposal suggests that the Social Services Act should include a provision authorizing the government to issue regulations regarding the required level of Swedish proficiency for staff providing care for elderly individuals.

This legislative change is proposed to come into effect on July 1, 2026.
