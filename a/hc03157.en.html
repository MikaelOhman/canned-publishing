Full text

The proposal suggests introducing a law for a state-funded program providing leisure cards for children's extracurricular activities. The leisure card aims to promote children's opportunities to participate in activities that contribute to an active and meaningful leisure time in a community setting. The e-health authority is proposed to handle applications for the leisure cards.

Furthermore, the proposal includes a new law regarding the e-health authority's processing of personal data in leisure card applications.

The proposal also suggests an amendment to the law on reporting obligations to prevent incorrect payments from welfare systems, as well as fraud, violations, and crime in the workplace. This amendment would extend the scope of the law to include the e-health authority.

Additionally, the proposal includes a suggested amendment to the social services act, ensuring that the leisure card support does not affect the calculation of subsistence benefits.

The proposal also contains certain assessments regarding the leisure card, including that the criminal law protection for the leisure card should be strengthened.

The proposed laws are suggested to come into force on September 8, 2025.
