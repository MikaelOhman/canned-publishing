The National Climate and Vulnerability Assessment was developed by the National Expert Council for Climate Adaptation, also known as the Climate Adaptation Council. The council's task was to analyze climate-related risks and the potential effects these could have on society, people, and the natural environment in Sweden. This assessment serves as a national knowledge base.

Mette Lindahl Olsson, head of department at the Swedish Civil Defence Agency, has been a member of the expert council since 2023. "It has been a very interesting and educational project where the expert council involved nearly 130 experts and analyzed large amounts of material."

The work covered several hundred climate-related effects within five overarching systems: health, ecosystems, built environment and infrastructure, food security, and business and natural resources.

The conclusion is that the pace of implementing climate adaptation measures needs to increase to keep up with the evolving risks. The biggest obstacles to climate adaptation are unclear or lacking legal frameworks, political strategies, and a lack of long-term funding.

For the Swedish Civil Defence Agency, there is a continued need to focus on the risks identified here and now: heavy rainfall and subsequent flooding, landslides, heatwaves, droughts, forest and vegetation fires, as well as emerging trends such as increased average precipitation, sea level rise, and transnational impacts – all of which are essential for protecting lives and health, the economy, critical infrastructure, the environment, cultural heritage, and contributing to a strong civil defense.

The biggest challenge is water: too much water, too little water, or insufficient water quality.

The Climate Adaptation Council is responsible for producing a report every five years, which serves as the basis for the next national strategy and the government's action plan for climate adaptation. The National Climate and Vulnerability Assessment (NKSA) is an integral part of the Climate Adaptation Council's report to the government. The final report will be presented in December 2026 and will include a summary analysis of the effects of climate change on society, a prioritization of measures, and proposals for the direction of national climate adaptation efforts.
