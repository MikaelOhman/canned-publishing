The government has proposed a plan to improve healthcare's ability to function effectively during wartime or major crises. They want to amend the Health and Medical Care Act to clearly state that municipalities and regions must assist each other. The proposal also includes clear regulations for situations where a disaster lasts for an extended period.

A key element of the proposal is that municipalities and regions must maintain a stock of essential medical supplies, often referred to as healthcare products. They must also plan for scenarios where a large number of people become ill or injured simultaneously. The goal is to ensure that healthcare can continue to provide assistance even during challenging times.

The proposal also includes provisions that could require healthcare personnel to provide information to the Armed Forces if the country is on high alert, meaning it is prepared for war. Furthermore, the regulations regarding assistance during evacuations and accommodation will be amended to prioritize the new healthcare regulations.

If the Parliament approves the proposals, most of the changes will take effect on January 1, 2026. The only exception is the requirement to maintain a stock of healthcare products, which will come into effect on January 1, 2027.

Full text

The proposal includes suggestions aimed at strengthening the healthcare system's preparedness in the event of a crisis or war.

The proposals clarify in the Health and Medical Care Act (2017:30) the obligations of municipalities and regions to provide care and assist each other during peacetime crises, heightened alert levels, and states of emergency. The proposals also clarify the obligations of municipalities, regions, and healthcare providers in situations where a state of emergency persists despite assistance being provided. It is proposed that municipalities and regions have a duty to plan for events involving a large number of injured and sick individuals. Furthermore, it is proposed that municipalities and regions be obligated to maintain stockpiles of medical supplies. In addition, certain new and amended authorizations are proposed.

The proposal also suggests an amendment to the Patient Safety Act (2010:659), which would expand the obligation of healthcare personnel to provide information to the Armed Forces during a heightened alert level. It also proposes an amendment to the Act (2006:546) concerning evacuation and accommodation, etc., during a heightened alert level, stating that the provisions of that Act regarding assistance should not apply in cases where the provisions regarding assistance in the Health and Medical Care Act are applicable.

The proposed amendments to the laws are intended to come into force on January 1, 2026, with the exception of the amendments regarding the obligation to maintain stockpiles, which are proposed to come into force on January 1, 2027.
