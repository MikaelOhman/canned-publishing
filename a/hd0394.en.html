Full text

The EU regulation on sustainability ratings will apply from July 2, 2026. The regulation introduces a common framework within the Union for providers of ratings related to environmental, social, and governance (ESG) factors. This framework means that anyone who professionally provides such ratings needs a license. Providers subject to the licensing requirement must also meet certain requirements regarding transparency, organization, and conduct. The purpose is to improve the quality of ratings and prevent "greenwashing" and other types of misleading information.

The EU regulation requires adjustments to national law. Therefore, the proposal includes a new law with supplementary provisions to the regulation. This new law designates the Financial Supervisory Authority as the competent authority and introduces provisions on assistance and enforcement. In addition, amendments to the law are proposed that give the Financial Supervisory Authority the power to supervise how sustainability ratings are used in marketing materials.

The new law and the amendments are proposed to come into force on April 2, 2026.
