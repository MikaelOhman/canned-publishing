Adaptation of Swedish law to the EU's new regulation on protected geographical indications and protected designations of origin for agricultural products and foodstuffs.

TL;DRSweden will be amending its laws to comply with new regulations from the EU regarding protected names for food and agricultural products. This is being done to ensure that the rules are consistent across the entire EU.
In plain language

The government has proposed changes to two Swedish laws. These laws concern the control and penalties for those who use incorrect names for food products and agricultural products. The purpose is to ensure that Swedish regulations are in line with the EU's new rules for protected designations of origin and geographical indications.

A protected designation is a name for a product that is specific to a particular region or a particular method of food production. With this change, the government or other authorities will have more power to determine how inspections are carried out and how groups of producers should be organized. This aims to clarify how to object if someone uses a protected name incorrectly.

If the Parliament approves the proposal, the new rules will come into effect on April 1, 2026. The proposal involves updating old references in the laws to point to the new and correct regulations from the EU.

Full text

The proposal includes the provisions necessary to adapt Act (2009:1424) on the control of protected designations for agricultural products and foodstuffs, and Act (2018:1654) on sanctions for infringements of the right to certain protected designations, to the EU's new regulation on protected designations. The changes involve updating the references to which EU regulations the Act on the control of protected designations for agricultural products and foodstuffs supplements. Furthermore, the changes introduce new authorizations in that Act for the government or the authority designated by the government, including the power to issue regulations regarding the national objection procedure and the composition of producer groups.

The proposed amendments are to come into force on April 1, 2026.

  2. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to Act (2018:1654) on sanctions for infringements of the right to certain protected designations.
