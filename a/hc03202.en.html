Expanded opportunities to serve prison sentences under electronic monitoring.

Source Riksdagen Read the original at Riksdagen →2025-08-26
TL;DRThe government wants to make it easier for offenders to serve their sentences with electronic monitoring (ankle bracelets) or in special facilities, instead of in prison. This applies to sentences of up to one and a half years.
In plain language

The government has proposed changes to the rules regarding how sentences are served. This means that more individuals will be eligible for electronic monitoring, often referred to as house arrest, in their own homes. This applies to sentences of up to one year and six months.

For individuals serving sentences longer than six months, authorities will conduct a more thorough assessment to determine if it is appropriate for them to serve their sentence at home. The purpose is to ensure that the monitoring is effective and safe.

A new form of punishment, called "supervised living," will also be introduced. This involves residing at a specific location that is monitored by authorities. The Swedish Prison and Probation Service, the agency responsible for prisons and supervision, will manage both the living facilities and the supervision itself.

The new rules are scheduled to come into effect on January 1, 2026. However, the regulations for the new "supervised living" program will take effect slightly later, on June 1, 2026.

Full text

The proposal suggests legal changes that increase the possibilities for serving prison sentences with electronic monitoring. The proposals include:

  • Expanding the scope of electronic monitoring in the convicted person's home to include prison sentences of up to one year and six months.
  • For sentences exceeding six months, a more in-depth suitability assessment must be conducted before a decision is made regarding serving the sentence in the convicted person's home.
  • Introducing a new form of serving prison sentences: electronic monitoring in a supervised residence, which can be applied to prison sentences of up to one year and six months.
  • The Swedish Prison and Probation Service will be responsible for the execution of prison sentences with electronic monitoring in supervised residences and for the operation of these residences.

The proposed legal changes are scheduled to come into effect on January 1, 2026. However, regarding serving sentences in supervised residences, the new law on the execution of prison sentences with electronic monitoring will be applied for the first time on June 1, 2026.
