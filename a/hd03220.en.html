Swedish contribution to NATO's enhanced presence in Finland.

TL;DRThe Swedish Parliament is proposed to approve that the government, starting in 2026, may deploy up to 1,200 Swedish soldiers to contribute to NATO's forward presence in Finland. This is part of Sweden's commitments within NATO.
In plain language

As a NATO member, Sweden participates in the alliance's collective defense. This includes NATO's forward presence near the alliance's eastern border.

The government proposes that the Swedish Parliament approve the deployment of up to 1,200 Swedish armed forces personnel to Finland in 2026, to contribute to NATO's presence there (FLF Finland).

The size of the deployment will be determined by NATO's operational planning.

Full text

The proposal suggests that the Parliament should decide that the government may, until December 31, 2026, deploy Swedish armed forces, up to a maximum of 1,200 personnel, to contribute, in accordance with international law, to NATO's enhanced forward presence in Finland (eFP Finland). The Swedish force contribution in 2026 will be determined by NATO's operational planning.
