In plain language

The government has proposed a new law for a national e-identification system. This means that the state will be able to issue a digital ID card that proves your identity when you use online services. The proposal also concerns electronic identification, which is a fancy term for securely logging in using technology.

Anyone who lives in Sweden can obtain this digital ID. This includes people with Swedish citizenship, foreign nationals who are registered residents here, and certain individuals with special rights, known as immunity. To obtain such an ID, you must be at least nine years old during the year you apply.

The purpose of the new law is to ensure that we in Sweden have good and secure ways to use digital services. It is also important for us to comply with the EU's regulations on how to digitally identify oneself. If the proposal is approved, the new law should come into effect on December 1, 2026.

Full text

The proposal suggests a new law regarding state-issued electronic identification and digital signatures. A state-issued electronic identification should be available to individuals with Swedish citizenship, foreign nationals registered in Sweden, and individuals who possess a so-called immunity number and are covered by the Act (1976:661) on immunity and privileges in certain cases. To obtain a state-issued electronic identification, the applicant must have reached the age of nine or be turning nine years old in the current calendar year. The proposal aims to ensure society's access to electronic identification and to ensure that the requirements of the EU regulation on electronic identification are met.

The new law is proposed to come into effect on December 1, 2026.
