In plain language

The government has proposed changes to several laws in Sweden. The aim is to better align with EU regulations concerning Environmental Impact Assessments (EIAs). An EIA is a study conducted to assess how a project might impact the environment.

A key part of the proposal is to make it easier for everyone to access information about decisions related to environmental impact. This means that the public should have a clear understanding of what has been decided. The proposal also applies to laws concerning roads, railways, electricity, and ports.

The proposal also clarifies that environmental organizations have the right to appeal decisions. If an authority has determined that an activity does not harm the environment, environmental organizations should be able to appeal that decision. The government also wants to be able to determine when certain work can begin.

If the Swedish Parliament approves the proposal, the new regulations will come into effect on August 1, 2025. The proposal affects a wide range of areas, including the construction of railways, nuclear power plants, and work at sea.

Full text

In the proposal, the government suggests amendments to the law to improve and clarify the implementation of the European Parliament and Council Directive 2011/92/EU on the assessment of the environmental impacts of certain public and private projects (the EIA Directive). These changes will be made to the Environmental Code and a number of sector-specific laws, including the Continental Shelf Act and the Road Act.

The proposed amendments to the Environmental Code include, among other things, making decisions regarding significant environmental impacts publicly available, granting the government the authority to issue regulations on when an activity requiring notification can begin, and clarifying that environmental organizations have the right to appeal decisions in which the supervisory authority has assessed whether an activity requiring notification is likely to have a significant environmental impact.

The amendments to the sector-specific laws include, among other things, making decisions regarding significant environmental impacts in cases under these laws publicly available.

The proposed amendments to the law are scheduled to come into force on August 1, 2025.

Proposed resolution for the Parliament

The government's proposal:

  1. The Parliament shall adopt the government's proposed amendment to the Environmental Code.
  2. The Parliament shall adopt the government's proposed amendment to the Act (1966:314) concerning the continental shelf.
  3. The Parliament shall adopt the government's proposed amendment to the Road Act (1971:948).
  4. The Parliament shall adopt the government's proposed amendment to the Act (1978:160) concerning certain pipelines.
  5. The Parliament shall adopt the government's proposed amendment to the Act (1983:293) concerning the establishment, expansion, and discontinuation of public waterways and public ports.
  6. The Parliament shall adopt the government's proposed amendment to the Act (1984:3) concerning nuclear activities.
  7. The Parliament shall adopt the government's proposed amendment to the Act (1992:1140) concerning Sweden's economic zone.
  8. The Parliament shall adopt the government's proposed amendment to the Act (1995:1649) concerning the construction of railways.
  9. The Parliament shall adopt the government's proposed amendment to the Electricity Act (1997:857).
  10. The Parliament shall adopt the government's proposed amendment to the Natural Gas Act (2005:403).
