TL;DRThe government wants to make it more difficult for criminals to use explosives by tightening controls on companies that handle hazardous materials. The proposal aims to prevent explosives from falling into the wrong hands.
In plain language

It has become more common for acts of sabotage to occur in Sweden, where criminals use explosives against both people and homes. Often, the explosives used are legally obtained but have been stolen or ended up in the wrong hands. To stop this, the government wants to change the rules for companies that work with flammable and explosive materials.

One of the proposals is that companies that have permission to handle these materials must quickly notify the authorities if key individuals, such as managers or owners, leave the company. The police authority should also be given greater opportunities to appeal decisions regarding companies or individuals being allowed to handle explosives. This is intended to make it easier to keep track of who has access to dangerous materials.

The government also wants to change the law regarding confidentiality, which are the rules that determine what is considered secret. It should become easier for the Swedish Defence Radio Agency (FRA) and other authorities to share important information with each other in international collaborations. This is being done to increase security in digital operations.

If the Parliament votes in favor of the proposals, the new rules will come into effect on July 1, 2026. The proposals involve changes to two different laws that relate to both security and the handling of dangerous substances.

Full text

In recent years, the number of explosions in society has increased significantly. Individuals involved in criminal activities are increasingly using explosives in public places, in areas frequented by the public, and directly targeting the residences of individuals as part of criminal disputes. These explosions are often carried out by individuals involved in organized crime, and legal explosives that have fallen into the wrong hands are often used.

It is of utmost importance to counter this development and prevent criminals from having access to explosive materials. Therefore, the government proposes the following in this proposal:

  • A business requiring a permit should promptly report to the licensing authority if a participant, manager, or person with significant influence no longer participates in, manages, or has such influence over the business.
  • The police authority should be able to appeal decisions to grant permits for flammable and explosive materials, as well as decisions to approve managers and participants in businesses dealing with explosive materials.

The government also proposes a legislative amendment that would allow the Swedish Defence Radio Agency and the supervisory authorities under the cybersecurity law (2025:1506) to exchange information under certain conditions, without being hindered by confidentiality in international cooperation.

These legislative amendments are proposed to come into force on July 1, 2026.

Proposed Parliamentary Decision

The government's proposal:

  1. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Public Access and Secrecy Act (2009:400).
  2. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Act (2010:1011) on flammable and explosive materials.
