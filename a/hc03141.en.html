Enhanced protection for public sector employees against violence, threats, harassment, and other forms of abuse.

Source Riksdagen Read the original at Riksdagen →2025-03-18
TL;DRThe government wants to amend the laws to protect individuals working in municipalities and government agencies from violence, threats, and harassment. The goal is to ensure that those who perform essential public services feel safe and confident enough to make the right decisions without fear.
In plain language

Many people working in the public sector, such as in schools or healthcare, are exposed to threats and violence in their work. This is a problem for society as a whole, as it can cause employees to be afraid to make important decisions for fear of what might happen to them.

To address this, the government proposes new laws that will come into effect on July 2, 2025. This includes, among other things, increasing the penalties for crimes against public officials. The government also wants to introduce a new offense called "insulting a public official."

There are also plans to increase the safety of employees. This includes the possibility of concealing the name of the person making a decision in certain written communications. In addition, the rules regarding confidentiality, i.e., what can and cannot be said, will be strengthened in personnel matters to protect the identity of employees.

The government has sent the proposal to the Parliament for a decision. The proposal involves changes to several laws, including the Criminal Code and the Administrative Law Act. If the Parliament approves it, these new rules will come into effect in the summer of 2025.

Full text

Public sector employees are frequently subjected to violence, threats, and harassment. This exposure has become increasingly common and occurs across virtually all areas of the public sector. Crimes and other inappropriate acts committed against a person because of their work represent an attack not only on the individual targeted, but also on democracy and society as a whole. It is unacceptable that some public sector employees, consciously or unconsciously, fail to take necessary actions or make correct decisions out of fear of, for example, harassment. This means that fundamental principles of objectivity, legality, and equal treatment are being compromised.

The issue of public sector employees' vulnerability is complex and spans multiple areas of society. To reduce the vulnerability of public sector employees, it is crucial to take measures in several different areas. The government therefore proposes, among other things, the following:

  • The criminal law protection for civil servants and those performing certain socially beneficial functions in the Swedish Code of Criminal Law will be modernized and strengthened.
  • The penalties will be increased, and a new crime of insulting a public official will be introduced.
  • Explicit support will be provided to, in certain cases, omit the name of the decision-maker in written notifications.
  • Confidentiality in personnel administration will be strengthened.

In the government proposal, it is also suggested that prosecutions where the actions involve classified information should be handled by the Stockholm District Court.

The proposed amendments to the law will come into force on July 2, 2025.

Proposal for a Parliamentary Decision

Proposal 2024/25:141

Government's Proposal:

  1. The Parliament shall approve the government's proposed amendment to the Criminal Code.
  2. The Parliament shall approve the government's proposed amendment to the Code of Judicial Procedure.
  3. The Parliament shall approve the government's proposed amendment to the Act on Public Access and Official Secrets (2009:400).
  4. The Parliament shall approve the government's proposed amendment to the Administrative Law Act (2017:900).
