TL;DRThe government wants to amend the law to better protect Sweden from individuals who pose a threat to the country's security. The proposal includes measures that would make it easier to detain individuals and increase the penalties for certain crimes.
In plain language

Sweden has a deteriorating security situation compared to the past. Therefore, the government has proposed new regulations in the law concerning special control of certain foreign nationals. The purpose is to strengthen protection against individuals considered a significant threat to Sweden's security.

The new regulations would give authorities greater opportunities to use detention. Detention means that a person is held in custody for a period of time. The government wants to allow adults to be detained for longer periods and that less evidence will be required to order someone into detention.

The government also wants to clarify when it is permissible to order a person to be deported from the country. In addition, it is proposed that the penalties for certain offenses related to this law should be increased.

If the Parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new laws will come into effect on March 1, 2027.

Full text

Given the deteriorating security situation, it is crucial that the protection against foreign nationals who pose a qualified threat to Sweden's security is effective and, in general, appropriate. In the government proposal, the government presents amendments to the Act (2022:700) on special control of certain foreign nationals, aiming to strengthen the protection against foreign nationals who pose a qualified security threat. The proposals include:

  • Expanded possibilities for detention, including longer detention periods and a reduced evidentiary requirement for the detention of adults.
  • A clarification regarding the conditions for deciding on deportation.
  • Stricter penalties for certain offenses under the Act.

The amendments to the law are proposed to come into force on March 1, 2027.
