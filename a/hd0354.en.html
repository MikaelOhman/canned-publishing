In plain language

The government has proposed a new law to align Swedish regulations with EU regulations for fertilizers. This law concerns fertilizer products that have a so-called CE marking, which is a symbol indicating that the product meets certain requirements and can be sold within the EU.

To ensure compliance with the regulations, authorities should be able to inspect products on the market. If an authority discovers that the regulations are not being followed, they can issue an order to correct the issue. Failure to comply with these orders may result in a fine, which is a type of penalty that must be paid if one fails to do what they have been instructed to do.

There are also proposals that companies that violate the regulations should be required to pay a sanction fee. A sanction fee is a penalty that the state levies when someone has violated the law. The purpose is to ensure that everyone complies with the common regulations for fertilizers in Europe.

If the parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new law will come into effect on June 1, 2026. This means that all companies that sell these types of fertilizer products in Sweden must comply with the new regulations from that date.

Full text

The proposal suggests a new law to align Swedish legislation with the EU's fertilizer product regulation. The provisions of the regulation apply to products placed on the internal market with a CE marking, designated as EU fertilizer products.

The new law incorporates provisions for market surveillance of EU fertilizer products into the law (2022:1107) on market surveillance of EU fertilizer products. Furthermore, it is proposed that the market surveillance authority should be able to issue additional requirements, and that these requirements should also be subject to fines. Certain violations of the provisions in the EU's fertilizer product regulation will result in a sanction fee.

The new law is proposed to come into force on June 1, 2026.
