Nils Gunnar Nordahl, born October 19, 1921, in Hörnefors, Västerbotten County, Sweden, and died September 15, 1995, in Alghero, Sardinia, Italy, was a Swedish football player (center forward) who became the first Swedish professional footballer at a high level when he was signed by Italian club AC Milan in 1949. Before that, Nordahl won four Swedish championships with IFK Norrköping and was the top scorer in the Allsvenskan league four times. He played for the Swedish national team that won gold at the Olympic Games in London in 1948, scoring a total of 43 goals in 33 international matches. As a professional in Italy, the Swede became one of the greatest goalscorers in Serie A history, winning the league's top scorer award five times between 1950 and 1956. He is still (as of January 2026) the third-highest goalscorer of all time in the league, after Silvio Piola and Francesco Totti. Nordahl was inducted into the Swedish Football Hall of Fame as member number 9.

== Sporting Career ==

=== Early Years === Nordahl began his football career with his brothers Bertil, Knut, Gösta, and Göran at Hörnefors IF in Västerbotten, strongly encouraged by their parents who actively supported the children in sports. He combined exceptional physical strength and a powerful, accurate shot with playing intelligence, qualities that early on made him a formidable goalscorer. In 41 matches in Division III, he scored no fewer than 68 goals between 1937 and 1940. From 1936 to 1940, he worked as an industrial worker at the Hörnefors sulfite factory, and from 1940 to 1943, he was a worker in Degerfors. From 1943 to 1948, he was a firefighter in Norrköping.

=== Allsvenskan debut, top scorer and Olympic gold === Gunnar Nordahl was given the opportunity to play in the Allsvenskan league early in his career with Degerfors IF, where he continued his goal-scoring. With 58 goals in 77 matches and a scoring title in 1943, several clubs took notice of the hard-shooting player from northern Sweden. IFK Norrköping was the club that won the competition for his signature, and it was there that Nordahl would quickly develop. Averaging more than one goal per match in Allsvenskan, winning the scoring title four more times, being named Swedish Footballer of the Year in 1947, and winning the national championship four years in a row showed that he was ready for a greater challenge. On November 12, 1944, in the match between IFK Norrköping and Landskrona BoIS (9-1), Nordahl scored seven goals, achieving a remarkable feat.

When he later played for the Swedish national team at the Olympics in London in 1948, where they defeated Yugoslavia in the final (3-1, with one goal from Nordahl), and also won the tournament's scoring title with seven goals, news of the Swede with the exceptional goal-scoring ability reached Italy. This achievement was significant because, at that time, the Olympics and the World Cup were considered equally important tournaments, as not all countries allowed professional players in the World Cup until Sweden in 1958.

=== Sweden's first professional === In 1949, Nordahl was transferred to Italy and AC Milan, becoming Sweden's first professional football player at a high level. The transfer fee was 75,000 kronor, and Gunnar himself received a monthly salary of 1,500 kronor. In his first season (which was only half a season), he scored 16 goals in 15 matches, and his success was immediate. The tough and (despite his weight) fast Swedish player quickly gained a reputation as a reliable goal scorer and one of the stars of Serie A. Due to the strict rules of the Swedish Football Association at that time, which only allowed amateurs to be eligible for national team selection, Nordahl had forfeited the opportunity to play for the Swedish national team by becoming a professional. Therefore, after winning the Olympic gold medal, he no longer played any more international matches, with Italy now being his new home.

=== A rarely seen goal scorer ===

During his first full season in Italy, Nordahl won the league's top scorer award with 35 goals – a record that stood for 66 years. At the same time, he was joined at the club by two other Swedes, Gunnar Gren and Nils Liedholm, and over the next few years, they would establish an international reputation as a formidable trio – Gre-No-Li.

During his professional career in Italy from 1949 to 1958, Nordahl won the league's top scorer award 5 times (a Serie A record) and became incredibly popular throughout the football-loving country. In total, he scored 225 goals in 291 matches in Italy. Besides the Italian Silvio Piola (274 league goals) and his compatriot Francesco Totti, no one has scored as many goals in the top division of Italian football. However, the Swede has a significantly better goal-scoring ratio than Piola (0.77 goals per match compared to 0.51), who played for 24 seasons in Serie A. In his final years in Italy, Nordahl played for AS Roma (1956–58), where he also took on the role of player-coach towards the end. He still scored some goals there, although his shooting power wasn't as explosive as it once was.

=== The End of His Playing Career - and the Beginning of a New One ===

Gunnar Nordahl ended his playing career at Karlstad BIK, first as a player-coach and then as a coach alone, from 1959 to 1961. The goal was to promote the club to Allsvenskan (the top Swedish league), but they didn't get any closer than third place in Division 2. Nordahl's plan was then to return to Italy and continue his coaching career there, but a "ban on foreign coaches" meant that he stayed in Sweden instead.
