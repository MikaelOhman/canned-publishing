TL;DRNew regulations make it easier to continue using areas for shellfish cultivation if they are already approved and monitored. Only new health inspections are required if there is a significant change in the environment of the area.
In plain language

The EU has changed the regulations regarding the inspection of areas where live mussels are cultivated and sold as food. Previously, the rules were strict about when authorities had to conduct a sanitary inspection, which is a check to ensure that the water and environment are clean and safe.

The new rule states that if an area is already approved and part of a monitoring program, continuous inspections are not always necessary. Instead, authorities only need to monitor for significant changes in the environment or water quality that could affect safety.

However, if there is a major and lasting change in the environment, authorities must conduct a new inspection to ensure that the mussels remain safe to eat. The purpose of this change is to simplify the work for mussel farmers as long as environmental conditions are stable.

COMMISSION IMPLEMENTING REGULATION (EU) 2026/1406

amending Implementing Regulation (EU) 2019/627 as regards the requirements for health checks for the classification and monitoring of production and relaying areas for live molluscs.

THE EUROPEAN COMMISSION HAS ADOPTED THIS REGULATION,

having regard to the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union,

having regard to Regulation (EU) 2017/625 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 15 March 2017 on official controls and other official activities performed by Member States to ensure compliance with food and feed safety legislation, animal health and welfare rules, plant protection products, and amending Regulations (EC) No 999/2001, (EC) No 396/2005, (EC) No 1069/2009, (EC) No 1107/2009, (EU) No 1151/2012, (EU) No 652/2014, (EU) 2016/429 and (EU) 2016/2031, Council Regulations (EC) No 1/2005 and (EC) No 1099/2009, Council Directives 98/58/EC, 1999/74/EC, 2007/43/EC, 2008/119/EC and 2008/120/EC, and amending Regulations (EC) No 854/2004 and (EC) No 882/2004 of the European Parliament and of the Council, Council Directives 89/608/EEC, 89/662/EEC, 90/425/EEC, 91/496/EEC, 96/23/EC, 96/93/EC and 97/78/EC and Council Decision 92/438/EEC (the "official controls Regulation") ( 1 ), particularly Article 18.8 b, and

WHEREAS Commission Implementing Regulation (EU) 2019/627 ( 2 ) lays down uniform practical arrangements for the performance of official controls and the taking of official measures with regard to the production of products of animal origin intended for human consumption, including specific requirements for the classification of production and relaying areas for live molluscs.

Article 56 of the implementing regulation (EU) 2019/627 establishes rules regarding the sanitary inspection that competent authorities must carry out before classifying a production or processing area. According to Article 56.2 of this implementing regulation, competent authorities must conduct a sanitary inspection that meets the requirements of that article in all classified production and processing areas, unless such an inspection has already been performed.

Experience has shown that it is not necessary to perform a sanitary inspection in areas that were classified before the entry into force of implementing regulation (EU) 2019/627 and which are subject to a monitoring program according to Article 57 of this implementing regulation, unless monitoring data indicates significant changes that could permanently affect the classification of the area.

Implementing regulation (EU) 2019/627 should therefore be amended accordingly.

The measures prescribed in this regulation are consistent with the opinion of the Standing Committee on Plants, Animals, Food and Feed.

Article 56 of implementing regulation (EU) 2019/627 shall be amended as follows:

In paragraph 1, the introductory phrase shall be replaced with the following:

"Competent authorities shall carry out a sanitary inspection before classifying a new production or processing area that has not yet been classified, and if monitoring data collected in accordance with Article 59, or other information, may indicate to competent authorities significant changes in the environmental conditions of already classified production and processing areas, which could substantially and permanently affect the results of the previous sanitary inspection.

The sanitary inspection shall include"

This regulation enters into force on the twentieth day after its publication in the Official Journal of the European Union.

This regulation is fully binding and directly applicable in all member states.
