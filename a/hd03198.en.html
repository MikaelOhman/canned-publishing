In plain language

The government has proposed changes to the rules in the Education Act. The goal is to improve collaboration between schools and the working world, making it easier to find the right skills for jobs. A major new development is the introduction of practical assessments in vocational programs at the upper secondary school level and in adult education. This means that students can demonstrate their abilities through a practical assessment instead of just writing an assignment, which can help them find jobs more easily.

To offer a wider range of educational programs, the government proposes that schools be allowed to purchase educational services from other public providers. This would allow other schools or government agencies to handle certain aspects of vocational training, including the practical assessments. This is known as outsourcing, which means hiring someone else to perform a task.

Students attending special needs upper secondary schools will also have stronger rights. Students enrolled in individual programs will have the right to complete their education within four years, even if their study plan changes during that time. This applies as long as the student has started their education with their school provider, which is the organization responsible for the school.

The new rules regarding the purchase of educational services and the right to complete one's education will come into effect on July 1, 2026. The other changes to the law, concerning practical assessments, will come into effect on July 2, 2028.

Full text

The proposal includes suggested amendments to the Education Act (2010:800), which aim to strengthen cooperation between schools and the working world, and to improve the supply of skilled labor. The proposals entail the following:

  • **Vocational internships** will be introduced to improve students' opportunities in the labor market. In vocational programs in upper secondary school and in the national programs in adapted upper secondary school, a vocational internship should replace the final project, if such an internship exists. Students in adapted upper secondary school should have the option to complete a final project instead of a vocational internship, even when an internship is available. Vocational internships should also be offered in municipal adult education.
  • To be able to offer a wide range of educational programs, it should be possible in upper secondary school and adapted upper secondary school to outsource teaching tasks related to vocational subjects to a public entity within the school system, and not only to a private individual or legal entity. Vocational internships in these school forms, final projects within a vocational education, and final projects in adapted upper secondary school should also be able to be outsourced.
  • Students in individual programs in adapted upper secondary school should have the right to complete their education.

The amendments to the Education Act regarding expanded opportunities for outsourcing and the right to complete education in adapted upper secondary school will take effect on July 1, 2026. The other amendments to the law will take effect on July 2, 2028.
