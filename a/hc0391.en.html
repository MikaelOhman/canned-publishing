In plain language

The government wants to amend the law to require veterinarians to send more information to the Swedish Board of Agriculture (Jordbruksverket). This information concerns the medications used to treat animals and other methods employed to assist them. This information is referred to as animal health data.

To facilitate this work, the government also wants the Swedish Board of Agriculture to be able to share this information with private organizations. These organizations are involved in animal breeding and health and need this data to perform their work effectively. This means that the information will not be confined to the government agency.

To protect the individuals involved in these organizations, a confidentiality rule will be implemented. This means that those working with the information are prohibited from disclosing it to others. This ensures that important data can be shared without compromising privacy.

The new regulations are scheduled to come into effect on October 1, 2025. This proposal builds upon existing regulations implemented since April 2023, which govern the reporting of medications that can contribute to antibiotic resistance.

Full text

The proposal suggests expanding the obligation for veterinarians to report information about the use of medications for animal treatment and other animal care measures, referred to as animal health data, to the Swedish Board of Agriculture (Jordbruksverket). It also proposes a provision that allows the Board of Agriculture to disclose such information to private breeding and animal health organizations that require it for their operations. Furthermore, the proposal includes a provision establishing a duty of confidentiality for individuals who participate or have participated in the activities of a breeding or animal health organization regarding animal health data. These proposals complement the regulations regarding the collection and disclosure of information about the use of antimicrobial drugs for animal treatment, which came into effect on April 1, 2023.

The proposed amendments to the law are intended to come into force on October 1, 2025.
