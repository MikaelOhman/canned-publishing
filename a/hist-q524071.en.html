Peter Artedi, also Latinized as Petrus Arctædius, was born on February 27, 1705, in Anundsjö, and died on September 27, 1735, in Amsterdam due to drowning. He was a Swedish biologist considered the "father of ichthyology" (the study of fish). Together with Carl von Linné, Artedi laid the foundation for modern biological nomenclature and systematics.

== Biography ==

=== Early Life === The family name Artedi is a variation of Arctædius, the surname adopted by Peter Artedi's grandfather, Petrus Martini Arctædius, a son of a farmer from Ytterhiske who became a pastor in Nordmaling and a co-pastor in the parish of Umeå. His wife, Anna Olofsdotter Grubb, belonged to the Bure clan. The father, Olaus Arctædius (1670–1728), was an assistant to his father in Nordmaling and later became a co-pastor in Anundsjö, where his son, Petrus, and two daughters were born. All three children were from Olaus' second marriage to Helena Sidenia, the daughter of the pastor in Grundsunda, Petrus Sidenius (1592–1666), from Sidensjö, and Maria Mört, a surveyor's daughter. According to oral tradition, the parsonage where Petrus Artedi was born was located in the village of Norrmesunda. The family likely later moved to the village of Näs, as the pastor, Olof, and his wife are listed under Näs in the communion register for 1708 (Communion Book 1708, Anundsjö L I/1). The grandfather became a jubilee priest (a priest serving for fifty years) before King Charles XII granted Olaus Arctædius the position of pastor in 1716. Petrus Artedi was then eleven years old. In addition to his parents, the household in the parsonage in Nordmaling included two aunts of the father and the father's elderly parents. The father became involved in some scandals during his tenure. He was reportedly warned by the bishop for his association with the adventuress Maja Stina Fröhling. He allegedly behaved "scandalously" with her and supported her with church funds. The matter was investigated by the Svea Court of Appeal, and he died shortly thereafter, in 1728.

=== Siblings === Olaus and Helena had five children, three of whom lived to adulthood. The eldest was their daughter, Anna Maria. Peter was the middle child, and Elisabeth was the youngest.

Anna Maria married the merchant Peter Biur in Umeå, and Elisabeth married the pastor Jonas Liungberg in Umeå. It is to these brothers-in-law that Artedi requests financial assistance in connection with his trip abroad in September 1734.

=== School Years ===

In the same year that his father took up the position of pastor in Nordmaling, Petrus Artedi, the only son, began attending the gymnasium in Härnösand. After being ranked first in his class, he was allowed to skip a grade, and this happened again during his time at the gymnasium. He enrolled at Uppsala University on October 30, 1724, under the name Petrus Archtædius, and unlike his paternal and maternal ancestors, he chose natural sciences as his main field of study rather than theology. After completing a few obligatory semesters in theological and philosophical subjects, he began studying at the medical faculty. Shortly after his father's scandal in 1728, and due to his father's impending death, he returned to Nordmaling. During his stay, he took the opportunity to study the local flora. When he returned to Uppsala, he started using the name Artedi. In the same year, he met Carl von Linné, who was a few years younger and had heard about his intelligence and knowledge. The extent of Artedi's influence on Linné's development is always difficult to determine due to the limited sources available, but from Linné's own writings and comparisons of their works, it is clear that Artedi's impact was very significant, something that Linné readily acknowledges. Their acquaintance was also intense on a personal level, as Linné himself wrote in his biography of him. For example, there are no longer any illustrations or other paintings of Artedi, but Linné describes him as "tall and slender, with long black hair, quite similar in appearance to John Ray, very reserved but with a quick, steady, mature judgment; a man of old-fashioned faith and honor."

In the memorial written in 1905, Einar Lönnberg expands on Linné's description using words like "elegant," "serious," "solemn," and "pensive" – creating a sharp contrast with Linné, who characterized himself as "small," "busy," "hasty," and "quick."

=== In Nordmaling === During his time in Nordmaling, Artedi compiled the work "Flora of Nordmaling," where he systematically categorized the plants of the region into classes and divisions, including trees and shrubs, as well as herbs and grasses. Flowers were classified based on their shape, number of petals, and the appearance of their seed pods. The work was completed and dated February 24, 1729, and Gunnar Eriksson, in his "History of Botany in Sweden up to 1800," argued that Linnaeus' classification closely resembled the "Flora of Nordmaling"; Linnaeus' "Hortus uplandicus" appeared the year after. Artedi also inspired Linnaeus to undertake his journey to Lapland.

=== A widely knowledgeable researcher === A quote from Linnaeus highlights Artedi's extensive knowledge: "He was exceptionally well-versed in the humanities and modern languages, a learned philosopher with solid knowledge of medicine, and he understood everything about all possible subjects."
