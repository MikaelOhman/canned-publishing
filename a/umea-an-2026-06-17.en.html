TL;DRThe Elderly Care Committee in Umeå has temporarily suspended its decision regarding which company will be awarded the contract for home care services. They want the individuals working on the case to submit additional information before a final decision is made.
In plain language

The Elderly Care Committee held a meeting on June 17, 2026, to discuss home care services in Umeå. This concerns a procurement process, meaning the municipality is looking for the right company to provide home care services for adults.

The municipality follows a law called LOU, which stands for the "Law on Public Procurement." This law ensures that all companies have a fair chance to compete for the opportunity to provide the services. Two different committees within the municipality are collaborating on this process.

A group responsible for procurement has reviewed the responses submitted by various companies. They have prepared a proposal outlining which companies should be awarded the contract. This proposal is called a decision protocol.

Despite the proposal, the Elderly Care Committee decided not to make a decision at this time. They have sent the matter back to the administration, which is responsible for preparing the decisions. They want the administration to supplement the documentation with more information so that the committee has a complete and clear basis for making a decision before it is finalized.
