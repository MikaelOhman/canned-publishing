Full text

In this document, the government provides an overview of the operations of the Swedish National Pension Funds (AP Funds) through 2024. The document includes, among other things, a summary of the AP Funds' annual reports for 2024 and an evaluation of the AP Funds' operations since 2001. The document is submitted annually to the Parliament no later than June 1st. The document includes the following information:

  • In 2024, the global stock markets experienced strong growth, while interest rates fell and the Swedish krona weakened against both the euro and the US dollar.
  • The total return for the First, Second, Third, Fourth, and Sixth AP Funds (the buffer funds) amounted to 188 billion Swedish krona after expenses, which corresponds to a return of 9.5 percent. This result was primarily due to the strong performance of the stock markets.
  • Since the current pension system was introduced in 2001, the average annual return on the total assets of the buffer funds has been 6.3 percent. At the end of 2024, the total capital in the buffer funds amounted to 2,130.2 billion Swedish krona. The buffer funds have thus contributed positively to the long-term financing of the general income pension system.
  • Each buffer fund's board of directors sets the overall return target for the fund. The government's evaluation shows that the buffer funds have achieved the long-term return targets set by the boards.
  • The First through Fourth AP Funds have also fulfilled their role as buffers by managing ongoing contributions and payments for income-related pensions.
