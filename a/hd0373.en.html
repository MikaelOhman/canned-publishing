TL;DRSweden plans to terminate special tax agreements with several island nations. This means that the rules for how income from savings in these countries is taxed will be changed.
In plain language

The government has proposed a plan for Sweden to terminate agreements related to taxes on savings. These agreements are with several territories, including Anguilla, the Cayman Islands, the Virgin Islands, Montserrat, and the Turks and Caicos Islands.

The purpose is to repeal the current law on tax treaties. This means that the special rules regarding how income from savings in these territories is taxed in Sweden will be removed.

When the new rules come into effect depends on a decision by the government. Currently, the focus is on changing how Sweden cooperates with these specific territories regarding taxes.

Full text

The proposal suggests that Sweden should terminate the agreements regarding the taxation of income from savings with Anguilla, the Cayman Islands, the Virgin Islands, Montserrat, and the Turks and Caicos Islands.

It is also proposed that the law (2005:343) concerning agreements on the taxation of income from savings with Anguilla, Aruba, the Cayman Islands, Guernsey, the Isle of Man, Jersey, the Virgin Islands, Montserrat, the Dutch Antilles, and the Turks and Caicos Islands should cease to be valid on the date determined by the government.
