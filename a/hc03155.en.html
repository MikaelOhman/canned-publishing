Full text

Sweden is facing a serious security situation. The threats to our society are numerous and complex. Sweden needs to be prepared to handle attacks from other states and hostile actors, as well as other events that could place significant strain on society, such as a major natural disaster or a serious viral outbreak.

The government proposes changes to the constitution in order to strengthen constitutional preparedness and ensure that Sweden can be governed in a democratic, efficient, and lawful manner, even in times of war and other severe crises. These proposals primarily aim to provide the Parliament (Riksdag) and the government with better conditions to fulfill their duties and make necessary decisions on a legal basis in such situations. The following is proposed, among other things:

  • The Parliament's ability to convene will be strengthened. For example, a new provision will be introduced in the constitution that allows for the possibility of holding entirely digital parliamentary sessions in certain difficult situations.
  • The Parliament's war delegation will be maintained for times of war and the threat of war, and the rules governing the delegation will be adjusted. For example, the Speaker will have a role similar to that within the Parliament. Furthermore, a member who becomes a minister will no longer be able to be part of the delegation.
  • New provisions regarding legislation in serious peacetime crisis situations will be introduced. This regulation means that the Parliament, with a qualified majority, should be able to grant the government special powers to issue regulations if necessary to manage the crisis situation. These powers can be revoked by the Parliament at any time.

The proposed changes to the laws are scheduled to come into effect on January 1, 2027.
