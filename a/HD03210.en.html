In plain language

The government has proposed new regulations to protect funds within the social security system. This means that individuals and companies who cause incorrect payments to be made may be required to pay an administrative penalty. This also applies to those receiving state-funded dental care.

The Swedish Social Insurance Agency and the Swedish Pensions Agency will be responsible for determining whether these penalties should be applied. If an individual has knowingly provided false information or has been extremely careless, the agencies may also decide that the individual will not be eligible for certain benefits for a specific period. This is known as a restriction.

The proposal also includes changes to the rules regarding when suspected crimes must be reported. If an individual is already subject to a penalty for a mistake, it may not always be necessary to report it as a crime. This is to avoid the same action being punished twice in the same way.

If the Parliament approves the proposal, the new regulations are planned to come into effect on July 1, 2026. The proposal includes changes to several different laws that govern how benefits and allowances function in Sweden.

Full text

The proposal suggests introducing administrative penalties within the social security system to prevent incorrect payments and protect the social security system.

It proposes an administrative penalty in the form of a penalty fee for individuals and legal entities, as stipulated in the Social Security Code, and for healthcare providers, as stipulated in the Act (2008:145) on State Dental Care Subsidies, who have caused compensation to be paid incorrectly or in an amount that is too high. The Swedish Social Insurance Agency (Försäkringskassan) and the Swedish Pensions Agency (Pensionsmyndigheten) will decide on the penalty fee, and in certain cases, the prosecutor will bring proceedings regarding such a fee in cases of social security fraud.

Since the penalty fee sometimes replaces a criminal penalty for social security fraud, it is proposed to limit the obligation to report suspected crimes according to the Social Security Fraud Act (2007:612). This limitation applies only if the same person may receive an administrative penalty fee for the matter in question.

Finally, an administrative penalty in the form of a temporary ban on receiving a benefit according to the Social Security Code is proposed. The Swedish Social Insurance Agency and the Swedish Pensions Agency will be authorized to impose such a ban on an individual who, with respect to a matter that is relevant to the right to or the amount of the benefit, has knowingly or through gross negligence provided false information or failed to fulfill a duty or reporting obligation.

The proposed amendments are to come into force on July 1, 2026.

Proposed Resolution of the Parliament

Prop. 2025/26:210

Government's proposal:

  1. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Social Security Code.
  2. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Act (2008:145) on State Dental Care Subsidies.
  3. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Social Security Fraud Act (2007:612).
  4. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an Act on legal proceedings regarding administrative penalty fees in cases of social security fraud.
