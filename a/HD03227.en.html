Full text

Serious crime is increasingly affecting younger age groups. This trend must be reversed, and children must be protected from exploitation and involvement in criminal activity. A key element in crime prevention efforts is to identify young people who are at risk of adopting a criminal lifestyle and provide them with adequate support. Continuous work on crime prevention measures is essential. Furthermore, from both a societal and a victim's perspective, it is crucial that crimes can be investigated effectively, regardless of the age of the perpetrator. Therefore, there is a need for more effective tools to investigate crimes committed by young offenders.

The government proposes, among other things, the following:

  • More coercive measures should be allowed to be used against children who have not reached the age of criminal responsibility.
  • The coercive measures of search, seizure, and body search should be allowed to be used against children who have not reached the age of criminal responsibility in more cases.
  • Biometric data should be allowed to be collected from children in more cases.

The government also proposes the introduction of a coercive measure for the detention of a group of people and a special regulation for coercive measures in a preliminary investigation that is reopened to investigate whether there is grounds for reopening the case.

The proposed amendments to the law are scheduled to come into force on July 1, 2026.
