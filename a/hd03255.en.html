Sampling of data on household debt.

TL;DRThe government wants to amend the law so that the Central Bureau of Statistics (SCB) can collect more information about the amount of debt held by households, as well as the interest rates and repayments they are making. This is being done to better understand economic risks in society.
In plain language

The government has proposed expanding the functionality of a system called Fasit. Fasit is a tool used by the Central Bureau of Statistics, also known as SCB, to analyze how various economic regulations affect people. By adding information about debts and payments, it becomes possible to better understand how sensitive households are to changes in the economy.

To make this work, banks and other companies that provide mortgages must provide SCB with information about household debts and payments. It is important to know how much money is being allocated to debt repayment in order to plan the country's economic policy. This helps authorities to assess whether high interest rates or other costs are causing significant problems for families.

Despite the collection of more information, the rules regarding personal data are strict. Only data that is necessary should be used, and sensitive information such as health or ethnic origin should not be used. The goal is to obtain good statistics without infringing on people's privacy.

If the proposal is approved and the parliament votes in favor, the new rules will come into effect on August 1, 2026.

Full text

It is important for both individuals and economic development that households have the opportunity to obtain credit for consumption and home purchases. At the same time, high levels of debt can create vulnerability for individuals, the financial system, and the overall economy. For individual households, for example, high interest and repayment payments in relation to income can lead to increased vulnerability in the event of negative life events. To understand these risks at both the individual and system level, reliable statistics are needed. However, information about household finances is sensitive. Therefore, statistics should be limited to the most necessary information about household finances.

The proposal suggests that Statistics Sweden's (SCB) microsimulation model, the distribution analysis statistical system for incomes and transfers (Fasit), should be expanded to include data on household sector debt and debt payments. By processing this data in Fasit, it enables better analysis of individuals' and households' resilience and sensitivity to economic shocks. This can contribute to the decision-making basis for economic policy and help identify appropriate measures.

The proposed amendments to the law should come into force on August 1, 2026.
