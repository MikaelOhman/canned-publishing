Full text

The proposal suggests changes to the abortion law (1974:595). The purpose of these changes is to modernize the language of the law and to adapt it to medical advancements and the needs of pregnant women.

It should be clarified that a woman who is pregnant may have an abortion until the end of the 18th week of pregnancy.

Abortion and termination of pregnancy should be provided within the framework of a general hospital or another healthcare facility specifically adapted for this purpose and approved by the Swedish Inspectorate for Healthcare and Welfare. Abortion should be provided promptly.

Home abortions should be made possible by removing the requirement that abortions or terminations of pregnancy must be performed in a general hospital or another healthcare facility. The proposal also opens up the possibility for more flexible medication management and remote care. By removing the requirement that only those authorized to practice medicine can perform abortions, it becomes possible for, among others, midwives to independently perform medical abortions.

The proposed changes to the law are scheduled to come into effect on January 1, 2027. A healthcare facility that has been approved by the Swedish Inspectorate for Healthcare and Welfare before the law comes into effect may continue to operate without a new approval from the Inspectorate for Healthcare and Welfare.
