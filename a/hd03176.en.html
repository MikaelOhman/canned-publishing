Concentration of certain activities within the County Administrative Boards.

TL;DRThe government wants to transfer certain tasks from several regional administrative boards (länsstyrelser) to fewer of them. The purpose is to make the work more efficient and to improve the quality of decision-making.
In plain language

The County Administrative Boards are the authorities responsible for a wide range of issues in each county. The government now wants to amend the law so that certain specific tasks no longer have to be handled by each individual County Administrative Board, but can be consolidated in fewer locations.

The tasks to be transferred involve smaller matters, such as certain types of lease agreements for land and personal cases. The government wants to be able to determine which County Administrative Boards will handle these specific tasks, rather than having it explicitly stated in the law.

Because the tasks being transferred are few, the change is not expected to significantly impact the work of the County Administrative Boards. The goal is to make it easier and better for both the authority and for the individuals seeking decisions.

If the proposal is approved by the Parliament, the new rules will come into effect on January 1, 2027.

Full text

This proposal outlines the legislative changes necessary for the government to consolidate certain activities of county administrative boards into a smaller number of such boards. The activities proposed for consolidation include the deposit of rent and lease payments according to the Land Code, the termination of established tenancy rights and land guilds, and individual cases.

The proposals in this document involve removing the legal provisions that specify which county administrative board is responsible for handling a particular matter, and instead, including a statement that the government appoints the county administrative board(s) responsible for certain tasks.

The activities proposed for consolidation are of very limited scope. The proposals are assessed to have limited consequences for the activities of county administrative boards and should, in the long term, result in efficiency gains and improved quality for both the boards and individuals.

The legislative changes are proposed to come into effect on January 1, 2027.
