Court issues fine for Nordic Best Energi's telemarketing. News Published: July 1, 2026

The Patent and Market Court agrees with the Swedish Consumers Agency (KO) and orders Nordic Best Energi AB to pay a fine of 400,000 SEK. The company has contacted consumers and falsely claimed to represent or collaborate with their existing electricity supplier. The court also prohibits Nordic Best Energi from continuing with the current marketing practices. If the company violates the ban again, they risk a higher fine of 800,000 SEK. The Swedish Consumers Agency (KO) issued a warning to the company in November 2024. This was after reports from consumers indicated that Nordic Best Energi's telemarketing involved claiming to be calling on behalf of or in collaboration with the consumer's existing electricity company. The warning included a fine of 400,000 SEK. Despite this, the company continued with the unauthorized marketing, which led KO to request that the fine be imposed in November 2025. The court's decision is clear: the company must pay the fine, and the court also prohibits Nordic Best Energi from continuing its marketing activities. The verdict can be appealed.
