Karl Henriksson (Horn), born around 1550 in Kankas, Masku, Finland, and died in 1601, was a Swedish district judge, lawman, governor, field marshal, and member of the royal council.

== Biography == In October 1566, Karl Henriksson was employed at King Erik XIV's court as a chamberlain. Because he was later described as learned, it has been assumed that he, like his younger brothers later on, had studied abroad. At the time of the Sture murders in May 1567, he belonged to the king's inner circle. When the king's brothers rebelled in July 1568, he was on the king's side and was tasked with securing Nyköping Castle for the king. This, along with a subsequent attempt at retaking the castle, failed. Even during the king's decisive defeat at Hölö, he remained loyal to the king, but subsequently left Sweden for Estonia.

=== The War in the East === The Nordic Twenty-Five Years' War began when Magnus of Ösel, with Russian support and troops, attacked Swedish Estonia. In August 1570, he led troops to Reval (Tallinn) and Wittenstein, both of which were besieged. Among the war leaders in Reval was Karl Henriksson, who just a week after the arrival of the Russians, led a successful sortie from the city. The Russian troops withdrew in March 1571 but did not leave Livonia. In July 1571, he launched an attack against remaining Russian forces, defeating them completely. From that year until 1575, he served as a cavalry commander for a unit of German cavalry. In August 1572, the Russians declared war on Sweden, and at the end of the year, they besieged Wittenstein, which fell. They then turned their attention to the fortresses in western Estonia but were unable to capture them. A Swedish army defeated the larger Russian army at Lode in January 1573. Karl Henriksson participated in the battle. The following year, Horn served as a subordinate commander under Pontus de la Gardie in various attempts to recapture fortresses in Estonia. In May 1574, Horn was appointed governor of Reval but continued to participate in De la Gardie's campaigns. In the autumn, Horn represented Reval in peace negotiations initiated by the Danes. That year, he was rewarded with the position of district judge in Borgå. This assignment lasted only a few years; later, he briefly served as district judge in Österbotten and in Masku.

Since De la Gardie left Estonia at the beginning of 1576 for diplomatic missions abroad, Karl Henriksson effectively acted as governor until his father, Henrik Klasson, took over in September, with his son serving as a subordinate commander. The difficult situation in Reval (Tallinn), the only remaining part of Swedish Estonia, led to an uprising in late summer 1576, when infantry from Dalarna deserted, and some cavalry from Uppland attempted to kill him. On January 22, 1577, a Russian siege army appeared outside Reval. The defense was organized so that Henrik Klasson was responsible for the castle, while Karl managed the city. The siege lasted until March 13 but did not lead to any decisive outcome. From Reval, the Russian troops moved on to Dagö and Ösel, where they plundered before turning south and capturing Pärnu. Henrik Klasson then became governor of Finland, with his son as field marshal. In the winter of 1578, he led a raiding expedition from Viborg to Ingermanland. He also participated in the Battle of Wenden, where a Swedish-Polish army defeated Russian troops. In September 1579, Swedes attacked Narva at the king's order, but Henrik Klasson became so ill that he had to relinquish command to his son, Karl. When the siege had to be broken off, Henrik fell out of favor with the king.

=== The Tide of War Turns === In 1580, King Johan appointed Pontus De la Gardie as the new military leader in the east, hoping for greater success. Karl was part of his war council. In November, Swedish forces captured the fortress of Kexholm, and he distinguished himself particularly there. He was appointed Field Marshal that same year. Later that autumn, he led an expedition to Ingermanland, but with limited success. In January 1581, the Swedish army once again crossed the ice of the Gulf of Finland into Ingermanland. He led the siege of Rakvere, which surrendered in March. The Swedish troops then returned to Viborg. While De la Gardie was in Stockholm during the summer of 1581 to obtain supplies for Livland, Karl Henriksson captured the fortresses in Wiek. After De la Gardie's return, the Swedish military turned eastward. Karl participated in the assault on Narva in September, and within a month, the Swedes captured the fortresses in western Ingermanland. This gave the Swedish kingdom control over the shores of the Gulf of Finland. The king rewarded him in various ways and appointed him governor of Narva. He was replaced by Herman Fleming in the autumn of 1582 but returned to the position the following spring. At that time, he was also appointed a member of the delegation tasked with negotiating peace with the Russians. On August 10, 1583, a three-year armistice was signed. He left his post in Narva in the summer of 1584, and the following spring, he was appointed as an assistant to Henrik Klasson, who was the governor of Finland and Field Marshal. He was also a member of the delegation that negotiated with the Russians in the autumn of 1585 regarding an extension of the armistice. The meeting took place at the mouth of the river Plyussa, just like the previous meeting.
