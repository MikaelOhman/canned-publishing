In plain language

A proposal has been submitted to establish a new national park in the Nämdö archipelago. A national park is an area with enhanced protection to ensure that nature can thrive undisturbed. This marine area boasts exceptional natural beauty that has remained largely untouched by human development.

The purpose of this initiative is to preserve the beautiful coastal landscape and archipelago. The goal is to ensure the health of the environment and biodiversity, meaning all the different plants and animals, and to prevent their destruction. The area should remain as close as possible to its current state.

This is a significant development, as it will be Sweden's very first marine national park in the Baltic Sea. This means that the protection will focus on the life beneath the surface and in the shallow coastal waters.

In addition to protecting nature, the park is expected to contribute to sustainable development in the area. This means that the aim is for both people and nature to be able to live and thrive in a way that benefits the future.

Full text

The proposal suggests the establishment of the Nämdö Archipelago National Park. The area intended to be included in the national park possesses exceptionally high qualities, representing an unspoiled, typical, and biologically valuable marine and archipelago environment. The area offers good opportunities for visitors. The purpose of the national park is to preserve a representative and diverse marine and archipelago landscape of the Baltic Sea in a largely unchanged state. The new national park will provide enhanced and strengthened protection for the marine environment and biodiversity in the area. The national park is expected to contribute to sustainable local and regional development. The Nämdö Archipelago National Park will be Sweden's first marine national park in the Baltic Sea.
