The Swedish National Audit Office is an agency that reviews how the state uses its money. They have written a report showing that there are problems with how certain agencies charge fees to companies. The problem is that the same agency is both responsible for determining how high the fee should be and for deciding how the money will be used.

When agencies are allowed to retain their own revenues, it violates important rules about how the state's budget should be managed. The National Audit Office believes that this can lead to inefficiency, meaning that the money is not used as effectively as it should be. They also point out that the National Agency for Public Management, an agency that oversees the state's finances, could do more to help with this issue.

Full text

In this document, the government addresses the observations and recommendations made by the Swedish National Audit Office (Riksrevisionen) in its report "Fee-based services – risk of inefficiency when authorities determine and manage the fees" (RiR 2025:9).

The Swedish National Audit Office believes that the governance of public authorities' fee-based services provided to businesses is not sufficiently effective. The audit office notes that authorities conducting public fee-based activities where they both determine the fee amount and manage the revenue represent exceptions to important budgetary principles, and that these exceptions pose a risk of inefficiency. The audit office also notes that the National Agency for Public Management (Ekonomistyrningsverket) plays an important role in authorities' fee setting, and that the agency has the potential to contribute further regarding other authorities' public fee-based services.

The government welcomes the audit and agrees in part with the Swedish National Audit Office's observations. The government intends to continue working on improving the reporting of fees to enhance the conditions for follow-up, and to increasingly assess whether the need for existing exemptions in the area of fees remains.

With this document, the government considers the Swedish National Audit Office's report to be finalized.
