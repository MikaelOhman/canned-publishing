Full text

The proposal includes suggested amendments to the Education Act (2010:800) aimed at improving support in schools. The proposals include the following:

It should be clarified in the Education Act that all children and students in instruction should be given guidance and encouragement to ensure they can follow the instruction. The regulations regarding the guarantee of early support measures and additional accommodations should be abolished. Instead, standardized tests will be conducted at the beginning of the autumn term in certain grade levels to identify students with support needs. Students should be provided with remedial instruction at an early stage in compulsory schools, upper secondary schools, and special upper secondary schools in the subjects of Swedish, Swedish as a second language, and mathematics.

The provisions regarding special support should be amended. The responsible teacher or mentor should report to the principal if a student's need for special support needs to be investigated, and such a need should be investigated earlier than currently. Decisions regarding special support in smaller teaching groups or as individual instruction should be facilitated. Decisions regarding an adapted study plan should only be made if all other possibilities for special support have been exhausted or are deemed inappropriate.

Teachers in municipal adult education should have access to, or the opportunity for, consultation with staff with special pedagogical expertise.

The changes to the Education Act are proposed to come into effect on July 1, 2028.
