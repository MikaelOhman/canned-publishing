The budget proposal contains the government's proposal for the state budget for 2026, as well as other proposals and assessments that follow from the Rules of Procedure of the Parliament and the Budget Act (2011:203).

Proposals for Parliamentary Decisions

Government Proposals:

  1. The Riksdag (Swedish Parliament) adopts the proposed amendment to the Budget Act (2011:203) (sections 3.1 and 5.5).

Economic Policy Guidelines and the Draft State Budget for 2026:

  1. The Riksdag approves the guidelines for economic and budget policy (section 1).
  2. The Riksdag sets the expenditure ceiling for the state, including the pension system, at 1,936 billion Swedish kronor for 2026 and 2,009 billion Swedish kronor for 2027, due to technical adjustments and fiscal reasons (section 5.2).
  3. The Riksdag sets the expenditure ceiling for the state, including the pension system, at 2,105 billion Swedish kronor for 2028 (section 5.2).
  4. The Riksdag establishes a goal for the financial savings of the public sector, aiming for a balanced saving over the economic cycle, starting in 2027 (section 5.5).
  5. The Riksdag approves the calculation of state revenue in the 2026 budget (section 6.1 and Appendix 1, section 2).
  6. The Riksdag approves the preliminary calculation of state revenue for 2027 and 2028 as a guideline for the government's budget work (section 6.1 and Table 6.1).
  7. The Riksdag decides on the allocation of expenditure to different areas for 2026 (section 7.2 and Table 7.1).
  8. The Riksdag approves the calculation of changes in appropriation reserves for 2026 (section 7.2 and Table 7.1).
  9. The Riksdag approves the calculation of expenditure for the pension system, separate from the state budget, for 2026 (section 7.2 and Table 7.1).
  10. The Riksdag approves the preliminary allocation of expenditure to different areas for 2027 and 2028 as a guideline for the government's budget work (section 7.3 and Table 7.3).

  13. The Riksdag approves the calculation of the cash flow correction for 2026 (section 8.2 and Table 8.1).
  14. The Riksdag authorizes the government to decide on loans from the National Debt Office for investments in fixed assets used in state activities, totaling up to 74,900,000,000 Swedish kronor, including previous borrowings (section 10.2).
  15. The Riksdag authorizes the government to decide on credits for the interest accounts of government agencies at the National Debt Office, totaling up to 23,400,000,000 Swedish kronor, including previously utilized credits (section 10.2).
  16. The Riksdag authorizes the government, within the limitations of Section 3, Paragraph 8, second sentence, of the Budget Act (2011:203), to exceed an appropriation not intended for administrative purposes if a parliamentary decision to change the appropriation cannot be awaited, and the exceedance falls within the established expenditure ceiling for the state (section 10.7).
