Stricter rules for young offenders.

TL;DRThe government wants to tighten the rules for young people who commit serious crimes. This includes making the penalties harsher and removing certain age-related reductions in sentences.
In plain language

The government has proposed new laws to address the increasing crime rate among young people. Starting September 10, 2026, stricter penalties will be imposed on individuals under 18 who commit serious crimes. A key change is that the age of criminal responsibility, meaning the age at which a person can be convicted of a crime, will be lowered to 14 years for serious offenses, but only for a period of five years.

For young people over 18 who are still considered "young" under the law, the so-called youth discount will be completely eliminated. This means they will no longer receive reduced sentences simply because of their age. For those under 18, the reduction in sentence will decrease, and the maximum possible prison sentence will be increased to 18 years.

In addition to harsher penalties, the monitoring of young offenders will also become stricter. This means that individuals subject to youth supervision will be monitored more closely. The purpose of all these changes is to make the justice system more credible and clear when it comes to dealing with juvenile crime.

Full text

In recent years, organized crime has increasingly impacted various aspects of society. Serious crimes committed by young people are particularly concerning and are occurring at younger ages. To ensure that legislation addresses the needs arising from this trend in criminal activity, and to maintain the credibility and legitimacy of the penal system, the rules for juvenile offenders need to be strengthened. Therefore, the government proposes the following measures:

  • The age of criminal responsibility will be lowered to 14 years for serious crimes, for a period of five years.
  • The youth discount will be completely abolished for young adults.
  • The youth discount will be reduced, and the maximum prison sentence will be increased to 18 years for offenders under 18 years old.
  • The supervision measure for juveniles will be strengthened.
  • The regulations regarding breaches of probation orders for juvenile care and community service will be tightened.

These legislative changes are proposed to come into effect on September 10, 2026.
