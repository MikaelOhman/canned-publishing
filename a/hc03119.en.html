A more cohesive framework for the liability of creditors of the Central Student Aid Agency.

The goal is for CSN to retain more responsibility for its own claims, rather than having the Swedish Tax Agency take over those responsibilities.
In plain language

Starting September 1, 2025, significant changes are planned in how government debts are managed. Previously, it was decided that the Swedish Tax Agency (Skatteverket) would take over information about debts from CSN (Centrala studiestödsnämnden – the Swedish Council for Higher Education Financing) when someone declares bankruptcy or undergoes debt restructuring. A creditor is someone who has the right to be paid when someone owes money.

The Swedish Tax Agency's responsibilities are now being modified through a new proposal from the government. Instead of the Tax Agency taking over the data, the responsibility for these debts will be distributed in a more efficient manner. This means that the Tax Agency will no longer take over information about the claims that CSN has made against its customers.

The proposal also includes changes affecting the Swedish Enforcement Agency (Kronofogdemyndigheten), the agency that helps to collect debts. The new regulations will determine how the Enforcement Agency can grant more time for payment or halt the collection of debts originating from CSN.

If the Swedish Parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new laws will come into effect on September 1, 2025. The purpose is to make the work related to these debts more coherent and clear for all involved agencies.

Full text

The proposal includes suggested amendments to the law (2007:324) concerning the Swedish Tax Agency's handling of certain creditor-related matters, and to the law (1993:891) concerning the collection of state debts, etc.

Due to already decided changes in the Code of Civil Procedure, the Swedish Tax Agency will take over the Central Student Aid Agency's (CSN) creditor-related tasks in cases of debt restructuring, bankruptcy, and settlements, starting on September 1, 2025. However, the changes now proposed mean that the Swedish Tax Agency will no longer take over any creditor-related tasks from CSN. These changes aim to ensure an efficient distribution of creditor responsibility for debts based on decisions made by CSN.

The proposal also includes certain consequential amendments regarding the Swedish Enforcement Agency's ability to grant postponements and suspend the collection of debts based on CSN decisions.

The proposed amendments to the laws are intended to come into force on September 1, 2025.
