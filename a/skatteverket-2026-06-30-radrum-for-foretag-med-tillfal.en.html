Temporary relief options for companies experiencing payment difficulties.

Source Skatteverket Read the original at Skatteverket →2026-06-30
TL;DRStarting June 30, 2026, a new regulation will be implemented that provides greater security for managers of well-managed companies in the event of unexpected difficulties paying taxes.
In plain language

A legal entity, such as a limited company or an association, must always pay its taxes and fees. If the manager of the company fails to ensure that these payments are made, the manager may become personally liable and be forced to pay with their own money if they have been grossly negligent.

To protect well-managed companies that experience sudden financial difficulties, a new measure called "leniency" is being introduced. This rule provides managers with more flexibility and security in certain situations. However, this does not mean that the company can avoid paying its taxes; rather, it means that the manager will not be personally penalized as long as the company fundamentally has sound finances.

The Swedish Tax Agency (Skatteverket) will grant this leniency to those who apply for it, provided there are no clear reasons against it. This could be the case if the company is poorly organized in its paperwork or if it has already received assistance with the same problem previously. The new rule comes into effect on June 30, 2026, and applications can be submitted through the Swedish Tax Agency's website.

A legal entity, such as a limited company, foundation, or association, is required to pay taxes and fees. The person representing the legal entity has a responsibility to ensure that payments are made and may therefore be personally liable if they have acted grossly negligently or intentionally.

The "buffer period" legislation is intended for representatives of companies that essentially have sound financial management but have experienced unexpected payment difficulties. It does not affect the legal entity's obligation to pay, meaning the company must still pay taxes and fees on time.

The Swedish Tax Agency should grant a "rådrum" (a form of legal protection) unless there are specific reasons against it. Reasons that might prevent the granting of a "rådrum" could include deficiencies in the company's accounting, or if the company or its representative has outstanding taxes or fees, in addition to those included in the "rådrum" application, totaling at least half a base amount. Another reason for denying a "rådrum" is if one has already been granted previously.

The regulations regarding "rådrum" aim to prevent well-managed companies from being shut down solely due to the risk of the representative facing personal liability.

This new regulation comes into effect on June 30, 2026. Applications can be submitted most easily online through your personal account on skatteverket.se.
