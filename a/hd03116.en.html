A new feature for operational crisis management in the financial sector.

Source Riksdagen Read the original at Riksdagen →2026-02-17
TL;DRSweden aims to establish a new group to assist in managing major technical failures within banking and payment systems. The goal is to prevent technical problems from quickly escalating into large-scale crises that affect society as a whole.
In plain language

If critical services such as banks and payment systems were to cease functioning, it could create significant problems for all people in Sweden. Therefore, a new group is proposed, called a function for operational crisis management. This group will work to prevent technical failures from escalating.

The Swedish National Bank (Riksbank), which is Sweden's central bank, will lead this effort. Other important authorities, such as the Swedish Financial Supervisory Authority (Finansinspektionen) and the National Debt Office (Riksgäldskontoret), will also participate. Certain private companies in the financial sector, such as banks and similar institutions, will also be involved in the group.

All members of the group must share the information needed to resolve problems. At the same time, there are regulations regarding confidentiality, which means that sensitive information about individuals and companies must be protected and not disseminated.

The proposal also means that the Riksbank must cooperate with other authorities, such as the Swedish Civil Contingencies Agency (Myndigheten för samhällsskydd och beredskap), in preparing Sweden for crises. If the proposal is approved, the new regulations will come into effect on July 1, 2026.

Full text

A serious disruption in the financial system, where critical financial services are interrupted, can quickly lead to a severe crisis in society. Such a disruption does not necessarily imply a financial crisis, but can nevertheless lead to a societal crisis. To prevent such crises from escalating, it is proposed to introduce a new operational crisis management function to handle serious disruptions in the financial system. This crisis management function will not be part of any authority and will not assume any formal powers from the participants.

Regarding the composition and operation of the crisis management function, the following is proposed:

  • The National Bank (Riksbanken) should be responsible for leading the crisis management function.
  • In addition to the National Bank, the Swedish Financial Supervisory Authority (Finansinspektionen), the National Debt Office (Riksgäldskontoret), and certain companies in the financial sector should be included in the function. The National Bank should also be able to invite other actors to participate in the function.
  • Authorities and companies participating in the crisis management function should provide the necessary information to other participants in the function.
  • The principle of confidentiality to protect information about the interests of individuals should apply to the activities of an authority that involve participation in the crisis management function.

In addition, the National Bank is proposed to have a duty to cooperate with the Swedish Financial Supervisory Authority, the National Debt Office, the Swedish Civil Defence Agency, and other relevant authorities in matters relating to peacetime crisis situations and heightened readiness.

The proposed amendments to the law are expected to come into force on July 1, 2026.
