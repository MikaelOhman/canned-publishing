Magnus Henriksson

TL;DRMagnus Henriksson was a Danish prince who became King of Sweden for one year in the 12th century. He died in battle shortly after assuming power.
In plain language

Magnus Henriksson was born in the early 12th century and was the son of a Danish prince. He married Birgitta, who was his step-sister. In 1160, he seized power as King of Sweden by killing the then-ruler, Saint Erik.

His reign as king was very short because he died in battle near Örebro the following year. After his death, another man named Karl Sverkersson took over as leader of the country. There are different historical opinions about the exact year that Erik died, which makes it difficult to know precisely how long Magnus held power.

There are traces of Magnus's presence in several places, for example, through land he granted to Vreta monastery. Although some memorials may be confused with other people named Magnus, it is clear that he was a significant figure during his time.

Full text

Magnus Henriksson, first mentioned in 1148 and died in 1161, was a Danish prince who ruled as King of Sweden from 1160 to 1161. He was the son of the Danish Prince Henrik Skadelår (of the Estrid dynasty) and Ingrid Ragnvaldsdotter, and the grandson of the Swedish King Inge the Elder (of the Stenkil dynasty).

Magnus married his step-sister Birgitta (also called Brigida), who was an illegitimate daughter of King Harald Gille, in her second of three marriages. He seized power in 1160 by attacking and killing Saint Eric. According to Snorre Sturlasson, he had his brother Ragnvald Henriksson as his jarl (earl). The following year, he himself was defeated and killed in the Battle of Örebro, and was succeeded by Karl Sverkersson, the leader of the opposition.

According to Bengt R. Jansson, Eric was actually killed in 1162, which complicates Magnus's position in history as a Swedish king.

Magnus and Birgitta had no known children.

According to Nils Ahnlund, who also cites Sture Bolin on this matter, there is strong evidence that the gravestone on a monument in Vreta Abbey, dedicated to King Magnus Nilsson (his father's cousin), actually refers to this King Magnus, and that his grave is located somewhere within the abbey grounds. It is said that Magnus, like his brother Ragnvald, donated land to the abbey. However, the burial chamber named after Magnus Nilsson is likely a family vault for the knight Magnus Bengtsson the Elder (died 1263) or his grandson, Magnus Bengtsson the Younger.
