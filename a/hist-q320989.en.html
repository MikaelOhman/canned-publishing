During his reign, he sent soldiers to Estonia to fight and attempt to spread Christianity. Many of his closest friends and advisors died in battle during this time. For the son of Sverker the Elder, see Johan Sverkersson the Elder.

Johan Sverkersson, also known as Johan I, often referred to as Jon in older sources, was born in 1201 and died on March 10, 1222. He was King of Sweden from 1216. Johan was the son of King Sverker the Younger and Ingegärd Birgersdotter, daughter of Birger Brosa. He died unmarried and without children. Johan Sverkersson was the last ruler from the Sverker dynasty.

== Biography == In 1216, he was elected king, likely due to the influence of his powerful maternal lineage, and crowned in Linköping in 1219. According to the Westrogothic Law's royal list, he was described as "immature" for his age, very benevolent, and ruled for three winters. He died unexpectedly on Visingsö and was buried in Alvastra monastery. During his short reign, a Swedish expedition was launched into Estonia. During this campaign, Johan's relative, Earl Karl Jarl, and his chancellor, Bishop Karl Magnusson (of the Bjälbo dynasty) in Linköping, were killed in the Battle of Leal (Lihula) on August 8, 1220, against the pagan Estonians, after King Johan had returned to Sweden. Johan and his allies did make some conquests in Estonia, but nothing that proved lasting. The Christianization efforts also failed because both Danes and the Teutonic Order had already been there, converted people, and left a German bishop behind. Johan maintained good relations with the Catholic hierarchy, even though the Pope preferred Erik Eriksson as king. He confirmed and expanded several privileges granted to church institutions by his predecessors. With him, the Sverker royal dynasty came to an end.

The designation "the Younger" is not contemporary; it first appears in the Little Rhyme Chronicle and in Ericus Olai's work, and likely stems from a misunderstanding of older Latin royal lists that referred to him as "Johannes, Suercheri junioris filius," meaning "Johan, son of Sverker the Younger."
