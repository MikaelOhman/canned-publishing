In plain language

The government has proposed changes to the laws governing the chartering and leasing of large vessels. This concerns what is known as "bareboat chartering," which means renting an entire ship without also renting the crew.

The proposal includes the creation of a new section within the Swedish ship register, which is the official list of all registered vessels in Sweden. This new section aims to make it easier to track foreign ships that are chartered in Sweden.

By clarifying the regulations, the hope is that Swedish shipping companies, i.e., companies that own and operate ships, will be able to plan their operations more effectively. It should also become easier for them to obtain funding and financing to purchase new ships.

If the Parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new regulations will come into effect on February 1, 2026. The proposal involves changes to several different laws related to maritime affairs, such as the Swedish Maritime Code and the law on the registration of vessels.

Full text

The proposal suggests expanding and amending the regulations regarding the chartering of foreign ships and the leasing of Swedish ships, a practice known as "bareboat charter" in English. This includes clarifying the specific conditions under which such chartering can occur and how the registration of foreign ships will be handled in the Swedish ship registry. The Swedish ship registry will be supplemented with a section specifically for bareboat charters, aiming to improve the opportunities for Swedish shipping companies to organize their operations efficiently and finance new vessels.

The proposed amendments to the laws are suggested to come into effect on February 1, 2026.
