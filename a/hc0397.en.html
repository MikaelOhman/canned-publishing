A new regulation regarding interest rate difference compensation – reduced obstacles to early repayment of mortgage loans.

Source Riksdagen Read the original at Riksdagen →2025-01-30

The government proposes a new model for calculating the interest difference compensation when a fixed-rate mortgage is paid off early. The current model results in unjustified costs for many consumers who want to pay off their mortgage early. Therefore, the government proposes a new standard rule for interest difference compensation in the proposal.

The proposal aims to strengthen consumer protection, increase competition in the mortgage market, and reduce obstacles for consumers who need to fix their interest rate. The new standard rule means that the consumer's agreed-upon interest rate will no longer be the basis for the calculation. Instead, the compensation will be determined based on the change in the general interest rate environment that corresponds to the lender's direct costs for the early repayment. The market interest rate for agreements to exchange interest payments (a so-called "swap rate") will be used as the reference rate.

The proposed amendment to the law is scheduled to come into effect on July 1, 2025.
