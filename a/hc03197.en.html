In plain language

The EU has adopted a media freedom regulation designed to protect the independence and diversity of the media. To ensure its full implementation in Sweden, the government proposes a new supplementary law.

This law includes provisions that require entities providing information under the EU regulation to submit that information, along with details about its location, to a supervisory authority.

It also introduces regulations regarding the assessment of mergers between media companies, the authority's supervisory powers, and the process for appealing the authority's decisions.

Full text

The proposal suggests a new law with supplementary provisions to the EU's Media Freedom Act. This law includes the provisions necessary to ensure that the Media Freedom Act can be implemented and have an effective impact in Sweden. It is proposed that entities providing information according to the EU regulation must submit this information and details on where it is accessible to the supervisory authority. The law also proposes provisions regarding assessments of company concentrations in the media market and regarding supervision. Furthermore, provisions are proposed regarding the supervisory authority's powers and the right to appeal the authority's decisions.

The proposal also includes a law amending the new law. These changes are necessary because certain provisions of the regulation are scheduled to come into effect at a later date.

The new law is proposed to come into force on January 1, 2026, and the amendments to the law are proposed to come into force on May 8, 2027.
