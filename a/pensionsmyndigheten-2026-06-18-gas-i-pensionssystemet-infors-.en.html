In plain language

The Swedish Parliament has decided to introduce a mechanism called a "gas" into the pension system. This means that if there is an excess of funds in the state's pension system, these funds will be distributed to individuals instead of remaining within the system. This applies to both those who have already retired and those who are still working and saving for their future pensions.

The Swedish Pensions Agency will calculate the amount of surplus funds each year. If the system has a significant surplus, meaning there is considerably more money than required for payouts, these excess funds will be distributed as an additional supplement to existing pensions. This will result in an extra portion added to your pension income.

These new rules will come into effect starting in 2027. The distributed funds will be added to both your basic pension and your supplementary pension. Even though the money appears alongside your regular pension statement, it is these additional funds that make a difference.

June 18, 2026 | News

On June 17th, the parliament decided to introduce what is known as a "buffer" into the pension system. This means that any surplus generated within the earnings-related pension system will be distributed to pensioners and pension savers.

The Swedish Pensions Agency (Pensionsmyndigheten) is responsible for calculating and providing the necessary information to determine whether any surplus payments will be made from year to year.

The changes to the law will come into effect on August 1, 2026, and will apply starting in 2027.

In the years when the buffer generates a payout, it will benefit both current pensioners and those who are still contributing to their pension. This means that individuals receiving pensions and those actively earning pension credits will both receive the benefit. The amount will be added to the earned pension and will appear in the statement (often referred to as the "orange envelope"), but will not be separately itemized. The affected types of pensions are earnings-related pension and supplementary pension.

The Pensions Group is a cross-party working group with representatives from all parliamentary parties, responsible for managing and developing the public pension system.

In August 2025, the group reached an agreement to introduce a buffer into the earnings-related pension system to enable the distribution of surplus funds accumulated within the system. Payouts should occur when the assets in the earnings-related pension system exceed liabilities by more than 15 percent, that is, at a balance ratio of 1.15.

Since the introduction of the current pension system, there has also been a balancing mechanism, or "brake," built into the system. This mechanism activates when there is a deficit in the earnings-related pension system.
