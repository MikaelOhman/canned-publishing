Adolf Johan of Palatinate-Zweibrücken, born on October 11, 1629, at Stegeborg Castle in Östergötland (now part of the municipality of Söderköping), and died there on October 14, 1689, was a Swedish Field Marshal, Lord High Constable, and Generalissimo. Adolf Johan was also Count Palatine and German Prince, and he became a Prince of Sweden as the brother of King Charles X Gustav.

== Biography == Adolf Johan of Palatinate-Zweibrücken was the youngest son of Count John Casimir of Palatinate-Zweibrücken, Duke of Palatinate-Kleeburg, and the daughter of the Swedish King Charles IX, Katarina Vasa (who died when Adolf Johan was nine years old). He held the title of Count Palatine in the Rhineland, as heir to the Duchy of Kleeburg. His father, John Casimir, inherited the County of Kleeburg in Alsace in 1604 from his father, John I of Palatinate-Zweibrücken, which consisted of two castles, a town, and about ten villages. It was in this capacity that he held the title of Count Palatine. Count Palatine was a German princely title. Adolf Johan's father, John Casimir, was an ancestor of the so-called Palatinate line of the Swedish throne, a branch of the House of Wittelsbach. In 1651, his father received Stegeborg as a maintenance fief in lieu of the bride price that had not been paid from his marriage to Gustav II Adolf's sister, Katarina Vasa.

Adolf Johan was therefore the brother of the Swedish King Charles X Gustav, and upon his death, Adolf Johan was initially buried in Riddarholms Church, but later moved to Strängnäs.

=== Adolf Johan's Upbringing ===

Adolf Johan was the brother of Christina Magdalena (1616–1662), who married Margrave Frederick VI of Baden-Durlach, and Karl X Gustav (1622–1660), who became King of Sweden. He also had sisters Maria Eufrosyne (1625–1687), who married Count, Lord Marshal, Lord High Constable, Lord Chancellor Magnus Gabriel De la Gardie, and Eleonora Katarina (1626–1692), who married Landgrave Frederick of Hesse-Eschwege. His sister Christina Magdalena was born in Nyköping Castle in 1616, and his brother Karl Gustav was born in 1622. His sisters Maria Eufrosyne and Eleonora Katarina were born at Stegeborg Castle in 1625 and 1626, respectively. Adolf Johan was also born at Stegeborg Castle and was the youngest of his siblings. Three of his siblings – born in 1618, 1619, and 1628 – died as children.

Adolf Johan's grandfather, Karl IX (1550–1611), was married twice. With his first wife, Maria of Palatinate (1561–1589), he had a daughter, Katarina Karlsdotter Vasa (1584–1638), who married Count John Casimir of Palatinate-Zweibrücken in 1615. With his second wife, Kristina of Holstein-Gottorp (1573–1625), he had a son, Gustav Adolf (1594–1632). Katarina Karlsdotter Vasa and Gustav II Adolf were half-siblings. Princess Katarina Karlsdotter Vasa married the German petty prince John Casimir, Count Palatine and Duke of Kleeburg. The Thirty Years' War forced the princely family to leave Kleeburg. After Gustav II Adolf's death, John Casimir and Katarina were granted Stegeborg in Östergötland as a fiefdom, and they moved there with their two sons: Karl Gustav, later Karl X Gustav, born in 1622, and Adolf Johan, born in 1629.

After Gustav II Adolf's death in 1632, his daughter Kristina, Karl Gustav, and Adolf Johan were raised together at Tre Kronor Castle in Stockholm, with Katarina Karlsdotter Vasa as their foster mother. After Gustav II Adolf's death in 1632, the family was in conflict with Kristina's regency government regarding the family's status and inheritance rights to Stegeborg. When John Casimir broke with the council in 1633, Katarina also left court and withdrew to Stegeborg. Katarina herself does not seem to have been interested in participating in politics or diplomacy. When Adolf Johan was 7 years old, in 1636, she officially became the guardian of her niece, Queen Kristina, and took responsibility for her upbringing. She was appointed at the request of Councilor Axel Oxenstierna, and reportedly accepted the position with some reluctance. This appointment strained her relationship with Maria Eleonora, who was Kristina's mother. Kristina herself later described the years she spent as Katarina's foster child as happy. Adolf Johan's mother, Katarina Karlsdotter Vasa, died in December 1638 when Adolf Johan was 9 years old, but he remained and was raised at court, as Kristina's spoiled little brother.

In the summer of 1633, it was time for King Gustav II Adolf's funeral procession to Sweden. The deceased king was carried down to the water in a mourning procession consisting of people from Sweden and surrounding areas. Banners from all counties and principalities, the blood banner, and the main banner were displayed. Symbols of the deceased included his armor (cuirass), sword, and riding horse. Queen Dowager Maria Eleonora participated in a carriage, but Gustav Adolf's young daughter Kristina was not present.

In the procession to Stockholm, eight war trophies from Lützen and several Leipzig trophy banners were carried, symbolizing Sweden's status as a great power. Five black-clad government officials carried the regalia, but Councilor Axel Oxenstierna did not participate. The government officials walked in front of the royal coffin, which lay on a bier decorated with black fabric. Immediately behind the bier was King Gustav Adolf's brother-in-law, Count Palatine John Casimir, with his sons Karl Gustav and Adolf Johan.
