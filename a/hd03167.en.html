TL;DRThe government wants to amend the laws to make it safer to control radiation and nuclear materials. The proposal aims to clearly define who is authorized to perform safety inspections and how to control small quantities of nuclear material.
In plain language

The government proposes new regulations for businesses that use radiation or nuclear technology. The purpose is to enshrine in law that there must be controls to demonstrate that all safety requirements are met. It will also specify exactly how these controls should be carried out and which individuals or companies are authorized to perform them.

New regulations will also be introduced to control nuclear materials, based on common rules within the EU (Euratom). For those who only have small amounts of nuclear material, a simpler solution called a national LOF (Licensing and Oversight Framework) will be created. This is intended to make it easier for smaller businesses to comply with the regulations for nuclear material control.

An authority will be tasked with ensuring that the new regulations for nuclear materials are followed. At the same time, the government proposes that an old penalty for certain violations be removed, as the new regulations replace the old ones.

If the parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new laws will come into effect on August 1, 2026. The new laws will include regulations on both nuclear technology and how to report businesses that deal with nuclear materials.

Full text

The proposal suggests changes to the Act (1984:3) on Nuclear Technology Activities and the Radiation Protection Act (2018:396), which would legally regulate the existing obligation to perform assessments of compliance with safety and radiation protection requirements in nuclear technology activities or other activities involving ionizing or non-ionizing radiation. The government or the authority designated by the government will be authorized to issue regulations regarding the procedures for compliance assessments and the requirements that a person or organization must meet to be authorized to perform such assessments.

The proposal also includes supplementary provisions for a new regulation on nuclear materials control, developed within the European Atomic Energy Community (Euratom). It should be possible to establish a so-called national "LOF" (a facility according to the nuclear materials control regulation) to which certain holders of small quantities of nuclear materials can belong, to facilitate matters for such holders. Furthermore, the authority designated by the government will supervise compliance with the requirements of the nuclear materials control regulation. Finally, the proposal suggests that the penalty provision in the Act on Nuclear Technology Activities, which concerns violations of the previous nuclear materials control regulation, be repealed, without introducing a new penalty provision for violations of the nuclear materials control regulation.

The proposed amendments to the law are intended to come into force on August 1, 2026.

Proposed Parliamentary Decision

The government's proposal:

  1. The Parliament approves the government's proposal for an amendment to the Act (1984:3) on Nuclear Technology Activities.
  2. The Parliament approves the government's proposal for an amendment to the Radiation Protection Act (2018:396).
