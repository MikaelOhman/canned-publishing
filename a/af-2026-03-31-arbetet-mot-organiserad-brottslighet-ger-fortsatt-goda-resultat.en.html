The Swedish Public Employment Service's work within the initiative to combat organized crime resulted in the agency increasing the amount of prevented fraudulent payments for the fourth consecutive year in 2025.

The picture shows the directors general and heads of agencies that collaborate against organized crime, with Minister of Justice Gunner Strömmer in the center.

In 2025, this collaboration between agencies led to the Swedish Public Employment Service preventing incorrect payments totaling 25 million krona and recovering 14 million krona.

"This is one of our prioritized areas within the agency, and the results are proof of that. The work against fraudulent payments will continue to intensify, including through a newly established operational area called 'Correct Payments'," said Thérése Frennberg, Head of Operational Area "Correct Payments."

Throughout the year, the initiative continued to focus on a broader crime prevention approach, with an emphasis on criminal finances. This includes prioritizing efforts in three key areas: crimes in port environments, welfare fraud, and environmental and waste crimes. In 2025, this work entered a more operational phase. The work against welfare fraud has increased significantly, with more cases initiated and reports that have deepened the understanding of the situation, including regarding assistance fraud.

Since 2009, government agencies have been collaborating in an initiative to combat organized crime (the "OC Initiative"). This initiative is carried out at the national, regional, and local levels, and is conducted on behalf of the government under the leadership of the National Police Board.

The initiative focuses on criminal individuals, networks, and types of crime, and is implemented through operational activities, intelligence gathering, and crime prevention efforts. Since 2025, this work has been largely organized around specific problem areas, which currently include crimes in port environments, welfare fraud, and environmental and waste crimes.

The initiative involves 14 government agencies: the Public Employment Service, the National Authority for Combating Economic Crime, the Swedish Social Insurance Agency, the Swedish Prison and Probation Services, the Enforcement Agency, the Coast Guard, the Migration Agency, the Pensions Agency, the Police, the Tax Agency, the Security Police, the Customs Agency, the Payments Agency, and the Prosecution Authority.

In addition, 13 other agencies participate in the initiative through information sharing, strategic collaboration, and involvement in certain operational activities.
