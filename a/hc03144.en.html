In plain language

The government has proposed changes to the rules regarding the high-cost protection system for prescription drugs. This system ensures that you don't have to pay an unlimited amount for your medications within a year. The proposal suggests increasing the maximum amount you pay by 900 Swedish kronor.

The proposal also includes changes to the "high-cost ladder," which determines the discount you receive on your medications based on how much you've already purchased during the year. The first step of the ladder is being modified so that you will now be responsible for paying 75% of the cost, instead of the previous 50%.

The purpose of this change is to ensure sufficient funds are available to cover the cost of all medications in the future. For individuals who purchase medications, this means that the total cost for all patients in Sweden is expected to increase by several billion kronor in the coming years.

If the proposal is approved, the new rules will come into effect on July 1, 2025. If you have already started your high-cost protection period before this date, the old, lower prices will apply until your period ends.

Full text

The proposal includes suggestions for updating the high-cost protection system for pharmaceuticals. The proposal suggests that patients should pay a higher co-payment within the high-cost protection system. The purpose of this adjustment is to improve the possibilities for sustainable financing of pharmaceutical costs. The adjustment involves increasing the cost ceiling for high-cost protection for pharmaceuticals, from 2,900 Swedish krona to 3,800 Swedish krona. The amounts within the so-called "high-cost ladder" are also increased at each step. In addition, the first step is changed so that the patient pays 75 percent, instead of 50 percent, of the cost within that range. Overall, the fees that the individual patient may have to pay are estimated to increase by 900 Swedish krona over a twelve-month period.

With the proposed increases and the design of the protection against high costs, the total co-payments for all patients for pharmaceuticals are estimated to increase by approximately 540 million Swedish krona in 2025, 2,160 million Swedish krona in 2026, and 2,700 million Swedish krona in 2027.

The proposed amendment to the law is suggested to come into effect on July 1, 2025.
