In plain language

The government wants to change the rules regarding the execution of sentences in cooperation with other countries. The aim is to make cooperation with countries outside the EU and the Nordic region more efficient. This means that Sweden can more easily request or provide assistance in collecting fines or carrying out prison sentences.

For individuals sentenced to prison in Sweden, it may become easier to serve their sentence in another country, particularly if the person is to be deported from Sweden. The new proposal allows for the transfer of short prison sentences without requiring special justification. The rules may also become clearer for individuals who have been sentenced to fines or ordered to pay damages.

Decisions regarding these transfers should primarily be made by authorities rather than in court. The Swedish Prison and Probation Service will determine cases involving prison sentences, while the Swedish Enforcement Agency will determine cases involving fines or payments. If one is not satisfied with the authorities' decisions, it may be possible to appeal to a regular court in certain cases.

The new rules are also intended to speed up the process. Authorities and courts will be required to act promptly in these matters. If the proposal is approved by the Parliament, the new laws are planned to come into effect on July 1, 2025.

Full text

The proposal outlines new legislation regarding the international enforcement of criminal sentences. The proposals aim to ensure that the regulations governing the transfer of prison sentences, fines, and confiscations to and from countries outside of the Nordic region and the EU are effective and aligned with Sweden's international commitments. The proposals include the following:

  • A more uniform regulation is introduced regarding when Sweden can request and provide assistance with the enforcement of criminal sentences. This regulation can be applied even without an international agreement on cooperation between Sweden and the other country.
  • The assessment of matters related to the transfer of enforcement will, as a general rule, be carried out at the administrative level. The Swedish Prison and Probation Service will decide on prison sentences, while the National Enforcement Agency will handle fines and confiscations. In certain cases, the decisions of these authorities can be appealed to a court of law.
  • The possibilities for transferring prison sentences from Sweden are expanded. A convicted person can, without their consent, serve their prison sentence in another country if, for example, they are to be deported from Sweden. Shorter prison sentences can be transferred without requiring special justification.
  • A requirement for prompt processing will apply to the Swedish Prison and Probation Service and the courts in matters related to the transfer of prison sentences. Other regulations are also introduced to expedite the transfer process.

The proposed amendments to the law are scheduled to come into effect on July 1, 2025.
