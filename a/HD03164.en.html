In plain language

The government proposes new regulations for how political meetings in municipalities and regions should be conducted. Currently, there is a requirement that all participants attending remotely must be able to see and hear each other in a specific way. This proposal aims to remove this requirement to make it easier to hold meetings remotely, provided that the chairperson ensures that everyone can follow what is happening.

There is also a proposal to increase oversight of private companies that perform tasks for the municipality or region. This means that the municipality's management must ensure that all committees check that the private companies are doing their jobs correctly. This is to prevent corruption and unscrupulous companies from committing crimes within the welfare system.

To ensure this works, the management of the municipality or region must annually report to the elected politicians on how the oversight of the private companies has been conducted. This will make it clearer how the private entities are fulfilling their responsibilities.

If the Parliament approves the proposal, the new regulations will come into effect on July 1, 2026.

Full text

In this proposal, the government suggests changes to the provisions of the Municipal Act (2017:725) regarding remote participation in municipal council and board meetings. The requirement that all members must be able to see and hear each other and participate on equal terms is proposed to be removed. Instead, it is proposed that the chairperson be given a specific role to ensure who is participating in the meeting, and that those participating remotely can follow the meeting and participate in the discussion of matters. It is also proposed that the municipal council's board should not be allowed to participate remotely, and that the municipal council should decide to what extent all members of a board may or must participate in the board's meetings remotely. The purpose of these proposals is to provide municipalities and regions with better conditions for making decisions remotely.

The proposal also suggests clarifying that the municipal or regional board's supervisory duty also includes the control and monitoring of other boards regarding municipal matters that have been delegated to private providers. Furthermore, it is proposed that the board should report annually to the municipal council on the control and monitoring of such delegated matters by other boards. The purpose of these proposals is to strengthen the governance and monitoring chain when municipal matters are delegated to private providers, in order to prevent welfare fraud, unreliable actors, and corruption within the welfare system.

The proposed amendments to the law are to come into force on July 1, 2026.
