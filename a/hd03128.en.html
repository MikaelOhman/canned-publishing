Measures to combat Value Added Tax (VAT) fraud.

Source Riksdagen Read the original at Riksdagen →2026-02-17
TL;DRSweden aims to combat VAT fraud by granting the Swedish Tax Agency (Skatteverket) greater authority to monitor and prevent companies from engaging in fraudulent activities. The new regulations are scheduled to come into effect on July 1, 2026.
In plain language

Many companies use incorrect methods to avoid paying VAT (Value Added Tax) when trading with other countries within the EU. This is called VAT fraud, and it results in significant financial losses for the government. The government now wants to change the law to stop this.

The Swedish Tax Agency, the authority responsible for tax control in Sweden, will be given more power. They will be able to scrutinize companies more thoroughly when they want to register for VAT. If the Tax Agency suspects fraud, they will be able to refuse a company registration or remove them from the system.

The Tax Agency will also be able to mark a registration number as invalid in a common European system called VIES (VAT Information Exchange System). This system is used throughout the EU to verify that a company is legitimate. This will make it more difficult for fraudsters to deceive other companies in Europe.

There is also a proposal that the Tax Agency may decide that companies should not receive refunds if they have paid too much VAT. The purpose of all these new rules is to reduce tax fraud and ensure that everyone pays the correct amount of tax. The proposal is scheduled to come into effect on July 1, 2026.

Full text

Tax evasion through VAT fraud is a significant problem in cross-border trade within the EU. To minimize the losses that such fraud causes to the state, regulations need to be introduced that limit the ability of those intending to commit such fraud to access the VAT system.

The proposal suggests new rules that, among other things, give the Swedish Tax Agency (Skatteverket) greater opportunities for control during VAT registration, the possibility to deny or deregister a person from VAT, and the ability to decide that a registration number should be displayed as invalid in the VAT Information Exchange System (VIES).

The proposal also includes suggestions that would allow the Swedish Tax Agency, under certain circumstances, to decide that excess input VAT should not be credited.

The purpose of the proposals is to prevent VAT fraud and the tax evasion that accompanies it.

The provisions are proposed to come into force on July 1, 2026.
