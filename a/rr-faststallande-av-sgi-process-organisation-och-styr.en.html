Despite these improvements, there are still issues with the verification process. This means that the decisions made do not always accurately reflect a person's actual earnings, particularly for those working part-time or whose information hasn't been updated recently.

There is also a risk when Försäkringskassan uses data from the Swedish Tax Agency (Skatteverket) without thoroughly verifying it. If the information from Skatteverket is incorrect, for example due to fraud, it can lead to incorrect benefit decisions.

Full text

The "Sjukpenninggrundande inkomst" (SGI), which translates to "income basis for sickness benefits," is a crucial component of the social security system. It forms the basis for the amount, and sometimes even the eligibility, for several benefits that collectively represent a significant portion of payments within the social security system. The Swedish Social Insurance Agency (Försäkringskassan) makes decisions regarding SGI on a massive scale each year, which places high demands on efficient and legally sound processes.

The National Audit Office's overall conclusion is that Försäkringskassan has improved its handling of SGI, but that the process is not yet fully efficient. The audit reveals that Försäkringskassan's decisions regarding SGI generally reflect an individual's actual income, but that controls are not always sufficient to ensure accurate decisions. Furthermore, certain aspects of the SGI process involve resource-intensive procedures with a risk of errors, unclear governance, and inadequate follow-up.

Overall, the National Audit Office assesses that Försäkringskassan needs to strengthen both the controls, governance, and monitoring of its SGI processes.

In recent years, Försäkringskassan has streamlined its SGI processes through increased digitalization and automation. Moreover, on a general level, SGI decisions reflect an individual's actual income situation when new income information is provided. However, systematic discrepancies exist between an individual's actual income and the SGI determined by Försäkringskassan in cases of part-time work or when a significant amount of time has passed since the last income verification.

The audit also shows that Försäkringskassan only to a limited extent considers risks specifically related to the quality of individual income data from the Swedish Tax Agency (Skatteverket).
