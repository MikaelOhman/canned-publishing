In plain language

The government has proposed a measure to better protect you when you purchase banking services, insurance, or loans online or through apps. Because digital agreements are often complex and difficult to understand, the aim is to introduce clearer rules for how information should be provided to you as a customer.

The new proposal means that you will have the right to explanations that help you understand whether a service actually meets your needs. It also aims to prevent websites and apps from trying to deceive customers. This is part of new regulations from the EU that will apply to all citizens in Europe.

An important part of the proposal is to make it easier to exercise your right of withdrawal. The right of withdrawal means that you have the right to cancel a purchase within a certain period. The government wants to create a simple function that allows you to cancel both financial services and other types of goods in the same way.

If the parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new rules will come into effect on June 19, 2026. The proposal involves changes to several different laws that relate to, among other things, payment services, securities, and insurance.

Full text

The government proposes amendments to laws aimed at strengthening consumer protection in distance contracts for financial services and instruments. Many consumers enter into agreements digitally on websites or in applications on a mobile phone. These products are often complex and difficult to understand. Therefore, clear rules and basic consumer protection in this area are essential.

The proposals in the government's proposal adapt the rules on the right of withdrawal and information to today's digital landscape. Consumer protection is strengthened when agreements are made online through protection against misleading websites and applications, and a right to explanations that can help the consumer understand whether a financial service meets their needs. It is important to be able to exercise the right of withdrawal easily, regardless of the goods and services involved. The government therefore proposes a mechanism for exercising the right of withdrawal that should apply to both distance contracts for financial services and instruments and other goods and services.

The proposals implement an EU directive amending the Consumer Rights Directive.

The amendments to the laws are proposed to come into force on June 19, 2026.

Proposed Resolutions of the Parliament

The government's proposals:

  1. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Act (2005:59) on distance contracts and contracts outside business premises.
  2. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Act (2007:528) on the securities market.
  3. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Act (2010:751) on payment services.
  4. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Consumer Credit Act (2010:1846).
  5. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Act (2018:1219) on insurance distribution.
  6. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Act (2010:751) on payment services.
