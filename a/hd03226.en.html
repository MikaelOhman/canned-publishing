In plain language

The Swedish National Audit Agency is an authority that ensures that the state's money is used correctly. They have investigated how Sida, which is Sweden's agency for international development cooperation, operates in the area of humanitarian aid. The report shows that Sida makes the right decisions when prioritizing the most significant crises, but that there are ways to work more efficiently.

The audit agency believes that Sida needs to simplify its processes when selecting partners and distributing funds. It is also important that decisions are clear and comply with the laws that govern how government agencies should operate. The Swedish National Audit Agency also wants Sida to improve its ability to monitor the results of its work and to utilize its staff at embassies effectively.

The government has now read the report and agrees with most of the proposals. They also describe the changes that Sida has already begun to implement to make aid more effective. The government therefore believes that the necessary measures following the audit are now complete.

In this document, the government presents its assessment of the observations and recommendations made by the Swedish National Audit Agency (Riksrevisionen) in the report "Saving Lives and Alleviating Suffering – Sida's Work with Humanitarian Aid" (RiR 2025:33). The National Audit Agency's review shows that Sida focuses on directing humanitarian aid to the crises where the needs are greatest, a central principle in humanitarian aid. At the same time, the National Audit Agency assesses that there is room for improvement in Sida's management of humanitarian aid.

The National Audit Agency's conclusions are that Sida should simplify the processes for selecting partners and distributing humanitarian aid, clarify decision-making, and ensure that decisions made comply with administrative law principles. Furthermore, the National Audit Agency emphasizes in its conclusions that the follow-up of humanitarian aid should be systematized, and that Sida should make better use of collaboration between departments and review whether the allocation of personnel resources at embassies is appropriate.

The National Audit Agency has not made any recommendations to the government, but has made several recommendations to Sida, which are in line with the conclusions mentioned above.

The government welcomes the National Audit Agency's review and largely shares the overall observations, assessments, and recommendations. In this document, the government describes the measures that Sida has taken and the government's ongoing work to ensure more efficient management of humanitarian aid. With this document, the government considers the review report to be finalized.
