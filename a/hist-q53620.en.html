Karl Hjalmar Branting, born on November 23, 1860, in the parish of Klara in Stockholm, and died on February 24, 1925, in the parish of Gustav Vasa in Stockholm, was a Swedish Social Democratic politician and journalist. He served as the leader of the Social Democrats from 1907 and became Sweden's first Social Democratic Prime Minister in 1920.

Branting held several important positions, including:

  • Member of Parliament (second chamber) from 1897 to 1925
  • Leader of the Social Democrats from 1907 to 1925
  • Minister of Finance from 1917 to 1918
  • Prime Minister in 1920, 1921–1923 (during which time he also served as Minister of Foreign Affairs), and 1924–1925.

In 1921, Hjalmar Branting was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize together with Christian Lange for their significant contributions to the League of Nations.

Branting married the journalist Anna Jäderin in 1884. They had two children: Georg (1887–1965), a lawyer, member of parliament, and publicist; and Sonja (1890–1981), a lawyer, feminist, and member of parliament, who was married to the lawyer Olof Westerståhl (1884–1948) and mother of the political scientist Jörgen Westerståhl. Hjalmar Branting was also stepfather to Vera von Kræmer (1878–1940) and Henry von Kræmer (1880–1957), children of Anna Branting from her marriage to Lieutenant Gustaf von Kræmer.

== Biography ==

=== Family and Upbringing === Hjalmar Branting was born on November 23, 1860, in Klara, Stockholm, as the only son of Professor Lars Gabriel Branting, who also served as a medical officer and director of the Gymnastics Central Institute, and Emma af Georgii. His parents married in 1839, divorced in 1869, but remarried in 1873. His maternal grandfather was Carl af Georgii (1787–1837), a former Lord Chamberlain, Secretary of State, who was ennobled in 1818. Hjalmar Branting grew up and spent most of his life at the Brantingska house on Norrtullsgatan 3 in Stockholm.

Branting came from a high-bourgeois background. On his father's side, he belonged to the fourth and second youngest branch of the Branting family, which originated in Norrköping. The name Branting can be traced back to Jöran Branting (died 1694), a regimental clerk in the Småland cavalry and resident in Nöbbele parish. During Hjalmar's childhood, he began attending M:lle Kumlins school very early on, in 1866, where he received his first lessons in French. In the spring semester of 1868, Hjalmar enrolled in the newly founded Beskowska School. The classical education made a strong impression on Hjalmar, and he retained sympathies for it throughout his life; his favorite subjects at that time were natural sciences and mathematics. During his school years at the Beskowska School, he was classmates with the future King Gustaf V. He also studied natural sciences, specifically astronomy, at Uppsala University from 1878 to 1882, but did not complete his degree. Through travels and literary studies, he became familiar with Marxism early on. As early as 1878–79, he called himself a social democrat. After the deaths of his parents in 1880–81, Branting focused on a journalistic and political career. He squandered a considerable fortune inherited from his parents within a few years through lavish parties and generous loans and guarantees. He mismanaged the money and often faced financial difficulties. Hjalmar Branting frequently clashed with the law and was convicted on several occasions of both blasphemy and violations of freedom of the press, resulting in prison sentences and fines.

=== Early Career === After working for the radical journal *Tiden*, Branting became associated with the newspaper *Social-Demokraten*, founded by August "mäster" Palm. He became its editor-in-chief in 1887, a position he held until 1917.

Branting quickly emerged as a leading figure in the young Social Democratic movement, particularly after the deaths of Axel Danielsson and Fredrik Sterky at the turn of the century (1900). He participated in the Social Democratic Party's founding congress in 1889 and served on the party's executive board from its formation in 1894, becoming chairman of the executive board's steering committee in 1907. Branting became the party's first congress-elected party leader, serving from 1908 until his death. In a well-known speech in Gävle in 1886, he argued that the labor movement needed to become socialist.

=== Member of Parliament and Minister ===

He was elected to the Riksdag (Swedish parliament) in 1896 on a liberal list. Among his various responsibilities, he served as vice-chairman of the Constitutional Committee from 1912 to 1914. Following Tsar Nicholas II's abdication during the Russian February Revolution in 1917, Sweden experienced widespread food riots. Hjalmar Branting and the leadership of the LO (Swedish Confederation of Labour) worked to restore calm and prevent an escalation of the unrest. In discussions with Prime Minister Carl Swartz, Branting took responsibility for maintaining law and order during May Day 1917, which allowed Swartz to ban bourgeois initiatives aimed at forming private militias. This averted the risk of street clashes that could have plunged the country into a very serious crisis. When parliamentary government definitively gained ground after the 1917 election, when the king could no longer prevent the incumbent right-wing government from resigning after losing the election, and a government was formed based on the election results, it was a major victory for Branting.
