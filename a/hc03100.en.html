In plain language

The government has presented a proposal called the "Economic Spring Proposal." This document outlines the government's plans for how to use the country's funds and how they intend to manage the Swedish economy. Its purpose is to lay the groundwork for the next major budget, which will apply for the year 2026.

The proposal examines the health of the national economy and the amount of money the state has available. It also investigates challenges related to increasing employment and the importance of investments for the country's development. Furthermore, it assesses whether the economic goals that have been set have been achieved.

The government also discusses its long-term vision for the state's finances to ensure their sustainability. This means ensuring that the economy functions well in the future. The proposal also suggests certain changes in how the various areas where the state spends its money are allocated.

Full text

The proposal outlines the government's proposed guidelines for economic and budgetary policy. The purpose of these guidelines is to define the direction of policy in preparation for the 2026 budget proposal.

The guidelines for economic policy are based on the other content of the proposal, which includes: a description of the overall direction of fiscal policy; an assessment of macroeconomic developments and the finances of the public sector; an overview of the challenges facing employment policy; the importance of productivity and public investment; a follow-up and assessment of the budgetary policy goals; a review of the state budget; and an assessment of the long-term sustainability of fiscal policy.

The proposal also includes suggestions for certain changes to the classification of expenditure areas.
