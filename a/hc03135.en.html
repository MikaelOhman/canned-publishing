In plain language

The government has proposed a plan to modernize the bankruptcy process in Sweden. Currently, the process is complicated because many different individuals and authorities must be involved in making decisions. By changing the rules, the aim is to make the process more efficient.

In the new proposal, the district court, which handles legal disputes, will only deal with the most complex issues. Instead, the bankruptcy trustee, who is responsible for managing the bankruptcy, will have more authority to make decisions about important aspects of the process. The supervisory authority, which ensures that everything is done correctly, will also take over certain tasks.

It will also become easier for those who have claims for money in a bankruptcy to participate. The goal is to eliminate some meetings and make it easier to resolve disputes without it taking too long. If someone is not satisfied with a decision, they will still be able to appeal to the district court.

If the proposal is approved, the new rules will come into effect on July 1, 2026. The proposal also includes changes to many other laws, such as laws regarding limited liability companies, savings, and how the Swedish Enforcement Agency handles its duties.

Full text

The government proposes to modernize and streamline the bankruptcy process. The proposals aim to clarify the role of the district court in the process and to transfer several tasks to the bankruptcy trustee and the supervisory authority in bankruptcy cases. At the same time, the process will be simplified, for example, by having tasks that currently require the involvement of multiple parties performed solely by the trustee or the supervisory authority. The proposals include the following:

  • After the district court has declared bankruptcy, it should only handle matters that require the court's specific expertise.
  • The bankruptcy trustee should take over the responsibility of deciding whether a creditors' meeting should be held in the bankruptcy case. The trustee should also be responsible for conducting the creditors' meeting and for the initial review of a proposed settlement.
  • In most cases, the supervisory authority should take over the responsibility of deciding whether to approve a distribution proposal and the trustee's remuneration.
  • The creditors' meeting process should be simplified, for example, by abolishing the conciliation meeting and allowing the party who made an objection to withdraw it or reach a settlement.
  • Most decisions made during the bankruptcy process should be appealable to the district court, which should also review matters such as creditor disputes and disputed distribution proposals.
  • The government should be given the power to prescribe which district courts are authorized to hear bankruptcy cases.

The proposed amendments to the law are scheduled to come into effect on July 1, 2026.
