In plain language

The General Inheritance Fund is a fund that distributes money to various important projects in Sweden. Currently, there is a rule that limits the amount of money that can be allocated annually to organizations that provide services for elderly people.

The government has now presented a proposal to the Parliament to remove this restriction. This would make it easier to use more money from the fund to create better conditions and support for the elderly.

If the Parliament approves the proposal, the new law will come into effect on January 1, 2026. The proposal is included in a so-called "proposition," which is an official proposal from the government to the Parliament.

In the proposal, the government suggests removing the restriction on the percentage of funds from the General Legacy Fund that can be allocated annually to activities benefiting elderly individuals. The proposed amendment to the law is scheduled to take effect on January 1, 2026.
