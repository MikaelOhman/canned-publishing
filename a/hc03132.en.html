A student apartment is a dwelling intended for individuals studying at a university, college, or adult education program. Even with these relaxed regulations, there are still requirements to ensure that people with mobility or orientation difficulties can access the apartments.

The new regulations are intended to facilitate the construction of housing for students. If the proposal is approved, the new laws will come into effect on July 1, 2025.

Full text

The proposal suggests changes to the Planning and Building Act (2010:900).

These changes would allow for exceptions to the requirements for accessibility and usability in buildings containing student housing. These exceptions would apply to a maximum of 80 percent of either the total number of student housing units in a building or the number of student housing units added when a modification is made to the building. However, any student housing unit subject to the exception must be accessible to a person with reduced mobility or orientation abilities. Regulations issued under the authority of a provision in the law will specify the requirements for a student housing unit to meet the standard for accessibility for individuals with reduced mobility or orientation abilities, and will also address any exceptions to that requirement that are justified by special circumstances. The proposal also includes a definition of the term "student housing," which is defined as housing intended for students at a university, college, other post-secondary education institution, or adult education program.

The proposed amendments to the law are scheduled to take effect on July 1, 2025.
