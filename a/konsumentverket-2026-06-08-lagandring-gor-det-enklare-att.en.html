In plain language

A new law, effective from June 19th, makes it easier for you when shopping online or through apps. Stores are now required to provide a clear and accessible function that allows you to indicate your intention to cancel your purchase directly on the page where you placed the order. When you use this feature, you should receive an automatic confirmation that your cancellation request has been received.

This applies to products for which you have a legal right to cancel, such as clothing or electronics. It does not apply to items like hygiene products or tickets to specific events. If a store does not offer this simple function, the time you have to cancel your purchase may be extended.

The Swedish Consumer Agency is the authority responsible for ensuring that these rules are followed. They encourage consumers to report any online stores that lack a clear cancellation button or where it is difficult to understand how to proceed with a cancellation.

News: Legislative changes make it easier to cancel online purchases. Published: June 8, 2026

Starting June 19th, it will be much easier to cancel something you've purchased online. On the same page where you made the purchase, you should now be able to easily choose to cancel it. The so-called "cancellation" feature is the biggest change in the law regarding distance contracts and contracts outside of business premises (DAL), which comes into effect on June 19th this year. The purpose of the legal amendment is to strengthen consumer protection in online commerce, making it easier to cancel an agreement. Those who are entitled to cancel under the law should be able to do so at the same place where the agreement was made. This means that online stores will be required to offer a clearly visible function where you can submit a message indicating that you want to exercise your right of cancellation. After doing so, you will receive an automatic confirmation receipt. This receipt is proof that you have chosen to cancel, but it does not mean that the store has approved the cancellation. A cancellation feature is legally required if the purchase was made through an "online interface," such as a website or mobile application. This does not apply, for example, to sales made over the phone. You must also be entitled to cancel under the law; for example, you do not have the right to cancel if it involves a hygiene product or a ticket to a cultural event that takes place on a specific day. If a company fails to comply with the requirements regarding the cancellation feature, this may result in the cancellation period being extended for you as a consumer. "To ensure that online stores comply with the legal amendment, we encourage consumers to report to us if they discover that a cancellation function is missing or, for example, not clearly visible," says Johanna Persson, a lawyer at the Swedish Consumer Agency.
