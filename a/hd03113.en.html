In this document, the government presents its assessment regarding the observations and recommendations made by the Swedish National Audit Office (Riksrevisionen) in its report "The State's Efforts for the Agricultural Sector's Climate Transition" (RiR 2025:24).

The Swedish National Audit Office's overall conclusion is that the existing policy instruments are largely well-designed and implemented, but they only cover a small portion of the agricultural sector's total greenhouse gas emissions. At the same time, the Swedish National Audit Office assesses that the government's policies have not effectively contributed to the agricultural sector achieving climate goals, and that there are effective measures that have not been implemented. The Swedish National Audit Office provides five recommendations to the government and two to the Swedish Board of Agriculture (Jordbruksverket) and the Swedish Environmental Protection Agency (Naturvårdsverket).

The government welcomes the Swedish National Audit Office's review and considers it a valuable contribution to developing governance and increasing the effectiveness of state efforts for the agricultural sector's climate work. The government notes that during the period the review was conducted, it has made several decisions that are in line with the Swedish National Audit Office's recommendations, including regarding the restoration of abandoned agricultural land. Regarding the recommendation on tax reductions for diesel in the agricultural sector, the government believes that there may be grounds to investigate the possibility of replacing the tax reduction with alternative forms of support. The government, like the Swedish National Audit Office, notes that there is a lack of a comprehensive evaluation of support for biogas production, and that future evaluations should better take into account the overall effect of the support.
