An enhanced high-cost protection system for dental care.

In plain language

The government has proposed a plan to make it easier to pay for dental care. The new support program is called "special dental care allowance." If you need dental care, you should no longer have to pay the difference between what the dentist charges and the price set by the state, known as the "reference price."

To make this work, the government proposes to change the rules regarding how prices are set in the law. This means that it will be clearer what applies to the cost. The government also wants to require dentists who are connected to the Swedish Social Insurance Agency's system to provide appropriate care to patients who are eligible for the allowance.

An important goal of the proposal is to ensure that those who need dental care the most receive help first. This will be included in the law on dental care. This will ensure that those who need the most care are prioritized for treatment.

If the proposal is approved by the Parliament, the new rules will come into effect on January 1, 2026. This means that several different laws regarding dental care and reimbursement for care received in other EU countries will be amended.

Full text

The proposal suggests that the current protection against high costs within the state-funded dental care system should be supplemented with a new form of support called "special dental care reimbursement." This special reimbursement should be provided for eligible dental procedures that have been completed under the conditions set by the government, or the authority designated by the government. For special dental care reimbursement to be granted, the price charged by the healthcare provider for the dental procedure must not exceed the reference price. The proposal aims to ensure that the funds invested benefit patients and that patients do not have to pay the difference between the provider's price and the reference price.

The proposal also suggests that a definition of the "provider's price" be included in the law on state-funded dental care.

Furthermore, it is proposed that healthcare providers connected to the Swedish Social Insurance Agency's (Försäkringskassan) electronic system for state-funded dental care should, unless there are special reasons, be obligated to provide eligible patients with the dental care they need.

The proposal also includes suggestions to incorporate the principle that those with the greatest need for dental care should be given priority into the dental care law.

The proposed amendments are scheduled to come into effect on January 1, 2026.
