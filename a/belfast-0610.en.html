A man who was the victim of an attempted decapitation in a public street in North Belfast is reportedly a Scottish man in his forties named Stephen Ogilvie, according to several British media outlets. At the same time, a Sudanese man has been charged with attempted murder, as confirmed by British police.

North Belfast is one of the city's most divided and historically violent areas. Antrim Road, near the location of the attack, was known as "Murder Mile" during the conflict known as The Troubles, which characterized Northern Ireland until the peace agreement in 1998.

Kinnaird Avenue, where the incident occurred, is located in the Water Works area near the lower Antrim Road in North Belfast, close to the republican and predominantly Catholic New Lodge. A journalist on the scene, reporting for GB News, describes the area as marked by poverty and old conflicts.

"This area in Belfast is very socially vulnerable," says the journalist.

"You can feel the anger over the large migration to this area."

"There are no borders in Northern Ireland; migrants are coming directly across the border into this part of the United Kingdom," he says.

"The neighborhood here is accustomed to violence, so politicians must take the lead here."

Political commentator Piers Pottinger shares the frustration.

"This is not the first time something like this has happened in Belfast this year," he told GB.

Vulnerable Neighbor Identified as Victim

Against this backdrop, the incident at Kinnaird Avenue occurred, where a man in his forties was attacked with a knife and nearly had his head cut off. He sustained serious injuries to his face, neck, and back.

Residents of the neighborhood have identified the victim to British media as Stephen Ogilvie, a man reportedly originally from Scotland who has lived in one of the apartments for several years. According to neighbors, he lived in the same neighborhood as the man now identified as the attacker.

A resident described to the Daily Mail how the rescue personnel took care of him.

"They spoke to Stephen while they were treating him. I heard them calling him by his name when he was helped into the ambulance," the resident said.

"He is a vulnerable person, and some of his neighbors look after him."

Suspect Charged with Attempted Murder

According to witnesses, a film circulating online (whose authenticity has not been verified) appears to show Ogilvie being stabbed multiple times in the head before the knife was brought towards his neck. The police have maintained that it was a stabbing incident and have not used the term "attempted decapitation."

The suspect, a 30-year-old man whom the police initially believed was from Somalia but who was later confirmed to be Sudanese, is in custody. The Northern Ireland police confirmed on Tuesday that he is being charged with attempted murder, possession of a pointed object in public, and making death threats. He is scheduled to appear at Belfast Magistrates' Court.

Deputy Police Chief Ryan Henderson described the incident as a critical event and stated that an investigation has been launched to determine a motive.

"I want to assure the local community that we are treating this attack with the utmost seriousness," Henderson said, according to PSNI.

The incident has also sparked a political dispute regarding the perpetrator's status, after Member of Parliament Gavin Robinson claimed that the man had been living in the country on a five-year visa. This information has not been confirmed by the authorities.
