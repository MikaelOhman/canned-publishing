Sweden needs to function – policies for stronger rural areas.

TL;DRThe government wants to amend the law to make it easier for people and businesses in rural areas to influence decisions. This means that regions must communicate more with associations, schools, and businesses before making decisions.
In plain language

The government has put forward a proposal to help strengthen rural areas in Sweden. The proposal involves amending a law that governs how regions should work on development projects. The most important aspect is that regions must consult with more people before making important decisions.

When regions plan for the future, they are now required to ask associations, private educational providers, and businesses for their opinions. This means that those who live and work in the area will have a greater voice. The government also wants to ensure that national minorities and their languages are explicitly included in these consultations.

The purpose of the new rules is to create a better life in rural areas. The government wants to make it easier for people to stay and for businesses to start up. They also want to make it easier to build homes and ensure that rural areas are a safe place to live.

If the parliament approves the proposal, the new rules will come into effect on July 1, 2026. This is part of a larger plan to ensure that all of Sweden functions well, regardless of whether people live in a large city or a small town.

Full text

The proposal suggests that the law (2010:630) on regional development responsibility should be clarified to state that a region's obligation to consult with organizations includes representatives of relevant organizations within civil society and individual educational providers in the higher education sector.

Furthermore, the proposal suggests that the law should include a provision informing about the consultation obligations arising from the law (2009:724) on national minorities and minority languages.

The proposed amendments to the law are to come into force on July 1, 2026.

In the proposal, the government provides a broader picture of the situation in Sweden's rural areas than the annual performance reports presented in the budget proposal (most recently in prop. 2025/26:1, area 23).

In addition, the government describes, divided into four main areas, how measures within the integrated rural policy and other policy areas have or will contribute to stronger rural areas throughout the country:

  • A stronger local community and a more effective rural policy.
  • Simplifications for residents and businesses in rural areas.
  • Better conditions for housing construction, entrepreneurship, and investment in rural areas.
  • Safe, attractive, robust, and interconnected rural areas.
