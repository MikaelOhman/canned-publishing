An exception in the coupon tax law for foreign states.

Source Riksdagen Read the original at Riksdagen →2026-01-27
TL;DRThe government wants to amend the law so that foreign states and similar organizations are exempt from paying taxes on dividends received from Swedish companies. This applies to countries that cooperate with Sweden on tax matters.
In plain language

The government has proposed a change to the law regarding withholding tax on dividends. Withholding tax is a tax paid when receiving dividends, which are payments from a company. The proposal states that foreign states and organizations similar to Swedish municipalities or regions should not have to pay this tax when receiving money from Swedish corporations or investment funds.

This exemption does not apply to all countries in the world. It only applies to countries that are part of the European Economic Area (EEA) or countries that have an agreement with Sweden. An important requirement is that these countries must be able to share information with Sweden regarding tax matters. This means they must cooperate with Swedish authorities to ensure that everything is done correctly.

If the Swedish Parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new rule will come into effect on July 1, 2026. The proposal is presented in a so-called "proposition," which is a document where the government proposes new laws for the Parliament to decide on.

Full text

The proposal suggests that foreign states and foreign entities equivalent to a Swedish region, municipality, or municipal association should be exempt from the obligation to pay withholding tax on dividends paid by Swedish limited liability companies, European companies with their registered office in Sweden, as well as investment funds and special funds. This exemption applies to foreign states within the European Economic Area or that have entered into an agreement with Sweden that allows for the exchange of information in tax matters. A foreign entity equivalent to a Swedish region, municipality, or municipal association must be located in such a state.

The proposed amendment to the law is scheduled to come into effect on July 1, 2026.
