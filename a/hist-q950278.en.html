Magnus, born July 25, 1542, at Stockholm Castle, and died June 20, 1595, at Kungsbro, was a Swedish prince and Duke of Östergötland. He was the son of Gustav Vasa and his second wife, Queen Margaret.

== Biography ==

=== Early Years === Of Gustav Vasa's sons, four lived to adulthood, and Magnus was the only one who did not become King of Sweden. In 1555, he was appointed Duke of Östergötland, as well as the districts of Kinda and Ydre in what was then Småland, the province of Dalsland, the district of Sundbo in Närke, Kåkind, Valla, and most of the district of Vadsbo in Västergötland.

=== Mental Illness === Magnus suffered from a mental illness that first manifested in 1562-63 and later became permanent. In the summer of 1563, his brother Erik sent him a physician, who believed that the illness – as Erik told his brother – stemmed from "the fact that your love is solitary and has no one to refresh it, something we must also consider for ourselves." At this time, Magnus was engaged to Maria Stuart in Scotland, and Erik expressed hope that his brother would receive "pleasant news" from her. However, when the reply arrived in the autumn of the same year, it was a rejection, couched in beautiful but evasive language.

Previously, Magnus had appeared to recover, but he fell ill again in the summer of 1564. However, this time too, he improved by the autumn. That winter, Erik invited his brother to come to Stockholm and "overcome his weakness through joy and pleasure." Magnus did go, and life in the new environment seemed beneficial; however, it was futile to hope for any lasting improvement. Soon, the mental illness worsened.

After a time, the Duke's madness manifested in violent outbursts, which sometimes led to him being confined. Contemporary documents recount instances such as one of his attendants requesting release from his duties because he had been "repeatedly and severely beaten and mistreated by the Duke during his severe illness, resulting in him becoming a mutilated man." There is also an expense record from 1568 for a barber who treated one of the Duke's guards, "who had been struck by His Gracious Highness," and from the same period, an expense for repairing "a Bible and a collection of sermons that His Royal Highness had torn apart."

Due to Magnus's mental illness, his duchy was administered from 1574 by King Johan III. A smaller portion of Magnus's estates went to Duke Karl. When Johan took over responsibility for Magnus, he wrote to his brother's caregivers instructing them to treat him with dignity. He had heard, he wrote, that they were treating him harshly and eating his food. He strictly forbade such actions. Later, he issued an order to all bishops throughout the kingdom to hold prayer meetings for the Duke's recovery from his mental illness.

However, the situation seemed hopeless. In a letter to his sister Anna in the spring of 1570, Johan wrote that Magnus "had lost all reason and memory, so that he does not know or remember anything about what His Majesty has said or done, or what will be said to Him." According to the letter, Magnus also did not remember anything from recent events.

Magnus's main residence was Vadstena Castle, which was rebuilt from a fortress into a palace for him. During his time, Vadstena Abbey existed in a declining post-Reformation state with very few nuns. According to a note by the papal legate Antonio Possevino, Magnus allegedly once brought three young women to the castle and had them murdered, and also placed religious images in the abbey in ridiculous costumes. Possevino claimed that this occurred before the onset of Magnus's mental illness, and he interpreted it as a punishment for his actions. Lars-Olof Larsson therefore questions the veracity of this account.

=== Death === Magnus also owned Kungs Norrby, located on the Motala River, and Kungsbro, situated at the outlet of the river into Lake Roxen. He passed away in 1595 at the latter estate. In the spring of that year, his health rapidly declined, and he became increasingly bedridden. Those around him attributed this to a pre-existing hernia that had worsened. Duke Karl, like his brothers, was deeply concerned about the sick man and did everything he could to secure medical assistance for him. However, there was nothing that could be done.

Magnus' body was carried in a procession to Vadstena and buried in the Klosterkyrkan church. According to tradition, his coffin was suspended by chains to prevent spiders from entering.

== Relationships == On several occasions, plans were made for Magnus to marry, but these never materialized. Instead, he entered into a relationship early on with a German woman named Valborg Eriksdotter, who often referred to herself as "Walborg, my gracious Prince Duke Magnus' mistress." They had a daughter together, Lucretia, who married a German nobleman and became the matriarch of the Swedish noble family Wernstedt. Valborg Eriksdotter was later replaced by another German woman, Anna von Haubitz, as his lover. With her, the duke also had a daughter, Helena, who married Duke Karl's marshal and became the matriarch of the noble family Yxkull.
