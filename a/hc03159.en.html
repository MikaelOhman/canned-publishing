To improve the conditions for starting, running, and developing businesses, and to prevent crime, the F-tax system needs to be designed to protect legitimate businesses and counteract unfair competition and criminal economic activity. Misuse of F-tax approval leads to unfair competition, negatively impacts the business environment, and can be used as a tool for organized crime.

The proposal suggests a new condition for approval and a new basis for revoking F-tax approval, specifically addressing incorrect payments related to tax deductions for the installation of green technology.

The proposal also includes suggestions for a new condition for approval and a new basis for revoking F-tax approval when a foreign company, without a permanent establishment in Sweden, fails to comply with a request to provide specific information as outlined in section 33, paragraph 6a of the law on tax proceedings, for the purpose of assessing tax liability under the income tax law.

The proposed regulations are scheduled to come into effect on November 1, 2025.
