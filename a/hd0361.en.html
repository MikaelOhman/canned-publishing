In plain language

The government has proposed new regulations to screen individuals who are about to start working for the municipality. The goal is to prevent individuals who are planning crimes or harmful actions from being employed within the municipality's operations. By reviewing criminal records and records of suspicions, which are databases of past crimes and suspicions of crimes, the aim is to reduce the risk of criminal activity.

The new regulations will primarily apply to individuals who will be working with children or who will be providing in-home care for elderly and adults with disabilities. They also apply to individuals who will be holding high-level and important leadership positions within the municipality. The purpose is to create a safer work environment and protect those who require extra care.

The proposal involves changes to several laws, including the law on criminal records and the social security code. The Parliament must vote in favor of these changes for them to become law.

If the Parliament approves the proposal, the new regulations are planned to come into effect on March 1, 2026. At that time, municipalities will have greater opportunities to screen new employees before they begin working.

Full text

To prevent infiltration and unauthorized influence, to enhance security, and to reduce the risk of individuals being subjected to crimes within municipal operations, the government intends to provide municipalities with better opportunities to control individuals who are to be employed. Municipalities should, in more cases than today, be able to access information from the criminal record database – and in certain cases, even the suspicion register – before hiring someone. The purpose is to prevent individuals with criminal or harmful intentions from being employed in municipal operations.

The regulations are proposed to apply to individuals who are to:

  • Work in the homes of elderly individuals or adults with disabilities,
  • Work with children, or
  • Be employed in certain leadership positions within municipalities.

The regulations will be introduced through two new laws and amendments to existing legislation. The laws and amendments are proposed to come into effect on March 1, 2026.

Proposed Parliamentary Decisions

The government's proposal:

  1. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for a law on background checks for individuals working in the homes of elderly individuals or adults with disabilities.
  2. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for a law on background checks for individuals being employed in leadership positions within municipalities.
  3. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for a law amending the Social Security Code.
  4. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for a law amending the Act (1998:620) on the criminal record database.
  5. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for a law amending the Act (1998:621) on the suspicion register.
  6. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for a law amending the Act (2010:479) on background checks for personnel providing certain services to children with disabilities.
  7. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for a law amending the Act (2013:852) on background checks for individuals who will be working with children.
