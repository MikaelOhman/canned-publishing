In plain language

The proposal aims to make healthcare more consistent and integrated for individuals who have a harmful substance use or addiction, along with other psychiatric conditions.

Regions must organize addiction care so that it is coordinated with other psychiatric care. The requirements for the individual care plan that must be created for individuals in care homes or residential settings are being strengthened.

If a region fails to fulfill its obligations regarding the individual care plan, it will be liable to compensate the municipality. The social services department must also notify the region when an individual is admitted to a care home or residential setting.

Full text

The proposal includes suggestions aimed at enabling a more consistent and cohesive care system for individuals with harmful substance use or addiction, and other psychiatric conditions. The suggestions stipulate that regions should organize care for individuals with harmful substance use or addiction in a way that ensures it is coordinated with other psychiatric care. The suggestions also include additional requirements for the individual care plan that must be developed for individuals admitted to care or residential facilities. It is proposed that a requirement be introduced for the region to compensate the municipality in cases where the region fails to fulfill its obligations regarding the individual care plan. Furthermore, it is proposed that the social services department should notify the region when an individual is admitted to a care or residential facility, so that the region is made aware of the need to develop an individual care plan.

The proposal also includes a new law on a coordinated care and support service. This service should be aimed at individuals who have special needs for coordinated healthcare and social services over a longer period due to a mental disability. The new law introduces an expanded requirement for cooperation between the municipality and the region. The purpose is to promote the individual's health, safety, and living conditions.

Furthermore, it is proposed that the term "abuse" be replaced with the terms "use" or "harmful use" and "addiction" in several regulations in the areas of healthcare and social services.

The legislative changes and the new law are proposed to come into force on January 1, 2028.
