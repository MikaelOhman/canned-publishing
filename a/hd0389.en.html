Full text

The Swedish National Audit Agency (Riksrevisionen) has, in the report "Labor Law Conditions in Public Procurement" (RiR 2025:21), presented its review of whether the government and authorities have taken effective measures to ensure fair working conditions in public procurement. In this document, the government addresses the observations made by the National Audit Agency and the recommendations made by the agency to the government.

The National Audit Agency assesses that there are opportunities to improve the efficiency of the authorities' work, that the monitoring of labor law requirements needs to be expanded, and that more support is needed. The agency recommends that the government ensure increased support and that it investigate how the monitoring of labor law conditions can be centralized.

The government agrees that there are opportunities to improve the efficiency of the authorities' work and has, among other things, initiated the following measures.

The government has decided to give the Swedish Agency for Public Procurement a broader mandate to strengthen support for public procurement.

The Swedish Agency for Public Procurement has, among other things, been tasked with informing and guiding contracting authorities about the roadmap for public sector activities 2025–2030, which the government has decided upon, and with developing support for labor law conditions and monitoring.

The government believes that these measures can make it easier for authorities to set labor law conditions and to monitor compliance with them.

With this document, the government considers the National Audit Agency's report to be finalized.
