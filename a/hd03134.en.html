TL;DRSweden aims to join new international regulations to prevent violence and harassment in the workplace, and to create a safer working environment.
In plain language

The government has sent a proposal to the Parliament for Sweden to approve new international regulations from the ILO. The ILO is an organization that works to improve working conditions for employees around the world. The new regulations aim to prevent violence and harassment in the workplace.

The proposal also includes new rules to ensure that all workers have a safe and healthy working environment. This means that employers must ensure that no one is injured or becomes ill as a result of their work. The government also describes new recommendations that provide guidance on these issues.

In addition to issues related to safety, the government also provides information on new guidelines for apprenticeship programs. An apprenticeship is a form of training where a person learns a trade through practical work. The goal is for these programs to maintain a high and good quality for all participants.

Full text

The proposal suggests that the Parliament should approve the ILO Convention (No. 190) on the elimination of violence and harassment in the workplace. The convention was adopted by the International Labour Conference in 2019. The government also informs the Parliament about the ILO Recommendation (No. 206) on the same subject.

Furthermore, the proposal suggests that the Parliament should approve the ILO Convention (No. 191) on occupational safety and health (amendments) which was adopted by the Labour Conference in 2023. The government also informs the Parliament about the ILO Recommendation (No. 207) on the same subject.

The government also informs the Parliament about the ILO Recommendation (No. 208) on quality apprenticeships, which was adopted by the Labour Conference in 2023.
