In plain language

The Swedish Social Insurance Agency (Försäkringskassan), the government agency responsible for managing parental leave benefits in Sweden, investigated whether major sporting events influence how fathers take their leave. They examined data from several years, focusing on the FIFA World Cup and the Olympic Games.

The analysis revealed that there is no difference in the number of men taking parental leave during these championships compared to other times of the year. Therefore, the idea that fathers choose their leave solely to watch sports is a myth.

Instead, the reason for variations in the number of fathers on leave throughout the year is related to the time of year. For example, many fathers take leave during the summer or before Christmas, regardless of whether there are any major sporting events taking place.

June 11, 2026

The FIFA World Cup and the Olympic Games do not affect how fathers take parental leave. This is shown by an analysis conducted by the Swedish Social Insurance Agency of fathers' utilization of parental benefits during major championships. "The idea that fathers take parental leave to watch football is a myth," says Patrik Zetterberg, an analyst at the Swedish Social Insurance Agency.

The Swedish Social Insurance Agency has analyzed how fathers have used parental benefits in connection with the FIFA World Cups of 2018 and 2022, as well as the Olympic Games of 2021 and 2024. The analysis compares fathers' use of parental benefits in the month before, during, and after the championships to how it usually looks in other years.

"The comparison shows that there does not seem to be any World Cup or Olympic effect at all. The number of men who take parental leave during major championships does not stand out compared to the same month in other years," says Patrik Zetterberg, an analyst at the Swedish Social Insurance Agency.

Despite this, there are differences between the months.

"The fact that the utilization is high in some months is more likely due to seasonal effects. For example, the FIFA World Cup 2022 was just before the Christmas holidays, and generally fewer people take parental leave during that time. The FIFA World Cup 2018 took place during the summer, which is also the period each year when most men take parental leave," says Patrik Zetterberg.
