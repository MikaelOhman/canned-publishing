The purpose is to comply with new EU regulations regarding so-called fluorinated gases (F-gases) and ozone-depleting substances. These gases are dangerous because they contribute to climate change and damage the ozone layer surrounding the Earth.

Violations of the new regulations should result in penalties. For example, it is proposed that it become punishable to release these gases into the air. The handling of these gases will also be more strictly controlled to prevent environmental damage. Full text

The proposal suggests changes to the environmental code's criminal provisions, aiming to complement the EU's new regulations on fluorinated greenhouse gases (F-gases) and ozone-depleting substances (ODS).

It is proposed that the criminal provisions regarding the handling of hazardous chemicals be adapted to the new regulations. Furthermore, it is proposed that violations of additional prohibitions and restrictions under the new regulations be subject to penalties. These changes include making it punishable to release fluorinated greenhouse gases and ozone-depleting substances into the atmosphere. Additionally, the proposed criminal liability would extend to additional stages in the handling of such gases. It is also proposed that violations of the documentation requirements when using sulfuryl fluoride for fumigation be punishable by fines.

Most of the proposed amendments to the law are scheduled to come into effect on January 1, 2026. The amendments concerning the prohibitions and restrictions on the use of fluorinated greenhouse gases for maintenance and servicing of stationary cooling equipment and electrical circuit breakers are proposed to come into effect on January 1, 2032, and January 1, 2035, respectively.
