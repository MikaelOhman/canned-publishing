TL;DRThe government wants to change the rules regarding the sale of weapons and military equipment to other countries. This is being done to align Sweden with its new membership in NATO.
In plain language

Sweden has become a member of NATO, which means we need to change our rules regarding the sale of military equipment to other countries. The government is therefore proposing new guidelines for the export of military equipment and that Sweden should join an international agreement on the control of defense equipment.

The proposal also includes a requirement for a permit under the weapons law to manufacture firearms and similar items. The government also wants to be able to grant exemptions for companies that only manufacture small parts for larger weapons systems. The purpose is to make the rules more modern and functional for today's needs.

To protect security, it is proposed that the penalties for serious violations of the law on military equipment should be stricter. It will also become more expensive to receive fines, so-called sanction fees, if the rules are violated. Certain information related to the control of weapons will also be subject to confidentiality, which means that it cannot be shared with anyone.

If the Parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new laws will come into effect on August 1, 2026. The proposal involves changes to several different laws, including the law on military equipment, the weapons law, and the law on public access and confidentiality.

Full text

The proposals in the government bill primarily aim to address the changed conditions resulting from Sweden's NATO membership and to create a more effective legal framework for the export of military equipment.

The proposals include:

  • Adjustments to Sweden's NATO membership should be made in the government's guidelines for export and other international cooperation.
  • The Parliament should approve Sweden's accession to the agreement on export controls in the defense sector.
  • A license according to the Weapons Act should be required to manufacture firearms and items classified as firearms under that law.
  • The government should be authorized to issue regulations regarding exemptions from the licensing requirement for subcontractors manufacturing parts for military equipment.
  • The penalties for serious violations of the law on military equipment should be increased.
  • The rules for reporting on bids or entering into contracts should be simplified.
  • The amount of the sanction fee should be increased, and the possibility of adjusting the fee should be slightly expanded.
  • Confidentiality should apply to certain information subject to control under the export control regulations.

The proposed amendments to the laws are intended to come into force on August 1, 2026.

Proposed Parliamentary Decisions

The government's proposals:

  1. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Act (1992:1300) on military equipment.
  2. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Public Access and Secrecy Act (2009:400).
  3. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Weapons Act (2026:000).
  4. The Parliament approves Sweden's accession to the agreement on export controls in the defense sector (section 12).
