TL;DRThe EU is updating the list of authorities responsible for child support payments in several countries, including Finland. This aims to clarify which offices are responsible for handling these cases.
In plain language

The European Union has updated the regulations regarding which authorities are responsible for handling child support matters. This concerns payments made to ensure children receive what they need after a divorce or separation. By updating the list, it becomes clearer for both citizens and authorities in different countries.

In Finland, the names of the authorities responsible for these cases have changed. For cases that began before 2023, the responsibility lies with the social services department. However, from 2023 onwards, the responsibility falls to welfare districts or specific cities, such as Helsinki and Åland.

These regulations also apply to other countries, including Latvia, England, Wales, and Scotland. The purpose of this change is to ensure that the correct authority handles cases related to children's financial support throughout the entire EU area.

COMMISSION IMPLEMENTING REGULATION (EU) 2026/1397

amending Annex X to Council Regulation (EC) No 4/2009 on jurisdiction, applicable law, recognition and enforcement of judgments in matrimonial matters and the cooperation in parental responsibility cases.

THE EUROPEAN COMMISSION HAS ADOPTED THIS REGULATION,

having regard to the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union,

having regard to Council Regulation (EC) No 4/2009 of 18 December 2008 on jurisdiction, applicable law, recognition and enforcement of judgments in matrimonial matters and the cooperation in parental responsibility cases ( 1 ), particularly Article 2.2, and

whereas Annex X to Regulation (EC) No 4/2009 contains a list of the administrative authorities referred to in Article 2.2 of that Regulation;

whereas Finland has notified the Commission of changes to the administrative authorities which are to be listed in Annex X to Regulation (EC) No 4/2009;

whereas the administrative authorities notified by Finland and listed in the Annex to this Regulation meet the requirements of Article 2.2 of Regulation (EC) No 4/2009;

(1) Whereas Annex X to Regulation (EC) No 4/2009 should therefore be amended accordingly;

whereas the measures provided for in this Regulation are consistent with the opinion of the Committee on Applicable Law, Jurisdiction and Enforcement of Judgments in Matrimonial Matters, Parental Responsibility Cases and Maintenance Obligations;

(1) Whereas Annex X to Regulation (EC) No 4/2009 is replaced by the text set out in the Annex to this Regulation;

This Regulation shall enter into force on the twentieth day after its publication in the Official Journal of the European Union.

This Regulation shall be binding in its entirety and directly applicable in the Member States in accordance with the Treaties.

( 1 ) OJ L 7, 10.1.2009, p. 1 , ELI: http://data.europa.eu/eli/reg/2009/4(1)/oj .

The administrative authorities referred to in Article 2.2 of Regulation (EC) No 4/2009 are as follows:

In Latvia, the Maintenance Guarantee Fund ("Uzturlīdzekļu Garantiju Fonds").

In Finland, the relevant authority for proceedings initiated no later than December 31, 2022, is the "sosiaalilautakunta" (social board); for proceedings initiated from January 1, 2023, it is the "hyvinvointialue" (well-being area), and in the city of Helsinki and on the Åland Islands, it is the municipality.

In England, Wales, and Scotland, the responsible authority is The Department for Work and Pensions (DWP), including its administrative branches: the Child Support Agency (CSA) and the Child Maintenance Service (CMS).

In Northern Ireland, the responsible authority is the Child Maintenance Service (CMS).

(1) This applies only to proceedings initiated before the end of the transitional period, as specified in Article 67 of the Agreement on the withdrawal of the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland from the European Union and the European Atomic Energy Community (OJ C 384I, November 12, 2019, p. 1).
