Johan Joachim Agrell, born February 1, 1701, in the parish of Löts, Memming district, Östergötland County, and died January 19, 1765, in Nuremberg, was a Swedish musician and composer.

== Biography == Johan Agrell was the son of pastor Johannes Agrelius and Margareta Landelius. He likely received his basic musical education at the gymnasium (secondary school) in Linköping under Eric Duræus and Anders Duræus. In 1721, he enrolled at Uppsala University and became a music scholarship recipient in 1723. He probably studied with Roman, and in 1723, he was invited by Prince Maximilian of Hesse-Kassel to become a chamber musician in Kassel. From there, in 1737, he moved to the court orchestra of Wilhelm VIII of Hesse-Kassel. In 1747, he became Stadtmusicus (city musician) in Nuremberg, where he remained until his death. Agrell made several trips, including to Italy, France, and England, and was well-received everywhere. He published solo works for piano and violin, piano concertos, symphonies, etc., and is of musical historical importance for the development of sonata form. No music from his early years in Sweden has been preserved. However, he dedicated his "Sei Sonate per il Cembalo Solo," six harpsichord sonatas, to King Adolf Frederick with a wish to be called back to Sweden. The king, however, already had a harpsichordist, Henrik Philip Johnsen, and Agrell never returned to Sweden.
