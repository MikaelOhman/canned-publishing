In plain language

The government has proposed a plan that would allow certain companies to delay the start of their sustainability reporting. Sustainability reporting is when companies are required to disclose how their operations impact the environment and people. The proposal stems from changes in EU regulations.

If the proposal is approved, the new rules will come into effect on December 31, 2025. This means that the companies affected will have an extra two years before they must comply with the new reporting requirements.

The proposal is now being sent to the Parliament for a decision. It involves amending three different laws that govern how companies must prepare their annual reports. These laws apply to companies such as banks, insurance companies, and other businesses that produce annual reports.

Full text

The government proposes that the obligation to report on sustainability, according to the new regulations for corporate sustainability reporting, be postponed by two years for certain companies. The changes implement the EU's amending directive regarding when companies should begin sustainability reporting. The proposed amendments are to come into force on December 31, 2025.
