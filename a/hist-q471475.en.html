Full text

Inge the Younger (Inge Halstensson) was King of Sweden from 1118 until the early 1120s. He was the son of Halsten, brother of Filip, and nephew of Inge the Elder. He belonged to the House of Stenkil. According to the royal chronicles *Hervar Saga* and *Lanfedgatal*, he is said to have co-ruled with his brother Filip, a claim not confirmed by Swedish sources. According to the Westrogothic Law's royal chronicle, he ascended the throne after Filip's death, which occurred in 1118 according to an account in *Islendingabok*. The royal chronicle states that Sweden prospered during his reign, but that he died from "poison" in Östergötland. There are no records of when he died, and the only certain information is Saxo Grammaticus's statement that after a Swedish king's death, the Danes appointed Prince Magnus as king, who was killed in 1134. According to the *Lilla rimkrönikan* (Little Rhyming Chronicle) and a copy of the *Prosakrönikan* (Prose Chronicle), Inge the Younger was married to Ragnhild, a woman known in the history of Södertälje. According to *Fagrskinna*, he was married to Ulvhild, daughter of the Norwegian chieftain Håkon Finnsson (of the House of Thjotta). She is also said to have been married to the Danish King Nils and later to the Swedish King Sverker. Little is known about Inge the Younger's reign. During the 1120s, according to Snorre Sturlasson (who seems to have based his account on a now only fragmentarily preserved passage in *Ágrip*), the Norwegian King Sigurd Jerusalemfarer, at the initiative of the Danish King Nils (who was supposed to participate but had some kind of impediment), led a crusade to the Kalmar region, known as the "Kalmar levy." The Norwegians reportedly brought with them 1,500 animals and other valuables. Furthermore, some pagan Smålanders are said to have been converted. However, it is unclear exactly how this levy should be interpreted. Inge the Younger had no known children, and with him, the House of Stenkil likely became extinct.
