Full text

Hermann Conring, born on November 9, 1606, and died on December 12, 1681, was a German scientist.

Conring studied theology and medicine in Leiden. In 1632, he became a professor of natural philosophy at the University of Helmstedt, but in 1637, he moved to the medical faculty there. King Louis XVI greatly admired Conring, and Queen Christina appointed him her personal physician from 1650 to 1651. Conring also received high international recognition for his legal expertise.

Among Conring's medical works, "De sanguinis generatione et motu naturali" (1643) is particularly noteworthy, as it helped spread William Harvey's understanding of the circulatory system. In the field of law, his valuable overview "De origine juris germanici" (1643) stands out.
