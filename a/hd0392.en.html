In plain language

The government has proposed new regulations for individuals who have a legal guardian or a legal representative. A legal guardian or legal representative is someone who helps others manage their finances or personal affairs. The new proposals aim to make the assistance more equitable and ensure that those receiving help can trust that everything is being handled correctly.

There are plans to create a new government agency responsible for overseeing the work. This agency will ensure that those working as legal representatives receive proper training and access to relevant information. A national registry will also be established. A registry is a list that makes it easier for banks and other authorities to verify that the person assisting you has the legal authority to do so.

The government also wants to change how the guardianship boards operate. Guardianship boards are the individuals within municipalities who monitor whether legal guardians are fulfilling their duties properly. They will be given more authority to make decisions in certain cases. There is also a desire to make it clearer that the wishes and needs of the person receiving assistance should be prioritized.

Most of the new regulations are intended to come into effect on July 1, 2026. The proposals involve changes to many different laws, including laws related to parents, social security, and corporate governance.

Full text

People who need a legal guardian or conservator to manage their financial or personal affairs should be able to rely on receiving the necessary help and support. This requires a fair and modern legal framework that puts the individual at the center. The government proposes a number of measures aimed at strengthening the individual's position, improving the quality of guardianship services, and creating better opportunities to recruit and retain competent guardians.

The work performed by a legal guardian or conservator is often a prerequisite for the individual to live as independently as possible. The government proposes that the scope of the guardian's duties be made clearer, with increased consideration for the individual's wishes and a greater focus on the individual's well-being.

Furthermore, the government proposes that a central government agency be given responsibility in the area of guardianship. The agency will provide guidance and supervision and, among other things, be able to provide information, issue regulations, and ensure that guardians receive training.

The government also proposes the establishment of a national guardianship registry. The registry will facilitate the supervision of legal guardians and conservators and make it easier for guardians to demonstrate their qualifications when dealing with authorities and banks.

In addition, proposals are being made to improve the work of guardianship boards. The guardianship boards will also be given the authority to make decisions in all guardianship cases that are not disputed, and the possibility of appointing an employed guardian will be regulated by law.

Most of the proposed amendments to the law are scheduled to come into force on July 1, 2026.
