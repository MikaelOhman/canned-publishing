In plain language

The runestone is located in Toresund, within the municipality of Strängnäs. It's a large granite stone, over two meters tall. The stone features carved runes, which were the ancient alphabet used in Sweden in the past.

Part of the text on the stone can be read, and it tells us that a person named Onese erected the stone in memory of his father, Olav. Because these stones were erected long ago, they are important historical sources that help us understand the past.

Some parts of the inscription on the right side are difficult to decipher and have not been fully translated. This means that researchers cannot be completely certain about every word, but the stone remains an important historical artifact registered by the Swedish National Heritage Board (Riksantikvarieämbetet).
