The government proposes a new law on cybersecurity. The proposal originates from a decision made by the EU in 2022 and is called the NIS 2 Directive. The purpose is to create a higher and more unified level of security against digital threats throughout Europe.

According to the new law, those who operate critical services, both government and private companies, must take measures to protect their networks and computer systems. If something serious happens, such as a major cyberattack, they must also report this to the authorities. It will also become easier for authorities to monitor compliance with the rules and to penalize those who do not fulfill their responsibilities.

The proposal also includes changes to other laws related to internet addresses, communication, and regulations for classified information. These changes are part of a broader effort to strengthen security in Sweden.

If the Parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new law and the other changes are planned to come into effect on January 15, 2026.

Full text

In 2022, the EU adopted a directive on measures for a high common level of cybersecurity within the EU, the so-called NIS 2 Directive. To implement the directive into Swedish law, the government proposes the introduction of a new cybersecurity law.

This new law will require public and private entities operating in certain designated sectors to take measures to protect their networks and information systems, and to report significant incidents. The new law also includes regulations on supervision and enforcement measures for entities that do not comply with the law's provisions. Furthermore, changes are proposed to other laws related to electronic communications, top-level domains, and privacy.

The new law and the other proposed amendments are scheduled to come into force on January 15, 2026.
