TL;DRThe government wants to terminate an agreement that grants the organization GWPO and its employees special rights and protections in Sweden. This means they will no longer have immunity, which means they will be subject to Swedish laws in the same way as everyone else.
In plain language

The government has proposed to the Parliament that the agreement with an international organization called GWPO be terminated. GWPO works on water-related issues worldwide. Through this agreement, the organization and its employees have been granted certain immunities and privileges. This means they have had special rights that have, at times, prevented them from being held accountable in court or from being subject to all Swedish laws in the same way as ordinary citizens.

To implement this, the government wants to amend an existing law from 1976. The purpose of the amendment is to ensure that these special rights no longer apply to anyone working for GWPO, including those with assignments from the organization, and also to their family members residing in Sweden.

Once the amendment is in place, these individuals and their families will no longer have this extra layer of protection and will be subject to the regular Swedish laws. The government will determine the exact date when the new law will come into effect.

Full text

In the proposal, the government suggests that the Parliament approves the termination of the agreement with the Global Water Partnership Organisation (GWPO) regarding the organization's and its staff's immunity and privileges, as soon as possible. Furthermore, the proposal includes an amendment to the law (1976:661) concerning immunity and privileges in certain cases, so that immunity and privileges will no longer apply to GWPO, individuals employed by or contracted by the organization, and their family members. The law amendment is proposed to come into effect on the date determined by the government.
