Rikissa of Denmark, born before 1183 and died on May 8, 1220, was a Danish princess who became Queen of Sweden through her marriage to King Eric Knutsson.

== Biography == Rikissa of Denmark was the daughter of King Valdemar the Great of Denmark and Sofia of Minsk. She was the granddaughter of Rikissa of Poland, who was also a Swedish queen. The marriage between Rikissa and Eric was intended to confirm peace between Sweden and Denmark. After his victory at Gestilren, where King Sverker Karlsson was killed, King Eric wrote to Valdemar Sejr of Denmark requesting his sister's hand in marriage. Through this marriage, he aimed to diminish the influence of the Sverkers dynasty in Denmark and increase the power of his own lineage.

Valdemar had previously supported the Sverkers dynasty, but after Sverker Karlsson's death in his attempt to regain the throne, Valdemar realized that Denmark would gain nothing from continuing the war against Sweden. Therefore, Valdemar agreed to the marriage, and Rikissa traveled to Sweden. Eric died in 1216 and was buried in Varnhem monastery church. Rikissa died in 1220 and was buried in Sankt Bendts Kirke, alongside her parents, in Ringsted.

== Children == Sofia (died before April 24, 1241), married to Prince Henry Burwin III of Mecklenburg (died 1277/1278). Marianne, a Pomeranian princess, also known as Mariana and Marina. Ingeborg, married to Birger Jarl. Eric, later known as Eric the Learned and Eric the Lame (posthumous, born in 1216 after his father's death).
