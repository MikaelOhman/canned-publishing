In plain language

The government wants to change how state funds will be used in 2026. Among the major decisions is a proposal to purchase a property in Solna for just over 3.4 billion Swedish krona. There are also proposals to lease a submarine of the A 17 type to Poland and to change the rules for dental care subsidies.

A significant part of the proposal concerns energy and nuclear power. The state plans to invest a large amount of money in the company Videberg Kraft AB. This includes purchasing shares to own a significant portion of the company, as well as paying for programs that manage nuclear waste. These costs could be very high in the coming years.

There are also proposals related to security and technology. The government wants to be able to use funds to protect individuals working in secret intelligence services. It is also proposed to provide funds to the space company SSC Space AB to strengthen Sweden's work in space. New rules are proposed for how student loans for police officers working in service should be handled.

Finally, there are smaller budget changes for various agencies and culture. The Royal Opera is given the opportunity to borrow money to renovate its building. Agencies such as the Swedish Medical Products Agency and the Swedish Defence Radio Agency will also have their budgets adjusted to enable them to carry out their tasks in 2026.

The proposal includes suggested changes regarding the state's expenditures for the fiscal year 2026.

Proposed Resolutions for the Parliament

Government Proposal:

  1. The Parliament approves the government's proposal to amend the Act (2008:145) on state dental care support (sections 2 and 4.2).
  2. The Parliament approves the government acquiring the property Solna Haga 2:8 in 2026 for a maximum of 3,483,000,000 Swedish krona (section 5.2).
  3. The Parliament approves that the interest rate on student loans should be determined in a way that does not include calculated future credit losses for the write-off of student loans based on service as a police officer (section 5.4).
  4. The Parliament approves that the write-off of student loans based on service as a police officer should be financed with appropriations in the year the loan is written off (section 5.4).
  5. The Parliament authorizes the government to decide on the acquisition of shares or holdings in companies, and on capital contributions to companies, to protect individuals or activities that will be carried out at the Swedish Intelligence Service, within the framework of a specifically decided protective identity according to the Act on Qualified Protective Identities, and to decide on the sale or liquidation of companies that have been acquired for similar purposes (section 5.5).
  6. The Parliament approves the proposed use of appropriation 1:8, the Swedish Defence Radio Agency, within expenditure area 6, Defence and Societal Crisis Preparedness (section 5.6).
  7. The Parliament authorizes the government to decide in 2026 on a credit at the National Debt Office to meet the Swedish Defence Materiel Agency's need for working capital, which, including previously utilized credit, amounts to a maximum of 47,000,000,000 Swedish krona (section 5.6).
  8. The Parliament approves the government leasing a submarine of the A 17 type to Poland (section 5.6).
  9. The Parliament authorizes the government, in 2026 and 2027, for appropriation 3:1, the Swedish Radiation Safety Authority, to enter into financial commitments in the form of agreements with Videberg Kraft AB for fixed costs for a new nuclear waste program based on established technology for spent nuclear fuel and radioactive waste from new nuclear power plants, totaling a maximum of 122 billion Swedish krona at the 2026 price level, for the period 2035–2159 (section 5.6).
  10. The Parliament authorizes the government, in 2026 and 2027, for appropriation 3:1, the Swedish Radiation Safety Authority, to enter into financial commitments in the form of agreements with Videberg Kraft AB for increases in fixed costs for a new nuclear waste program based on established technology for spent nuclear fuel and radioactive waste from new nuclear power plants, totaling a maximum of 61 billion Swedish krona at the 2026 price level, for the period 2035–2159 (section 5.6).
  11. The Parliament approves the proposed use of appropriation 3:1, the Swedish Radiation Safety Authority, within expenditure area 6, Defence and Societal Crisis Preparedness (section 5.6).
  12. The Parliament approves the proposed use of appropriation 1:9, the Swedish Medical Products Agency, within expenditure area 9, Health Care, Medical Care, and Social Services (section 5.9).
  13. The Parliament authorizes the government to decide in 2026 that the Royal Opera AB may take out loans from the National Debt Office for investments resulting from the renovation and reconstruction of the opera house, which, including previous borrowing, amounts to a maximum of 130,000,000 Swedish krona (section 5.14).
  14. The Parliament approves the proposed use of appropriation 1:7, Traffic Agreements, within expenditure area 22, Communications (section 5.17).
