Gustaf VI Adolf, born November 11, 1882, at the Royal Palace in Stockholm, and died September 15, 1973, at Helsingborg Hospital in Helsingborg, was King of Sweden from October 29, 1950, until his death. He was the son of Gustaf V and Victoria, and grandfather of Carl XVI Gustaf. He married Princess Margaret of Great Britain and Ireland (1882–1920) in 1905, and remarried Louise of Battenberg (1889–1965) in 1923. He was born as a Prince of Sweden and Norway, and Duke of Skåne (after the dissolution of the union in 1905, he was no longer a Prince of Norway), and served as Crown Prince of Sweden from 1907 to 1950, following his father's accession to the throne. In his declaration upon becoming king, Gustaf VI Adolf used the title "By the Grace of God, King of Sweden, the Goths and the Vandals," a title not used by his successor, Carl XVI Gustaf.

== Childhood ==

=== Early Years ===

On Saturday, November 11, 1882, at 4:25 PM, Princess Victoria of Baden gave birth to the eldest son of Crown Prince Gustaf. The witnesses were Prime Minister Count Arvid Posse and Foreign Minister Baron Carl Fredrik Hochschild, along with their wives. Shortly after, the announcement of the prince's birth was made at Artilleriplan, and a salute of 42 shots was fired.

The Duke of Skåne, Prince Oscar Fredrik Wilhelm Olaf Gustaf Adolf, was baptized in the White Sea Hall at Stockholm Palace on November 25th of the same year by Archbishop Anton Niklas Sundberg. The water used for the baptism was supposedly taken from the Jordan River, but it actually came from the palace's well. Bishop Claes Herman Rundgren of the Karlstad diocese and the Order Bishop Frithiof Grafström were also present at the altar. Karl XII's cradle served as the ceremonial bassinet for the newly baptized prince.

Louise Rinman was the children's nurse, and they affectionately called her "Vass." The first governess was Miss Ida Borin, but she was replaced in 1890 by tutor Dr. Carl Svedelius. In woodworking, the princes were taught by instructor Hjalmar Berg.

A significant portion of their childhood summers were spent either at Tullgarns Castle or at Mainau Castle with their grandparents. Grandmother Queen Sofia often sent for the princes to Ulriksdals Palace, which was her favorite residence.

=== Education ===

Gustaf Adolf received private tutoring and passed his final exams in 1900. In the spring of 1901, the Crown Prince enrolled at Uppsala University and joined the Stockholms nation on February 8, 1901. His lecturers included the historian Harald Hjärne, the political scientist Simon Boëthius, the economist David Davidsson, and the archaeologist Oscar Almgren. From this period comes the famous Bronze Age finds that the Duke of Skåne discovered during excavations at the Hågahögen burial mound, now housed in the Swedish National Historical Museum. While at Uppsala, Gustaf Adolf also enjoyed student life, participating in the Philochorus student association, which promoted folk music and dance.

During his studies in Uppsala, he also received military training and obtained his officer's commission in 1902. After completing a course at the Military Academy, he was promoted to captain (cavalry) in 1909, major in 1913, lieutenant colonel in 1916, colonel in 1918, major general in 1923, and lieutenant general in 1928.

In addition to his native Swedish, Gustaf VI Adolf was fluent in English, French, and German. He also read Italian with ease and understood Latin. On May 10, 1906, he was elected an honorary member of the Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences.

== Family ==

On January 26, 1905, Gustaf Adolf was in Cairo, Egypt, where he met Princess Margaret, the eldest daughter of Prince Arthur, at a birthday banquet for Khedive Abbas Hilmi Pasha of Egypt. The prince was originally supposed to be seated next to Margaret's younger sister, Patricia, who was rumored to be his future bride, but instead, he chose Margaret as his table companion. They became engaged on February 26, 1905, in Cairo, and married on June 15 at St. George's Chapel in Windsor Castle.

As a wedding gift, the couple received Sofiero Palace from Gustaf Adolf's grandfather, Oscar II. Gustaf VI Adolf retained Sofiero Palace throughout his life as a recreational and summer residence, and he lived most of his life in the Kansliflygeln (office wing) at the Royal Palace.

=== Children === With Princess Margaret:

Gustaf Adolf (1906–1947), Duke of Västerbotten, father of King Carl XVI Gustaf. Sigvard (1907–2002), Duke of Uppland until 1934, Count of Wisborg. Ingrid (1910–2000), Queen of Denmark from 1947, mother of the Danish Queen Margrethe II. Bertil (1912–1997), Duke of Halland. Carl Johan (1916–2012), Duke of Dalarna until 1946, Count of Wisborg. Crown Princess Margaret fell ill and died in the spring of 1920 from acute sepsis while eight months pregnant. After his wife's death, Crown Prince Gustaf Adolf traveled extensively, including to England in 1923, where he met Louise of Battenberg at a dinner party among friends.

The engagement to Louise of Battenberg was announced on July 1, 1923, and the couple were married on November 3 of the same year in the Chapel Royal at St. James's Palace in London by the Archbishop of Canterbury.
