In plain language

The government has proposed a new law regarding how municipalities and regions can operate ports. The purpose is to ensure that Swedish regulations are in line with EU rules on state aid, which refers to funds provided by the state or municipality to companies.

If a municipality or region operates a port that functions as a regular market, they must establish a separate municipal limited liability company for this purpose. This means that the port should be operated as a regular business with a focus on profitability. However, there is an exception for ports that are only used by local individuals and that do not affect trade between different countries within the EU.

The new law is scheduled to come into effect on August 1, 2026. If a municipality already operates its port as part of its regular operations, it can continue to do so in that manner until December 31, 2028.

Full text

The proposal suggests a new law regarding municipal port operations. The proposal is submitted to ensure that Swedish regulations on municipal port operations are considered consistent with EU rules on state aid. The proposal stipulates that a municipality or region that operates port services in a market must conduct the operations through a municipal limited liability company. This municipal company must operate the port services on a commercial basis. However, port operations may be conducted as part of the municipal administration if the port is primarily used by local users and the operations cannot be considered to affect trade between member states within the European Union.

The new law is proposed to come into force on August 1, 2026. A municipality or region that, before the law comes into force, operates port services as part of the municipal administration may, despite what is stated in the law, continue to operate the port services within the administration until December 31, 2028.
