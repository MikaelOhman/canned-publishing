TL;DRThe government wants to amend the law to give the Swedish Migration Agency (Migrationsverket) more authority to monitor individuals held in detention centers. The aim is to increase safety and order within these facilities.
In plain language

The government has proposed a plan that would allow the Swedish Migration Agency (Migrationsverket) to use more controls and coercive measures. This means that staff will be able to more easily inspect packages, conduct body searches, and search residential areas to ensure safety.

There are also proposals for new ways to control visitors, such as so-called "glass-walled visits" where direct contact is avoided. In addition, the Migration Agency may introduce security checks at entry points. To monitor individuals who are not physically present within the facilities, the proposal suggests that security guards should be authorized to perform certain tasks.

The proposal concerns changes to the so-called "Foreigners Act." If the Parliament approves the proposal, the new rules will come into effect on August 1, 2025.

Full text

The proposal suggests that the Swedish Migration Agency (Migrationsverket) should be given expanded possibilities to use coercive and control measures at detention centers, in order to improve the ability to maintain order and security. The proposals include:

  • Body searches should be allowed in more cases and with a lower level of suspicion than currently.
  • Packages should be inspected with a lower level of suspicion than currently.
  • Detainees' rooms and other enclosed areas within the detention center should be subject to inspection.
  • The Migration Agency should be able to introduce general entry controls if necessary.
  • A new form of visitor control, known as "glass-walled visits," should be implemented.

In addition, it is proposed that security personnel should be allowed to perform certain security tasks when a detained foreign national is outside the detention center premises. Certain changes to the regulations regarding oral proceedings in the migration court are also proposed.

The proposed amendments to the law are intended to come into force on August 1, 2025.
