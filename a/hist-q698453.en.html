Full text

Georg von Derfflinger, born March 20, 1606, and died March 14, 1695, was an Austrian imperial baron and military figure.

Derfflinger was a Protestant peasant's son from Austria. During the Thirty Years' War, he served in the Swedish army and became known as one of the most capable cavalry commanders. After Johan Banér's death in 1641, Derfflinger acted as the representative of the cavalry officers in their negotiations with representatives of the Swedish government. In 1643, he was promoted to major general but retired to his estates in Gusow after the peace treaty in 1648. In 1654, he entered service with Brandenburg and played a distinguished role in the war until 1660, including at the Battle of Warsaw, and rose to the rank of field marshal. After the peace, he returned to his estates but remained in service and was promoted to field marshal in 1670. In recognition of his participation in the war against France in 1672, Derfflinger was made a hereditary baron in 1674. In the subsequent war against Sweden, Derfflinger surprised the Swedes at Rathenow and contributed significantly to the victory at the Battle of Fehrbellin in 1675. In 1676, he successfully led the siege of Stettin, and in 1678, he captured Rügen and Stralsund. In 1679, he repelled the Swedish attack on Prussia but retired that same year after the peace treaty. Derfflinger was considered a daring and skilled cavalry general and one of the first organizers of the Brandenburg-Prussian army.
