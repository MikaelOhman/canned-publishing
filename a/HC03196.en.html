The Swedish National Audit Office's report on higher education institutions' efforts to prevent dropouts in vocational training programs.

Source Riksdagen Read the original at Riksdagen →2025-06-24

In this document, the government presents its assessment of the observations, conclusions, and recommendations made by the Swedish National Audit Agency (Riksrevisionen) in its review report, "Higher Education Institutions' Efforts to Prevent Dropout from Programs with Skills Shortages" (RiR 2025:3).

The Swedish National Audit Agency's overall conclusion is that higher education institutions offer many support measures to prevent dropouts from the reviewed programs with skills shortages, but there are several significant obstacles to effective work, some of which are beyond the control of the institutions. The Swedish National Audit Agency notes that higher education institutions need to increase their knowledge of student dropouts and provides several recommendations to the government to increase this knowledge so that the right support measures can be implemented.

The government welcomes the review and largely agrees with the Swedish National Audit Agency's observations. The government has already taken several measures to improve the quality of higher education, including increasing the compensation for certain educational areas to enable more teacher-led time and improved learning environments. Furthermore, the government has invested in higher education pedagogy in engineering programs and expanded preparatory courses. This creates good conditions for increasing completion rates and reducing dropouts. However, the report provides valuable knowledge about dropouts and completion rates in higher education. Together with the work already being done, it contributes to improving the government's and higher education institutions' knowledge of this issue.

With this document, the government considers the Swedish National Audit Agency's report to be finalized.
