TL;DRThe Swedish National Audit Agency has reviewed how the government and LKAB plan to transition to producing sponge iron. They believe the work is good, but that the rules for setting financial goals need to be clarified.
In plain language

The Swedish National Audit Office is an agency that monitors how the state and its funds are used. They have investigated whether the government and the state-owned mining company LKAB have worked in a good and business-like manner when planning their new operations. They have examined how the company will transition from solely mining ore to also producing sponge iron, which is an important part of the green transition.

The audit shows that both the government and LKAB have done a good job in considering economic and business aspects. However, the National Audit Office points out that there are shortcomings in how economic goals are determined for state-owned companies. They want the government to ensure that the goals for how much money the company should earn (profitability) are used in the same way each time.

The government agrees that it would be beneficial if the documentation, i.e., the written evidence of the decisions made, were clearer. To address this, the Government Offices, which is the part of the state that assists the government in its work, has already reviewed its regulations. They want to ensure that all important decisions and assumptions are documented clearly in the future.

Despite the criticism regarding the documentation, the government does not believe that the process of setting the goals was flawed. Therefore, the government does not plan to make any further changes as a result of this report. They consider the matter to be closed.

Full text

In this document, the government presents its assessment of the observations and recommendations made by the Swedish National Audit Agency (Riksrevisionen) in the report "LKAB's Transformation – Planning for a New Value Chain" (RiR 2025:7). The National Audit Agency reviewed whether the government and Luossavaara-Kiirunavaara Aktiebolag (LKAB) have acted in a businesslike manner in the planning of the company's transformation to the production of iron sponge. The agency assesses that both the government, as the owner, and LKAB have, in principle, acted in a businesslike manner. However, the National Audit Agency considers that the process for setting financial goals for state-owned companies is flawed and recommends that the government ensure that the calculated profitability target is applied consistently.

The government agrees with the National Audit Agency's assessment that the documentation of certain decisions made in connection with the development of the financial goals for LKAB could have been clearer. A review of the guidelines for this process has been carried out in the Government Offices, among other things, to ensure that the assumptions underlying the owner's decisions are clearly documented. The government does not otherwise believe that the process for developing the current goals has been flawed.

Against this background, the government does not intend to take any further action as a result of the National Audit Agency's report, and with this document, considers the report to be finalized.
