TL;DRSweden aims to modify the regulations governing when Swedish military personnel can participate in NATO's defense. The goal is to clarify who has the authority to make decisions regarding different types of military operations.
In plain language

The government has proposed a measure to improve Sweden's cooperation with NATO in military matters. This concerns how Sweden utilizes its armed forces when participating in NATO's joint efforts to protect and deter potential adversaries.

As a general rule, the Swedish Parliament (Riksdagen), consisting of elected politicians, must make decisions regarding the deployment of Swedish soldiers on missions. The Parliament can decide whether Sweden will participate for a full year or during a specific military operation. This will be enshrined in a new law called the "Law on Operational Military Cooperation."

However, the proposal includes an exception. In situations requiring rapid decisions, or if there is a significant risk to Sweden's interests, the government should be able to deploy a limited number of Swedish forces without waiting for parliamentary approval. This exception, however, applies only for a limited period.

The proposal also requires amendments to another law, the "Protection Act." If the Parliament approves the proposal, the new regulations are planned to come into effect on December 1, 2025.

Full text

The proposal includes suggestions aimed at creating better conditions for Sweden to participate in operational military cooperation within NATO. Sweden will continuously participate in NATO's collective activities for deterrence and defense. It is proposed that the law include regulations on how decisions should be made to deploy Swedish armed forces in such activities. According to the proposals, participation should, as a general rule, require a decision by the Parliament. Such a decision could cover activities for a calendar year or other period, but also participation in a specific operation or activity. Furthermore, it is proposed that the government, even without a prior decision by the Parliament, should be able to deploy a Swedish armed force with a limited number of personnel for a limited time if the need for Swedish participation arises with short notice, or if a delay could lead to significant harm to important Swedish interests.

It is proposed that the regulations be included in the law on operational military support, which is proposed to be renamed the law on operational military cooperation. As a result of the change in the law's name, changes are also proposed to the Protection Act.

The amendments to the law are proposed to come into force on December 1, 2025.

Proposed Parliamentary Decisions

The government's proposals:

  1. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the law (2020:782) on operational military support.
  2. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Protection Act (2010:305).
