The government has proposed measures to make society safer. The new proposal would make it a crime to escape from detention centers or prisons. It also aims to impose stricter penalties on individuals who commit crimes repeatedly.

For individuals subject to supervised release, a form of monitoring, the government wants to be able to impose restrictions on where they can reside. If a person has connections to criminal gangs, they may be prohibited from visiting certain places or areas. This is intended to protect people and prevent serious crime from continuing.

When the police and courts decide whether a person is eligible for temporary release or when a person is to be released from prison, the person's behavior and connections to criminal groups will be given greater weight. This could also mean that a person being released will be subject to restrictions, such as only being allowed to leave their home at certain times.

If these changes are implemented, the new rules will come into effect on July 2, 2026. The proposals involve changes to several laws, including the Penal Code and the Prison Act.

Full text

The government's proposal includes several measures aimed at strengthening societal security in cases of serious and repeated crime, reducing the risk of recidivism, increasing safety for victims of crime, and increasing the severity of responses to misconduct or relapse into crime. These proposals include the following:

  • Escaping from certain forms of detention, including prisons and holding facilities, will be criminalized.
  • Prior convictions will have a greater impact on sentencing.
  • The possibilities of combining supervised release with imprisonment will be expanded.
  • Individuals sentenced to supervised release or conditionally released will be subject to restrictions on their freedom of movement (residence requirement) if they are involved with or have connections to a group engaged in serious crime, and pose a risk of committing or otherwise participating in serious crime in a specific location or area.
  • Residence requirements may also be imposed to protect victims.
  • The law will state that the behavior of an inmate must be considered when making decisions about temporary release and pre-release measures.
  • The law will state that involvement with or connections to a group engaged in serious crime must be considered when making decisions about temporary release.
  • The law will state that, in certain cases, a pre-release measure must be accompanied by a condition that the inmate may not leave their home except at specified times.

The proposed amendments to the law will come into effect on July 2, 2026.
