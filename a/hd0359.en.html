Accessibility requirements for certain media

Source Riksdagen Read the original at Riksdagen →2025-12-02
TL;DRNew regulations are being proposed to make more information and services easier to use for people with disabilities. The proposal includes the possibility of introducing accessibility requirements for, among other things, e-books and online stores, starting in 2027.
In plain language

The government wants to amend the Swedish constitution, which comprises the most important laws in Sweden. The purpose is to comply with a decision from the EU regarding the need for products and services to be easy to use for everyone, including people with disabilities.

These changes may introduce new requirements for how information should be presented. This could involve making information on packaging easier to read, or ensuring that e-books and online stores function well for all users. It will also become possible to require that services such as subtitles and interpretation are available not only for regular TV channels, but also for other platforms.

For these new rules to be implemented, they must be designed to help people with disabilities. The new rules must also be based on the requirements established within the EU. If the proposal is approved and the parliament votes in favor, the new laws will come into effect on January 1, 2027.

Full text

The proposal suggests changes to the Freedom of the Press Act and the Act on Freedom of Expression, which will allow for more requirements regarding accessibility to be placed on products and services covered by constitutional protection. The purpose of these changes is to ensure that the requirements outlined in the European Parliament and Council Directive (EU) 2019/882 of April 17, 2019, on accessibility requirements for products and services (the Accessibility Directive), can be fully implemented.

The proposal introduces new delegation provisions in the Freedom of the Press Act and the Act on Freedom of Expression, enabling the regulation of certain accessibility requirements through ordinary legislation. A primary condition for the application of these provisions is that the purpose of the requirements is to ensure accessibility for people with disabilities. A second condition is that the regulations are based on an obligation arising from membership in the European Union. The changes include the following:

  • The scope for requiring the inclusion and design of product information, for example on packaging, is expanded.
  • It will be possible to impose accessibility requirements related to information, format, and functionality on, among other things, e-books and e-commerce services.
  • The obligation for website owners to provide accessibility services, such as subtitles and interpretation, may apply to programs other than those of public service broadcasters.

The proposed amendments to the law are scheduled to come into effect on January 1, 2027.
