In plain language

The government has proposed changes to several Swedish laws. The purpose of these changes is to align the new regulations with new rules from the European Union. The changes primarily concern how we view emissions from large vehicles, such as trucks.

The changes will classify more types of heavy vehicles as zero-emission vehicles. This means they will no longer be considered environmentally harmful in the same way as before. This affects laws related to infrastructure charges on roads and how vehicles are categorized based on their carbon dioxide emissions.

The proposal also includes changes to how reference values for carbon dioxide emissions are calculated. There are also changes to the regulations that apply during a transition period, which is the time when we move from the old rules to the new ones.

If the Parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new regulations will come into effect on July 1, 2025. This applies to the laws concerning road charges and charges for heavy vehicles.

Full text

The proposal suggests amendments to the Infrastructure Charges Act (2024:172) and the Act on the Classification of Heavy Vehicles by Carbon Dioxide Emissions (2024:173). These amendments concern which vehicles are considered to be emission-free when applying these laws. The changes are being made because the underlying EU regulations have been amended, resulting in more heavy vehicles being considered emission-free. Regarding the classification of heavy vehicles by carbon dioxide emissions, a consequential amendment is also proposed to the definition of the term "reference value for carbon dioxide emissions." Furthermore, due to the amended EU regulations, changes are proposed to the transitional provisions of the Infrastructure Charges Act and to the transitional provisions of two previous amendments to the Road Toll Act for Certain Heavy Vehicles (1997:1137).

The amendments to the Infrastructure Charges Act, the Act on the Classification of Heavy Vehicles by Carbon Dioxide Emissions, and one of the amendments concerning the Road Toll Act for Certain Heavy Vehicles are proposed to take effect on July 1, 2025.
