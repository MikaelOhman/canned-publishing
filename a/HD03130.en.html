In plain language

The government has sent a report to the Parliament regarding the operations of the national pension funds, known as the AP funds. The report details the performance of the funds and how they have managed their operations up to the year 2025. Each year, the government must provide this information by June 1st.

During the year 2025, changes were implemented to make the management of pension funds more efficient. All eight parties in Parliament agreed to these changes. New laws have also been introduced, requiring those who manage the funds to possess the necessary knowledge and expertise to manage the money effectively. The aim is to make the system more secure and better for the future.

The global economy was uncertain during 2025, but the stock market performed well, and interest rates decreased. At the same time, the Swedish krona became significantly more valuable compared to, for example, the euro and the dollar. Because the krona became so strong, the profits from foreign stocks were lower than initially anticipated.

Despite the impact of the strong krona, the five funds – First AP Fund, Second AP Fund, Third AP Fund, Fourth AP Fund, and Sixth AP Fund – performed well. The total return for these funds was 122 billion krona after all costs were deducted.

Full text

In the report, the government provides an overview of the operations of the National Pension Funds (AP Funds) up to 2025. The report includes, among other things, a summary of the AP Funds' annual reports for 2025 and an evaluation of the AP Funds' operations since 2001. The report is submitted annually to the Parliament no later than June 1st. The report includes the following information:

  • In 2025, a consolidation of the buffer funds was decided to increase the efficiency of managing the buffer capital in the Swedish pension system. All eight parliamentary parties, through the Pension Group, supported the proposals, which, in addition to organizational changes, also included a legal requirement that the board of each AP Fund collectively possess sufficient expertise in areas that are particularly important for the management of the fund, as well as certain changes to the administration of the AP Funds. This reform creates the conditions for a more robust and long-term efficient management of the buffer capital.
  • In 2025, despite significant macroeconomic uncertainty, the global stock markets experienced strong growth, while interest rates fell. The Swedish krona strengthened against both the euro and the US dollar. The significant strengthening of the krona against several major currencies significantly dampened the AP Funds' returns on foreign stock investments.
  • The total result for the First, Second, Third, Fourth, and Sixth AP Funds (the buffer funds) amounted to 122 billion kronor after expenses.
