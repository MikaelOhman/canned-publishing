In plain language

The European Central Bank (ECB) has published new figures regarding banks within the EU. This information details the size of banks' assets and their earnings. The data is compiled from over 2000 different banks and banking groups, providing a comprehensive overview of the entire European banking system.

The report contains important information about the financial health of banks. It includes details on the amount of capital banks have to absorb potential losses, as well as the size of their non-performing loans. It also examines the relationship between banks' capital and their risk exposure, known as capital adequacy.

The figures also reveal the profitability of banks by measuring their profits in relation to their capital. While some of the information is based on international regulations, some smaller banks use their own national regulations to calculate their figures. This can lead to variations in certain parts of the statistics depending on the country in which the bank is located.

All the data presented applies to the 27 countries within the EU. The report also includes revisions to previously published figures. The purpose of releasing this information is to enable analysis of the strength of the European banking system at the end of the year 2025.

The European Central Bank (ECB) has published consolidated bank data as of the end of December 2025, a dataset for the European banking system compiled at the group level.

The annual data provide information needed to analyze the European banking sector and are a subset of the information available in the annual report. The data cover 332 bank groups and 2292 independent credit institutions, as well as non-EU controlled subsidiaries and branches operating in the EU, which corresponds to almost 100% of the EU's banking sector balance sheet. They include a comprehensive range of indicators on profitability and efficiency, balance sheet composition, liquidity and financing, asset quality, loan-to-value ratio, capital adequacy, and solvency. Aggregated data and indicators are published for the reporting population.

Reporting institutions generally apply international accounting standards and the European Banking Authority's (EBA) technical implementation standards for supervisory reporting. However, some smaller and medium-sized reporting institutions may apply national accounting standards. Consequently, aggregated data and indicators may include some data based on national accounting standards, depending on the availability of underlying information.

In addition to data from December 2025 onwards, the published figures also include certain adjustments to previous data.
