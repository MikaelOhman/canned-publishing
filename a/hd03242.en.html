A clear set of rules for active forest management.

TL;DRThe government wants to change the regulations for forestry to make it simpler and clearer to manage forests. The goal is to increase forest growth and reduce complicated rules and costs for landowners.
In plain language

The government proposes new regulations regarding forest harvesting. They want to eliminate certain requirements to obtain permits from authorities under the Environmental Code, unless the government decides otherwise. This is intended to simplify the process for forest owners.

It should also become cheaper for landowners to access the information needed to plan their forestry operations. The government wants the cost of acquiring knowledge to be proportional to the value of the land. Furthermore, it should be easier for forest owners to find out if there are any special plants or animals on their land that require special consideration.

To avoid confusion, the government wants all decisions made by the Swedish Forest Agency to be reviewed by the same type of court, namely the environmental and land court. They also want to change the rules regarding how environmental organizations can appeal decisions, so that the process is more fair and transparent for all parties involved.

Finally, the government wants to do more to promote better forest growth and to ensure access to more Swedish raw materials from the forest. They also want to begin more closely monitoring whether the long-term goals for Swedish forestry are actually being achieved.

Full text

The government's proposal states that the link in the ordinance regarding notification for consultation between a felling notification under the Forest Act and a notification for consultation under the Environmental Code should be removed. The consultation obligation under the Environmental Code should only apply to forestry activities under the Forest Act in cases where the government determines this in regulations.

The government proposes that the cost incurred by the landowner to obtain the necessary knowledge should be limited and related to the value of the property.

Furthermore, the government proposes that the principle of confidentiality, intended to protect the interest in preserving animal or plant species, should not prevent information from being provided to a landowner if the information is needed for the landowner to assess whether a notification for consultation under the Environmental Code is required when planning a forestry activity.

To ensure that the same action by the Forest Agency cannot be reviewed by two different courts, the government proposes that decisions by the Forest Agency under the Forest Act should be appealed to the environmental and administrative court instead of the administrative court.

It is also proposed that the deadline for non-governmental environmental organizations to appeal decisions regarding forestry or forestry activities should be calculated from the date the decision was issued, and that the Forest Agency should be granted the status of a party when environmental organizations appeal the agency's decisions.

Finally, the government assesses that further measures should be taken to increase forest growth and increase the availability of sustainably produced domestic biomass, and that a systematic follow-up should be carried out of the forestry policy production target.
