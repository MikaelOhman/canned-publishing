Revised regulations regarding permits and supervision for the Total Defence Research Institute.

TL;DRThe government wants to change the regulations governing how the Total Defense Research Institute (FFI) is controlled in terms of environment, health, and food safety. The goal is for it to follow the same rules as other parts of the defense sector.
In plain language

The government has proposed changes to the laws governing the Total Defense Research Institute (FFI). The proposal aims to bring the agency under the same regulations for permits and controls as other defense authorities, such as the Armed Forces. This applies to areas such as environmental protection, health, radiation, and food safety.

If FFI wants to start activities that could affect the environment, the county administrative board must review their application. If a court, known as the Environmental and Land Court, makes a decision regarding FFI, the government will have the opportunity to review the decision and make a determination. This means that there will be a clearer process for how decisions are made.

The proposal also means that environmental organizations will have less opportunity to appeal decisions related to FFI's activities in the areas of environment and health. At the same time, it will be easier for a regulatory authority to assign a special inspector to oversee safety related to radiation.

If the Parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new rules will come into effect on July 2, 2026. The proposal involves changes to several laws, including the Environmental Code and the Radiation Protection Act.

Full text

The proposal suggests that the Total Defence Research Institute should be subject to the same regulations as the Armed Forces, the Swedish Defence Property Agency, the Defence Materiel Administration, and the Defence Radio Agency, both in terms of permits and supervision within the environmental and occupational health area, and in terms of supervision in the areas of radiation protection and food safety. The proposals in the proposal aim to create uniformity in the regulation of permits for environmentally hazardous activities and supervision of defence activities. The proposals include the following:

  • Applications for permits for environmentally hazardous activities concerning the Total Defence Research Institute will be reviewed by the county administrative board.
  • The environmental and administrative court will forward decisions by government agencies concerning the Total Defence Research Institute to the government for review.
  • The special right of environmental organizations to appeal decisions concerning the Total Defence Research Institute in the area of environmental and occupational health will be limited.
  • The possibility for a supervisory authority to delegate supervisory tasks to the Defence Inspector for Health and Environment in the area of radiation protection is extended to include activities carried out by the Total Defence Research Institute.

The proposed amendments to the law are scheduled to come into force on July 2, 2026.

Proposed Parliamentary Decision

The government's proposal:

  1. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Environmental Code.
  2. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Act (2006:805) on feed and animal by-products.
  3. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Planning and Building Act (2010:900).
  4. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Radiation Protection Act (2018:396).
