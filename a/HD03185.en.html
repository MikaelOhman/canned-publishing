In plain language

The government has proposed a new law that would allow Swedish prison sentences to be served abroad for a certain period. The purpose of the proposal is to assist the Swedish Prison and Probation Service, the agency responsible for prisons, as there is currently a shortage of places in Sweden.

An agreement between Sweden and Estonia was signed on June 18, 2025, which means that Swedish prisoners can serve their sentences in Estonian prisons. The new law is intended to be broad enough to be used with other countries that Sweden chooses to cooperate with in the future.

The law will establish rules for how the Prison and Probation Service will operate in this context. This includes rules regarding which prisoners are eligible to be transferred abroad, regulations for visits and correspondence, and how to appeal decisions if they are deemed unfair. The proposal also includes regulations on how this will affect other laws, such as those related to taxation and employment.

For the agreement with Estonia to be valid, it must first be approved by the Swedish Parliament. This also means that certain tasks and decisions related to the prison sentences will be handled by the Estonian authorities. If the proposal is approved, the new law is planned to come into effect on July 3, 2026.

Full text

The proposal outlines a new law regarding the temporary execution of Swedish prison sentences abroad. This new law would allow Swedish prison sentences to be served for a certain period in a correctional facility in a foreign country. The purpose of these proposals is to help alleviate the serious lack of space within the Swedish Prison and Probation Service. The proposals have been developed in light of the agreement signed between Sweden and Estonia on June 18, 2025, regarding the execution of Swedish prison sentences in Estonia. However, the law is proposed to be generally designed to be applicable to all countries with which Sweden has entered into or may enter into similar agreements.

The new law includes provisions on various aspects, such as the Prison and Probation Service's procedures, the selection of inmates who may be eligible for serving their sentences abroad, permissions, mail, termination of the sentence, and the possibility of appealing decisions.

The proposal also includes suggestions and assessments on issues related to, among other things, immigration law, tort law, labor law, social law, and tax law.

The proposal also suggests that the Swedish Parliament should approve the agreement between Sweden and Estonia regarding the execution of Swedish prison sentences in Estonia, and delegate judicial and administrative tasks involving the exercise of authority to Estonia, in accordance with the agreement.

The proposed laws are scheduled to come into force on July 3, 2026.
