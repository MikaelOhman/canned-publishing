Trial period while receiving sick pay.

Source Riksdagen Read the original at Riksdagen →2025-11-11
TL;DRStarting in March 2026, it will be easier to test returning to work while still being sick. Individuals who receive full sick pay will be able to try working with their employer without losing their benefits from the Swedish Social Insurance Agency (Försäkringskassan).
In plain language

The government has proposed a change to the rules in the Social Security Code, which is the law governing how sick pay works. The goal is to help people who have been ill return to work by allowing them to test their ability to work for a shorter period.

The new system is called a "work trial" (or "trial period of employment"). It means that a person who is entitled to full sick pay can work for their employer for short periods. During these periods, the person continues to receive their sick pay as usual, so that they do not lose their income while testing the job.

A work trial can last for two different periods, totaling 365 days. Each period can be no longer than 14 days. There must be a minimum of 30 days between the two periods. For this to apply, both the employee and the employer must sign a registration form for the Swedish Social Insurance Agency (Försäkringskassan) at least 14 days before the trial period begins.

The proposal is intended to come into effect on March 1, 2026. When the Swedish Social Insurance Agency later assesses how much a person can work, they will also consider how it went during these work trials.

Full text

The proposal suggests changes to the Social Security Code, aiming to lower the barriers to returning to work by allowing individuals who are eligible for full sick pay to, in agreement with their employer, trial work with their employer while continuing to receive sick pay for a limited period.

The proposed legislative changes are scheduled to come into effect on March 1, 2026.
