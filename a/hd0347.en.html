In plain language

The Swedish National Audit Office is an agency that monitors how the state and its responsibilities are managed. They have investigated whether the system for teacher and preschool teacher certification is functioning effectively. The results show that the system has not achieved its goals, in part because there is a shortage of teachers, and also because the system is not effective enough at preventing unqualified individuals from working in schools.

To improve the situation, the National Audit Office proposes several measures to the government. They suggest, among other things, that regular checks should be conducted on the exceptions made to the requirement for certification. They also want the Swedish National Agency for Education, the agency responsible for schools, to have the ability to check criminal records when someone applies for certification.

The government agrees with most of the points of criticism and wants to develop the system. The goal is to create a safe and secure school environment where teachers can focus entirely on teaching students. With this decision, the work of reviewing the report in this matter is concluded.

In this document, the government presents its assessment of the observations, conclusions, and recommendations made by the Swedish National Audit Agency (Riksrevisionen) in its report "The System for Teacher Certification – Design, Governance, and Follow-up" (RiR 2025:12).

The Swedish National Audit Agency reviewed whether the certification system for teachers and preschool teachers is functioning effectively. The agency's overall conclusion is that the certification system has not had the impact that was expected, partly due to the teacher shortage and because the system does not sufficiently prevent unsuitable teachers from working in schools. The agency's overall assessment is that the teacher certification system is not functioning effectively.

The Swedish National Audit Agency's recommendations to the government include regularly evaluating which exemptions from the certification requirements remain justified, granting the Swedish National Agency for Education the ability to check criminal records when processing applications for teacher and preschool teacher certification, and expanding background checks for personnel in the school system.

The government largely agrees with the Swedish National Audit Agency's observations regarding how the system for teacher certification can be developed. A safe and secure school environment, and a school where teachers have the opportunity to focus on their teaching and students' learning, are high priorities for the government. With this document, the government considers the Swedish National Audit Agency's report to be finalized.
