The Swedish Migration Agency (Migrationsverket) is adopting a new logo featuring a heraldic coat of arms. The purpose is to more clearly represent the agency's role as Sweden's national migration authority and as part of the government. At the same time, this change marks a new phase in the agency's mission and its European cooperation within the framework of the EU's Migration and Asylum Pact. The introduction of the new logo will begin on September 1st and will be implemented gradually.

"I am proud and pleased to present our new heraldic coat of arms," said Director General Maria Mindhammar. "When people interact with the Swedish Migration Agency, it should be clear that they are dealing with Sweden's official migration authority. The heraldic coat of arms strengthens the connection to the state and contributes to increased clarity and legitimacy."

The decision to adopt the new logo, which also includes an English version, comes as the EU's Migration and Asylum Pact is being implemented, bringing a common framework and closer European cooperation.

"Significant changes are taking place in the field of migration, impacting our mission and the context in which we operate," said Maria Mindhammar. "It feels natural to mark this change and clarify our role as a government agency responsible for Sweden's migration and asylum issues."

While the symbol is being renewed, important elements of the Swedish Migration Agency's previous logo are being retained. The red color and the bridge symbol, which have long been part of the agency's visual identity, remain as an integral part of the coat of arms. The bridge symbol has been present since the State Immigration Board became the Swedish Migration Agency in 2000, and it represents movement in both directions, encompassing immigration and return migration, according to current legislation.

"It is about combining development and continuity," said Maria Mindhammar. "We are retaining elements that many associate with the Swedish Migration Agency while strengthening the connection to the state."

Heraldic coats of arms are subject to special protection regulations and are clearly linked to public activities.

"This also makes the emblem provide stronger protection against counterfeiting, fraud, and misuse of the agency's identity," says Maria Mindhammar.

The new logo will be introduced starting September 1, 2026. The implementation will be phased in across the Swedish Migration Agency's digital and physical channels throughout the fall, as existing materials are updated. The changes will include websites, e-services, signage, flags, and informational materials.

The graphic design work for the new logo was entirely completed internally by a small working group. The agency's contracted communications firm was engaged to conduct focus group interviews with an external target audience and to provide some consultation. The cost of this external support amounts to approximately 200,000 Swedish krona.

The complete emblem and a simplified version will be used when the format requires it. Finalization of the original design is ongoing until its implementation on September 1st.

All Swedish government agencies can use a royal crown and shield as their symbols. The Swedish Migration Agency's emblem was designed in consultation with Davor Zovko, the state herald at the National Archives. The basis for heraldic emblems is its description in text, known as the blazon: "In silver, two red bars shaped like bows, the upper one tapering towards the left (sinister), and the lower one tapering towards the right (dexter). The shield is crowned with a royal crown."
