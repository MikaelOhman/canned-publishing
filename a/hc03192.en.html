An improved model for calculating presumptive rent.

Source Riksdagen Read the original at Riksdagen →2025-06-24

The government proposes changes to the rules regarding rents for newly constructed apartments, known as "presumptive rents." The purpose is to create better conditions for building new rental apartments.

The possibilities to adjust presumptive rents on an ongoing basis should be expanded. As a general rule, these rents should be able to be changed by an amount that corresponds to the general rent development in the area, unless there are special reasons to change the rent by a different amount. This could lead to it being more profitable to invest in the construction of new rental apartments in more cases. The initial rents can also be set lower, which is positive for those who need an apartment.

New possibilities will also be introduced to resolve disputes regarding initial presumptive rents and disputes regarding ongoing adjustments to such rents. The collectively negotiating parties in the rental market should be able to agree that such disputes can be reviewed by a special arbitrator, appointed by the housing authority. This can contribute to more effective negotiations regarding presumptive rents and that disputes can be resolved in a simple and quick way if the parties cannot reach an agreement.

In order to make the presumptive rent model more flexible, it is also proposed that the rent should be able to be changed in more cases. The rent should be able to be changed due to individual requests from the tenant for additions or removals of features. In order to enable important modernization of a property, the rent should also be able to be changed by an amount that is reasonable, taking into account a significant interest.

The proposed changes to the law are expected to come into effect on January 1, 2026.
