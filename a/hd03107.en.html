Modernization of the Swedish Tax Agency's control tools.

Source Riksdagen Read the original at Riksdagen →2026-02-03
TL;DRThe government wants to change the laws so that the Swedish Tax Agency (Skatteverket) can use modern technology to audit companies. This means that they will be able to more easily access digital information, even if it is stored in cloud services or abroad.
In plain language

The Swedish Tax Agency (Skatteverket) wants to acquire better tools to conduct its audits. Currently, there are restrictions on using certain types of networks during investigations, but the government wants to remove these restrictions. This would allow the agency to use its own technical equipment to collect digital information more efficiently.

An important proposal concerns the ability to secure evidence remotely. This means that the Tax Agency should be able to search for and copy digital documents in systems used for communication or file storage. This is important because much information is now stored in so-called cloud services, where the information is not stored on a physical computer within the company, but on the internet.

The new rules also mean that the Tax Agency should be able to retrieve information even if it is stored in another country. The proposal also covers technical aids needed to carry out these digital audits. The purpose is to ensure that the laws are adapted to modern technology without compromising the security of those being audited.

If the Swedish Parliament approves the proposal, the new rules will come into effect on July 1, 2026. The proposal involves changes to three different laws related to tax audits, the control of certain goods, and support for structural changes in the labor market.

Full text

The proposal suggests removing the prohibitions on conducting audits via telecommunications networks that are currently in place in the Swedish Tax Procedure Act (2011:1244) and the Act (1998:506) on the movement and control of certain goods subject to excise tax. These prohibitions currently apply during audits, evidence gathering, and excise tax inspections. The proposal would give the Swedish Tax Agency greater opportunities to use its own technical equipment to obtain electronic information needed for an inspection. The purpose is to enable new and more efficient working methods and to facilitate cooperation with companies being inspected.

Furthermore, the proposal includes new provisions in the Tax Procedure Act regarding remote evidence gathering, which would allow for the search and copying of documents in readable information systems (e.g., user accounts on communication services and storage services). It also proposes the possibility of searching for and using technical and other aids that can be used to implement a decision on remote evidence gathering. Remote evidence gathering may be carried out even if the information being sought is stored abroad. As a result of this proposal, an amendment is also proposed to the Act (2020:548) on restructuring support, regarding the possibility of searching for and using technical and other aids. The proposals aim to adapt the rules on evidence gathering to the technological developments that have occurred, which means that electronic information is now often stored externally, for example, in cloud services, without compromising legal certainty.

The new provisions are proposed to come into force on July 1, 2026.
