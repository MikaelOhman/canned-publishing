Full text

In this document, the government presents its assessment of the observations and recommendations made by the Swedish National Audit Office (Riksrevisionen) in its report "Government Implementation of the Fiscal Framework 2024" (RiR 2024:25). The report assesses that the proposed fiscal policy is presented in accordance with the fiscal framework, but not fully designed in accordance with it. The agency believes, among other things, that the plan for returning to the surplus target, as presented by the government, is too brief and recommends that it be clarified. The Swedish National Audit Office also believes that the government should improve the comparison of the forecast for structural savings and supplement the monitoring of the balance sheet requirements of the municipal sector with separate reporting for municipalities and regions. Furthermore, according to the agency, the government needs to develop the presentation regarding the proposed level of the expenditure ceiling to clarify the government's view on what constitutes a reasonable level of taxation in the long term. The agency also recommends that the government, in bills proposing changes to the state budget, clarify the reasons for such proposals and provide a clearer explanation of the funding for the proposals.

The government agrees with several of the Swedish National Audit Office's assessments and is continuously working to develop the content of the budgetary proposals in the direction recommended by the agency. However, the government does not believe that it is justified to provide a more in-depth, forward-looking account of the economic differences between municipalities and regions, or that there is a need to develop the justifications for the proposals for changes to the state budget. With this document, the government considers the audit report to be finalized.
