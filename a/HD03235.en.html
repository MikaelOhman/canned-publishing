Full text

The proposal includes suggestions that would allow for more decisions to deport foreign nationals who commit crimes. The proposals include:

  • Crimes will, as a general rule, be grounds for deportation when the penalty imposed is stricter than a fine.
  • The weighing of reasons for and against deportation will, in more cases, result in deportation.
  • The strict requirements for deporting foreign nationals who came to Sweden before the age of 15 will be removed.
  • Enforcement obstacles will no longer be considered by the district court when deciding whether or not to order deportation.
  • Re-entry bans will become longer and, in more cases, without a time limit.
  • Prosecutors will have a duty to request deportation when a foreign national is charged with crimes that are grounds for deportation.

The proposal also includes, among other things, stricter penalties for certain offenses under the Aliens Act.

The proposed amendments are to come into force on September 1, 2026.
