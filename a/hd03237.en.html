A key part of the proposal is that individuals studying to become police officers should not have to repay their student loans during the period they are eligible to have the debt waived. The government also wants to make it tax-free to have the debt written off, so that individuals do not have to pay extra tax for this benefit.

To protect those working in law enforcement, the government also wants to introduce stricter rules regarding confidentiality. This means that information that could be used to locate or identify police students and employees should be better protected.

If the parliament votes in favor of the proposal and changes the laws regarding taxes, student loans, and confidentiality, the new rules are planned to come into effect on January 1, 2027.

Full text

In order to increase the number of police officers and the density of police presence, police education should be funded. As part of this reform, the government proposes the following legislative changes:

  • A borrower should not be required to make repayments on student loans for police education during the period they are eligible for loan forgiveness.
  • An extended protection of confidentiality should be introduced for information that could be used to identify police students and police officers.
  • The benefit of having student loans forgiven should be tax-free.

These legislative changes are proposed to come into effect on January 1, 2027.
