In this document, the government presents its assessment of the observations and recommendations made by the Swedish National Audit Office (Riksrevisionen) in its review report "Admission to Police Education" (RiR 2025:13). The National Audit Office reviewed whether the admission process to police education is efficient. The review covers the period 2017–2024. The National Audit Office's overall conclusion is that the admission process to police education is not efficient. It is not ensured that the admission process is adapted to select suitable candidates for the police profession, and the admission process does not promote efficient use of resources and could be more efficient.

Based on its observations, the National Audit Office makes two recommendations to the government and a number of recommendations to the Swedish Police Authority and the Swedish Authority for Pre-trial Detention and Probation. One recommendation to the government concerns conducting a review of the current growth target, which aims for the Swedish Police Authority to achieve a police density that is at least equivalent to the average in the EU.

The government partially agrees with the assessment that there are challenges with the current growth target, but believes that a benchmark is necessary to ensure more police officers and increased police presence throughout the country in order to curb crime. The government therefore believes that the current growth target should remain in place.

The second recommendation concerns the government conducting a review of the funding of the Swedish Authority for Pre-trial Detention and Probation's testing capacity for police examinations, and that the funding should be based on the actual number of examinations conducted. The government believes that it is important to have appropriate and cost-effective funding.
