Improved Design of the EU Electricity Market

TL;DRThe government wants to change the rules of the electricity market to make it safer and easier for consumers. The proposal includes measures to make it easier to understand electricity contracts and to allow large electricity companies to offer fixed prices.
In plain language

The government has proposed new regulations for the electricity market, which are scheduled to come into effect on January 1, 2026. The purpose is to comply with new rules from the EU and to improve the electricity market for all consumers.

For those who have an electricity contract, the proposal means that you will receive a simple summary of the most important rules in your contract before you sign or renew it. Large companies that sell electricity to more than 200,000 customers should also be able to offer contracts with a fixed price for a specified period.

There will also be stricter requirements for the companies that supply electricity and those that own the electricity grids. Electricity companies must have plans to ensure that electricity is actually delivered, and they may not terminate a contract if there is an ongoing legal dispute about the matter. Grid companies, which are responsible for the power lines, must also become better at sharing information and be fair to all suppliers.

The government also wants to amend the law on electricity certificates. Electricity certificates are a system used to promote renewable energy. The proposal involves fewer companies needing to participate in this system, and that the authority that manages the accounts should be able to charge certain fees.

Full text

The government's proposal suggests changes to the Electricity Act (1997:857), including implementing amendments to the EU's Electricity Market Directive.

The proposals primarily entail the following:

  • An electricity supplier with supply agreements for more than 200,000 electricity consumers should be able to offer consumers supply agreements with a fixed term and price.
  • Consumers should receive a summary of the most important contractual terms before an agreement for the supply of electricity is entered into or extended.
  • An electricity supplier should have strategies to limit the risk of supply interruptions.
  • A network company should select a designated electricity supplier in a fair, transparent, and non-discriminatory manner.
  • An electricity supplier should not be able to terminate a supply agreement or request that the supply of electricity to a consumer be interrupted if the circumstance on which the supplier wishes to base the action is subject to dispute resolution outside of court.
  • The obligation for distribution network companies to provide information to facilitate connections is expanded.
  • The obligation for electricity suppliers to accept electricity is expanded.
  • Requirements are introduced for network companies to make information available regarding the electricity being supplied.

Changes are also proposed to the Act (2011:200) on electricity certificates, aiming to reduce the number of obligated entities in the electricity certificate system and to enable the account management authority to collect certain types of fees.

The proposed legislative changes are intended to take effect on January 1, 2026.

  2. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Act (2011:1200) on electricity certificates.
