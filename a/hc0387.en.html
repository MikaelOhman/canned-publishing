In this document, the government presents its assessment regarding:

The Swedish National Audit Agency's observations and recommendations in the report "The State's Efforts to Reduce Shipping Emissions of Harmful Substances into the Sea" (RiR 2024:18). The Swedish National Audit Agency reviewed whether the state's efforts to reduce shipping emissions are effective.

The Swedish National Audit Agency states that the marine environment in Swedish waters is heavily impacted, and that good environmental quality is not being achieved. Furthermore, negative consequences for both the environment and society are expected if comprehensive measures are not taken. The Swedish National Audit Agency also emphasizes that shipping contributes to several environmental burdens in the sea. The review focuses on emissions of scrubber water, emissions of cargo residues, and the reception of shipping waste in ports.

The Swedish National Audit Agency's main recommendations to the government are to:

  • As soon as possible, introduce a ban on the discharge of scrubber water in all of Sweden's territorial waters.
  • Review Swedish regulations to ensure they are in compliance with international convention requirements for monitoring and approval of tank cleaning of cargo tanks, and for "proceeding en route" as a basis for allowing the discharge of cargo residues.

The government largely agrees with the Swedish National Audit Agency's recommendations and has taken or is preparing to take measures to address the issues raised by the recommendations. The government also provides comments on the Swedish National Audit Agency's observations and recommendations that relate to government agencies.
