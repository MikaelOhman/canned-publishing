Hilma af Klint (pronounced [ˈɑːv ˈklɪnːt] in standard Swedish), born October 26, 1862, at Karlberg Castle in the parish of Solna, Stockholm County, and died October 21, 1944, in Danderyd, Stockholm County, was a Swedish painter, theosophist, and pioneer of abstract art.

Af Klint's esoteric paintings can be described as "visual representations of complex philosophical ideas and spiritual concepts." Af Klint is one of those artists who were largely unrecognized during her lifetime but received significant attention and recognition long after her death. The series of paintings titled "Paintings for the Temple," created by Hilma af Klint between 1906 and 1915, has been part of Sweden's cultural canon since 2025.

Af Klint's life has been portrayed in the documentary film "Beyond the Visible" (2019) and the drama film "Hilma" (2022). The latter was directed by Lasse Hallström, and in the film, af Klint is played by Tora Hallström and Lena Olin.

== Background ==

=== Family and Childhood === Hilma af Klint was born on October 26, 1862, at Karlberg Castle. She was the daughter of Commander Fredrik Victor af Klint and Andrietta Mathilda, née Sontag, and the great-granddaughter of Vice Admiral and cartographer Gustaf af Klint. She grew up in a family of naval officers and cartographers and belonged to the af Klint family. She spent her summers at the estate of Hanmora on Adelsö in Lake Mälaren during her childhood. She showed artistic talent from an early age.

=== Education === In 1880, she began studying at the Technical School (now known as Konstfack), where she received training in portrait painting under Kerstin Cardon. From 1882 to 1887, she studied at the Royal Academy of Fine Arts in Stockholm. In the late 19th century, it was uncommon for women in Sweden to pursue higher education. The Royal Academy of Fine Arts had admitted its first female students in 1849, and in 1864, a special "Women's Department" was established for 18 female students.

Hilma af Klint completed her studies with excellent grades and, as a scholarship, she was granted access to a studio in the so-called "Studio Building," which the Royal Academy of Fine Arts owned at Hamngatan 5, at the intersection of Hamngatan and Kungsträdgården.

== Spiritualism ==

In 1879, at the age of 17, Hilma af Klint began participating in spiritualist séances. Three years later (1882), she withdrew from the movement because she considered it not entirely serious. [Source needed]

Hilma af Klint's spiritual quest led her to explore various philosophical directions. She joined the Theosophical Society in 1905, and according to her family, she was initially intended to become its chairwoman. Hilma af Klint participated in the Theosophers' world congress in Stockholm in 1913. From 1896 to 1897, she was a member of the Edelweiss Association.

Together with four other members, she left the Edelweiss Association and formed the group "The Five." The members were Anna Cassel, Cornelia Cederberg, Mathilda Nilsson, and Sigrid Hedman. During the period from 1896 to 1907, they met for spiritual gatherings, about which they kept notes in a total of eight books. They read and studied the New Testament, followed by séances where Sigrid Hedman channeled spirits, and the group members performed various exercises, such as automatic drawing. Her spirituality included eating vegetarian food, studying Theosophy, and meditating. This ten-year period was for Hilma af Klint a preparatory phase for her later spiritually inspired painting, resulting in her creating her first series of 26 abstract paintings in 1906. In 1907, the group "The Five" dissolved, but Hilma af Klint gathered other women around her who helped her with the creation of the abstract paintings. Among these, we know today Anna Cassel, Gusten Andersson, Miss Henström, Helene Westerberg, Inga Jehander, Alma Arnell, Lotten Rönquist, Sigrid Lancén, and Thomasine Andersson. The latter was a nurse for Hilma af Klint's sick mother, and the two women became very close friends for the rest of their lives. From 1906 onwards, Hilma af Klint dedicated herself to esoteric painting until her death in 1944.

=== Steiner and Theosophy === In 1917, Hilma af Klint began using her new studio on the island of Munsö. It was built next to her summer house, "Furuheim," located on the Bona fideikommiss property. In 1918, Hilma af Klint moved to Furuheim with her mother. After her mother's death in 1920, Hilma af Klint moved to Helsingborg for the winter months. She shared an apartment there with her mother's nurse, Thomasine Andersson. Hilma af Klint and Thomasine Andersson became inseparable friends for life. They both spent time annually in Dornach, Switzerland, between 1920 and 1930, where Hilma af Klint unsuccessfully searched for answers about the meaning of her works, "Paintings for the Temple."
