TL;DRA correction has been made to the Estonian text regarding EU regulations concerning plastics used for food. This correction does not change the regulations for other languages or countries.
In plain language

The European Commission, which is the government of the EU, has identified an error in the Estonian translation of a key regulation. The regulation concerns plastic materials and products used for storing or handling food.

The error is in a section that describes how clean the material must be. Because the text in Estonian was incorrect, the meaning of what is actually required was altered. Now, a correction has been made so that the Estonian text aligns with the other language versions.

This change only affects the regulation in the Estonian language. All other language versions of the regulation are already correct and do not need to be changed.

The new correction will take effect twenty days after it is published in the EU's official journal. The regulation applies immediately and uniformly in all countries that are members of the EU.

Full text

THE EUROPEAN COMMISSION HAS ADOPTED THIS REGULATION:

Having regard to the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union,

Having regard to Regulation (EC) No 1935/2004 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 27 October 2004 on materials and articles intended to come into contact with food and repealing Directives 80/590/EEC and 89/109/EEC (1), in particular Article 5.1 c, and

WHEREAS the Estonian language version of Commission Regulation (EU) No 10/2011 (2) contains an error in Article 8.1, first paragraph, regarding the purity level, which alters the meaning of the provision;

WHEREAS the Estonian language version of Regulation (EU) No 10/2011 should therefore be corrected accordingly; other language versions are not affected;

WHEREAS the measures laid down in this Regulation are consistent with the opinion of the Standing Committee on Plants, Animals, Food and Feed, which was delivered before the adoption of Commission Regulation (EU) 2025/351 (3);

THIS REGULATION SHALL ENTER INTO FORCE ON THE TWENTIETH DAY AFTER ITS PUBLICATION IN THE OFFICIAL JOURNAL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION.

THIS REGULATION IS BINDING IN ITS ENTIRETY AND DIRECTLY APPLICABLE IN ALL MEMBER STATES.

(1) OJ L 338, 13.11.2004, p. 4, ELI: http://data.europa.eu/eli/reg/2004/1935/oj.

(2) Commission Regulation (EU) No 10/2011 of 14 January 2011 on plastic materials and articles intended to come into contact with foodstuffs (OJ L 12, 15.1.2011, p. 1, ELI: http://data.europa.eu/eli/reg/2011/10/oj).

(3) Commission Regulation (EU) 2025/351 of February 21, 2025, amending Regulation (EU) No 10/2011 on plastic materials and articles intended to come into contact with foodstuffs, and amending Regulation (EU) 2022/1616 on recycled plastic materials and plastic articles intended to come into contact with foodstuffs, and repealing Regulation (EC) No 282/2008, and amending Regulation (EC) No 2023/2006 on good manufacturing practice with regard to materials and articles intended to come into contact with foodstuffs, with regard to recycled plastic and other issues relating to quality control and the manufacture of plastic materials and articles intended to come into contact with foodstuffs (OJ L, 2025/351, 24.2.2025, ELI: http://data.europa.eu/eli/reg/2025/351/oj).
