TL;DRThe government intends to grant the Coast Guard more authority to prevent and stop crimes at sea, and to modify the regulations regarding the handling of classified information. The proposed changes are scheduled to take effect on March 1, 2026.
In plain language

The government has proposed a bill to the Parliament to enable the Coast Guard to work more effectively in detecting and preventing crimes at sea. This would give the agency a broader mandate to independently search for and prevent illegal activities related to its work on the water.

To achieve this more effectively, the government proposes new rules for how information is handled. This involves making certain data in the Coast Guard's database, called "Sjöbasis," confidential. This is to protect both the public and individuals.

There will also be stricter rules on how the Coast Guard can use personal data when conducting analyses of information. The purpose is to protect people's privacy and important societal interests.

If the Parliament approves the proposal, the new laws will come into effect on March 1, 2026. The proposal involves changes to several laws, including the Coast Guard Act and the Act on Official Secrets.

Full text

The proposal outlines measures to enable the Coast Guard to develop its capabilities and working methods. Therefore, the government proposes:

  • Expanding the Coast Guard's mandate to independently prevent, deter, and detect criminal activity to include all crimes committed in the maritime environment that are related to the agency's mission.
  • Extending the confidentiality of information in the Coast Guard's registry, Sjöbasis, to protect the public interest and individual privacy.
  • Establishing confidentiality for information used in the Coast Guard's analytical activities, to protect the public interest and individual privacy.
  • Implementing regulations regarding the processing of personal data by the Coast Guard in its analytical activities.

The proposed amendments are scheduled to come into effect on March 1, 2026.

Proposed Parliamentary Decision

Government's proposal:

  1. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Act on Public Access and Official Secrets (2009:400).
  2. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Coast Guard Act (2019:32).
  3. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Coast Guard Data Act (2019:429).
