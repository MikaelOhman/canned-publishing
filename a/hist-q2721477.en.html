Full text

Katarina Sunesdotter, also known as Katarina of Ymseborg [source needed], was born around 1215 and last mentioned in historical records on January 17, 1251. She died before February 1253. She was the Queen of Sweden, married to King Erik Eriksson (later nicknamed "the lame" and "the hunchbacked").

Katarina was the daughter of Sune Folkesson and Helena Sverkersdotter (daughter of King Sverker the Younger). In 1244, she married King Erik Eriksson, who was known as "the lame" and "the hunchbacked" (died February 2, 1250), but they had no children. On June 11, 1250, she made a significant donation to Gudhems Monastery, where she intended to retire. The same year, she bequeathed Söderköping to her sister Benedicta.

A sculpture depicting Katarina Sunesdotter is located at Hagatorget in Söderköping. The sculpture was created by S Dahlström in 1965. A wooden sculpture of her husband can be found in Hjo.
