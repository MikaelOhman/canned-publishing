Eligibility for social security.

Source Riksdagen Read the original at Riksdagen →2026-02-23

A reform of social benefits is needed to break the cycle of dependency on benefits and reduce social exclusion. A well-functioning, comprehensive welfare system requires strong incentives for individuals settling in Sweden to quickly find employment and achieve financial independence. This helps to prevent social exclusion. Furthermore, there are reasons to limit access to certain benefits tied to residency to prevent them from acting as "pull factors" for migrants.

Therefore, the proposal suggests that a requirement for legal residency be established to be considered a resident in Sweden under the Social Security Code, and thus eligible for social security benefits tied to residency. Furthermore, a requirement for qualification to certain residency-based benefits is proposed, which can be met by residing in Sweden for a period of five years within a fifteen-year timeframe, or by residing in Sweden in combination with earned income that reaches a certain level over a specific period. An exception to the qualification requirement is also proposed for cases where there are compelling reasons.

The proposed changes to the law are scheduled to take effect on January 1, 2027.
