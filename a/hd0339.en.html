The Council on Legislation is an agency that ensures new laws function effectively. Currently, the Council on Legislation is limited to a maximum of five departments, but the government wants to amend the law to allow for six departments for a limited time.

The reason for this proposal is that it is believed there will be a very large amount of work to be done in the first part of 2026. By having an additional department, the agency hopes to be able to handle all tasks without problems.

If the Parliament approves the proposal, the new rule will come into effect on January 20, 2026. The additional department will then be removed again when June 2026 is over.

Full text

The proposal suggests a change to the law (2003:333) concerning the Legal Council, which would allow the Legal Council to consist of a maximum of six divisions instead of a maximum of five. This change aims to create better conditions for the Legal Council to fulfill its review function during the periods of high workload that are likely to occur during the first half of 2026.

The change is proposed to take effect on January 20, 2026, and to expire at the end of June 2026.
