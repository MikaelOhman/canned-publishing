Full text

In this document, the government presents its assessments of the observations made by the Swedish National Audit Office (Riksrevisionen) in the report "Achievement of the Housing Allowance's Objectives – Overcrowding and Absolute Poverty" (RiR 2024:15). The National Audit Office has reviewed whether the housing allowance is effectively designed in relation to its housing and family policy objectives.

The National Audit Office recommends that the government review the design of the housing allowance to enable better achievement of its objectives, and to regularly monitor the achievement of these objectives in terms of both family and housing policy, and to report any changes to the Parliament. The National Audit Office also recommends that the government review whether the housing allowance is an appropriate benefit for supporting economically vulnerable households during difficult economic times. This refers to the temporary supplementary allowance for families with children that has been in place in recent years.

The government welcomes the National Audit Office's review and notes that it provides a basis for future development of the housing allowance. The government largely agrees with the National Audit Office's assessments of the government's follow-up and reporting on the achievement of the housing allowance's family and housing policy objectives.

In the budget proposal for 2025, the government has proposed a gradual reduction of the temporary supplementary allowance for families with children within the housing allowance. The government believes that the National Audit Office's recommendations do not warrant any further action at this time.

With this document, the government considers the National Audit Office's report to be finalized.
