Distribution of surplus in the income pension system.

TL;DRThe government proposes new rules for how surplus funds in the pension system should be distributed to citizens. If there is a surplus in the system, those who contribute to the pension should receive a share of the funds.
In plain language

The government has proposed a solution for managing surplus funds within the income pension system. If the system has more money than needed, as measured by a specific figure called the balance ratio, those who are entitled to a pension will have the right to receive a portion of these funds.

The proposal also includes the elimination of the state's old debt to the pension system. This means that the outstanding debt, which arose from previous changes to the pension system, will now be considered fully repaid. This is part of an agreement reached by the Pension Group, a body comprised of all eight parties in parliament, to ensure that pension regulations are followed.

If the parliament approves the proposal, the new rules will come into effect on August 1, 2024. These new rules will then be used to calculate pensions starting in the year 2027.

Full text

The proposal outlines a framework for determining when a distributable surplus exists within the distribution system and how such a surplus should be handled. A distributable surplus will be deemed to exist if the balance figure for a year exceeds 1.1500. If the balance figure exceeds this level, those insured within the system will receive a dividend according to established rules. Furthermore, the proposal includes a provision to write off the remaining debt that the income pension system has to the state, as a result of the pension reform, and thereby finalize this matter. The proposals are designed in accordance with an agreement reached within the Pension Group in August 2025. The Pension Group consists of all eight parties in the Swedish Parliament and is responsible for safeguarding the pension agreement.

The proposed amendments to the law are scheduled to come into effect on August 1, 2026, and will be applied for the first time for the year 2027.
