June 22, 2026

The Swedish Migration Agency will temporarily be unable to provide complete statistics regarding international protection and asylum. These limitations apply from June 12th and are due to the implementation of the EU's new common migration rules.

On June 12th, the EU's common migration rules, known as the migration and asylum pact, came into effect. These new regulations involve significant changes in how migration is managed to and from the EU, including changes to the process for international protection (asylum).

These changes affect how the Migration Agency registers, defines, and tracks data related to international protection. To ensure the accuracy of the statistics, the agency's systems need to be adapted, and the technical infrastructure for tracking needs to be in place.

"The system adaptations are not yet complete, so there is a risk that statistics produced after June 12th may be inaccurate or misleading. Therefore, we will not release preliminary or manually compiled statistical summaries for the affected areas," says Eric Ramstedt, Planning Director at the Swedish Migration Agency.

These limitations affect statistics on applications for international protection (asylum), obstacles to enforcement, screening processes, and returns. Statistics related to other areas may also be affected if the data needs to be linked to the process for international protection. The limitation also affects statistics on asylum applications received before June 12th that have not been decided by that date.

The Swedish Migration Agency will provide this statistical information retrospectively once the necessary IT infrastructure for tracking is in place. This work is being carried out gradually throughout the fall of 2026.

"We cannot currently say definitively when it will be possible to produce the statistics, but we are working to make it available as soon as possible," says Eric Ramstedt.

Regarding the statistics on the Swedish Migration Agency's website, the change will affect publications starting July 15th.

While the statistics for international protection are being developed, the Migration Agency will publish data on the number of registered applications for international protection. This data is extracted from the agency's case management system and will be published starting July 15th.

"This information should not be considered official statistics and therefore should be interpreted with caution as it may contain inaccuracies. Furthermore, this data is not comparable to previously reported statistics on asylum applications," says Eric Ramstedt.
