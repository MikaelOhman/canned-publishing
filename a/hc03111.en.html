Suspension or reduction of social security benefits for individuals who evade or obstruct the execution of a detention order or a sentence of imprisonment.

TL;DRThe government aims to reduce or eliminate financial support for individuals who evade arrest to avoid imprisonment or pre-trial detention. The proposed measure would prevent individuals from receiving social security benefits simply because the police have not yet located them.
In plain language

The government has proposed changes to the Social Security Code, the law governing various types of benefits in Sweden. The aim is to ensure that individuals who evade arrest or fail to appear to serve a prison sentence do not continue to receive social security benefits.

Currently, individuals serving time in prison or a youth detention facility do not receive reduced benefits. The new proposal would extend this principle to individuals who have not yet been apprehended. If authorities are unable to locate a person to serve a sentence, this will be treated as if the person is deprived of their liberty when calculating the amount of benefits they are entitled to.

This applies to various types of benefits, such as activity allowances, disability benefits, and pensions. The idea is to ensure fairness and prevent individuals from using the social security system to receive money while evading justice.

If the proposal is approved, the new rules will come into effect on July 1, 2025. This means that the new regulations regarding benefit amounts for those in custody and in prison will take effect during the summer of next year.

Full text

In the proposed legislation, the government suggests that individuals who have been arrested in their absence or who have failed to appear to serve a sentence in a correctional facility or a secure youth detention center should be subject to the provisions of the Social Security Code regarding the suspension or reduction of benefits for those who are deprived of their liberty. The purpose is to prevent an insured person from receiving benefits from the social security system solely because law enforcement authorities have been unable to locate them to carry out the arrest or serve the sentence in the form of imprisonment or secure youth care. The proposals aim to achieve a more equitable access to benefits from the social security system between, on the one hand, those who are arrested and convicted and who are serving a sentence, and, on the other hand, those who are arrested and convicted but for whom serving the sentence has not been possible. Furthermore, the proposals aim to protect the legitimacy of the social security system.

The proposed amendments to the law are scheduled to come into effect on July 1, 2025.
