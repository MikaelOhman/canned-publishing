In plain language

The government has proposed measures to increase safety in schools and preschools. The goal is to protect children and students from violence, abuse, and negative influences. To achieve this, the government wants to change the rules regarding background checks for individuals starting work in the education sector.

Currently, only criminal record checks are performed, which are registers of convicted individuals. The government now proposes to also review suspicion registers, which contain information about individuals suspected of crimes. They also want the checks to apply to those working in municipal adult education programs.

There will also be requirements for more frequent checks. Previously, a record extract could be up to a year old, but now the proposal is that it should not be older than six months. Even for individuals already employed by the same employer, a new extract must be presented every six months instead of every year.

If the parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new rules will come into effect on July 15, 2026. The government also believes that the Swedish Schools Inspectorate and municipalities should be given the opportunity to review the suspicion register when they audit the ownership and management of schools.

Full text

Preschools and schools should be safe places. To increase the protection of children and students against violence and other abuse, as well as against inappropriate influence, the government is proposing changes that will expand the background checks required when someone is hired or otherwise employed within the school system and other educational activities. The proposals include the following:

  • The background checks will also include certain information from the suspicion register, not just the criminal record register.
  • The background check certificate must not be older than six months when presented, compared to the current one year.
  • The time limit for when someone can be employed or otherwise engaged by the same employer without having to present a new background check certificate will be reduced from one year to six months.
  • Background checks will also be required for those who are to be employed or otherwise engaged within municipal adult education.

In the proposal, the government also presents its assessment that more crimes should be covered by the background checks. The government also presents its assessment that the Swedish Schools Inspectorate and municipalities should be given the opportunity to check the suspicion register during ownership and management reviews.

The proposed changes to the law will come into force on July 15, 2026.
