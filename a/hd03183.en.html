The government has proposed a plan to reduce the tax on alcohol for small producers. This means that those who produce smaller quantities of beer, wine, and other beverages will not have to pay as much tax as the large corporations. The proposal aims to support independent microbreweries and other small-scale manufacturers.

The amount of the tax reduction depends on the amount of beverage the company produces each year. The smallest producers will receive the largest reduction, with the tax decreasing by 50 percent. The more beverages a company produces, the smaller the tax reduction will be. Full text

On July 1, 2025, Swedish regulations were introduced regarding a reduced tax rate for beer from independent microbreweries. The proposal suggests a reduced alcohol tax for other alcoholic beverages from independent small producers, under certain conditions.

It is proposed that the reduction should apply to alcoholic beverages produced by producers whose annual production does not exceed 100,000 liters of wine, 1.5 million liters of other fermented beverages, 25,000 liters of intermediate products, or, in the case of ethyl alcohol, 1,000 liters of pure alcohol. The producers who are eligible for the reduced tax rate are divided into five different categories based on annual production. Alcoholic beverages from producers in the category with the lowest production receive a tax reduction of 50 percent. The reduction then decreases gradually by 10 percentage points per category.

The changes are proposed to come into effect on July 1, 2026.
