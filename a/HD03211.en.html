Full text

The government's proposal suggests amendments to the Hunting Act (1987:259) aimed at simplifying hunting opportunities, including improving the implementation of EU regulations in the area of hunting.

These changes would authorize the government to issue regulations allowing the capture of wildlife on another person's land for marking, investigation, or other measures to protect and conserve wildlife. A new provision would also be introduced, enabling the holder of hunting rights to search for, track, or pursue wildlife for the purpose of humane killing.

To improve the conditions for professional fishing and to facilitate hunting on public waters, the government proposes that hunting be permitted on such waters after notification. The government also proposes allowing the hunting of seals in Sweden's economic zone, which would also be subject to notification.

Furthermore, the government proposes introducing a new confidentiality provision in the Public Access and Secrecy Act (2009:400) to protect information that could reveal the identity of an individual in cases involving the hunting of large carnivores and compensation for damage caused by large carnivores. This confidentiality would apply unless it is clear that the information can be disclosed without the individual or a close relative being exposed to threats or violence, or suffering other serious harm.

The proposed amendments are scheduled to come into effect on July 1, 2026.

Proposed Parliamentary Decision

The government's proposal:

  1. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Hunting Act (1987:259).
  2. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Act (1992:1140) on Sweden's Economic Zone.
  3. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Public Access and Secrecy Act (2009:400).
