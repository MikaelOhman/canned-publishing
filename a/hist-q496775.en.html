Full text

Anders (originally Andreas) Dahl, born March 17, 1751, in Varnhem, Skaraborg County, and died May 25, 1789, was a Swedish botanist. He was a half-brother to Olof Kolmodin Jr., and a full brother to Christopher Dahl and Erik Dahl. At the age of 4, he moved to Saleby.

He enrolled at Uppsala University on April 3, 1770, where he became a student of Carl von Linné. He also worked to preserve Linné's extensive herbarium. During his studies, Dahl was employed for several years by Clas Alströmer, overseeing his garden and collections. In his youth, he was a member of the Swedish Topographic Society, founded in Skara in 1769, which conducted natural history studies.

In 1786, Dahl obtained a medical doctorate from Kiel. He became a botanical demonstrator and a medical lecturer in Åbo in 1787. He published "Observationes botanicæ cirka Systema vegetabilium divi a Linné editum" (1787). After Linné's death, Dahl attempted to secure his collections for Sweden and offered to purchase them for the same amount as J. E. Smith, but was unsuccessful. The Spanish botanist Antonio José Cavanilles named the *Dahlia* genus after Anders Dahl. The author's name "Dahl" can be used to refer to Anders Dahl in connection with a scientific name in botany; see Wikipedia articles that link to the author's name.
