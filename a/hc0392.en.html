In plain language

The government has proposed new laws that are scheduled to come into effect on April 1, 2025. The aim is to ensure that more individuals who have been ordered to leave Sweden, a process known as "removal," actually return to their home countries.

A key element of the proposal is that a decision to remove someone from the country will remain in effect for five years after the individual has returned home. If the person has been issued a re-entry ban, meaning they are prohibited from returning to Sweden, this period could be extended. It will also become possible to impose longer re-entry bans if the person has not voluntarily left the country within the specified timeframe.

The government also wants to change the rules regarding what is known as "switching tracks." Currently, individuals whose asylum applications have been rejected may sometimes apply to stay in the country to work. The new rules will eliminate this system, meaning that it will no longer be possible to switch from asylum status to a work permit while already residing in Sweden.

The proposal also includes clearer rules regarding when a re-entry ban takes effect. This will be calculated from the day the person leaves EU countries or leaves Sweden, if the person is being sent to another EU country.

Full text

In the proposal, the government suggests amendments to the law, aiming, among other things, to increase the number of individuals with deportation orders who return to their home countries. The proposed changes include:

  • A deportation order will be valid for five years from the date the foreign national leaves the country, or for a longer period if a re-entry ban is in place.
  • The possibility of imposing longer re-entry bans than currently allowed if a foreign national has not left the country by the deadline for voluntary departure.
  • A provision clarifying that a re-entry ban will begin on the day the foreign national leaves the territory of the member states, or, if the individual is being deported to a member state, on the day the foreign national leaves Sweden.
  • The abolition of the so-called "change of status" system, which allows individuals to apply for a residence permit for work from within the country after being denied asylum.

The proposed amendments are scheduled to come into effect on April 1, 2025.
