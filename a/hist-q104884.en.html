Caspar David Friedrich, born September 5, 1774, in Greifswald, Swedish Pomerania, and died May 7, 1840, in Dresden, was a German artist. He was one of the most famous landscape painters of the Romantic era.

== Biography == Caspar David Friedrich was born in Greifswald, which at that time belonged to Sweden. He had nine siblings. In 1790, he began his education with Johann Gottfried Quistorp, who was a builder and art teacher at the University of Greifswald. From 1794 to 1798, Friedrich studied at the Art Academy in Copenhagen, where his teachers were Jens Juel, Nicolai Abildgaard, and Christian August Lorentzen. After completing his studies, he moved to Dresden, which was a center for early German Romanticism. During the summers of 1801 and 1802, Friedrich visited his hometown and the island of Rügen. There, he created several sketches that later served as inspiration for larger paintings. His most famous paintings are coastal landscapes, often featuring sailboats, and dramatic mountain landscapes. He was a deeply religious and introspective person, which is reflected in his work.

Friedrich belonged to a new generation of painters who were not employed by individuals with high social status. Instead, they exhibited their works in galleries and sold their paintings on the open market.

Friedrich's artistic development was also influenced by his friends. These included the painters Philipp Otto Runge, Georg Friedrich Kersting, and Louise Seidler, Johan Christian Dahl, the sculptor Christian Gottlieb Kühn, the poets Ludwig Tieck and Heinrich von Kleist, and the patriot Ernst Moritz Arndt. In Sweden, Friedrich is known as an inspiration for the Meditation Grove at the Skogskyrkogården cemetery, where the architects Gunnar Asplund and Sigurd Lewerentz are believed to have used Friedrich's work "Hill and plowed field near Dresden" (1830) as a direct model for its design. In 1818, Caspar Friedrich married Christine Caroline Bommer. They had three children together: Emma, Agnes, and Gustav Adolf (named after the Swedish King Gustav IV Adolf, whom he admired). In 1835, Friedrich suffered a stroke that forced him to stop painting. He died in Dresden, where he is buried in the Trinitatis cemetery.

== Swedish Representation == The National Museum in Stockholm presented approximately ninety works by Friedrich from October 2, 2009, to January 10, 2010, and noted that "Despite his connection to Sweden, there is not a single work by Friedrich's hand in our country, and his name is today far less well-known than it deserves to be."

== Film == In 1986, the German film director Peter Schamoni made a film about the artist Caspar David Friedrich. It is titled "Grenzen der Zeit" ("Limits of Time").

== Legacy == The asteroid 5296 Friedrich is named after him.
