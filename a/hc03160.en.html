In plain language

The government has proposed changes to the school law. The goal is to ensure that everyone who works or studies in schools feels safe. The new proposal requires schools to have clear plans for how to handle serious violence or threats.

Those responsible for schools, also known as governing bodies, must ensure that individuals who are not authorized to be there do not enter school premises during school hours. There must also be ongoing efforts to prevent crime and create a positive environment for everyone.

The principal now has the authority to require that bags or other items brought by students be searched. This can only be done if there is a valid reason, such as to prevent crime or to ensure that no one is subjected to harassment. The search must not be more extensive than necessary.

If a student is suspected of committing a crime, the principal must report this to the police. This applies to all types of schools, from preschool to upper secondary school and adult education. However, there are exceptions if there are very strong reasons not to report the incident.

Full text

The proposal includes suggested amendments to the Education Act (2010:800) aimed at ensuring a safe and secure learning and working environment for children, students, teachers, principals, and other school staff.

The proposed changes include:

  • Regulations regarding the school's safety work, with the aim of preventing crime, will be included in a new chapter of the Education Act.
  • The responsible authority (e.g., municipality or school board) must ensure that each preschool and school unit has a contingency plan and ongoing procedures in place to ensure preparedness for handling a serious violent situation or a threat of such a situation.
  • The responsible authority must ensure that measures are taken to prevent unauthorized individuals from accessing the premises of a preschool or school unit during operating hours.
  • The principal shall be authorized to decide on the inspection of bags and other items brought to school by students, or to school activities outside the school unit, if it is justified to limit the risk of crime, disorder, or harassment, or to maintain safety. Such an inspection must only be carried out if it is proportionate to its purpose and other circumstances.
  • If there is suspicion that a crime has been committed by a student, the principal shall report the crime to the Police Authority unless there are overriding reasons not to do so. This applies if the crime was committed in connection with activities in preschool, primary school, special primary school, special school, Sami school, after-school care, upper secondary school, adapted upper secondary school, or municipal adult education.
