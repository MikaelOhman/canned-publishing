In plain language

The European Commission, the body responsible for governing the EU's common rules, proposes to extend the special protection granted to individuals from Ukraine. This means that those who have fled the war will be able to reside in and receive assistance from European countries for an additional year.

This decision is being made to demonstrate a commitment to helping people who have been forced to leave their homes due to Russia's aggression. The aim is to ensure that support continues as long as it is needed, allowing those who have fled to live safely.

When determining the duration of this protection, consideration is also given to how Ukraine is coping with the war. The purpose of the extension is to provide clear and unwavering support to Ukrainian citizens who have been displaced by the violence.
