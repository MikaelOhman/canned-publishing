In plain language

The government wants to change the rules regarding the transportation of large amounts of money between countries within the EU. If you are carrying more than 10,000 euros in cash when entering or leaving Sweden from another EU country, you are now required to declare it to the Swedish Customs Agency (Tullverket).

This means you have a duty to declare your money, similar to when traveling to countries outside the EU. If you fail to declare the money, the Customs Agency may confiscate the cash to investigate it further. This is done to ensure that the money is not derived from criminal activity.

Those who violate the new rules and fail to declare their money may be subject to fines. The proposal involves changes to several laws that govern how the Customs Agency and other authorities can operate.

The new rules are planned to come into effect on April 1, 2026. It is important to remember that 10,000 euros is approximately equivalent to 115,000 Swedish krona.

Full text

The proposal suggests that the Customs Agency should be given the authority to control the import and export of cash at the border with other EU countries. The regulation proposed largely corresponds to the one currently in place for cash at the border with countries outside the EU. It is proposed that there should be a reporting and documentation requirement for cash with a value of at least 10,000 euros that is transported across the border to another EU country. Furthermore, it is proposed that the Customs Agency should have the authority to take measures to control compliance with the reporting and documentation requirements and to temporarily withhold cash for further inspection. A criminal penalty is also proposed, meaning that anyone who violates the reporting or documentation requirement may be subject to a fine.

The proposed amendments to the law are scheduled to come into force on April 1, 2026.
