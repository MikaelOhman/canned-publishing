In plain language

A tachograph is a device in vehicles that records mileage and driving times. The government is now proposing a new law to make it more difficult to tamper with this equipment. Those who use or manufacture tools to manipulate the tachograph may be subject to penalties.

The proposal also means that the police and vehicle inspectors will have more authority. They will have the right to conduct inspections on private property to ensure that vehicles comply with the regulations. This is known as "mobile inspections," meaning they can check the equipment without prior notice.

The proposal also includes changes to other laws, such as the law on the protection of geographical information. This is to ensure that the rules are consistent with the new inspection possibilities. If the proposal is approved by the parliament, the new regulations will come into effect on July 1, 2026.

Full text

The proposal outlines measures aimed at strengthening the ability to detect and intervene in cases of manipulation or serious misuse of vehicle tachographs.

A new law – the Act on Measures to Combat Manipulation and Misuse of Tachographs – is proposed, with provisions for enhanced powers for police officers and vehicle inspectors in cases of suspected violations, a stricter penalty for manipulating tachographs, and liability for those who manufacture, transfer, or otherwise handle manipulation equipment.

The proposal also includes amendments to the Vehicle Act (2002:574), including provisions granting control officers the right of access to private areas to carry out spot checks and other inspections of vehicles and their equipment. Furthermore, an amendment is proposed to the Act (2016:319) on the protection of geographical information.

The proposed legislative changes are scheduled to come into effect on July 1, 2026.

The Government's proposal:

  2. The Parliament adopts the Government's proposal for an amendment to the Vehicle Act (2002:574).
  3. The Parliament adopts the Government's proposal for an amendment to the Act (2016:319) on the protection of geographical information.
