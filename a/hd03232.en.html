TL;DRSweden intends to join an international agreement to establish a commission that will pay compensation to Ukraine. To ensure this functions effectively, the government proposes that certain individuals within the commission be granted special rights and protections within Sweden.
In plain language

The government has decided that Sweden will sign an agreement to establish an international commission for compensation to Ukraine. The government has now sent a proposal to the Parliament to obtain a final approval for Sweden to join this agreement.

To ensure the commission can operate smoothly in Sweden, the government proposes an amendment to an existing law. This law concerns so-called immunity and privileges. This means that certain individuals working in the commission, such as members and managers, will receive special protection and certain benefits while performing their duties.

This protection also applies to employees at the commission's office, their family members, and experts appointed to assist. The purpose is to ensure that those working on compensation issues can perform their jobs safely and independently.

If the Parliament approves the proposal, the new rule will come into effect on a date to be determined by the government later.

Full text

On December 11, 2025, the government decided that the convention for the establishment of an international compensation commission for Ukraine would be signed. The convention was adopted on December 16, 2025, and Sweden signed it on the same day. This proposal suggests that the Swedish Parliament should approve Sweden's accession to the convention. According to the convention, the compensation commission and certain individuals will be granted certain immunities and privileges. Therefore, it is proposed that the Parliament decide on an amendment to the law (1976:661) concerning immunities and privileges in certain cases. The law amendment is proposed to enter into force on the date determined by the government.
