In plain language

The Swedish National Audit Office is an agency that reviews how other government agencies operate. They conducted an investigation into how the Swedish Tax Agency (Skatteverket) is trying to combat undeclared work, which involves people working without paying taxes. The National Audit Office believes that the Tax Agency is not doing enough to prioritize the right things and that they are not adequately monitoring the results of their work.

The government has read the report and believes that the criticism is important. They agree that the Tax Agency needs to improve its use of risk assessments to determine where to focus its efforts. The government is already in discussions with the Tax Agency about how to improve its operations.

The Tax Agency has already begun to correct several of the issues identified by the National Audit Office. Work is also underway to improve areas that have not yet been resolved. The government is also working on new proposals within the Government Offices, which is the part of the state that assists the government in its work, to make it even more difficult to engage in undeclared work.

Currently, the government does not believe that any major changes or new decisions are needed. They believe that the ongoing work is sufficient to address the problems highlighted in the National Audit Office's report.

The Swedish National Audit Office's overall conclusion is that there are several shortcomings in how the Swedish Tax Agency prioritizes measures, how effective those measures are, and how they are followed up.

The government welcomes the Swedish National Audit Office's audit. The report is an important contribution to the ongoing efforts to improve the Swedish Tax Agency's operations and the government's oversight of the agency.

The government agrees, to some extent, with the Swedish National Audit Office's criticism regarding the need for the Swedish Tax Agency to improve its follow-up of the results of its measures and to develop a stronger link between risk analysis and those measures. The government has raised the Swedish National Audit Office's observations and recommendations in its dialogue with the Swedish Tax Agency. Several of the observations and recommendations made by the Swedish National Audit Office have already been addressed by the Swedish Tax Agency, and work is underway to address the remaining observations and recommendations. The government has implemented several measures to combat undeclared work. Proposals are being prepared within the Government Offices that will contribute to strengthening the Swedish Tax Agency's ability to counter undeclared work. Given the ongoing work, the government does not currently believe that there is a need to take any other measures in response to the Swedish National Audit Office's observations.
