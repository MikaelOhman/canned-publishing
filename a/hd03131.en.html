In plain language

The government has proposed a change to the law regarding land acquisition. The Land Acquisition Act governs who is allowed to purchase land and agricultural buildings. The proposal aims to make certain purchases faster and easier.

This means that if you are buying a farm or agricultural business from a parent or sibling, you will no longer need to wait for a special permit. The same applies if a couple buys the property together, and one of them is a parent or sibling of the seller.

The proposal also addresses what happens if the state denies someone the right to purchase a farm. The state has a responsibility to redeem the land in certain cases when a purchase does not go through.

If the Parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new rules will come into effect on July 1, 2026.

Full text

The proposal suggests that certain acquisitions of agricultural land should be excluded from the scope of the Land Acquisition Act. This applies to acquisitions made by a sibling or parent of the seller, or, if spouses are acquiring jointly, acquisitions where one of the spouses is the seller's parent or sibling. The amendment to the Land Acquisition Act means that such acquisitions will no longer require a permit.

The proposal also addresses some issues related to the state's obligation to redeem the property if a permit for the acquisition of agricultural land is denied.

The amendment is proposed to come into force on July 1, 2026.
