Full text

In this document, the government addresses the observations and recommendations made by the Swedish National Audit Agency (Riksrevisionen) in its report "The Gambling Authority's Supervision of the Gambling Market" (RiR 2024:19). The Swedish National Audit Agency's overall conclusion in the report is that the supervision of the gambling market is not sufficiently effective to meet the expectations expressed by the Parliament and the government during the deregulation of the gambling market in 2019. The Swedish National Audit Agency assesses that the Gambling Authority conducts too few inspections, does not select the companies subject to supervision based on well-founded risk analyses, and does not adequately follow up to ensure that identified shortcomings are rectified. The Swedish National Audit Agency also notes that the current scope of the Gambling Act (2018:1138) hinders efforts to combat illegal gambling.

The government welcomes the review and agrees with the Swedish National Audit Agency's assessment that the Gambling Authority has, at times, not had the resources to conduct supervision to a sufficient extent. The government has taken several measures to strengthen the authority's ability to conduct supervision and to ensure that reliable information about the scope and results of the supervision is provided. An expert has been commissioned to assist the Ministry of Finance in developing proposals for how the scope of the Gambling Act should be defined.

With this document, the government considers the Swedish National Audit Agency's report to be finalized.
