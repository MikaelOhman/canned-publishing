Full text

In the letter, the government outlines its assessment of the conclusions and recommendations presented in the report "Environmental Rescue in the Event of Major Maritime Accidents – Rare Events with Potentially Extensive Consequences" (RiR 2025:27) by the Swedish National Audit Office.

The Swedish National Audit Office's overall conclusion is that the work is not fully effective and that there are shortcomings in both the practical conditions necessary to maintain capabilities, and in planning and preparation. At the same time, the Swedish National Audit Office notes that, following the major accidents of recent years, the authorities under review are aware of the challenges they need to focus on.

The government believes that Sweden's preparedness for handling large-scale releases of harmful substances at sea is relatively comprehensive compared to other Baltic Sea states. Furthermore, the government believes that the measures taken to strengthen Sweden's readiness also contribute to increasing society's ability to respond to major accidents, such as extensive releases of harmful substances at sea, during peacetime. However, the government shares the Swedish National Audit Office's assessment that there are certain areas for improvement and has therefore taken measures, among other things, to enable more effective collaboration between the actors involved in this area.

The government considers that the Swedish National Audit Office's report is finalized with this letter.
