TL;DRStarting July 1, 2026, the penalties for providing false information to the Swedish Social Insurance Agency (Försäkringskassan) will be stricter, while the process of applying for parental benefits will become simpler.
In plain language

Starting July 1, 2026, new regulations will be implemented in the social security system. If someone knowingly provides false information or fails to report changes in circumstances that result in them receiving benefits they are not entitled to, the Swedish Social Insurance Agency (Försäkringskassan) or the National Pension Agency (Pensionsmyndigheten) may impose an additional fee. This fee will be 25 percent of the amount incorrectly paid out and will never be less than 2,368 SEK.

A so-called "benefit suspension" will also be introduced for individuals who knowingly or through gross negligence cause incorrect payments. This means that the individual may be temporarily suspended from receiving certain benefits for a period ranging from a few months to three years. The purpose of these regulations is to reduce fraud and strengthen trust in the welfare system.

At the same time, it will become easier for parents to receive their benefits. Starting July 1, 2026, only one application to Försäkringskassan will be required to obtain parental leave benefits. Previously, parents were required to submit a preliminary notification before submitting the actual application, but this requirement is now being removed.

July 1, 2026

The Swedish Parliament has approved three amendments to social security laws that will take effect today, July 1st. Two of these amendments are intended to reduce incorrect payments and welfare fraud.

The Parliament has decided that, starting July 1, 2026, the Social Insurance Agency and the National Pension Agency can impose a penalty fee when someone provides inaccurate information or fails to report changes in circumstances that result in an incorrect payment. The purpose is to reduce the number of incorrect payments and strengthen trust in the welfare system.

The penalty fee is 25 percent of the amount incorrectly paid out, which must be repaid. There is a minimum limit, ensuring that the fee is always at least 2,368 Swedish krona (4 percent of the base amount). Learn more about the penalty fee.

A new sanction is being introduced: individuals who intentionally or through gross negligence cause incorrect payments may be temporarily excluded from certain benefits. This exclusion can range from a few months to up to three years, depending on the circumstances. The Parliament's intention is to create stronger protection against systematic abuse of social security.

Learn more about the benefit suspension.

Starting July 1, 2026, it will be sufficient to submit an application to the Social Insurance Agency for parental leave when you want to take time off work to care for your child. Previously, it was required to first register before applying for parental leave.

The government is eliminating the requirement to register before applying for parental leave (regeringen.se).
