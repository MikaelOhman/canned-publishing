TL;DRThe government wants to amend the laws to enhance safety during demonstrations and large events. The proposal would grant the police greater authority to regulate where and when activities can take place, in order to protect people's lives and health.
In plain language

The government has proposed changes to two important laws: the Police Act and the Public Order Act. The purpose is to protect people from violence and accidents when they participate in demonstrations or other large events.

If the police assess that there is a risk to safety, they may gain more authority to change the time or location of a gathering. Instead of completely prohibiting an event, the police may, in certain cases, decide that it must be moved or take place at a different time.

It will also become possible to cancel an event entirely if there is a risk to people's lives or health. The police will also have greater opportunities to intervene against individuals who disrupt order and do not comply with police instructions.

If the Parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new rules should come into effect on July 1, 2026. The proposal is documented in a government bill with the number 2025/26:133.

Full text

The freedom of assembly and the right to demonstration are cornerstones of Swedish democracy. They guarantee every individual's right to gather and express their opinions. It is a fundamental principle that these rights must be protected and actively defended. Protecting democracy also means safeguarding both those who participate in it and others – from dangers, accidents, and violence. Creating safe conditions for individuals to exercise their freedom of assembly and right to demonstration is crucial for these rights to be fully exercised.

To enhance security at public gatherings and events, the government proposes amendments to the Public Order Act and the Police Act. These amendments include the following:

  • The police authority is given clearer powers to change the time or location of a public gathering.
  • The police authority may, in certain cases, prohibit a public gathering from being held at a specific time or location, instead of prohibiting it altogether.
  • A public gathering and event may be cancelled if the safety of people's lives or health is at risk.
  • The police authority is given expanded powers to intervene against individuals who disrupt public order and fail to comply with decisions.

The proposed amendments are scheduled to come into effect on July 1, 2026.
