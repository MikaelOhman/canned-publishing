The government has proposed a new law regarding the distribution of revenue from wind power. This means that people living near wind turbines will receive compensation, known as a "wind power compensation." This compensation will be paid by the company operating the wind turbines.

To be eligible for the compensation, one must reside in a residential building located near the turbines. The distance is calculated based on nine times the height of the wind turbines. The closer one lives to the wind turbine, the more compensation they will receive.

Full text

The proposal suggests a new law regarding revenue sharing from wind power plants. The proposal stipulates that residents living near wind power plants are entitled to compensation equivalent to a portion of the annual revenue generated by a wind power plant (wind power compensation). This compensation is to be paid by the operator of a wind power plant to the owners of residential buildings located within nine times the height of a wind turbine from a wind turbine within the wind power plant. The proposed wind power compensation is higher the closer the residential building is to the plant.

The proposal also includes a change to the income tax law, stipulating that wind power compensation is tax-free to the extent that it relates to a private residence.

The new law and the amendment to the law are proposed to come into effect on July 1, 2026.
