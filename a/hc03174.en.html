The government has proposed changes to the laws to regulate emissions from ships. If a ship does not comply with the EU's emissions trading system, authorities will be able to use stricter measures. This could include prohibiting a ship from entering a port or detaining the ship in port.

To ensure compliance with the rules, the government proposes that fines can be imposed. A fine is a type of penalty that must be paid if someone fails to do what they are required to do. If there are questions about the amount of these fines, the Administrative and Environmental Court will determine the amount.

The Coast Guard and the Police will assist in ensuring that the decisions are followed. They should be able to use the methods necessary to stop violations. In certain specific situations, personnel working for the Coast Guard will also have the right to use force to carry out their duties.

These changes to the laws are intended to come into effect on January 1, 2026. The proposal includes changes to both the Coast Guard Act and the law on emissions of greenhouse gases.

Full text

The proposal suggests legislative changes aimed at ensuring compliance with decisions regarding enforcement measures against ships within the EU's emissions trading system.

Decisions regarding the denial of entry and detention of ships, as well as prohibitions against ships entering ports, should be enforceable with fines. The Environmental and Maritime Court will review cases involving the imposition of fines. The Coast Guard and the Police Authority shall assist the authority that has made a decision to detain a ship or prohibit a ship from entering a port, providing the necessary measures to enforce or prevent violations of such decisions. In certain situations, a Coast Guard officer may be authorized to use force.

The proposed legislative changes are scheduled to come into effect on January 1, 2026.
