Law Regarding Fees for Area-Based Collaboration.

Source Riksdagen Read the original at Riksdagen →2026-03-10
TL;DRThe government wants to introduce a new law that would allow groups of property owners to levy a fee to improve safety and development within a specific area. The law is proposed to come into effect on August 1, 2026.
In plain language

The government has proposed a new law on area-based collaboration. This means that groups of people or companies that own properties can come together to work on issues that prevent crime and make an area more attractive. The purpose is to create more safety and help local businesses grow.

If such a group is approved by the municipality, they may have the right to charge a fee from property owners who are affected by the work. Each owner pays a portion of the cost based on how much benefit they receive from the work. This fee can only be used for costs that the state or municipality does not already have a responsibility to pay.

In order to be allowed to charge fees, the group must be approved by the municipality as a so-called "main contractor." It is also required that those who lead the work represent at least two-thirds of the property owners in the area. The work must not be run for the purpose of making a profit.

If a dispute or disagreement arises regarding the fee between those who lead the work and the property owners, the matter will be resolved in a regular court. The proposal is that the new law will come into effect on August 1, 2026.

Full text

The government proposes a new law regarding fees for area-based collaboration.

With the support of this law, an entity engaged in area-based collaboration may have the right to charge a fee from the property owners involved. This entity could be an association of property owners and other stakeholders.

The right to charge a fee under the law, and the corresponding obligation to pay the fee, makes it possible to distribute the costs of the collaboration among all property owners in proportion to the benefit they may derive from the collaborative work. The fee can only cover costs for activities that are not covered by the responsibilities of the state and the municipality.

To be entitled to charge a fee, there must be area-based collaboration in the sense that different actors, within a geographically defined area in the built environment, collaborate to analyze the needs and take measures to develop the area in a way that prevents crime, promotes safety, and enhances the attractiveness of the area, as well as supporting local businesses, thereby benefiting the area's common good in the long term.

To be entitled to charge a fee, the entity conducting the collaborative work must represent a qualified majority of the property owners involved. There must be a need for area-based collaboration that is not being met by any other means.

To be entitled to charge a fee under the law, the entity conducting the area-based collaboration must be approved as the main operator for the collaborative work. The approval process is assessed by the municipality.

The municipality may, when the need for area-based collaboration is high and not being met by any other means, decide to act as the main operator itself. In all cases regarding the main operator, the municipality must provide the property owners with the opportunity to express their views.

Disputes between a main operator and property owners regarding the fee are to be resolved by a court of law.

According to the proposal, the law will come into force on August 1, 2026.

Prop. 2025/26:157 2 Proposed Law on Fees for Area-Based Collaboration

Hereby is prescribed the following. Chapter 1. Content of the Law Section 1. This law applies to the right of those who conduct area-based collaboration to charge fees from owners of properties and buildings to cover collaboration costs.

Definitions in the Law Section 2. In this law, the following terms have the following meanings: area-based collaboration: a collaboration that, within a geographically defined area in the built environment, analyzes the need for and implements measures to develop the area in a way that contributes to preventing crime, creating safety, and strengthening the area's attractiveness and local economy, thereby contributing to the area's overall well-being. collaboration area: the geographically defined area covered by an area-based collaboration. collaboration property: a property or building within a collaboration area, but not a single-family or two-family house and accessory buildings to such houses, or a property consisting only of such houses. collaboration property owner: the owner of a collaboration property or the owner of a building on another's collaboration property, but not a single-family or two-family house or an accessory building to such a house. collaboration plan: an annual updated operational plan that describes the work and measures planned within the framework of area-based collaboration, both in the short term and in the long term. collaboration cost: a cost that may be covered by a fee according to this law. collaboration fee: a fee that, according to this law, may be charged to cover a collaboration cost. benefit factor: a number, according to this law, that indicates the proportional share of benefit that a collaboration property derives from what a collaboration fee may cover.

Section 3. In this law, the term "collaboration budget" means an annual updated statement of:

  1. the costs that are expected to be incurred in implementing the collaboration plan, broken down by calendar year in which the costs are expected to arise, with information on:

a) which costs are collaboration costs, and b) which costs are covered by other legislation or need to be covered in another way;

  1. how the benefit factors of collaboration properties are determined for the distribution of collaboration fees; and
  2. how any surplus or deficit resulting from the difference between collected collaboration fees and collaboration costs is planned to be balanced through refunds or by being transferred to the following calendar year.

Section 4. In this law, the term "collaboration manager" means the entity that conducts area-based collaboration and is approved by the municipality as the main coordinator of the collaboration work. If the municipality, with the support of this law, has undertaken to conduct area-based collaboration, the municipality is the collaboration manager.

Chapter 2. Requirements for Being a Collaboration Manager Approval as a Collaboration Manager Section 1. Only the following may be approved as a collaboration manager:

  1. an entity that demonstrates that area-based collaboration is needed throughout the collaboration area and that this need is not met anywhere within the area by any other entity;
  2. an entity that has a collaboration plan and a collaboration budget that make it likely that collaboration fees will be collected according to this law;
  3. an entity that demonstrates that the benefit factors of collaboration properties have been determined according to this law and are reasonable;
  4. an entity that represents at least two-thirds of the collaboration property owners and two-thirds of the benefit factors of the collaboration properties;
  5. an entity that does not conduct the collaboration work for profit;
  6. an entity that provides collaboration property owners who wish to participate in the collaboration work with the opportunity to participate in the decision-making of the activity on reasonable and transparent terms; and
  7. an entity that is not, or is not represented by, an entity that is in default, has been declared bankrupt, or may be considered unable or unwilling to fulfill its obligations, comply with the law, or has other circumstances of importance regarding its reputation.

Section 2. Questions regarding approval as a collaboration manager are assessed by the municipality where the collaboration area is located. If the collaboration area is located in more than one municipality, the approval question is assessed by the municipality where the largest part of the collaboration area is located, or by the municipality that the affected municipalities agree upon. The municipality that assesses the question shall give the other municipalities the opportunity to comment before the case is decided.

Section 3. An application for approval as a collaboration manager shall include:

  1. the applicant's name, personal or organizational identification number, and address;
  2. a list of the collaboration properties;
  3. a list of the collaboration property owners represented by the applicant;
  4. a list of the collaboration property owners not represented by the applicant;
  5. a description of who participates in the collaboration work and an account of the consultations with other relevant actors that the applicant has conducted to assess the need for area-based collaboration and what the collaboration should include; and
  6. the information and documents that the applicant relies on to demonstrate that the conditions in Section 1 are met.

Section 4. Before the municipality approves an application to be a collaboration manager, the municipality shall notify the collaboration property owners not represented by the applicant and give them the opportunity to comment. The notification shall be made in accordance with the Act on Formal Notification (2010:1932) and shall include:

  1. information about the main content of this law;
  2. a copy of the application and the documents relied upon by the applicant;
  3. an explanation that the entity approved as a collaboration manager has the right to charge a collaboration fee from the collaboration property owners for costs covered by Section 1, paragraph 3, subsection 1, a, distributed according to Section 1, paragraph 3, subsection 2;
  4. a request to submit any comments within a specified period, which may not be less than four weeks;
  5. information on how the collaboration property owner can follow the case and obtain the municipality's decision in the case;
  6. information on whether simplified notification may be used in the case; and
  7. information about the provisions in this law regarding appeals of the municipality's decision on approval, as well as disputes regarding the amount and payment of the collaboration fee.

Requirements for a Municipality to Be a Collaboration Manager Section 5. A municipality may decide to be a collaboration manager only if:

  1. there is a very significant need for area-based collaboration throughout the collaboration area, and this need is not met anywhere within the area by any other entity;
  2. there is no entity that has applied to become a collaboration manager and has the prerequisites to do so;
  3. the municipality has a collaboration plan and a collaboration budget that make it likely that a collaboration fee will be collected according to this law;
  4. the benefit factors of the collaboration properties have been determined according to this law and are reasonable; and
  5. the municipality provides collaboration property owners who wish to participate in the activity with the opportunity to participate in the decision-making of the activity on reasonable and transparent terms.

Section 6. Before the municipality decides to be a collaboration manager, the municipality shall notify the collaboration property owners and give them the opportunity to comment. The notification shall be made in accordance with the Act on Formal Notification (2010:1932) and shall include:

  1. information about the main content of this law;
  2. an explanation of how the municipality has concluded that there are grounds for the municipality to be a collaboration manager according to Section 5;
  3. an explanation that the collaboration manager has the right to charge a collaboration fee from the collaboration property owners for costs covered by Section 1, paragraph 3, subsection 1, a, distributed according to Section 1, paragraph 3, subsection 2;
  4. a request to submit any comments within a period of not less than four weeks;
  5. information on how the collaboration property owner can follow the case and obtain the municipality's decision in the case;
  6. information on whether simplified notification may be used in the case; and
  7. information about the provisions in this law regarding appeals of the municipality's decision on approval, as well as disputes regarding the amount and payment of the fee.

Annual Follow-up Section 7. A collaboration manager shall submit an updated collaboration plan and collaboration budget to the municipality that has decided on approval as a collaboration manager by March 31 of each year. If the municipality is the collaboration manager, the municipality shall have an updated collaboration plan and collaboration budget by March 31 of each year. The collaboration manager shall send a copy of the plan and budget to the collaboration property owners or make the plan and budget available to the collaboration property owners on a website. If a new collaboration property owner has been added due to a change in ownership of a property or due to the addition of a new building on a collaboration property, the collaboration manager shall send a copy of the plan and budget to the new owner, along with information about the main content of this law and how the new owner can follow the collaboration work and participate in it.

Section 8. The municipality that has approved a collaboration manager shall annually assess whether the conditions for approval are still met. If the municipality is the collaboration manager, the municipality shall annually assess whether the conditions for being a collaboration manager are still met.

Request to Terminate a Collaboration Manager Status Section 9. At the request of a collaboration property owner, the municipality shall assess whether the conditions for an approval according to Section 1 or a decision to be a collaboration manager according to Section 5 are still met.

Termination of a Collaboration Manager Status Section 10. If the conditions for being a collaboration manager are no longer met, the municipality shall decide that the manager status shall cease to be valid and notify the collaboration property owners of this.

Fees for the Municipality's Assessment Section 11. A municipality may issue regulations regarding fees for:

  1. assessing an application for approval according to Section 1;
  2. annual follow-up according to Section 7; and
  3. assessing a request according to Section 9 to terminate a manager status.

Appeal of Municipal Decisions Section 12. Decisions made by the municipality according to this chapter may be appealed to an administrative court. A request for permission to appeal is required when appealing to the appellate court.

Chapter 3. Relationship Between a Collaboration Manager and a Collaboration Property Owner Collaboration Fee Section 1. A collaboration manager has the right to charge a collaboration fee from a collaboration property owner.

Section 2. A collaboration fee may only be charged to cover the reasonable costs of the collaboration manager for:

  1. conducting the collaboration work; and
  2. implementing measures that are appropriate to achieve the purpose of area-based collaboration.

The fee may not be charged for costs for which responsibility for the costs or their distribution is regulated in other legislation, or for measures that, according to other legislation, must be implemented by a state or regional authority or by the municipality.

Section 3. The collaboration fee shall be determined so that the costs are distributed among the collaboration property owners, taking into account the benefit that the owners may be considered to derive from the costs that may be covered by the collaboration fee according to Section 2. The benefit shall be considered proportional to the size and importance of the properties and buildings in the collaboration area, and a benefit factor shall be specified for each collaboration property.

Payment of the Collaboration Fee Section 4. A collaboration property owner shall pay the collaboration fee to the collaboration manager at the request of the manager.

Refund of the Collaboration Fee Section 5. If a collaboration manager status ends, the collaboration manager shall refund any collaboration fees that have not been used.

Disputes Between a Collaboration Manager and a Collaboration Property Owner Section 6. Disputes between a collaboration manager and a collaboration property owner regarding a collaboration fee shall be decided in an ordinary court. The Code of Judicial Procedure contains provisions on the proper court in disputes.

This law comes into force on August 1, 2026.
