Frederick VI of Baden-Durlach, born on November 16, 1617, at Karlsburg Castle, and died there on January 10, 1677, was the Margrave of Baden-Durlach from 1659.

== Biography == Frederick VI was the son of Frederick V of Baden-Durlach and Barbara of Württemberg. Frederick VI participated with the Weimar, Hessian, and Swedish troops in the final stages of the Thirty Years' War. In 1642, he married Christina Magdalena of Palatinate-Zweibrücken, sister of Charles X Gustav. In 1655, this monarch appointed Frederick VI as a general in the Swedish cavalry. In this role, he participated in the war against Poland but returned home upon his father's death in 1659 and assumed the margraviate. Later, as an Imperial Field Marshal, Frederick VI participated in the war against France from 1674 to 1676. Children (selection):
