TL;DRThe EU is correcting an error in the regulations regarding the additional customs duties on fiberglass from Bahrain, Egypt, and Thailand. The purpose is to ensure that the descriptions of the products and their corresponding codes are accurate and consistent with previous decisions.
In plain language

The European Commission has identified errors in the previous regulations regarding additional duties on certain types of fiberglass. An additional duty, known as an anti-dumping duty, is used to protect the EU market when goods are sold at unfairly low prices from countries outside the EU.

The error concerns inaccuracies in the product descriptions. There were also errors in the so-called TARIC numbers. These are special codes that customs authorities use to identify exactly which product is subject to which duty.

The Commission has now revised the text to ensure that the product descriptions align with the investigation that was conducted. They have also corrected the numbers to match the existing regulations for imports from Egypt. This is being done to ensure that customs can operate correctly.

The new corrections will take effect from April 15, 2026. The regulations apply to the import of, among other things, fiberglass in small pieces and fiberglass mats from the companies Jushi Egypt for Fiberglass Industry S.A.E. and Asia Composite Materials (Thailand) Co., Ltd.

COMMISSION IMPLEMENTING REGULATION (EU) 2026/1371

Correcting Implementing Regulation (EU) 2026/831 laying down the definitive anti-dumping duty on imports of certain products consisting of endless glass fiber filament originating in Bahrain, Egypt and Thailand.

THE EUROPEAN COMMISSION HAS ADOPTED THIS REGULATION

having regard to the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union,

having regard to Regulation (EU) 2016/1036 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 8 June 2016 on protection against dumped imports from countries not members of the European Union ( 1 ), in particular Article 9 and Article 14.1, and

Whereas, by Commission Implementing Regulation (EU) 2026/831 ( 2 ), the Commission imposed a definitive anti-dumping duty on imports of certain products consisting of endless glass fiber filament originating in Bahrain, Egypt and Thailand.

Whereas, by Commission Implementing Regulation (EU) 2020/870 ( 3 ), the Commission imposed a definitive countervailing duty on imports of certain products consisting of endless glass fiber filament originating in Egypt.

When implementing Implementing Regulation (EU) 2026/831, the national customs authorities have found that the product description in Article 1 of Implementing Regulation (EU) 2026/831 does not fully reflect the product definition used in the investigation, as described in recital 49 of the Regulation. This is due to a drafting error in the regulatory part of the Regulation. Therefore, the product description in Article 1 of Implementing Regulation (EU) 2026/831 should be corrected to ensure consistency with the scope of the investigation, as set out in recital 49 of the Regulation.

On the basis of the above, the Commission has decided to correct the product description in Article 1 of Implementing Regulation (EU) 2026/831.

The national customs authorities have also noted that the TARIC additional numbers assigned in Implementing Regulation (EU) 2026/831 to imports of endless glass fiber filaments originating in Egypt, both for the single specific exporting producer and for all other imports, do not correspond to the TARIC additional numbers assigned to the same exporting producer and all other imports of the same product in Implementing Regulation (EU) 2020/870.

In light of the above, the Commission has decided to correct the TARIC additional numbers in Article 1 of Implementing Regulation (EU) 2026/831 to ensure consistency with those specified in Implementing Regulation (EU) 2020/870, and to ensure the correct implementation of the measures.

The corrections should take effect from the date on which Implementing Regulation (EU) 2026/831 enters into force.

The measures prescribed in this regulation are consistent with the opinion of the committee established pursuant to Article 15.1 of Regulation (EU) 2016/1036.

In Implementing Regulation (EU) 2026/831, Article 1 shall be replaced by the following:

A definitive anti-dumping duty is introduced on imports of cut glass fibers in lengths of no more than 50 mm; roving of glass fibers, other than roving of glass fibers that have been impregnated and treated with a surface coating and have a draw-down loss of more than 3% (according to ISO standard 1887); and mats of glass fiber filaments, other than mats of glass wool, which are currently classified under CN code 7019 11 00, excluding 7019 12 00 (TARIC numbers 7019 12 00 22, 7019 12 00 25, 7019 12 00 26, 7019 12 00 39), 7019 14 00 and 7019 15 00 and originating in Bahrain, Egypt and Thailand.

The following definitive anti-dumping duty rates shall be applied to the net price at the point of entry into the Union, excluding duties, for the product described in point 1 and produced by the companies listed below:

Jushi Egypt for Fiberglass Industry S.A.E. Asia Composite Materials (Thailand) Co., Ltd.

This regulation shall enter into force the day after its publication in the Official Journal of the European Union.

It shall apply starting April 15, 2026.

This regulation is fully binding and directly applicable in all Member States.
