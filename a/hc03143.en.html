The government has proposed changes to the rules governing schools in Sweden. This proposal would eliminate preschool classes and replace them with a new first grade in primary school. This means that primary school will become ten years long instead of nine.

This change also applies to other types of schools, such as special primary schools, schools for students with disabilities, and Sami schools. For most schools, this means that the early years of primary school will be one year longer. Students will also receive 534 more hours of instruction per year at their school.

To ensure a smooth transition, information about the child will be transferred when they move from preschool to the new first grade. The proposal also includes changes to several other laws, such as laws regarding parental leave, working environment, and discrimination, to ensure everything works together.

If the proposal is approved, the new rules in the Education Act will come into effect on July 1, 2026. The new rules for students will be implemented in schools starting after June 30, 2028.

Full text

The proposal includes suggested amendments to the Education Act (2010:800), aiming to strengthen the quality of education. The proposed changes are as follows:

  • The primary school should be expanded to become a ten-year program by discontinuing preschool as a school form and replacing it with a new first grade in primary school.
  • The adapted primary school, special school, and Sami school should also be expanded with a new first grade, making the adapted primary school a ten-year program, the special school an eleven-year program, and the Sami school a seven-year program.
  • This new first grade extends the early years of primary school by one year in the primary school, the adapted primary school, the special school, and the Sami school, and increases the guaranteed teaching time by 534 hours per school form.
  • The regulations that apply to the various school forms will also apply to this new first grade, although the education provided to students should be adapted to their age.
  • Information necessary to facilitate a child's transition should be provided when a child moves from preschool or childcare to primary school, the adapted primary school, the special school, or the Sami school.
  • Necessary consequential amendments are made to both the Education Act and several other laws.

The amendments to the Education Act are proposed to take effect on July 1, 2026, and will be applied for the first time to education and other activities conducted after June 30, 2028. The other proposed legal changes are scheduled to take effect on July 1, 2028.
