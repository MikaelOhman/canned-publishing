Enhanced control to combat fraud in the food supply chain.

TL;DRThe government wants to change the laws to make it easier to prevent fraud related to food and agricultural products. The goal is to enable better cooperation between authorities and to ensure that regulations are followed.
In plain language

The government has proposed a plan to stop deliberate fraud in the food chain. This aims to prevent companies from deceiving customers or violating important regulations regarding food safety. To achieve this, the government wants to give authorities such as the National Food Agency and the Swedish Board of Agriculture more power to take control in certain cases involving fraud.

The proposal also includes that various authorities that control food and animals must share important information with each other. By giving authorities better access to information, it will be easier to detect inaccuracies and lies in the production of food and feed.

The proposal includes changes to many different laws, such as laws on animal welfare, organic production, and control of plants. The purpose is to create stronger protection for both animals, plants, and the food that humans consume.

If the Parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new rules are planned to come into effect on January 1, 2028.

Full text

It is crucial to strengthen the work of detecting and preventing deliberate violations of material regulations in the food chain, committed through fraudulent or misleading actions. Therefore, the proposal suggests introducing the possibility for authorities such as the Swedish Food Agency and the Swedish Board of Agriculture to take over responsibility for public control and other public activities in a specific type of case involving such fraud.

To further improve the control authorities' access to information and their ability to conduct effective control, provisions on reporting obligations between the control authorities in the food chain are also proposed.

The proposed amendments to the law are expected to come into force on January 1, 2028.
