According to Verian's annual reputation survey, the Swedish Tax Agency's reputation among the public is increasing in 2026. The Swedish Tax Agency continues to rank highly in terms of reputation.

Each year, Verian measures the Swedish people's perception of Swedish government agencies. The Swedish Tax Agency's index has increased this year from 52 to 54, remaining above average. This year's survey covers 51 government agencies and confirms that trust in Swedish government agencies generally tends to increase during times of uncertainty and turbulence. This year's increase is welcome after a slight decrease in figures in 2025.

"Last year, we observed a slight decrease in public trust towards Swedish authorities, including our organization. The fact that we are now increasing again is positive, but it also demonstrates the need for us to continuously work to maintain this trust," says Anders Ågren, Communications Director.

Verian's reputation index for government agencies consists of questions about overall reputation, personal impression, trustworthiness, quality of services, and success. The survey was conducted as an online questionnaire between May 5th and May 26th, 2026, and a total of 11,175 people aged 18 to 79 were interviewed.
