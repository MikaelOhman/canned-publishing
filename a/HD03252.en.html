A restriction on the right to social security benefits for individuals serving a prison sentence in a controlled living environment or in a secure detention facility.

This applies to the new forms of enforcement and will take effect on August 1, 2026.
In plain language

Sweden is introducing new ways to serve sentences: electronic monitoring in supervised housing, and a new type of penalty called "secure custody." The proposal adapts social security regulations to accommodate these changes.

The proposal means that individuals serving sentences in these ways will have limited access to certain benefits, and will be obligated to pay for their upkeep.

The changes to the Social Security Code are proposed to come into effect on August 1, 2026.

Full text

The proposal includes suggestions for amendments to the Social Security Code, prompted by the introduction of the new enforcement method of electronic monitoring in supervised housing and the new penalty of secure detention. The proposals include, among other things, that individuals serving prison sentences with electronic monitoring in supervised housing or serving a sentence of secure detention will have a limited right to certain benefits and an obligation to pay for their upkeep.

The proposed amendments to the law are scheduled to come into effect on August 1, 2026.
