Full text

In this proposal, the government suggests amendments to the law that introduce specific information requirements for licensees who offer lotteries benefiting certain political organizations. These requirements include providing information about the licensee or an organization that receives support from the licensee, both when offering the lottery and in commercial advertisements about the lottery.

The proposal also suggests amendments to the law that stipulate that lotteries offered by a licensee who is themselves, or who provides support to, certain political organizations should not be subject to the relaxed conditions in gambling regulations that apply to other non-profit lotteries. Consequently, such lotteries will no longer be exempt from gambling tax or from the restrictions on credit and bonuses in the gambling laws.

These changes aim to ensure that consumers receive clear information about the fact that by purchasing a lottery ticket, they may be supporting a political organization, and to eliminate unjustified preferential treatment for politically-oriented lotteries.

The proposed amendments are scheduled to come into effect on January 1, 2026.
