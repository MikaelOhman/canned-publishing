TL;DRThe government wants to amend the law to make it easier for the police and the Security Police to use cameras in the fight against crime. The new regulations are proposed to come into effect on May 1, 2025.
In plain language

The government has proposed new regulations for the use of surveillance cameras. The aim is to enable the police and the Security Police to use cameras more effectively to prevent serious crime. This includes, among other things, that authorities will no longer need to obtain permission to use cameras in certain ways.

The police will now be allowed to use cameras in more locations, including places that are important for preventing crime, even if there is not an immediate risk of crime. It will also be easier for the police to use cameras in emergency situations that require quick decisions. In addition, the police will be allowed to use technology that can automatically read license plates on cars throughout the country.

The proposal also makes it easier for the police to use drones and cameras mounted on vehicles without always having to inform everyone who sees them. These changes involve amending several laws that govern how the police and the Security Police handle personal data and work on crime prevention measures.

If the Parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new regulations will come into effect on May 1, 2025. This is part of the government's efforts to strengthen crime prevention in Sweden.

Full text

In the proposal, the government suggests amendments to the law on camera surveillance (2018:1200). These proposals aim to make the rules regarding camera surveillance more effective and to better meet the current needs for surveillance. The proposals include the following:

  • The requirement for a permit for camera surveillance is removed.
  • Law enforcement agencies are given the opportunity to conduct camera surveillance in more locations. Surveillance can be implemented in areas where, based on a forward-looking assessment, there is a risk of serious crime. Surveillance can also be conducted in locations that are strategically important for preventing serious crime, even if there is no immediate risk of crime in that location.
  • The possibilities for law enforcement agencies to conduct camera surveillance in urgent situations are improved.
  • A new exception is introduced to the requirement of conducting an assessment of interests when implementing surveillance, allowing the Police Authority and the Security Police to use technology with automatic license plate recognition (ANPR technology) throughout the country.
  • New and expanded exceptions are introduced to the requirements of providing information and notification about camera surveillance conducted by law enforcement agencies from a civilian vehicle or drone.

These legislative changes are proposed to come into effect on May 1, 2025.

Proposed resolution for the Parliament

Proposal 2024/25:93

Government's Proposals:

  1. The Parliament shall approve the government's proposed amendment to the Act on Camera Surveillance (2018:1200).
  2. The Parliament shall approve the government's proposed amendment to the Act (2018:1693) on the Police's Handling of Personal Data within the Scope of the Criminal Records Act.
  3. The Parliament shall approve the government's proposed amendment to the Act (2019:1182) on the Security Police's Handling of Personal Data.
  4. The Parliament shall approve the government's proposed amendment to the Act (2023:196) on Municipalities' Responsibility for Crime Prevention.
  5. The Parliament shall approve the government's proposed amendment to the Act (2023:474) on Police Powers in Border Areas.
