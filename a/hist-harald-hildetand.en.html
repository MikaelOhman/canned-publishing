Full text

Harald Hildetand ("battle-tooth"), whose Old West Norse name is Haraldr hilditönn, is a mythological king of Svitjod, Denmark, Norway, and the lands of the Vandals. He was the son of Hrörek of Lejre from the Sköldunga clan and Aud Djupaudga, daughter of Ivar Vidfamne. According to other sources (Sögubrot and Hyndlas song), he was the son of Radbard of Gårdarike. He is said to have grown up in Gårdarike. After his maternal grandfather died, he returned to Scania, from where he conquered Svitjod, Denmark, Norway, and northern Germany. He is said to have resided at the fortress of Beritzholm, near the present-day Bjärsjölagårds castle. His brother, Randver, ruled over Northumbria. When he was old, he made his half-brother Randver's son, Sigurd Ring, a sub-king over Svitjod. When Harald realized that he was getting old, 150 years old, and did not want to die from the plague, he challenged Sigurd Ring to a confrontation in the legendary Battle of Bråvalla. The sources about Harald Hildetand are Saxo Grammaticus's *Gesta Danorum* and some Icelandic sagas, primarily *Hervararsaga*.
