TL;DRThe municipality of Härjedalen has decided where and when people can vote in the 2026 election. They have also created a plan to ensure the election runs smoothly and securely, and they have selected individuals who will be responsible for the digital voting system.
In plain language

On September 13, 2026, during the parliamentary, municipal, and regional elections, there will be several polling stations available. In Sveg, Härjedalssalen at Folkan and the ski stadium will be used. In Funäsdalen, the dining hall area of Centralskolan will be used, and in Hede-Vemdalen, Per-Albinfjöset heritage center will be used. The polling stations will be open from 8:00 AM to 8:00 PM on election day.

Advance voting will also be available in the weeks leading up to the election. The election committee has decided on locations and times for this advance voting to make it easy for everyone to participate. The municipality has also developed a plan for election security. This plan aims to ensure that the election is conducted in a safe manner and that preparations are in place in case of any unexpected events during the voting process.

To ensure the smooth operation of the digital processes, three people have been appointed as administrators. Belinda Härjebäck, Sarah Tjärn_nås, and Johan Höglund will be responsible for managing permissions within the national system called Valid. This means they will have the authority to grant other staff members access to the parts of the system needed to conduct the election.

All of these decisions have been made to prepare the municipality for the upcoming election. The costs for renting the venues will be covered by the existing municipal budget, and the new responsibilities for the administrators will not incur any additional costs for the municipality.
