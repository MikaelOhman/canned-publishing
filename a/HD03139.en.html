In plain language

Since 1998, Sweden has been part of a collaboration where a fee is charged for operating heavy trucks on certain roads. Previously, the Dutch Tax Authority has been responsible for collecting these payments from countries not participating in the collaboration.

Now, the Netherlands will cease this work on July 1, 2026. To ensure the system continues to function, the Swedish Tax Agency has been tasked with taking over this responsibility. The Swedish Tax Agency will begin this new responsibility on January 1, 2026.

To make this possible, the government needs to amend certain laws. This includes the law regarding road tolls for heavy vehicles and the law on public access and confidentiality, which governs what information can be disclosed regarding the agency's work.

The government has submitted a proposal to the Swedish Parliament regarding these amendments. If the Parliament approves, the new regulations will come into effect on August 2, 2026.

Full text

Sweden has been part of a collaboration since January 1, 1998, involving a joint system for collecting road tolls for heavy goods transport when using certain roads. The tax authority in the Netherlands has been responsible for the ongoing management of an agreement for collecting road tolls for vehicles from countries not participating in the collaboration. The Netherlands will stop collecting road tolls on July 1, 2026. To ensure that the system for collecting road tolls for vehicles from countries not participating in the collaboration can continue, the Swedish Tax Agency (Skatteverket) has taken over the responsibility for managing the agreement for collecting road tolls, effective January 1, 2026.

In this proposal, the government presents suggestions for certain amendments to the law that should be made as a result of this transfer of responsibility. The changes are proposed to come into effect on August 2, 2026.
