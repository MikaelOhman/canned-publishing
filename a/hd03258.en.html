In plain language

The government wants to introduce new regulations to increase transparency in politics. This includes that political parties will no longer be allowed to receive money from anonymous donors or from abroad, regardless of the amount of money involved. The parties must also report their finances in a clearer way, and the control of this will be stricter.

A new law will also be introduced to regulate lobbyists, i.e., individuals who attempt to influence political decisions. These individuals must register with the National Agency for Public Management, which is the authority responsible for ensuring that the regulations are followed. Lobbyists must also disclose what they have discussed with politicians.

In addition, new regulations are proposed for organizations in the labor market, such as trade unions. If a member of such an organization wants their membership fees not to be used to provide funds for political purposes, they should have the right to object. The organization must then listen and inform all its members about this right.

If the Parliament votes in favor of these proposals, the government plans for the new laws to come into effect on July 1, 2027.

Full text

The proposal suggests legislative changes to further enhance public access to information regarding political processes.

The government proposes, among other things, amendments to the Act (2018:90) on Transparency in the Funding of Political Parties, which would introduce a ban on parties and other entities covered by the law from receiving anonymous and foreign contributions, regardless of amount. The proposal also includes expanding the reporting obligations of political parties and strengthening the oversight of compliance with the regulations.

Furthermore, a new law on transparency in communication aimed at influencing political decisions is proposed. This law would require so-called "influencers" (lobbyists) to register with the National Agency for Public Management and to disclose information about their communication with certain political decision-makers. The National Agency for Public Management would serve as the supervisory authority and would be empowered to issue the necessary directives for oversight.

A new law is also proposed, outlining regulations regarding the contributions of labor market organizations for party-political purposes. This new law would grant individual members of labor market organizations who contribute to party-political purposes the right to provide a declaration stating that their membership fees should not be used for such contributions. These declarations would be binding on the organization, which would also be required to inform its members about the right to provide such a declaration.

The legislative changes are proposed to come into effect on July 1, 2027.
