The government has proposed that individuals receiving social assistance, also known as financial aid, must participate in certain activities. This applies to individuals who have received assistance for at least three months in a row. The purpose is to help people improve their Swedish language skills, increase their chances of finding employment, or participate in practical training in a workplace.

The responsibility for offering these activities lies with the municipality's social welfare committee. The activities should be tailored to what each individual is capable of. At the same time, information about who is participating will be sent to the Public Employment Service, the agency that helps unemployed people find jobs.

If municipalities do not comply with the new rules, they may be required to pay a fee, known as a sanction fee. The agency Inspektionen för våorg och omsorg (IVO) is responsible for determining whether a municipality must pay this fee.

Full text

A reform of social welfare benefits is needed to break the cycle of dependency and reduce social exclusion. This proposal includes draft legislation regarding an activity requirement as a condition for receiving social assistance. The purpose is to counteract social exclusion, introduce a uniform and regulated activity requirement, and contribute to utilizing the workforce. The activity requirement is proposed to complement other measures, such as participation in government-sponsored labor market programs and educational initiatives.

The government proposes, among other things, that the social welfare board should be obligated to provide activities, and that the activity requirement should apply to individuals who have received social assistance for at least three consecutive months. The activities should contribute to increasing the individual's ability to obtain employment, improving their language skills, enhancing their ability to search for jobs, or consist of workplace-based activities. Participation should be adapted to the individual's capabilities.

In addition, the proposal includes, among other things, provisions for the transfer of information from the social welfare board to the Public Employment Service regarding individuals participating in activities. According to the proposal, the Swedish Inspectorate for Welfare and Social Services should be authorized to impose a sanction fee on the municipality in cases of systematic deficiencies in the implementation of the activity requirement.

The provisions regarding the activity requirement are proposed to come into force on July 1, 2026. However, the provision requiring the social welfare board to request that the individual participate in activities should be applied for the first time to social assistance covering October 2026. The amendments to the law regarding the sanction fee for municipalities are proposed to come into force on July 1, 2027.
