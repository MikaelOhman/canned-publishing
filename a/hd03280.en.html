In this document, the government presents its assessment of the conclusions and recommendations made by the Swedish National Audit Agency (Riksrevisionen) in the report "Bridging Language Barriers – Handling Cases Where Individuals Do Not Speak Swedish" (RiR 2026:2).

The Swedish National Audit Agency's overall conclusion is that the processes used by the Swedish Social Insurance Agency (Försäkringskassan), the Swedish Pensions Agency (Pensionsmyndigheten), and the Swedish Tax Agency (Skatteverket) to handle cases involving individuals who do not speak Swedish function well in many respects, but could be more efficient. Furthermore, the Swedish National Audit Agency assesses that the agencies need to take a greater overall responsibility for the handling of these cases, particularly in terms of quality assurance, division of responsibilities, and follow-up. The Swedish National Audit Agency makes several recommendations to the agencies, including clarifying the roles of employees, increasing the use of translations of relevant information into a language that the individual understands, and monitoring the costs of interpretation and translation.

The government welcomes the Swedish National Audit Agency's review. It is essential for legal certainty that individuals understand and can respond to the decisions made by government agencies. It is also important to prevent incorrect decisions, including incorrect payments, and to achieve cost-effective case processing. The Swedish National Audit Agency's review is an important contribution to further improving the efficiency of the processes used by the Swedish Social Insurance Agency, the Swedish Pensions Agency, and the Swedish Tax Agency in cases involving individuals who do not speak Swedish.

With this document, the government considers the Swedish National Audit Agency's report to be finalized.
