Full text

The proposal includes suggestions aimed at improving the conditions for effective oversight that protects individual rights and ensures quality within activities governed by the Social Services Act (2025:400) and the Act (1993:387) on support and services for certain individuals with disabilities, commonly known as the LSS.

The government proposes that the Swedish Inspectorate for Care and Social Services (IVO) be given expanded powers to intervene against activities that do not meet the requirements of the legislation, including the introduction of sanction fees in two cases and the immediate prohibition of unauthorized activities. The government also proposes that licenses for activities that have been dormant for a longer period can be revoked. A criminal penalty is proposed for anyone who continues to operate an activity in violation of a prohibition under the LSS. It is also proposed to clarify that the suitability assessment that must be carried out during the licensing process should also include the legal entity.

To strengthen IVO's ability to provide effective oversight, the government proposes that municipalities be given an expanded reporting obligation to the authority. The reporting obligation should apply to activities under the Social Services Act and the LSS, both for activities operated by the municipalities themselves and for activities operated by private providers.

The government assesses that the special fee for favorable decisions regarding services that are not implemented within a reasonable time and the reporting interval for such decisions should remain in place and proposes that the special fee be increased.

The proposed amendments to the law are scheduled to come into force on January 1, 2026.

Proposed Resolutions of the Parliament

The government's proposals:

  1. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Act (1993:387) on support and services for certain individuals with disabilities.
  2. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Social Services Act (2025:400).
