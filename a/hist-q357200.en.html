Valdemar Birgersson, born around 1238 or 1239, died on December 26, 1302, at Nyköping Castle. He was King of Sweden from 1250 to 1275, the son of Birger Jarl and Ingeborg Eriksdotter of Sweden, and married Sofia Eriksdotter of Denmark in 1260.

From Valdemar's reign onwards, a well-established Swedish noble family from Bjälbo was elevated to the Swedish royal lineage through marriage into the Erik dynasty, which became extinct with Valdemar's uncle, King Eric.

== Biography == Valdemar succeeded his maternal uncle, Eric Eriksson, as king in 1250, at a young age, because he descended from four powerful families. His paternal grandfather was Magnus Minnesköld, who fell in battle at Gestilren, one of the battles against the Sverker dynasty. His paternal grandmother was Ingrid Ylva, who may have been a granddaughter of Sverker the Elder. His maternal grandfather was King Eric Knutsson of Sweden, and his maternal grandmother was Rikissa of Denmark, daughter of Valdemar the Great. Valdemar was crowned king at the age of eleven. Olaus Petri's account is somewhat inaccurate; the election of a king hardly resolved any disputes. Conflicts with other claimants to the throne, who, along with the people of Uppland, opposed Birger Jarl's central authority, and deep conflicts between siblings within the royal family, some ending in death, would continue for at least a hundred years. After 25 years as king, Valdemar and his wife Sofia were forced to flee the country, and Valdemar later died imprisoned at Nyköpingshus by his brother Magnus Ladulås.

The rejected claimants Filip Knutsson and Knut Magnusson led an uprising after the election of the king, representing the Folkung faction, and assembled a mixed army of Norwegians and German mercenaries. However, this rebel army was defeated by Valdemar's forces, along with those of his father, Birger Jarl, at the Battle of Herrevadsbro in Västmanland. The rebels, as well as several German soldiers, were executed.

For the first sixteen years, Valdemar's father, Birger Jarl, was the actual ruler. During his reign, so-called "peace laws" were introduced, regulating domestic peace, church peace, judicial proceedings, and the protection of women. In 1260, Valdemar married Sofia Eriksdotter of Denmark. The marriage likely took place in Jönköping, but it may also have been in Öninge, near Ödeshög; the Erik Chronicle mentions Ymninge. In 1266, when Valdemar was approximately 26 years old, his father, Birger Jarl, died. Valdemar continued as king until 1275, just nine years after his father's death.

In 1274, due to his romantic affairs, he made a penance journey to Rome.

=== Conflict with Brothers === Upon returning from Rome, King Valdemar appears to have become embroiled in conflict with his brothers. Valdemar seems to have had a dispute over inheritance with his brother Erik. Erik received support from the nobleman Johan Filipsson, but Valdemar imprisoned them both. Erik fled and now gained the support of his brother Magnus. This led to an uprising in 1275. Erik and Magnus also obtained Danish mercenaries from King Eric Klipping of Denmark, and Valdemar was defeated by them at the Battle of Hova. Valdemar and Sofia fled to Norway.

=== Valdemar is Deposed === Shortly after, when Valdemar returned to Sweden to resume the conflict, he was captured. His brother, Magnus Ladulås, seized power and was elected king. However, Valdemar was eventually released and appointed Duke of Götaland. [Source needed]

In 1276, because Magnus had not paid his debt to Eric Klipping, Magnus joined forces with Valdemar. War broke out between Sweden and Denmark. King Magnus then chose to pay the debt and pledge the city of Lödöse as collateral. The king also pursued negotiations with Valdemar. In 1277, Valdemar was granted the entire province of Götaland as a duchy in exchange for renouncing his claim to the throne and recognizing Magnus as his superior.

It is uncertain whether Valdemar was involved in the Folkung uprising, which nearly overthrew Magnus from the throne. However, Valdemar was stripped of his holdings and forced to renounce the throne again. He then spent nine years in Denmark, where he held the position of governor of Malmö during 1284–85.

=== Imprisoned and Dies in Nyköping === For reasons unknown, Valdemar returned to Sweden. In 1288, his brother Magnus had him imprisoned in Nyköpingshus, officially because he was deemed mentally unstable and had caused offense through his frivolous behavior. He died in Nyköpingshus in 1302. According to traditional accounts, he is buried in Vreta Abbey. A more recent hypothesis suggests that he may be buried in Riddarholmskyrkan in Stockholm.

=== Legacy === During his 25-year reign, King Valdemar left relatively few lasting marks. However, he is remembered more for his romantic affairs.

== Family == Valdemar was married to Sofia Eriksdotter of Denmark. He also had several known mistresses, primarily his sister-in-law, Princess Jutta of Denmark, and possibly subsequent wives after Sofia: "Kristina of Denmark" (of unknown lineage), Katarina of Gützkow (daughter of Duke Jaxa of Salzwedel), and finally Lukardis, whom he likely married.
