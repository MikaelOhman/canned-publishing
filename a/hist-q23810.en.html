== Childhood ==

=== Parents and siblings ===

Alfred Nobel was born on October 21, 1833, at Norrlandsgatan 11 in Stockholm, as one of eight children (only four of whom lived to adulthood), to the factory owner Immanuel Nobel the younger, who was also an inventor, and Andriette Nobel, n√©e Ahlsell. He was also the grandson of the surgeon Immanuel Nobel the elder, who in turn belonged to a branch of the Nobel family, which originated from Petter Nobelius (died 1707), a district governor from Uppland. He and his brothers Robert, Ludvig, and Emil lived under difficult conditions with a very ill father.

=== Russia === In 1842, the family moved to Saint Petersburg, in the then-Russian Empire, after the father's mechanical workshop went bankrupt in Sweden. At that time, the family was very poor, and the wife worked as a shop assistant in a milk and vegetable store, while Alfred himself helped sell matches on the street. Once in Saint Petersburg, he studied chemistry and also became multilingual. The family's business later became successful and grew into an industrial giant that flourished until the Russian Revolution. After financial difficulties, Nobel and his father returned to Sweden in 1859.

== Experiments == After returning to Sweden from Russia, Alfred Nobel began studying explosives, particularly nitroglycerin. The experiments took place at the Heleneborg experimental factory in Stockholm. On September 3, 1864, an explosion occurred at Heleneborg during the production of blasting oil and nitroglycerin. His brother Emil Nobel, who was 21 years old, and four other people present were killed. Nobel had met the discoverer of nitroglycerin, Ascanio Sobrero, during his time in Paris, and realized that its power needed to be controlled in order to be useful.

=== Inventions and Patents ===

Alfred Nobel invented several things, including:

  • Nobel's powder (a mixture of nitroglycerin and black powder) in 1863.
  • The detonator cap in 1864.
  • Dynamite in an unspecified year.
  • Blasting gelatine in 1875.
  • Extradynamite in 1887.
  • Ballistite (a smokeless powder consisting of nitrocellulose and nitroglycerin in equal parts) in 1887.
  • The "Mignon Naftaslup" yacht (launched in 1892, 12 meters long, 1.8 meters wide, with a draft of 0.9 meters, and powered by a 6 horsepower steam engine).

He obtained Swedish patents for the detonator cap in 1864 and for dynamite on September 19, 1867.

Dynamite offered significant opportunities to transport explosive materials safely. Despite nitroglycerin, the active ingredient in dynamite, being highly shock-sensitive, the process of "flegmatization" prevents it from detonating even under fairly hard impacts.

There are accounts suggesting that Nobel accidentally invented blasting gelatine by inadvertently mixing nitroglycerin and collodium solution. He was kept awake all night by the pain, and he began to consider a suitable way to incorporate cotton powder with nitroglycerin. He then thought that it might work better with nitrocellulose of a lower degree of nitration.
