Katarina Vasa, born June 6, 1539, in Stockholm, and died December 21, 1610, in Berum, East Frisia, was a Swedish princess, the daughter of King Gustav Vasa and Queen Margaret Leijonhufvud. She was politically active in East Frisia as countess between 1561 and 1599. Between 1599 and 1610, she ruled Berum and Norden, which were originally intended to be her life annuity, as an autonomous regional ruler.

== Biography ==

=== Background === During her upbringing, Katarina was cared for by staff at the "Children's Home," where Birgitta Lass Andersson, the noble widow Ingrid Amundsdotter, and her mother's friend "Miss Margareta" were reportedly responsible for daily care. Her grandmother, Ebba Eriksdotter (of the Vasa family), likely held overall responsibility, especially during her parents' absences. While descriptions exist of her brothers' education, information about the upbringing of Gustav Vasa's daughters is scarce and mostly indirect. As princesses during the Renaissance, their role was to benefit their homeland abroad through diplomatic marriages, acting as ambassadors. Katarina, like her sisters, would have been educated in German language, household management, dancing, music, and public speaking.

After her mother's death in 1551, she was placed under the care of Kristina Gyllenstierna, followed by her aunts, Queen Märta and Brita Eriksdotter Leijonhufvud, before her father remarried to Katarina Stenbock.

In 1556, like her sisters, Katarina had her portrait painted, poems praising her virtues were written by the court poet Henricus Mollerus, and she was provided with a dowry of 100,000 daler to be presented on the political marriage market. As early as 1541 in Brömsebro, proposals had been made for her to marry the heir to the Danish throne, but these plans never materialized.

Katarina married Count Edzard II of East Frisia (June 24, 1532 – September 1, 1599). The marriage between Katarina and Edzard was arranged for political reasons. East Frisia was a strategic ally against Denmark. The port city of Emden in East Frisia was an important trading center that competed with Lübeck, and a trade agreement would break the Hanseatic League's control over Swedish trade. In 1556, Gustav Vasa sent an envoy to East Frisia offering a trade agreement and a marriage alliance. The envoy carried a portrait of Katarina. The trade agreement was concluded in 1557, and it was decided that Edzard would travel to Sweden the following year to finalize the marriage negotiations.

In July 1558, Edzard arrived in Stockholm. He met both Katarina and her sister Cecilia and chose Katarina. The negotiations included Gustav Vasa's unsuccessful attempts to prevent Edzard's mother from dividing East Frisia among her sons. On August 12, 1558, the betrothal was signed. Katarina was guaranteed a pension, a court appropriate to her status, the estates of Berum and Norden as widow's dower, and a place in any potential regency council should Edzard die before their heir came of age. Edzard returned to Sweden in August 1559, but the wedding was postponed several times, and it appears that Katarina herself worked to expedite the negotiations until the marriage contract could finally be signed on September 29.

The wedding took place on October 1, 1559, in Stockholm. In November 1559, the newlyweds left Stockholm to travel to East Frisia. They were accompanied by a large retinue, including Katarina's younger sister Cecilia Vasa and Edzard's younger brother Johan of East Frisia. These two young people had developed an affection for each other, and their relationship caused what is known as the "Vadstena Riot" during their stay at Vadstena Castle. The complications arising from this scandal led to the newly married Katarina being forced to remain in Sweden until early 1561 before she could depart for East Frisia. Katarina intervened on Cecilia's behalf when her father wanted to confiscate Cecilia's jewelry. Katarina and Edzard were placed under a form of house arrest at Västerås Castle. Katarina mediated and negotiated with her father, stepmother Katarina Stenbock, and brother Erik to obtain permission to leave the country and have her brother-in-law Johan released from prison. Due to her pregnancy, childbirth, and Gustav Vasa's death, her departure was further delayed.

=== Countess of Ostfriesland === Katarina arrived in Ostfriesland in April 1561. She was the daughter of Gustav Vasa who most closely resembled her father in character. She actively participated in her husband's domestic political conflicts. Katarina took a stand for Lutheranism against Calvinism in Ostfriesland. She used the contacts she made in Ostfriesland to benefit her family. For example, she helped Karl with import and export businesses and acquired ships for the Swedish navy during the war against Russia. However, from 1564 to 1565, she avoided helping Erik XIV with the mercenary troops he requested, and instead responded to his request by demanding that Johan be released and that Erik deserved to be brought to justice in Friesland for having imprisoned his brother.
