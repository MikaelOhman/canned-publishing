In plain language

The government has proposed changes to the electoral law, which governs how elections are conducted in Sweden. The aim is to protect the integrity of the elections and ensure that everyone who is eligible to vote can do so without difficulty. The proposed changes are scheduled to take effect on December 1, 2025.

A key element is that the Swedish Electoral Authority, the agency responsible for elections, will be given a clearer responsibility to protect the electoral process. If any errors or problems occur during the voting process, municipalities and other authorities must promptly report them to the Electoral Authority. This is to ensure that any incidents that could affect the election can be addressed quickly.

It will also become easier for certain groups to vote. For example, individuals residing in specific youth homes will be able to receive assistance from poll workers who will visit them at their homes. For Swedes living abroad, there will be new opportunities to vote in Sweden if they are not included on the list of eligible voters.

Finally, the government wants to make it easier to access information. They propose allowing voting cards to be sent digitally to individuals who use a secure digital mailbox. A digital mailbox is a secure online platform where government agencies can send important messages to individuals.

Full text

To protect the integrity of elections and strengthen opportunities for citizens to exercise their right to vote, the government proposes amendments to the Electoral Act (2005:837). These proposals include the following:

  • The Swedish Electoral Authority will be explicitly given the responsibility to support and coordinate efforts to protect elections.
  • County Administrative Boards, municipalities, and foreign missions that have established polling stations must promptly report any incidents that could affect the conduct of elections to the Swedish Electoral Authority.
  • The requirements for ensuring that voting is accessible to all voters will be clarified.
  • More voters, including individuals residing in special youth homes, will be given the opportunity to vote using mobile polling stations.
  • Swedes living abroad who have been removed from the electoral register will be able to vote in advance in Sweden, and their registration in the electoral register will be reactivated through this advance vote.
  • Furthermore, the government believes that it should be possible to send digital voting cards to voters who have chosen to receive communications from government agencies in a secure digital mailbox.

The proposed amendments to the law are scheduled to come into effect on December 1, 2025.
