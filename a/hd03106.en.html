Full text

The government proposes measures aimed at combating crime in the real estate market and preventing circumvention of legislation regarding condominiums.

It is proposed that the requirements for providing identification information in land registration applications be tightened. Anyone acquiring a property must always provide a personal identification number, a coordination number, or an organizational number. The purpose is to create better control over who owns a property. Increased requirements for identification information can contribute to the police and other authorities' ability to prevent and combat crime.

Measures are also proposed to prevent circumvention of legislation when rental properties are converted into condominiums. To ensure that only those who actually live in the property vote on a conversion, only those who have been registered as residents at the property for at least six months immediately preceding the association meeting where the decision is made should be allowed to participate in the vote.

It is also proposed that the same rules that apply when converting from a rental property to a condominium should apply when an economic association is reregistered as a condominium association.

The proposed amendments to the law are scheduled to come into effect on July 1, 2026.
