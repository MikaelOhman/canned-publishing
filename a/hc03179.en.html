Full text

The proposal includes suggestions for expanded investigative powers and reporting obligations that aim to strengthen the ability of the Swedish Social Insurance Agency (Försäkringskassan), the Swedish Pensions Agency (Pensionsmyndigheten), and the social welfare committees (socialnämnder) of the municipalities to make correct decisions and prevent incorrect payments.

The proposal suggests that the Swedish Social Insurance Agency and the Swedish Pensions Agency should have expanded opportunities to obtain information about both the insured individual and other individuals, as well as about legal entities. The proposals include expanded powers for the Swedish Social Insurance Agency and the Swedish Pensions Agency to request information, as well as expanded obligations for various entities, including government agencies, providers of personal assistance, insurance companies, and credit institutions, to provide information to the Swedish Social Insurance Agency and the Swedish Pensions Agency upon request. Furthermore, the proposal suggests that it should be explicitly regulated that the social welfare committees have the right to access information from the Swedish Tax Agency (Skatteverket), including information contained in employer declarations.

The proposal also includes suggestions for enhanced protection of confidential information held by the Swedish Social Insurance Agency and the Swedish Pensions Agency regarding the business and operational activities of individuals.

The proposed amendments to the laws are suggested to come into effect on December 1, 2025.
