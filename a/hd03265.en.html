TL;DRThe government wants to change the rules regarding how individuals from other countries who are in Sweden are checked or detained. The aim is to make the migration process more transparent and secure.
In plain language

The government has proposed changes to the immigration law. This would make it easier to monitor individuals present in the country. A new method called "enhanced surveillance" is proposed, which may include electronic monitoring, such as an ankle bracelet, to ensure that a person remains in a specific location.

The proposal also clarifies which authorities are responsible for determining whether someone should be under surveillance or in detention. Detention means that a person is held in custody for a period of time. The government also wants to make it clearer when these rules can be applied. This could also mean that certain individuals may be held in detention for a longer period than previously.

For individuals considered a security risk, a new plan is in place. These individuals may be placed in a special security unit. This is proposed to reduce the number of people sent to regular prisons, which are operated by the Swedish Prison and Probation Service.

The new rules are intended to come into effect in July 2026. Some parts of the proposal, such as the new methods for monitoring individuals, will come into effect later, for example, in October 2026 or December 2027.

Full text

Appropriate conditions for supervision and detention of foreign nationals are an important part of a well-organized and efficient immigration process. To ensure that the rules regarding supervision and detention better serve this purpose, the government proposes amendments that include the following:

  • More forms of supervision will be introduced, including a residency requirement that can be monitored electronically (enhanced supervision).
  • The conditions for placing a foreign national under supervision and for detaining a foreign national will be clarified and expanded.
  • It will be clearer which authority is responsible for deciding on supervision and detention at each stage of the immigration process.
  • A foreign national held in detention who poses a security risk should be able to be placed in a secure unit. This will reduce the need to place foreign nationals in external correctional facilities.
  • In certain cases, a foreign national held in detention may be held in detention for a longer period if necessary.

The proposed amendments to the law will come into force on July 21, 2026, with the exception of certain provisions regarding a new basis for supervision and detention, and regarding enhanced supervision, which are proposed to come into force on October 1, 2026, and December 1, 2027, respectively, or an earlier date determined by the government.
