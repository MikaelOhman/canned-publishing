A more effective regulation of the ethical review process for research involving human subjects.

TL;DRThe government wants to change the rules for how research involving human subjects is regulated, in order to make the process more streamlined. Certain research projects that handle sensitive personal data will no longer require formal approval, but the researcher must personally ensure that the regulations are followed.
In plain language

The government has proposed changes to the law governing the ethical review of research. The aim is to make the oversight of research involving humans more efficient and clear. The proposal includes, among other things, that certain types of research will no longer require a specific approval if they handle sensitive personal data or information about criminal offenses.

The person leading the research, known as the research principal, will be responsible for assessing whether the research falls under the new exemptions. There will also be an option to apply for a voluntary review if one wants to be completely certain that everything is being done correctly.

The work of ensuring that the rules are followed will also be changed. The Appeals Board for Ethical Review, known as Önep, will be given more power to comment on whether research has gone wrong. The board will also be able to provide guidance to ensure that the rules are applied consistently throughout the country. Decisions regarding research will no longer be appealable, and the board will not be required to report suspected crimes to the police.

If the proposal is approved, the new rules will come into effect on July 1, 2027. The proposal also includes changes to laws governing how personal data is handled by government agencies such as IFAU and the Swedish Social Insurance Agency.

Full text

The proposal includes suggested amendments to the Act (2003:460) on ethical review of research involving human subjects (the ethical review act), aiming for a more efficient ethical review process and more effective oversight. The proposals essentially entail the following:

Exemptions are introduced from the requirement for ethical approval for certain research involving the processing of sensitive personal data or personal data related to criminal offenses. The research organization must assess whether the research falls under any of the exemptions. There should be an opportunity to apply for voluntary ethical review of such research.

Furthermore, certain changes are to be made regarding the oversight of compliance with the ethical review act. The Appeals Board for Ethical Review (Önep) should, within the scope of its oversight, be able to express its opinion in its decisions on whether research has been conducted in violation of the ethical review act, regulations issued pursuant to the act, or conditions imposed in connection with ethical review approval or an application for voluntary ethical review (review decisions). Önep should also be able to make statements in such decisions aimed at promoting a uniform and efficient application of the act. Review decisions should not be appealable. In addition, the board should not be obliged to file a criminal complaint if there is suspicion of a violation of the ethical review act.

The proposed amendments to the law are to come into force on July 1, 2027.
