The new proposal would create a separate crime in the criminal code, which is the law that contains regulations on various offenses. This concerns preventing anyone from being subjected to repeated acts of abuse, such as malicious statements, threats, or the improper monitoring of someone.

For something to be considered psychological abuse, the acts of abuse must have seriously damaged the person's self-esteem. It can also involve someone being monitored for an extended period in a way that is harmful. If someone is convicted of this crime, they could face a prison sentence of up to four years.

Full text

Psychological violence is a serious social problem and often occurs as part of men's violence against women, domestic violence, and honor-related violence and oppression. Psychological violence is particularly prevalent in young people's relationships and often has a digital dimension. The negative effects on those subjected to psychological violence are significant, both in the short and long term. Against this background, it is considered necessary to strengthen the criminal law protection against psychological violence.

Therefore, it is proposed that a new crime, called "psychological violence," be introduced into the criminal code. This regulation would make it punishable to repeatedly subject another person to insults in the form of accusations, derogatory statements, humiliating behavior, undue threats, undue coercion, or undue surveillance, if the insults, taken together, are intended to seriously damage the person's self-esteem. It is also proposed that it should be punishable to subject someone to undue surveillance that is persistent, if the act is intended to seriously damage the person's self-esteem. The proposed penalty is imprisonment for a maximum of four years.

Furthermore, it is proposed that the crime of "psychological violence" can be included as part of crimes related to child protection, aggravated violation of peace, aggravated violation of women's peace, unlawful harassment, and honor-based oppression.

In addition, it is proposed that the crime of "insult" can be included as part of aggravated violation of peace, aggravated violation of women's peace, unlawful harassment, and honor-based oppression.

The proposed amendments to the law are scheduled to come into force on July 1, 2026.
