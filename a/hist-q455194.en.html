Carl Alexander Clerck, likely born in Stockholm around 1709 and died there on July 22, 1765, was a Swedish entomologist.

== Biography == Carl Alexander Clerck was the son of bookkeeper Alexander Clerck and Elisabeth Gram; his father belonged to a non-noble branch of the Clerck family (number 1382). Clerck served as a commissioner at Stockholm's city mediation commission and became a member of the Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences in Uppsala before 1757, and a member of the Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences in 1764. He had a strong interest in the natural history of invertebrates, particularly spiders.
