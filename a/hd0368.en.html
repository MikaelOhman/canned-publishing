Deviation from the provisions on deemed approval in the EU's Gigabit Infrastructure Regulation.

TL;DRSweden wants to change the rules regarding when a permit is automatically approved if authorities do not respond within the specified timeframe. This applies to the expansion of high-speed internet, also known as gigabit infrastructure.
In plain language

There is an EU regulation that states that if an authority, such as a court, does not respond to an application within a certain time, the application is considered approved. This is known as "tacit approval." This rule is intended to speed up the process of expanding high-speed internet across Europe.

Now, the Swedish government wants Sweden to utilize a provision within the EU regulations that allows it to not follow this specific rule. This means that a permit will not be automatically approved simply because the deadline has passed if the court has not yet made a decision.

The government plans to achieve this by enacting a new law that supplements the EU regulations. The purpose is to change the way applications for permits related to the crucial infrastructure for high-speed internet are handled.

The new law is intended to come into effect on March 12, 2026. This means that the rules governing how authorities make decisions regarding the expansion of internet infrastructure will be different in Sweden after that date.

Full text

According to the European Parliament and Council Regulation (EU) 2024/1309 (the EU regulation on gigabit infrastructure), the regulation includes provisions regarding the timeframe within which a competent authority, such as an environmental court, must grant or reject an application for a permit. If a decision is not made within the timeframe stipulated in the regulation, the permit shall be deemed granted upon the expiration of the deadline (a so-called "tacit approval"). Member States may, under certain conditions specified in the regulation, deviate from the provisions on tacit approval.

The government proposes that Sweden, through a new law containing supplementary provisions to the EU regulation on gigabit infrastructure, should deviate from the provisions on tacit approval that, according to the regulation, apply when a court has not reviewed a permit application covered by the regulation within the timeframes stipulated.

The law is proposed to come into force on March 12, 2026.
