In plain language

The government proposes new regulations for how tax-free fuel for aircraft and boats should be handled. The goal is to make it easier for authorities such as the Police, the Armed Forces, and the Coast Guard to cooperate. It should be simpler for these authorities to provide fuel to each other, especially when it is needed during rescue operations or in crisis situations.

The proposal also includes regulations on how taxes should be reimbursed to military units from other countries that are members of NATO. This applies to several different types of taxes, such as taxes on energy, chemicals in electronics, nicotine products, tobacco, and alcohol.

In addition, the government wants to clarify the rules for how authorities should control and manage taxes. This involves making the rules in the laws clearer for everyone involved. If the Parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new regulations will come into effect on April 1, 2026.

Full text

The proposal includes several smaller suggestions within the area of excise taxes. First, it proposes certain simplifications regarding the handling of tax-exempt aviation and marine fuels to facilitate cooperation between government agencies and to make it easier for these agencies to assist civil society in rescue operations and crisis situations. The proposed amendments to the Energy Tax Act (1994:1776) include measures to facilitate the operations of the Swedish Armed Forces, the Defence Materiel Administration, the Coast Guard, the Police Authority, the Swedish Maritime Administration, and the Swedish Transport Administration by expanding their ability to provide tax-exempt fuel to each other. It is also proposed that the Swedish Armed Forces, the Coast Guard, and the Swedish Maritime Administration should be given the possibility to provide tax-exempt aviation and marine fuels to other public authorities that are tax-exempt consumers, within the framework of rescue operations and crisis situations.

Second, the proposal includes supplementary provisions regarding the refund of taxes for armed forces belonging to other states that are parties to NATO, in the Energy Tax Act, the Act on Tax on Chemicals in Certain Electronics (2016:1067), the Act on Tax on Certain Nicotine-Containing Products (2018:696), the Tobacco Tax Act (2022:155), and the Alcohol Tax Act (2022:156).

Finally, the proposal includes certain clarifications to the procedural rules in the Energy Tax Act, the Act on Tax on Chemicals in Certain Electronics, the Act on Tax on Certain Nicotine-Containing Products, the Tobacco Tax Act, the Alcohol Tax Act, and the Customs Powers Act (2024:710).

The changes are proposed to come into force on April 1, 2026.

Proposed Resolutions of the Riksdag (Parliament)

The government's proposal:

  1. The Parliament shall approve the government's proposed amendment to the law (1994:1776) regarding taxes on energy.
  2. The Parliament shall approve the government's proposed amendment to the law (2016:1067) regarding taxes on chemicals in certain electronic devices.
  3. The Parliament shall approve the government's proposed amendment to the law (2018:696) regarding taxes on certain nicotine-containing products.
  4. The Parliament shall approve the government's proposed amendment to the law (2022:155) regarding tobacco taxes.
  5. The Parliament shall approve the government's proposed amendment to the law (2022:156) regarding alcohol taxes.
  6. The Parliament shall approve the government's proposed amendment to the customs authority law (2024:710).
