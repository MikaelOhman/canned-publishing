Adapting Swedish law to the revised VIS regulation.

In plain language

VIS is a joint system within the EU used to share information about visas for short trips. The rules are now being changed so that the system will also include information about longer visas and residence permits. This is being done to improve cooperation between countries in the EU.

The Swedish government has proposed new laws in Sweden to make this possible. An important part of this is to make it clearer when individuals applying for a visa are required to provide fingerprints and have their photo taken. This is necessary for authorities to verify the information in the system.

The proposal also addresses how personal data should be handled and protected. There are new rules about how the police and other authorities can use the information in the system. The purpose is to make it easier for the police to use the information to combat crime.

The new laws will amend several different laws, such as the Immigration Act and laws regarding how the police can use personal data. The new rules will come into effect on a date determined by the EU Commission or the Swedish government.

Full text

The Visa Information System (VIS) is a joint EU information system for the exchange of information between member states regarding short-stay visas, also known as Schengen visas. In July 2021, an EU regulation was adopted to reform the VIS system. The changes include that the VIS system will also contain information about long-stay visas, which in Swedish law are called national visas, and about residence permits. Furthermore, access to the VIS system for law enforcement agencies will be improved.

The regulation is directly applicable and has the force of law in Sweden. It is to be fully implemented on a date to be determined by the Commission.

In the proposal, the government suggests the legal amendments necessary to adapt Swedish law to the EU's revised rules on the VIS system. The proposals concern:

  • Clarifying in which situations a foreign national is obliged to provide a photograph and fingerprints in order to carry out a check against the system.
  • Introducing a requirement to provide a photograph and fingerprints in cases involving national visas.
  • How personal data should be processed.
  • How the regulations should relate to other regulations regarding the processing of personal data.

The proposed amendments to the law are to come into force on the date determined by the government.

Proposed Resolutions for the Parliament

The government's proposals:

  1. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Aliens Act (2005:716).
  2. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Aliens Data Act (2016:27).
  3. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Act (2017:496) on international police cooperation.
  4. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Act (2018:1693) on the police's processing of personal data within the scope of the Criminal Data Act.
