TL;DRSweden aims to establish new food reserves to ensure food security within the country, even during times of war or crisis. The proposal involves the state owning and controlling these reserves to protect the food supply.
In plain language

The government has proposed a new law regarding the establishment of food reserves. The purpose is to ensure that there is sufficient food in Sweden in the event of war, the threat of war, or other major crises that could otherwise lead to food shortages. This applies to all essential food items, but drinking water is not included in this proposal.

The state will own the food in these reserves. A government-appointed agency will be responsible for maintaining the reserves. This agency may also enter into agreements with private companies to assist in the storage of the food. To ensure that the food remains available even if a company experiences financial difficulties, the state will have enhanced protection for its goods.

Those who work on storing the food in these reserves must adhere to confidentiality regulations. This means that they are not allowed to disclose confidential information about the layout of the reserves or their contents. This is important to protect the country's security.

If the proposal becomes law, it will come into effect on July 1, 2026. The government also wants to amend another law, called the Public Access and Secrecy Act, to ensure that everything functions correctly with the new regulations.

Full text

This proposal outlines a new law regarding strategic reserves of goods within the food supply chain.

The proposal stipulates that Sweden should maintain strategic reserves of goods within the food supply chain. The purpose of this stockpiling is to ensure the availability of essential foodstuffs, excluding drinking water, and foodstuffs that are crucial for maintaining national defense capabilities, in the event that Sweden is at war or faces the threat of war, or if there is a shortage of food in the country due to an extraordinary event, or if there is a significant risk of such a shortage occurring.

A strategic reserve established under this law must be held by the authority designated by the government, or by a private entity with whom the authority enters into an agreement. The state will own the goods held in the reserve and will be granted creditor protection through the agreement when purchasing goods from a private stockpile holder. Enhanced protection for the state against the creditors of private stockpile holders will apply, particularly during periods of heightened alert. This legal protection will apply to agreements entered into by the responsible authority both before and after the law comes into effect, but after January 1, 2026.

A confidentiality obligation will apply to individuals who participate in the operations of a private entity to maintain a strategic reserve on behalf of an authority, and who, through their participation, gain access to sensitive information.

The new law is proposed to come into effect on July 1, 2026.
