The Swedish National Audit Office notes that the government's initiatives can provide a good foundation for improving conditions for cycling through the development of knowledge, monitoring, and goal-setting. However, the Swedish National Audit Office believes that the state's efforts in the form of regulations, infrastructure, and other forms of governance have not sufficiently contributed to increased and safer cycling. The Swedish National Audit Office makes several recommendations, including to the government.

The government partially agrees with the Swedish National Audit Office's observations. The government believes that the principle of socio-economic profitability should guide the prioritization of measures in transport infrastructure, in conjunction with other aspects of the transport policy goals.
