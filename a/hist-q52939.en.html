Karl IX, previously known as Duke Charles, was born on October 4, 1550, in Stockholm, and died on October 30, 1611, in Nyköping. He reigned as King of Sweden from 1604 to 1611. He had served as Regent de facto from 1599 to 1604, after seizing power from his nephew Sigismund following the Battle of Stångebro. For a long time, he opposed his brother Johan III's attempts to steer the Church of Sweden in a Roman Catholic direction. Upon taking power, he suppressed the nobility and executed several of its leading figures during the Linköping Bloodbath. At the time of his death, Sweden was at war with three neighboring countries simultaneously: Denmark-Norway, Poland, and Russia.

Karl IX was the son of King Gustav Vasa and Queen Margaret, half-brother to Erik XIV, brother to Johan III and Duke Magnus, father to Gustav II Adolf, grandfather to Queen Christina, and great-grandfather to Charles X Gustav. He married Maria of Palatinate in 1579 and Kristina of Holstein-Gottorp in 1592.

== Duke Charles == Karl IX was born on October 4, 1550, at Tre Kronor Castle in Stockholm, as the youngest son of Gustav Vasa and Margaret Eriksdotter (Leijonhuvud). As a child, he was educated by his private tutor, Jean d'Herboville. Upon Gustav Vasa's death in 1560, Karl received a duchy encompassing most of Södermanland and Närke, several parishes in Västmanland, part of the Vadsbo district in Västergötland, and the entire province of Värmland. According to Gustav Vasa's will, the dukes Johan and Karl were to have a semi-independent status as vassal princes under their elder brother Erik. However, after Erik was crowned king, a Diet convened in Arboga in 1561 decided to curtail the independence of the duchies, enacting what became known as the Articles of Arboga. At the age of 15, Karl participated in the Nordic Seven Years' War, where he commanded the artillery during the capture of Varberg in 1565.

=== The Rebellion Against Erik ===

On July 4, 1568, Erik XIV married Karin Månsdotter, and the following day she was crowned queen. Their son Gustav, born in January of the same year, became the official heir to the throne. Duke Karl and his brother Johan were not present at the wedding or coronation. Instead, five days later, they departed from Eskilstuna royal estate, which belonged to Karl, along with some members of the nobility and three hundred of Karl's cavalrymen, heading towards Östergötland. There, they seized Vadstena Castle. Additional members of the nobility joined the rebellion against the dukes. Duke Karl then proceeded to Stegeborg, where the castle surrendered without a fight, and subsequently to Norrköping, which he also conquered. Meanwhile, Duke Johan declared himself Regent and urged the nobility and common people to rise up in a general rebellion against Erik. The brothers also negotiated a ceasefire in the war with Denmark and paid the foreign mercenaries who had not been paid by the king. Before the king could organize any resistance to the rebellion, the brothers had already captured more castles and towns, and at the end of July, Karl seized Nyköpingshus. In August, the rebel army continued north through Södermanland, and battles were fought with the king's troops. Erik won a battle against the dukes in Botkyrka parish, but the rebel army outflanked the king's army by crossing Lake Mälaren and then marching towards Stockholm from the north via Västerås and Uppsala. In mid-September, they halted on the plains north of Stockholm. Negotiations began with the besieged king, who was forced to hand over his secretary, Jöran Persson, among other things. At the end of September, a force from the dukes' army was allowed into the city, where the king finally surrendered to Duke Karl. Erik abdicated the crown and was imprisoned, where he eventually died. The crown passed to his brother Johan.

Johan III abolished the restrictions in the Arboga Articles, and Duke Karl, now nineteen years old, was able to take possession of his duchy. However, the parishes in Västmanland went to Gustav Vasa's widow, Katarina Stenbock, and instead, Karl received the remainder of Vadsbo and the entire Valle district following the declaration of legal incompetence of his older brother, Magnus.

During John's reign, disagreements arose between the king and his brother Charles in several areas. Duke Charles sought to strengthen the independence of his duchy from the king, and he pursued an economic policy within his duchy that was partly directed towards the inhabitants of the rest of the kingdom, which led to some disputes between the brothers.
