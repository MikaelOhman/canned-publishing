Full text

Jürgen von Farensbach, born in 1551 and died in 1602, was a military figure of German-Baltic origin. He was the father of Georg Wolmar von Fahrensbach.

After serving in various armies, including the Swedish army, Farensbach entered Danish service and was appointed governor of Ösel (Saaremaa) by Frederick II. He held this position for some time after entering Polish service in 1580, but eventually fully joined Poland. He followed Sigismund during his military campaigns in Sweden and participated in the Battle of Stångebro.
