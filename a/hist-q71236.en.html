Maria Eufrosyne of Palatinate, born February 14, 1625, at Stegeborg Castle in Östergötland, and died October 24, 1687, at Höjentorp in Västergötland, was Countess of Palatinate and Princess of Sweden. She was the daughter of John Casimir of Palatinate-Zweibrücken and Catherine of Sweden, sister to Charles X Gustav, and wife of Count Magnus Gabriel De la Gardie. Maria Eufrosyne acted as an intermediary for people seeking support or assistance from her husband, brother, and nephew.

== Biography ==

=== Childhood === Maria Eufrosyne was born and raised in Sweden along with Queen Christina, as her parents had left Germany in 1622, early in the Thirty Years' War, to seek refuge in her mother's homeland. She was initially raised at Stegeborg, her mother's estate, but later followed her mother to court when, after the king's death in 1632, the queen assumed responsibility for Queen Christina. She remained at court with Christina and her siblings even after their mother's death in 1638, while their father spent his time at Stegeborg. Thanks to Councilor Axel Banér, she received an annual pension of 3,000 silver coins (dir smt) and 500 riksdaler each New Year's Day.

Maria Eufrosyne received an unusually good education because she had been appointed as a study companion to her cousin, Queen Christina, who received an education reserved for men. However, her studies were interrupted: according to Anders Fryxell, this was partly because she was not considered equally gifted, but also because, as a monarch, Christina would receive a different education than what was generally considered appropriate for people of her gender. In 1643, she received a marriage proposal from Count Henry of Nassau. This proposal was supported by the Prince of Orange, the States General, his mother, Queen Christina, and the regency government. According to Christina, Henry was "a rich and handsome prince and also liked by Maria Eufrosyne" [source needed]. However, her father was hesitant because Henry was not considered wealthy enough, and he declined the offer in 1645, after Christina stated that she could arrange a marriage of equal status for Maria Eufrosyne in Sweden.

=== During Christina's Reign ===

On March 15, 1645, Maria Eufrosyne became engaged to Magnus Gabriel De la Gardie, Kristina's new favorite, and the wedding took place on March 7, 1647, in the royal chapel at Stockholm Castle, Tre Kronor. The marriage was arranged by Queen Christina. According to Maria Eufrosyne herself, this was a way for Kristina to show favor to both of them: Magnus had reportedly fallen in love with her when he was eighteen years old, and they supposedly mutually desired to marry each other.

The wedding was celebrated with great festivities, including ring ceremonies, tournaments, and parades. Kristina personally gave Maria Eufrosyne to Magnus, saying that she was giving him the dearest thing she possessed. According to tradition, this was a love triangle, where both Kristina and Maria Eufrosyne were in love with Magnus and wanted to marry him: Kristina felt compelled to give up for political reasons and therefore gave Magnus to Maria Eufrosyne with the words: "I bestow upon you one whom I myself cannot possess." How much of this is true remains unknown. A play has been written about this alleged love triangle. As a wedding gift, Kristina gave her Höjentorp in Västergötland, her grandmother Maria of Palatinate's favorite estate, as well as a personal allowance of 3,000 daler silver (daler), and every New Year during Kristina's reign, jewelry worth between 2,000 and 3,000 daler. The marriage is not described as happy. However, the couple reportedly exchanged letters during times when they were separated, in which she at least seemed to express feelings that suggest love.

Maria Eufrosyne acted as an intermediary between Kristina and her brother, Charles Gustav, doing everything she could to try to persuade them to enter into the planned marriage. In 1653, Magnus fell out of favor with Kristina, and the couple was forced to leave court. She then acted as a mediator between him and Kristina. The following year, the couple returned to court at the accession of Maria Eufrosyne's brother.

=== During the Reigns of Charles X and Charles XI === Maria Eufrosyne had a good relationship with her brother, Charles Gustav. At Charles X Gustav's coronation in 1654, Maria Eufrosyne was granted the title and status of Princess of Sweden. This was questioned by some members of the nobility. Through her marriage, Maria Eufrosyne held the right to the title Countess de la Gardie, but she was generally referred to as "Princess" rather than "Countess."

Maria Eufrosyne visited Magnus in the field in both Germany and, in 1656, in Riga in Swedish Livonia. However, just two weeks later, she was forced to flee after Riga was set on fire by Russian troops. In September 1658, she traveled with her sister-in-law, Hedvig Eleonora, to Kronborg Castle in Denmark, which had recently been captured by Admiral Wrangel. At Kronborg, they were received by the foreign envoys who accompanied the Swedish army and also received visits from the king. After the Treaty of Roskilde in 1658, she visited her brother in Gothenburg. He informed her that he wanted to appoint her husband as Chancellor of the Realm, but she persuaded him not to do so. She was present at Charles Gustav's deathbed in Gothenburg in 1660. At that time, he promised her "beautiful estates" in the Danish provinces and pensions for her.
