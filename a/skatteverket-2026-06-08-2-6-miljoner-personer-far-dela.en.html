In plain language

In June, the Swedish Tax Agency will distribute over 35 billion kronor to 2.6 million individuals who are eligible for a tax refund. This means these people will receive money back that they paid in excess to the government. However, if you have not registered a bank account with the Tax Agency, your payment may take slightly longer.

At the same time, approximately 700,000 people will receive notification of an outstanding tax balance, meaning they need to pay more money to the government. These individuals have roughly 90 days to make the payment indicated in their notice.

If you are due a small amount, less than 100 kronor, this money will not be paid out immediately but will remain on your tax account until next year. If you have outstanding debts with the Swedish Enforcement Agency (Kronofogdemyndigheten), these funds will instead be used to pay off those debts.

You can view your information and receive notification of your final tax assessment on the Tax Agency's website starting June 12th. If you do not receive your notification when you expect it, it may be because the agency needs to carefully verify your information.

2026-06-08

Tax refunds are being processed. This year, 2.6 million people will share over 35.7 billion Swedish krona. At the same time, 700,000 people will receive notification of additional taxes owed, totaling 16.3 billion Swedish krona.

From June 9th to 12th, tax refunds will be paid out to those who made changes or additions to their tax return and to those who filed their return digitally or on paper by May 4th and did not receive a payment in April. In total, 2.6 million people will share 35.7 billion Swedish krona.

More than 700,000 people will receive notification of additional taxes owed, totaling 16.3 billion Swedish krona. If you receive a notice of additional taxes owed, you must pay within approximately 90 days. You can find the final payment date on your tax assessment notice.

If you have a digital mailbox, you will receive your tax assessment there. If you do not have a digital mailbox, you will receive it on paper. You can also view your final tax assessment on "My Pages" at skatteverket.se starting June 12th.

However, more than 54,000 people who are due to receive a tax refund in June will not receive an automatic payment because they have not registered a bank account for the payout.

"Those who have not yet registered a bank account with the Swedish Tax Agency can still do so," says Johan Schauman, tax expert at the Swedish Tax Agency. "In that case, the money will be deposited into their account a little later. The Swedish Tax Agency makes payments throughout June."

Tax refunds of less than 100 Swedish krona will not be paid out but will remain in the taxpayer's account until the following year. If you have debts with the National Enforcement Agency (Kronofogdemyndigheten), the refund will instead be used to pay those debts.

"Those who were granted an extension and filed their tax return after May 4th will receive their tax assessment and any refund in early August, or in some exceptional cases, in December," says Johan Schauman.

If you don't receive your tax refund or your final tax assessment when you expect it, it may be because the Swedish Tax Agency needs to verify information in your tax return.
