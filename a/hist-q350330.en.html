Full text

Erik Magnusson, sometimes incorrectly referred to as Eric "XII," was King of Sweden and Lord of Scania from 1356 to 1359. He was born in 1339 and died in 1359. He was the son of King Magnus Eriksson and Queen Blanche of Namur.

In 1344, he was elected as heir apparent to the Swedish throne at the young age of five, after his younger brother, Håkan Magnusson, had been chosen as King of Norway in 1343. In the mid-1350s, Erik entered into contact with Swedish noble factions opposed to his father and emerged as a visible leader of their rebellion in 1356. The actual leader was likely Nils Turesson (Bielke). Dissatisfaction had grown due to the favor that King Magnus Eriksson showed to Duke Bengt Algotsson, who had been appointed Duke of Finland. This dissatisfaction with the king's policies erupted in a rebellion initiated by Erik along with several powerful figures in the realm in 1356. As a result, Duke Bengt was forced to flee the country, and King Magnus Eriksson (in 1357) was compelled to cede a significant portion of the kingdom to his son.

Erik adopted the title of king that same year and, during negotiations in Jönköping in 1357, forced his father to relinquish Scania, southern Halland, Blekinge, and parts of Småland, Östergötland, and Finland to Erik. Through a new agreement later that year, he also gained control of Svealand and Norrland. The now-powerless father sought support from Valdemar Atterdag against his son, and in 1359, Valdemar invaded Scania. Erik then entered Scania, but Valdemar had already withdrawn from the country.

Shortly thereafter, Erik died suddenly, likely from a plague. Magnus Eriksson's opponents spread rumors that Erik and his wife, Beatrix of Bavaria, had been poisoned by Erik's mother, Blanche of Namur, which was almost certainly false.
