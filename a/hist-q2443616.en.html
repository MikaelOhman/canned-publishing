Sigrid Eriksdotter Vasa, born on October 15, 1566, at Svartsjö Castle, and died on April 24, 1633, at Liuksiala royal estate, was a Swedish princess. According to "Äldre svenska frälsesläkter" (1:3, page 290), which refers to King Erik XIV's diary entries, she was the daughter of King Erik XIV and Karin Månsdotter. Sigrid Eriksdotter Vasa became the lady of Sjundby Castle in her first marriage and is buried in Turku Cathedral.

== Biography == Sigrid was born before her parents' wedding, but from the beginning, she was treated as a royal child and was considered legitimate when King Erik married her mother two years later. She was cared for by the French noblewoman Johanna (Jeanne) de Herboville, who was married to the aristocratic French refugee, the Huguenot Jean d'Herboville. At her parents' wedding and her mother's coronation, she stood beside them under a canopy with her brother, as a way to demonstrate her new legitimacy and status. When her parents were imprisoned, she was allowed to live outside their supervised quarters and was periodically cared for by Herboville and Katarina Stenbock. She was taken with her mother to Turku Castle in 1573, and they were both released in 1577.

=== Hovdam === Karin Månsdotter is believed to have been a caring mother, but Sigrid's upbringing in Finland seems to have focused primarily on practical skills. It is unclear whether Johan III considered her a princess or a noblewoman, but it is likely that he saw her as something in between. In 1582, she followed her mother to the court in Stockholm. It is uncertain whether Sigrid returned with her mother to Finland or remained at the court, but in 1586, she is confirmed to have been a lady-in-waiting to her cousin, Princess Anna.

In 1587, Sigrid was granted feudal rights and inheritance rights to her mother's royal estate in Finland, which provided her with an independent income. It is unclear whether this grant of feudal rights meant that ownership of the property passed to Sigrid instead of Karin. The same year, she accompanied her cousin Anna to Poland. There, she reportedly met her brother Gustav, or at least received information about him, which she then relayed to her mother. There are no other records indicating that the siblings ever met again after this.

=== Marriage === It is unclear how long Sigrid served as a lady-in-waiting for Anna, but she likely moved to Finland in 1596. She married Henrik Klasson Tott in 1597. At the time of her marriage in 1597, she received Anna's permission, as she was considered under Anna's authority in her role as a lady-in-waiting. In 1599, Sigrid was forced to accompany her husband into exile in Riga for political reasons, as he was viewed unfavorably by Duke Charles, who was a supporter of Sigismund. They were unable to return due to her husband's health. Only after his death in 1603 could she return to Finland. She then lived alternately on her husband's and mother's estates, and it is confirmed that she and her mother visited each other.

She married Nils Nilsson (Natt och Dag) (1554–1613) on September 10, 1609, at Tre Kronor Castle in Stockholm. After her second marriage in 1609, Sigrid lived at court, where her husband held a position. It is documented that she was involved in several disputes with Charles IX on several occasions, as he failed to fulfill financial promises he had made to her, and that she was sometimes called "bastard," but she appears to have been a fully accepted member of the court. She was the mother of Åke Henriksson Tott (1598–1640), Erik Henriksson Tott (died in 1621), and two other children who died young.
