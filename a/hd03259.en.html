TL;DRThe government has established long-term plans for how roads and railways in Sweden will function between the years 2026 and 2037. The plan focuses on investing wisely in transportation and making the system more environmentally friendly.
In plain language

The government has now presented a plan for how Sweden's transportation system will look and function during the period 2026 to 2037. The plan covers all types of transportation, such as cars, trucks, and trains. At the same time, decisions have been made regarding the amount of funding that will be allocated to different regions to improve roads and railways throughout the country.

The work also focuses on making transportation more efficient and environmentally friendly. The government will ensure that it becomes easier to transport goods and people in a smart way. The goal is also to improve vehicles and to increase the use of electricity instead of fuels that harm the environment.

An important part of the plan is to transition from fossil fuels, such as gasoline and diesel, to renewable and sustainable alternatives. This is a response to previous demands from the Parliament that Sweden must create a transportation system that functions in the long term without damaging the climate. The goal is for technology to help us reduce emissions in the future.

In the document, the government outlines the planning process and its decision regarding the establishment of a national, cross-modal plan for transport infrastructure for the period 2026-2037, as well as the final financial framework for cross-modal regional plans for transport infrastructure for the same period.
