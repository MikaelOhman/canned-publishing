Security detention – a new, indefinite form of deprivation of liberty.

Source Riksdagen Read the original at Riksdagen →2026-01-22

The government's proposal suggests the introduction of a new, indefinite form of deprivation of liberty, to be called "security detention." This measure should be applicable to individuals who have committed serious crimes involving attacks on another person's life, health, freedom, or peace, and who pose a high risk of reoffending. The proposals include the following:

  • A new Chapter 33 will be introduced into the Penal Code, titled "On Security Detention." This chapter will regulate when the measure can be imposed and the timeframes that apply to it.
  • When the court determines the sentence to be security detention, it must specify the minimum duration of the deprivation of liberty and the total duration of the sentence's execution.
  • The maximum duration may be extended by up to three years at a time if continued deprivation of liberty is absolutely necessary to prevent the convicted person from reoffending in serious crimes.
  • The sentence will be served in a correctional facility, and a new implementation law will be introduced.

The proposed amendments to the law are scheduled to come into effect on April 15, 2026.
