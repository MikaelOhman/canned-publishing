Supplementary provisions to EU regulations on chemicals and waste.

TL;DRSweden intends to implement stricter regulations regarding chemicals and waste in order to comply with new EU laws. It may become punishable to fail to properly label hazardous substances when they are transferred into containers.
In plain language

The government has proposed amendments to the Environmental Code, the law that governs how we protect nature. The proposal aims to implement new regulations from the EU concerning chemicals, packaging, and the transport of waste.

A key proposal is that failure to comply with the rules for labeling hazardous substances should be considered a criminal offense. For example, when filling chemicals into containers at a station, there must be clear information about the contents and that the packaging is safe. Failure to do so may result in penalties.

It is also proposed that authorities should have the right to take possession of or retain waste if necessary. This is to ensure that the rules for how waste can be transported between countries are followed. The authorities should be able to intervene if there is a risk that the rules are being violated.

Furthermore, the government or another authority should have the power to determine whether there are exceptions to certain packaging regulations. This could also involve allowing entities other than those who manufacture the packaging to charge a deposit fee when goods are sold. The new regulations are intended to come into effect on January 2, 2027.

Full text

The proposal includes changes aimed at supplementing the EU Regulation on the classification, labeling, and packaging of substances and mixtures (CLP Regulation), the new EU Regulation on waste transport, and the new EU Regulation on packaging.

It is proposed that violations of new requirements in the CLP Regulation should be subject to penalties. It should be punishable to supply hazardous substances and mixtures through a filling station if the substance or mixture is not authorized to be supplied through a filling station, or if the filled containers do not meet the labeling and packaging requirements. It should also be punishable to violate regulations governing the provision of labeling information other than by labeling substances and mixtures.

Furthermore, it is proposed that a supervisory authority should be given the power to seize or detain waste if necessary, due to prohibitions in the new EU Regulation on waste transport or directives issued in connection with the regulation.

It is also proposed that the government or the authority designated by the government should be authorized to issue regulations regarding exemptions from obligations in the new EU Regulation on packaging, concerning measures to prevent waste, and regarding the collection of a deposit by actors other than producers at the point of sale.

The proposed amendments to the law are scheduled to come into force on January 2, 2027.
