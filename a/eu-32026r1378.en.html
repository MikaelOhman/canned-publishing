TL;DRThe EU has approved new regulations regarding the use of seeds from the Jatropha curcas plant as food. These seeds can now also be sold as flour and paste, and the amount that can be used in food products has been increased.
In plain language

The European Commission has decided to change the regulations regarding the use of seeds from the Jatropha curcas plant (an edible variety) as food. Previously, there were limited regulations, but now new forms of the plant are also approved, namely flour and pasta made entirely from ground seeds.

The regulations are also being changed to allow for a greater amount of these seeds in finished food products. Previously, only 5 grams could be used per 100 grams, but this limit is now being raised to 30 grams per 100 grams. The requirements for the amount of fat the product can contain have also been adjusted slightly to accommodate the new forms.

The company Chuta Nut GmbH has received this approval. Because the company possesses crucial information about how the product is manufactured and its impact on health, they are granted special protection. This means that only Chuta Nut GmbH is allowed to sell these new forms of seeds on the European market for the next five years.

The European Food Safety Authority (EFSA) has reviewed the changes. They have concluded that it is completely safe to use the seeds as flour and pasta, and to use larger quantities in food products, according to the new regulations.

COMMISSION IMPLEMENTING REGULATION (EU) 2026/1378

amending Implementing Regulation (EU) 2017/2470 as regards the conditions of use and the specifications for the novel food "kernels from Jatropha curcas L. (edible variety)".

THE EUROPEAN COMMISSION HAS ADOPTED THIS REGULATION

having regard to the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union,

having regard to Regulation (EU) 2015/2283 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 25 November 2015 on novel foods and amending Regulation (EU) No 1169/2011 of the European Parliament and of the Council and Council Regulation (EC) No 258/97 and Commission Regulation (EC) No 1852/2001 ( 1 ), in particular Article 12.1, and

whereas, pursuant to Regulation (EU) 2015/2283, only novel foods which have been authorized and included in the Union list of novel foods may be placed on the market in the Union;

whereas, in accordance with Article 8 of Regulation (EU) 2015/2283, a Union list of novel foods has been established by Commission Implementing Regulation (EU) 2017/2470 ( 2 );

whereas the Union list in Annex I to Implementing Regulation (EU) 2017/2470 includes "kernels from Jatropha curcas L. (edible variety)" as a novel food;

whereas Commission Implementing Regulation (EU) 2022/965 ( 3 ) authorized the placing on the market of "kernels from Jatropha curcas L. (edible variety)" as a novel food, including intellectual property rights and exclusive rights on the market, for the company JatroSolutions GmbH, which was acquired in October 2022 by the company Chuta Nut GmbH.

On May 9, 2023, the company Chuta Nut GmbH (the applicant) applied to the Commission, in accordance with Article 10.1 of Regulation (EU) 2015/2283, for a change in the conditions of use and specifications for the new food product, kernels from *Jatropha curcas L.* (the edible variety). The applicant requested the introduction of two additional forms of the new food product: kernels in the form of flour and kernels in the form of paste, both consisting of 100% ground kernels. The applicant also requested an increase in the maximum permitted level of the new food product in the previously approved food categories, from 5 g/100 g to 30 g/100 g. Furthermore, the applicant requested a change in the specifications to increase the fat content in the final new food product from 54–61% to 54–64%.

On May 9, 2023, the applicant also applied to the Commission for protection of legally protected data regarding the production process (4), information on the composition (5), nutritional information (6), and toxicological information (7).

On January 19, 2024, the Commission requested that the European Food Safety Authority (EFSA) provide a scientific opinion on the proposed change in the conditions of use and specifications for the new food product, kernels from *Jatropha curcas L.* (the edible variety).

On September 29, 2025, EFSA adopted its scientific opinion, "Safety of changes in the conditions of use and the specifications of edible *Jatropha curcas L.* (Chuta) as a novel food pursuant to Regulation (EU) 2015/2283" (8), in accordance with Article 11 of Regulation (EU) 2015/2283.

In its scientific opinion, EFSA concluded that the new food product, kernels, flour, and paste from *Jatropha curcas L.* (the edible variety), is safe under the proposed new conditions of use and specifications.

In its scientific opinion, the food safety authority also stated that its conclusion regarding the safety of the new food was based on the proprietary data concerning the production process, information about the composition, nutritional information, and toxicological information. Without this data, the food safety authority would not have been able to assess the new food and reach its conclusion.

The Commission requested that the applicant provide further clarification on the justification for its claim of ownership of this data and studies, and to clarify its claim of exclusive rights to use them, in accordance with Article 26.2(b) of Regulation (EU) 2015/2283.

The applicant stated that, at the time of the application, the applicant owned and had exclusive rights to the relevant studies, and that third parties could not legally access, use, or refer to this data.

The Commission assessed all the information provided by the applicant and considers that the applicant has provided sufficient evidence that the conditions of Article 26.2 of Regulation (EU) 2015/2283 are met. Therefore, the data concerning the production process, information about the composition, nutritional information, and toxicological information should be protected in accordance with Article 27.1 of Regulation (EU) 2015/2283. Consequently, only the applicant should be authorized to market seeds of *Jatropha curcas L.* (the edible variety) with the extended conditions of use in the Union for a period of five years from the date this regulation enters into force.

However, the fact that the approval applies only to the applicant, and that only the applicant is allowed to refer to the information included in the application, does not prevent subsequent applicants from applying for approval to market the same new food, provided that their application is based on legally obtained data to support such approval.

The previously approved food category "kernels as such, candied or sugar-preserved, and processed nuts" should be reviewed to ensure clarity regarding which foods fall under this category. Therefore, this food category should be defined as "kernels as such, or processed whole kernels intended to be consumed as snacks (e.g., roasted, candied, salted, or similar), including mixtures with other nuts, seeds, and/or dried fruit."

The annex to Implementing Regulation (EU) 2017/2470 should therefore be amended accordingly.

The measures prescribed in this regulation are consistent with the opinion of the Standing Committee on Plants, Animals, Food and Feed.

The annex to Implementing Regulation (EU) 2017/2470 shall be amended in accordance with the annex to this regulation.

Only the company Chuta Nut GmbH (9) may place the new food, kernels from Jatropha curcas L. (the edible variety), with the expanded conditions of use, on the market in the Union, for a period of five years from July 14, 2026, unless a later applicant obtains approval for the new food without reference to the scientific data protected in accordance with Article 3 or with the consent of the company Chuta Nut GmbH.

The scientific data included in the application and which meet the conditions of Article 26.2 of Regulation (EU) 2015/2283 may not, without the consent of the company Chuta Nut GmbH, be used to the benefit of a later applicant for a period of five years from the date on which this regulation enters into force.

This regulation shall enter into force on the twentieth day after its publication in the Official Journal of the European Union.

This regulation is binding in its entirety and directly applicable in all Member States.
