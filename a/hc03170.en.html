TL;DRThe government wants to regulate the individuals who manage companies that perform vehicle inspections. The aim is to ensure that those in positions of responsibility are suitable for their roles.
In plain language

The government has proposed a change to the law regarding vehicle inspections. The proposal stipulates that all members of the board of directors, and their deputies, of companies that perform vehicle inspections must undergo a suitability assessment. This assessment is designed to ensure that these individuals possess the necessary qualities for their roles.

This applies to companies that are either limited liability companies or economic associations. An economic association is a type of organization where the members collectively own the company. This control is important to ensure that vehicle inspections are carried out safely and accurately.

If the proposal is approved by the parliament, the new regulations will come into effect on January 1, 2026. This means that all companies affected must have the right people in their board of directors, according to the new requirements, before the start of the new year.

Full text

The proposal suggests changes to the Vehicle Act (2002:574). These changes involve implementing a suitability assessment for all board members and deputy board members in inspection organizations that are limited companies or economic associations.

The proposed changes are scheduled to come into effect on January 1, 2026.
