In this document, the government presents its assessment of the observations and recommendations made by the Swedish National Audit Office (Riksrevisionen) in its audit report "Police Reform 2015 – Intentions Not Yet Achieved" (RiR 2025:26). The National Audit Office has reviewed the work of the Police Authority to achieve the objectives of the 2015 police reform. The National Audit Office's overall conclusion is that the Police Authority has not worked effectively enough to achieve these objectives, and that the authority has not achieved the reform's goals. According to the National Audit Office, this is because the Police Authority still needs to strengthen its local operations, has difficulty improving its performance, and that internal governance and monitoring have not contributed sufficiently to achieving the objectives.

Based on its observations, the National Audit Office makes a recommendation to the government and a number of recommendations to the Police Authority. The recommendation to the government concerns being attentive to the effects of how the Police Authority implements the government's priorities and directives. In its governance, the government should also avoid setting too many targets and focus on outcomes.

The government shares the National Audit Office's assessment that it is important to be attentive to the effects of how the Police Authority implements the government's priorities and directives. Regarding targets, the government notes that such targets do not constitute a prominent part of the government's governance. The government does not believe that there are grounds for taking any further action as a result of the National Audit Office's audit report. With this document, the government considers the National Audit Office's report to be finalized.
