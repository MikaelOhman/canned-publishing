In plain language

Incorrect registration of residence – where individuals are registered at the wrong address – is often used to fraudulently obtain benefits and facilitate other criminal activities. The government wants to provide the Swedish Tax Agency (Skatteverket) with better tools to combat this.

A new crime is being introduced: promoting false registration of residence. It will become punishable to knowingly and significantly contribute to a false statement being used as the basis for a registration decision, if the purpose is personal gain or to enable other criminal activity.

The Swedish Tax Agency will also be given expanded opportunities to process biometric data, such as fingerprints and facial images, in the registration system.

Full text

The government proposes the introduction of a new crime: facilitating false registration in the population register. Criminal liability will apply when someone, through an intentional act, significantly promotes the inclusion of inaccurate information that could be used as the basis for decisions regarding population registration. It will also be required that the person committed the act with the intention of benefiting themselves or someone else, or with the intention of enabling other crimes other than those related to population registration. The area of minor offenses that are not punishable will also be expanded.

The government also proposes expanding the possibilities for the Swedish Tax Agency (Skatteverket) to process biometric data in population registration activities. The Swedish Tax Agency will register fingerprints and facial images, as well as the biometric data derived from them, for a person registered in the population register and for a person assigned a personal identification number. The Swedish Tax Agency and the Swedish Migration Agency will be authorized to compare fingerprints and facial images with corresponding data held in the other agency's registers. The police will be authorized to compare facial images and fingerprints with data registered with the Swedish Tax Agency in the course of criminal investigations. The government also proposes that passport authorities, in cases involving personal identification numbers, will be authorized to compare fingerprints and facial images with the data held by the Swedish Tax Agency.

The proposed legislative changes are scheduled to come into effect on December 1, 2026.
