In 2022, The Ahmadi religion of peace and light sold the Bergslagsgården estate in Sävsjön, near Hällefors in the county of Örebro. Bergslagsgården, located in western Västmanland, has served as a conference center, bed and breakfast, and asylum accommodation.

The estate was purchased by a man at the end of 2018. The man was a member of The Ahmadi religion of peace and light, the same religion he later sold the estate to.

But, like many religions that border on cults, tensions arose between the local residents and the newly arrived practitioners.

When Bulletin contacted some of the residents of Sävsjön, they said that the practitioners at the estate were secretive. They also reportedly behaved aggressively and confrontational towards anyone who approached the estate. One of the residents said that the group dug mysterious holes in the garden.

"These were very secretive people. Damn. They were secretive devils," said one of the residents to Bulletin.

Were they all from the Middle East?

"There were probably people of different nationalities. They had a lot of money."

"This is our land"

One person who lived in Sävsjön saw, one summer evening, that members of the newly arrived religion were out on the lake in inflatable boats. He then decided to take his own raft out to Bergslagsgården to introduce himself.

– When I came to the dock, a whole army appeared up there, walking arm in arm. I went up and introduced myself. But when I raised my hand to greet them, I was hit in the chest, and I dropped my glasses, said a resident of Sävsjön to Bulletin.

The members were shouting over each other in various languages. The man said that he asked if they could speak Swedish, which resulted in them calling him a racist.

– Then I tried to explain to him that I had dropped my glasses. He just said, "This is our land."

The group's spokesperson, Jasmine Shareef, claimed in an interview with Expressen that the man trespassed on private property and used violence against the members.

– I went and looked at them on YouTube. It was quite impressive. If you have a somewhat fragile psyche, you might easily be drawn to that, said the man.

An Unusual Form of Islam

The Ahmadi religion of peace and light was founded by Abdullah Hashem, an Egyptian-American Sunni Muslim with a Christian mother. Hashem's religious movement is described as originating from Shia Islam.

According to his own beliefs, Hashem is a successor to Jesus, Saint Peter, Muhammad, the Mahdi, and Ahmed al-Hasan. He also considers himself the only and true pope.

However, the Administrative Court in Karlstad did not approve of the prophet. In 2024, they deported him along with his family.

The religion stands out from mainstream Islam. For example, the religion believes in reincarnation. The Kaaba is believed to be located in Petra, Jordan, instead of Mecca. Friday prayers are not considered very important; they accept alcohol in moderation, and LGBTQ+ people are tolerated and accepted.

In total, the religion has 7,000 followers spread across 40 countries, according to The Independent.

New Resistance in Great Britain

At the same time that the sect sold Bergslagsgården for 2.5 million Swedish krona, they moved to a former children's home in Crewe, near Manchester, in Great Britain. In April, the police raided the group's new headquarters.

Several individuals from the sect were arrested, and ten members are currently on trial for charges including human trafficking, forced marriage, rape, sexual harassment, and slavery. In May, the members were released pending further investigation.

Just over a week ago, between 30 and 40 people from Crewe marched towards the sect's new residence. Outside, they reportedly shook the gates of the former orphanage and threw stones at the house, according to Manchester Evening News.

"After more than a month of hostile protests, including several reported assault incidents, attempted arson, trespassing, vandalism, racist attacks, and hate propaganda, the events of last night marked a serious escalation," writes The Ahmadi religion of peace and light on its website.
