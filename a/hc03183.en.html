Intelligent Transport Systems – Implementation of the Amendment Directive.

TL;DRSweden is implementing an EU directive concerning so-called intelligent transport systems for road traffic. A wider range of traffic data and digital services will be made available in a machine-readable format through a national access point, enabling their use throughout the EU.
In plain language

Intelligent transport systems are digital services and data used to make road traffic smarter and safer, such as real-time information about traffic and roads.

The government proposes changes to Swedish law to implement an EU directive. These changes expand the types of data and services that are covered.

Data must be made available in a machine-readable format through a national access point and cover certain geographical areas at specific times, so that the services can function throughout the EU.

Full text

The proposal suggests changes to the Act (2013:315) on Intelligent Transport Systems for road transport. These changes are part of the implementation of EU Directive (EU) 2023/2661 on intelligent transport systems in the road transport sector.

The changes in the directive expand its scope to include more data types and services for intelligent transport systems. These data types should be made available through a national access point in machine-readable formats and have a certain geographical coverage by specific dates. The services should also be provided with a certain geographical coverage by a specified date. The main new elements are in two new annexes to the directive (Annex III and IV). The annexes and the directive are very comprehensive and contain detailed information. According to the proposals, both the amending directive and the original directive will in the future be implemented through regulations at a lower level than the law. The law, which is being revised, proposes authorizations that make such implementation possible.

The amending directive must be implemented by December 21, 2025. The changes to the law are proposed to take effect on December 15, 2025.
