The government's proposal outlines a new firearms law that simplifies the regulations for issuing permits and possessing firearms for legal gun owners, such as hunters and sports shooters. The new law also aims to combat the prevalence of illegal firearms and the criminal use of weapons. The proposed law includes the following:

  • The requirements for possessing firearms, for both individuals and organizations, are clarified.
  • Easements are introduced for legal gun owners, such as more flexible storage regulations and the implementation of the EU's simplified import procedure for sports shooters and hunters.
  • The five-year permits for fully automatic weapons and single-hand firearms for multiple shots are abolished and replaced with a supervisory procedure.
  • A ban is introduced on issuing new permits for certain types of semi-automatic rifles for hunting.
  • A clearer distinction is made between crimes typically committed by illegal gun owners and other types of violations of the firearms law.

The new law and its associated amendments are proposed to come into effect on June 1, 2026.
