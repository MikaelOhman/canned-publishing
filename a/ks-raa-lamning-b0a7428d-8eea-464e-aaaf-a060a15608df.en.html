TL;DRAn ancient runestone has been discovered in Västergötland and is being described. It was erected by two individuals who are commemorating a man named Gunnar.
In plain language

The runestone is located near Larv in the province of Västergötland. It's a large granite stone with an inscription written in runes, which is an ancient alphabet. The stone also features a large cross with decorative details.

The text on the stone tells us that the people Ölver and Åskatla erected the stone to honor Gunnar. He is described as a good young man, and the text concludes with a prayer asking God to care for his soul.

Because it's difficult to know exactly when the stone was erected, experts in historical monuments (ancient traces in the landscape) use various methods to estimate its age. Today, the runes on the stone are painted to make them more visible.

Full text

Runestone (Vg 127), made of granite, is 2.5 meters high, 1.5-1.8 meters wide (northwest to southeast), and 0.5-0.4 meters thick. On the northeast side, there's a runic spiral located 0.1-0.3 meters from the edge, a large ring cross with palm-like figures between its arms, and some runes outside the spiral in the lower northwest corner, as well as inside the spiral in the lower southeast corner. The height of the runes is approximately 0.15 meters. The runes are relatively shallowly carved. According to the "Runestones of Västergötland" inscription: "Ölver(?) and Åskatla raised this stone after Gunnar, Sigtrygg's son, a good young man. May the Lord God keep his soul." It was photographed in 1968 and 1987.

Location: Sweden, Västra Götaland, Vara, Västergötland, Larv.

Runestone identification number: Vg 127.
