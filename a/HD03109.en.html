The government has proposed a measure to reduce the tax on attending dance events and similar performances. The tax on these tickets is called Value Added Tax (VAT), which is the same as sales tax. Currently, the tax is 25 percent, but the proposal suggests reducing it to 6 percent.

If the parliament approves the proposal, the new regulations will come into effect on July 1, 2026. This means that it will be cheaper for organizers to host events and for people to purchase tickets.

The proposal also includes changes to other laws, such as the law on taxes on energy. The purpose of the proposal is to modify the rules regarding the amount of tax that is paid for admission to various dance events.

The proposal suggests that the value-added tax (VAT) rate on admission to dance events be reduced from 25 percent to 6 percent. This proposal entails changes to the Value Added Tax Act (2023:200).

The proposal is scheduled to come into effect on July 1, 2026.
