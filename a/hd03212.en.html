In plain language

Many households struggle to obtain their own rental agreements, for example, because the landlord is hesitant about their financial situation. A rental guarantee means that the municipality acts as a guarantor to ensure that the rent is paid.

The government proposes that it should be able to decide on a requirement for municipalities to offer such rental guarantees to individual households.

The purpose is to enable more people to access housing. At the same time, confidentiality measures are being introduced to protect information about individuals' personal circumstances in cases involving rental guarantees.

Full text

The government proposes amendments to the Act (2000:1383) on the responsibility of municipalities for housing provision, as well as the Public Access and Secrecy Act (2009:400).

These amendments would introduce a provision authorizing the government to issue regulations requiring municipalities to assist individuals by providing guarantees to ensure that rental agreements for apartments are fulfilled (rental guarantee), and a provision regarding the confidentiality of individuals' personal circumstances in cases involving municipal rental guarantees.

The purpose of these amendments is to facilitate access to housing for individuals who lack their own housing, and to ensure adequate protection for information about individuals' personal circumstances when municipalities handle cases involving rental guarantees.

The proposed amendments are scheduled to come into effect on July 1, 2026.
