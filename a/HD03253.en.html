EU Banking Package

Source Riksdagen Read the original at Riksdagen →2026-04-23

In the EU's capital adequacy directive, requirements are placed on the activities of banks and credit market companies (credit institutions), very large securities firms, and certain holding companies.

The proposal includes draft amendments to the laws necessary to implement the changes made to the capital adequacy directive within the framework of the so-called banking package. The proposals include the following:

  • A licensing requirement is introduced for companies from countries outside the European Economic Area that wish to provide central bank-related services from a branch in Sweden.
  • The suitability requirements that apply to the management of credit institutions will apply to very large securities firms and certain holding companies.
  • A requirement is introduced for certain companies to pre-notify information about new management personnel.
  • A requirement is introduced for companies to assess the suitability of certain individuals in leading positions.
  • Application procedures are introduced for certain transactions.
  • A waiting period is introduced for certain employees and contractors at the Swedish Financial Supervisory Authority.

The amendments to the law are proposed to enter into force on July 1, 2026, except for the amendments regarding a special licensing requirement and special supervisory provisions for companies from countries outside the European Economic Area that wish to provide central bank-related services from a branch in Sweden, which will enter into force on January 11, 2027.
