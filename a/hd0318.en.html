Improved conditions for Sweden within NATO – a supplementary confidentiality provision for NATO information.

TL;DRSweden intends to amend the laws to better protect classified information in its cooperation with NATO. This is being done to ensure that we can securely share important information with other countries.
In plain language

Sweden became a member of NATO on March 7, 2024. For the cooperation to function, Sweden must be able to share information with the other countries in the alliance without secrets being leaked. It is essential that we handle all information in a correct and secure manner.

The government is now proposing new rules in the so-called "Freedom of Information and Confidentiality Act." This means that information originating from other countries within NATO will be protected by confidentiality. Confidentiality means that the information is secret and must not be shared with anyone. The same protection should apply to information that Swedish authorities send to other NATO countries.

The purpose of the new rules is to ensure that Sweden can continue to participate in the cooperation without risking the disclosure of information. If information is leaked, it could become more difficult for Sweden to be part of NATO's work. Therefore, clear laws are needed that specify what is classified.

The new laws are intended to come into effect on January 1, 2026. The Swedish Parliament must vote on the proposal and decide whether to approve the change in the law.

Full text

Sweden has been a member of NATO since March 7, 2024. This collaboration requires extensive information sharing between NATO and its member states, which in turn relies on the ability to exchange information securely and confidentially. NATO membership therefore places several requirements on Sweden's ability to handle the alliance's information correctly. The proposal includes certain legislative changes aimed at ensuring that Sweden can meet NATO's requirements for information management.

The proposals include, among other things, the introduction of a supplementary confidentiality provision in Chapter 15 of the Public Access and Secrecy Act. This provision would mean that confidentiality applies to information provided by a foreign entity within the framework of NATO cooperation, if it can be assumed that Sweden's ability to participate in the cooperation would be impaired if the information is disclosed, and that the same confidentiality should apply to information that an authority has provided to a foreign entity within the framework of NATO cooperation.

In addition, certain consequential amendments and changes to the right to disclose and publish information are proposed, in light of the proposed confidentiality provision.

The legislative changes are proposed to come into force on January 1, 2026.

Proposed Parliamentary Decision

The Government's proposal: The Parliament adopts the Government's proposal for an amendment to the Public Access and Secrecy Act (2009:400).
