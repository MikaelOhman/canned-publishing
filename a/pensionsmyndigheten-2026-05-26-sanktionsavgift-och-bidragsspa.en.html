May 26, 2026 | News

Two new tools are being introduced to strengthen the protection of social welfare systems: a benefit suspension and a sanction fee. The purpose is to prevent incorrect payments and benefit fraud.

The Parliament decided on May 26, 2026, to introduce a sanction fee and a benefit suspension in social security. The new regulations will come into effect on July 1, 2026, and are intended to encourage more people to provide accurate information.

A sanction fee is a charge that is usually levied if you have caused an incorrect payment from us. This applies if you have received a payment or a higher amount than you were entitled to, and a decision for repayment has been made.

If you have received an incorrect payment, you must repay all or part of the amount. In addition to the repayment, we will generally also decide on a sanction fee.

The sanction fee corresponds to 25 percent of the amount that needs to be repaid. At the same time, there is a minimum level of 4 percent of the income base amount. For 2026, this means that the sanction fee will be at least 2,368 Swedish krona.

In certain situations, the sanction fee may be lower or waived altogether. This may be the case if you have had difficulty providing information to us due to cognitive difficulties, or if the sanction fee risks affecting another person, such as a child.

A benefit suspension means that for a period of time, you lose your right to a certain benefit. This applies if you have knowingly or through gross negligence provided incorrect information or failed to provide the required information.

The suspension period is between three months and three years. The length of the period depends on how serious the violation is and what consequences the suspension has for you.

A benefit suspension means that you are not entitled to a certain benefit during the suspension period, even if you otherwise meet the requirements.

Income pension, supplementary pension, premium pension, or premium pension for survivors cannot be suspended.
