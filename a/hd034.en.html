The EU has established new regulations to increase transparency in political advertising. This means that it should be clear to all citizens when an advertisement is political and who is responsible for the message. The regulations apply to all services used to disseminate political messages in Europe.

The government is now proposing a new Swedish law to help implement these EU regulations. This law will, among other things, determine the level of fines or penalties that companies or organizations will face if they do not comply with the regulations. The aim is to create order and clarity when political messages are used in advertising.

To ensure that everything is proceeding correctly, authorities will be tasked with monitoring the advertising. The proposal also includes changes to the rules for the administrative courts that handle cases against authorities. This is important to ensure that decisions can be reviewed in a fair and just manner.

If the Swedish Parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new laws will come into effect on January 1, 2026. This means that Sweden will have regulations and authorities in place to take action if anyone violates the common rules for political advertising in the EU.

Full text

The EU regulation on political advertising establishes uniform rules within the European Union regarding transparency requirements for political advertising services, i.e., services used to develop, place, promote, publish, provide, or disseminate political messages. The regulation also includes rules on the use of targeting and advertising delivery techniques for political advertising. According to the regulation, member states must, among other things, establish rules on sanctions or, if necessary, other measures for violations of certain articles of the regulation. Furthermore, member states must designate one or more competent authorities to monitor the implementation of the regulation.

The proposal includes suggestions for a new law with supplementary provisions to the regulation. The law contains provisions on, among other things, sanction fees, as well as the procedures and powers of the competent authorities. An amendment to the law (1971:289) on administrative courts is also proposed.

The new law and the amendment to the law on administrative courts are proposed to come into force on January 1, 2026.

Proposal for a Parliamentary Decision

The government's proposal:

  1. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for a law with supplementary provisions to the EU regulation on political advertising.
  2. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for a law amending the law (1971:289) on administrative courts.
