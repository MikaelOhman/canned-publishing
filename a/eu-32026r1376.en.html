COMMISSION IMPLEMENTING REGULATION (EU) 2026/1376

concerning exemptions from Implementing Regulation (EU) 2019/2072 regarding the introduction into the territory of the Union of non-rooted cuttings for planting of Calibrachoa spp., Petunia spp. and hybrids of these from Uganda.

THE EUROPEAN COMMISSION HAS ADOPTED THIS REGULATION:

Having regard to the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union, Having regard to Regulation (EU) 2016/2031 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 26 October 2016 on protective measures against plant pests, amending Regulations (EU) No 228/2013, (EU) No 652/2014 and (EU) No 1143/2014, and Directive 69/464/EEC, 74/647/EEC, 93/85/EEC, 98/57/EC, 2000/29/EC, 2006/91/EC and 2007/33/EC ( 1 ), in particular Articles 30.1 and 42a.1, and Having regard to Article 7 of Commission Implementing Regulation (EU) 2019/2072 ( 2 ), and in the light of point 18 of Annex VI to that Implementing Regulation, the introduction of plants for planting from the family Solanaceae from certain third countries into the Union is prohibited.

Several Member States have expressed an interest in importing non-rooted cuttings for planting of Calibrachoa spp. and Petunia spp. and hybrids of these from Uganda (the specified plants), from which imports are currently prohibited. On 6 October 2021, these Member States submitted technical documentation describing the procedures for the production of the specified plants in Uganda.

In December 2025, the European Food Safety Authority (EFSA) adopted a scientific opinion on the risk assessment of non-rooted plants of Petunia spp. and Calibrachoa spp. from Uganda ( 3 ).

The Swedish Food Agency identified the following pests as significant threats to the specified plants:

  • *Bemisia tabaci* Genn. (non-European populations)
  • Cowpea mild mottle virus
  • *Nipaecoccus viridis* (Newstead)
  • Potato leafroll virus (non-EU isolates)
  • Potato spindle tuber viroid
  • Potato virus S (non-EU isolates)
  • Potato virus X (non-EU isolates)
  • *Ralstonia pseudosolanacearum* Safni et al.
  • *Ralstonia solanacearum* (Smith) Yabuuchi et al. Emend. Safni et al.
  • *Scirtothrips dorsalis* Hood
  • Tomato leaf curl Uganda virus
  • Tomato spotted wilt tospovirus
  • Tomato yellow leaf curl virus

The Swedish Food Agency evaluated the risk-reducing measures outlined in the documentation, concerning the production of the specified plants in Uganda, for the identified pests, and estimated the likelihood that the specified plants would be free from these pests.

Based on this assessment, the import of the specified plants poses a risk that can be reduced to an acceptable level by implementing the measures necessary to manage the relevant plant health risks, as described in this regulation.

*Bemisia tabaci* Genn. (non-European populations), Cowpea mild mottle virus, potato leafroll virus (non-EU isolates), potato virus S (non-EU isolates), potato virus X (non-EU isolates), *Ralstonia pseudosolanacearum* Safni et al., *Ralstonia solanacearum* (Smith) Yabuuchi et al. Emend. Safni et al., *Scirtothrips dorsalis* Hood, and Tomato leaf curl Uganda virus are listed as EU quarantine pests in Annex II of Implementing Regulation (EU) 2019/2072. Since the import of the specified plants into the territory of the Union is currently prohibited, they are not subject to the specific requirements of Annex VII of Implementing Regulation (EU) 2019/2072. In this regard, these pests should be considered as specified pests subject to specific requirements when applying this regulation.

Potato spindle tuber viroid, Tomato spotted wilt tospovirus, and Tomato yellow leaf curl virus are listed in Annex IV of Implementing Regulation (EU) 2019/2072, which regulates EU non-quarantine pests for goods other than plants of the Petunia spp. and Calibrachoa spp. Since it is not stated that these pests have an economic impact on the production of Petunia spp. and Calibrachoa spp. within the territory of the Union, they should not be considered listed pests when applying this regulation.

Nipaecoccus viridis (Newstead) is not listed as an EU quarantine pest in Annex II of Implementing Regulation (EU) 2019/2072. According to the opinion of the Food and Feed Safety Authority, this pest is considered significant for the specified plants. Therefore, the Commission concludes that it meets the criteria for inclusion in the list of EU quarantine pests and the criteria in section 3, subsection 2 of Annex I of Regulation (EU) 2016/2031. It should therefore be subject to the temporary measures in Article 30.1 of that regulation and considered a listed pest when applying this regulation.

To ensure that the introduction of the specified plants does not pose a risk of introducing the specified pests into the territory of the Union, the plants should be grown from plants originating within the territory of the Union to prevent the presence of pests in the starting material.

The production of the specified plants should take place in production facilities approved by the Ugandan National Plant Protection Organization for the production of specified plants intended for export to the Union, and which can be identified with a unique traceability code that allows the competent authorities, if a specified pest is found on a specified plant within the territory of the Union, to identify the production facility from which the specified plant originated.

To ensure that the specified plants are free from the specified pests, they must also be physically protected during cultivation and inspected before export.

Since signs of the presence of the specified pests may not yet be visible on non-rooted cuttings imported into the Union, the specified plants, after being imported into the Union's territory, should be planted and rooted in facilities belonging to professional operators who are specifically authorized to issue plant passports for these plants originating from Uganda, or to professional operators for whom the competent authorities issue plant passports, provided that they are subject to regular official inspections. Professional operators who will cultivate the specified plants or allow them to root should notify the competent authorities before receiving the specified plants, so that the competent authorities can plan the official inspections in advance.

To prevent the specified plants from being moved during the initial transport to facilities belonging to professional operators who are not authorized to issue plant passports for the specified plants, the importer must provide a document issued by the competent authority, confirming that the plants will be transported to facilities belonging to professional operators who, in accordance with Article 89.1 of Regulation (EU) 2016/2031, are specifically authorized to issue plant passports for plants with roots or that have been grown from the specified plants, or to facilities belonging to professional operators.
