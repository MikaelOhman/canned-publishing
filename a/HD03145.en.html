Suspension of enforcement – a new approach for certain foreign nationals in cases of temporary enforcement obstacles.

TL;DRThe government proposes new regulations for individuals who are to be deported but are temporarily unable to leave Sweden. The purpose is to increase security and make it easier for the state to send back individuals who have committed crimes or pose a threat.
In plain language

The government wants to change the rules for individuals who have been ordered to be deported or refused entry from Sweden, but who, for various reasons, cannot leave the country at this time. This primarily concerns individuals who have committed crimes or who are considered a threat to security. Instead of granting these individuals a temporary residence permit, the government wants to pause the deportation process while maintaining strict control over them.

According to the proposed regulations, those subject to these rules must register regularly and are not allowed to leave a designated area. If a person violates these rules, they may be sentenced to up to one year in prison. Individuals must also reside in accommodation operated by the Swedish Migration Agency in order to be eligible for financial support from the state.

To increase control, the government also proposes to limit individuals' right to work and access to various benefits in society. The proposal involves changes to many different laws, such as laws regarding education, social services, and healthcare, to regulate how these individuals should be cared for while they await deportation.

If the Parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new laws are planned to come into effect on June 1, 2026.

Full text

The proposal includes suggestions for a new framework for certain foreign nationals when there is a temporary obstacle to enforcing a decision regarding deportation or expulsion. This new framework includes, among other things, foreign nationals who are involved in security cases under the Aliens Act (2005:716) or who have been expelled due to criminal offenses. The purpose is to create conditions for more efficient return procedures and to strengthen the state's ability to manage individuals who pose a threat to public order or security and who cannot be removed from the country for a certain period. The proposals include, among other things:

  • The enforcement will, as a general rule, be postponed (inhibited) instead of the foreign national being granted a temporary residence permit.
  • The foreign national may be required to register and simultaneously be prohibited from leaving a designated area (area restriction).
  • Anyone who violates the registration requirement or area restriction may be sentenced to imprisonment for a maximum of one year.
  • The foreign national will be assigned a place of residence at one of the Swedish Migration Agency's facilities and, as a general rule, must reside there exclusively in order to be eligible for financial assistance.
  • The foreign national's right to work and access to social benefits will be restricted.

The proposed amendments to the law will come into force on June 1, 2026.
