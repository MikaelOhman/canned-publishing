TL;DRSweden aims to amend its laws to better protect the country against cyberattacks. By changing the regulations, government agencies will be able to more easily share important information with each other.
In plain language

The National Cyber Security Centre, known as NCSC, is part of the Swedish Defence Radio Agency, also known as FRA. Their job is to detect and stop digital threats and attacks against Sweden's computers and networks. To do a good job, they need to be able to communicate with other authorities and share information.

Currently, there are regulations regarding confidentiality that make it difficult for authorities to share the information needed. Therefore, the government wants to introduce new laws. They propose a new law that requires authorities to share information with each other, and a new law regarding how FRA can handle personal data in its work.

The government also wants to amend the law called the Public Access and Secrecy Act. The purpose is to protect individuals while also enabling cooperation against IT threats. These changes are important for making Sweden's digital society more secure.

If the Parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new laws will come into effect on July 15, 2026.

Full text

The National Cyber Security Centre (NCSC), which is part of the Swedish Defence Radio Agency (FRA), has the task of developing and strengthening Sweden's ability to prevent, detect, and manage hostile cyber threats and other IT incidents. FRA is to cooperate with the so-called collaborating agencies in the activities carried out within the framework of the centre.

A fundamental prerequisite for the NCSC to conduct effective work is that FRA and the collaborating agencies can exchange necessary information with each other. Current confidentiality regulations mean that there are no conditions for a meaningful exchange of information, and this is an obstacle to the cooperation required to strengthen cybersecurity in our society.

The government therefore proposes a new law on data obligations that will apply to the authorities that cooperate within the NCSC. The government also proposes a new law on FRA's processing of personal data within the framework of the centre's activities, and a confidentiality provision to protect individuals with regard to information held by the centre. In addition, an amendment to the Public Access and Secrecy Act is proposed.

The legislative changes are proposed to come into force on July 15, 2026.

Proposed Parliamentary Decision

The government's proposal:

  1. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for a law on data obligations in cooperation within the National Cyber Security Centre.
  2. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for a law on the Swedish Defence Radio Agency's processing of personal data within the National Cyber Security Centre.
  3. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for a law amending the Public Access and Secrecy Act (2009:400).
