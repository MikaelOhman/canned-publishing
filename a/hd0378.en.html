In plain language

The government has sent a proposal to the Parliament regarding a change to the form of government, which is one of Sweden's most important laws. The first major proposal is to enshrine the right to abortion in the constitution. This is being done to better protect the right to abortion.

The government also wants to be able to revoke Swedish citizenship from individuals who have obtained it through deception by providing false information. This also applies to individuals who are citizens of another country but who have been convicted of crimes that harm Sweden's vital interests.

Another proposal concerns freedom of association, which is the right to participate in various groups or organizations. The government wants to be able to restrict this right for groups that engage in serious criminal activity to make money or obtain other prohibited benefits. The purpose is to combat organized crime that threatens society.

If the Parliament votes in favor of the proposal, Prop. 2025/26:78, the new rules will come into effect on January 1, 2027.

Full text

The government's proposal suggests changes to the provisions of the Instrument of Government regarding fundamental rights and freedoms.

The right to abortion enjoys strong public support, and all parties in the Riksdag (Swedish Parliament) support the abortion legislation. The right to abortion and the interests it protects are of such importance that the right should be enshrined in the constitution. Therefore, the government proposes that the right to abortion be introduced as a new fundamental right and freedom in the Instrument of Government.

Furthermore, the proposal includes a provision allowing for the possibility of revoking Swedish citizenship from individuals who are also citizens of another country and who have acquired citizenship through false or misleading information, or through other improper conduct, or who have been convicted of crimes that seriously harm Sweden's vital interests.

Organized crime poses a systemic threat. The constitutional protection of freedom of association currently prevents the complete criminalization of participation in criminal organizations. The government therefore proposes that freedom of association be limited with regard to organizations that engage in serious criminal activity to achieve financial gain or other improper advantages.

The proposed amendment is scheduled to come into effect on January 1, 2027.
