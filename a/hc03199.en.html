Confidentiality in Certain Cases Regarding Strategic Net-Zero Projects.

Source Riksdagen Read the original at Riksdagen →2025-08-26
TL;DRGovernment agencies will implement confidentiality measures in cases involving the recognition – or withdrawal of recognition – of so-called strategic net-zero projects, as stipulated by the EU regulation on the net-zero industry. The purpose of this measure is to protect companies' trade secrets. The proposed regulations are scheduled to take effect from December 1, 2025.
In plain language

The EU regulation on the net-zero industry aims to strengthen Europe's manufacturing of the technologies needed for the transition to a climate-neutral economy. Certain projects may be recognized as strategic net-zero projects.

The government proposes that confidentiality should apply within government agencies in cases concerning the recognition of such projects, and in cases concerning the revocation of such recognition.

The confidentiality provision is intended to protect information about individuals' business and operational activities, if the disclosure of such information could harm the individual. This rule will be incorporated into the Public Access and Secrecy Act and is proposed to take effect from December 1, 2025.

Full text

The proposal suggests that confidentiality should apply to government agencies in cases concerning the recognition of projects as strategic net-zero projects under the EU regulation on the net-zero industry, and in cases concerning the revocation of such recognition. This confidentiality is intended to protect information about individuals' business or operational activities, if the disclosure of such information could cause harm to the individual. The new provision will be introduced into the law on public access and confidentiality.

The proposed amendment to the law is scheduled to come into effect on December 1, 2025.

Proposed resolution for the Parliament:

Prop. 2024/25:199

Government Proposal: The Parliament shall approve the government's proposal for an amendment to the Public Access and Secrecy Act (2009:400). 3

Prop. 2024/25:199 2 Draft law amending the Public Access and Secrecy Act (2009:400)

Hereby, it is stipulated that a new paragraph, 31 Chapter, Section 23a, shall be introduced into the Public Access and Secrecy Act (2009:400), and a new heading shall be inserted immediately before 31 Chapter, Section 23a, with the following wording:

Current wording | Proposed wording ---|--- 31 Chapter | Strategic Net-Zero Projects 23 a § | 23 a § Secrecy applies to information concerning an individual's business or operational affairs if it can be assumed that the individual will suffer harm if the information is disclosed, and the information appears in a case before a government agency regarding: | Secrecy applies to decisions in a case.

  1. Recognition of a project as a strategic net-zero project according to Article 13.1, 13.3, 13.4, or 13.5 of the European Parliament and Council Regulation (EU) 2024/1735 of June 13, 2024, on establishing a framework for strengthening Europe's ecosystem for the manufacturing of net-zero technologies and amending Regulation (EU) 2018/1724, or | For information in a public document, the period of secrecy shall be no more than twenty years.
  2. Revocation of a recognition as referred to in 1, according to Article 14.7 of Regulation (EU) 2024/1735. |

This law shall enter into force on December 1, 2025. 4
