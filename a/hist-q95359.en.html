Johan of Hoya, who died on June 11, 1535, in Funen, Denmark, was a German count from Lübeck and the son of Count Jobst I of the House of Hoya.

== Biography == In 1524, Johan of Hoya came to Sweden, where he married Margareta Eriksdotter (Vasa), Gustav Vasa's sister, in 1525. She was a widow after Joakim Brahe (executed during the Stockholm Bloodbath). The same year, he was appointed to the council and granted the governorships of Viborg and Nyslott in Finland, later also Kumogårds län. Hoya was involved in several diplomatic missions, including negotiations regarding the execution of Daljunkern and as a negotiator with Lübeck concerning Sweden's large debt. The sum negotiated in 1529 was not approved by Gustav Vasa, and during this time – possibly due to this – a serious animosity developed between the king and Hoya. As a result, his brother-in-law fled to Germany in June 1534. Shortly after, he entered the service of Duke Albert of Mecklenburg. During the Count's Feud, in December 1534, he joined Markus Meyer with a military force in Skåne, but was decisively defeated at Hälsingborg in January 1535. Not long after, he was killed in the Battle of Öxnebjerg in Funen on June 11, 1535. Hoya's son, Johan, died in 1574 as bishop of Osnabrück, Münster, and Paderborn.
