Swedish participation in air defense operations within the framework of NATO.

Source Riksdagen Read the original at Riksdagen →2025-03-06
TL;DRSweden plans to send eight fighter jets to Poland during the spring and summer of 2025. The purpose is to assist NATO in protecting vital shipments to Ukraine.
In plain language

The government wants the Swedish Parliament to approve Sweden's participation in a military operation alongside NATO. This involves protecting Polish airspace for a period starting on May 1, 2025, and ending on August 31, 2025.

Sweden will deploy up to eight fighter jets and the personnel required to operate them. This operation is part of NATO's efforts to maintain security in the region.

The primary objective of the plan is to protect a so-called logistics hub in Poland. A logistics hub is a crucial location where goods and materials are collected and distributed. This facility is used to provide both military and civilian support to Ukraine.

Full text

The proposal suggests that the Parliament should approve the government's plan to provide a Swedish armed force, consisting of a maximum of eight fighter jets and associated personnel, from May 1, 2025, to August 31, 2025. This force would participate in NATO's air defense operation in Poland, which is tasked with protecting and maintaining the security of the logistics hub used to provide military and civilian support to Ukraine.
