Hedvig Eleonora of Holstein-Gottorp, born October 23, 1636, and died November 24, 1715, was a Swedish queen and regent, married on October 24, 1654, to Charles X Gustav.

She was Queen of Sweden for five years and remained the Dowager Queen until her death in 1715. She served as Regent of Sweden, acting as guardian for her son, Charles XI, from 1660 to 1672, and later as regent for her grandson, Charles XII, in 1697. She also represented Charles XII in Sweden from 1700 to 1713.

Hedvig Eleonora was married to Charles X Gustav for political reasons, and she accompanied him on many of his military campaigns. She was with him during his campaigns in Poland in 1656 and in Denmark in 1658.

In 2015, Hedvig Eleonora was commemorated on the 300th anniversary of her death at various locations, including Drottningholm Palace, Gripsholm Castle, and Strömsholm Castle. The exhibition program opened at Drottningholm on April 30 with a flower exhibition featuring historical tulip varieties.

== Biography ==

=== Background === Hedvig Eleonora was the daughter of Frederick III of Holstein-Gottorp and Marie Elisabeth of Saxony. She herself used the name Leonore. Her marriage was arranged as an alliance between Sweden and Holstein-Gottorp. The reason was that Sweden needed a secure route to its possessions in Germany, which should not be dependent on Denmark, who was also a common enemy of both states. The marriage was proposed by Queen Christina while she was traveling through Holstein-Gottorp en route to Rome, and the proposal was immediately accepted by Holstein-Gottorp, who agreed to all the demands made by Sweden. Christina recommended Hedvig Eleonora's sister, Magdalena Sibylla of Holstein-Gottorp, but when Charles Gustav saw portraits of both sisters, he chose Hedwig Eleonora because of her appearance: "a pleasant and modest young woman." At this time, she was described as being sweet and as: "a beautiful little girl, virtuous and exceptionally charming!" At the time, she was engaged to a duke of Mecklenburg, Gustaf Adolf of Mecklenburg-Güstrow, but he was instead betrothed to Magdalena Sibylla.

=== Wedding ===

Main article: The wedding of Charles X Gustav and Hedwig Eleanor The marriage contract was signed on Michaelmas Day (September 29) 1654, and she was guaranteed a dowry of 20,000 riksdaler, a morning gift of 32,000 riksdaler, or 2,000 riksdaler annually as an allowance, and for life, the estates of Gripsholm, Eskilstuna, and Strömsholm, with an annual income of 34,000 riksdaler. (Charles X Gustav was a cousin to Hedwig Eleanor's mother. Charles X Gustav and Hedwig Eleanor were also double cousins.) Hedwig Eleanor arrived in Dalarö on October 5, 1654, where she was welcomed by the king. She then stayed at Karlberg Castle until October 24, when she made her official entry into Stockholm, escorted by the king. She was received at Stockholm Castle by Queen Dowager Maria Eleonora, who made her last public appearance at the wedding. The same day, the ceremony took place at the castle. Hedwig Eleanor was wearing a magnificent dress of silver fabric.

== Queen ==

On October 27th, Hedwig Eleanor was crowned in the Stockholm Cathedral. Shortly after the wedding, the war against Poland began. Hedwig Eleanor remained in Sweden until the birth of her first child, Charles XI, on November 24, 1655, and during the following Christmas season, but then joined Charles X Gustav in the field. During this time, she reportedly demonstrated both physical and mental strength. She was present at the Battle of Warsaw in 1656, where she, along with her husband, received the army's allegiance.

She returned to Sweden in the autumn of 1656. Hedwig Eleanor immediately began managing her personal estate. She reunited with Charles X Gustav in Gothenburg at his request after his first Danish war in 1658. She then followed him to Gottorp and, in August, was present at the Swedish military camp in Wismar. In September, she traveled with her sister-in-law, Maria Eufrosyne of Palatinate, to Kronborg Castle in Denmark, which had recently been captured by Admiral Wrangel. At Kronborg, Hedwig Eleanor was entertained by the foreign ambassadors accompanying the Swedish army, received visits from her husband, went hunting with the English ambassador, and occasionally visited Frederiksborg Castle. During the military campaign on Falster, she hosted the foreign ambassadors at Nyköbinghus. Hedwig Eleanor left Denmark in December 1659 and traveled to Gothenburg, where the Diet was to be held in January 1660.

According to her husband's will, Hedvig Eleonora was to be the chair and regent with two votes, holding a decisive casting vote in the regency government for her son, Charles XI.
