Measures against driving licence tourism

TL;DRThe government wants to amend the law to prevent individuals from countries outside the EU/EEA from using loopholes to obtain a Swedish driver's license. The aim is to make it more difficult to circumvent the Swedish regulations for driver's licenses.
In plain language

Today, there is a way to obtain a Swedish driver's license by first exchanging a driver's license from a country outside the EU/EEA for a driver's license from another EU country. Then, this EU driver's license is exchanged for a Swedish one. This practice is known as "driver's license tourism," and it is what the government wants to stop.

The new regulations propose that you can only exchange a driver's license for a Swedish one if you have held a valid driver's license in an EU country for at least five years before permanently moving to Sweden. There are also exceptions for certain specific countries, such as the Faroe Islands, the United Kingdom, Japan, and Switzerland.

To be eligible to exchange a driver's license, you must also meet the same health and medical requirements as everyone else. You must also have a driver's license that covers the types of vehicles you wish to drive in Sweden.

If the proposal is approved and the Swedish Parliament votes in favor, the new regulations will come into effect on May 1, 2026. The proposal originates from the government and is documented in a publication called "proposition 2025/26:37."

Full text

The proposal suggests changes to the driving license law (1998:488) to prevent so-called "driving license tourism." This refers to the practice where a driving license from a country outside the EEA (a third country) is first exchanged for a driving license from another EEA member state (a foreign EEA driving license), which is then exchanged for a Swedish driving license. The purpose of these changes is to limit the opportunities for individuals with driving licenses from third countries, which Sweden does not allow to be directly exchanged, to circumvent Swedish regulations through such driving license tourism. A foreign EEA driving license that has been issued after an exchange of a third-country driving license can only be exchanged for a Swedish driving license in the following cases:

  • The driving license holder has held a driving license with equivalent driving privileges in one or more EEA member states for at least five years before establishing permanent residency in Sweden.
  • The third-country driving license was issued in the Faroe Islands, the United Kingdom, Japan, or Switzerland.
  • The EEA driving license is valid for categories for which there is an approved driving test in an EEA member state.

The personal and medical requirements must be met so that the driving licenses are equivalent to those issued from third countries that Sweden directly recognizes.

The changes are proposed to come into effect on May 1, 2026.
