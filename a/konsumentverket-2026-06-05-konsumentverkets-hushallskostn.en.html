Consumer Agency's household cost estimates for 2026 are now available. News Published: November 18, 2025

The Swedish Consumer Agency is releasing its annual forecast of household expenses for the coming years today. The agency has made adjustments that significantly impact the calculations, particularly for food. At the same time, new meal plans are being launched. Each year, the Consumer Agency calculates what can be considered reasonable costs for some of the most common expenses in a household. These calculations aim to provide guidance on what is needed to live a good everyday life – without overconsumption, but also without living in deprivation. The calculations are independent of the actual income of the household.

A major change for 2026 is that the food cost calculations, especially for adults, have decreased. The main reasons for this decrease in the Consumer Agency's food cost estimates are:

  • New meal plans, used to calculate food costs, have been adapted to the latest Nordic nutrition recommendations published in 2023 and the Swedish National Food Agency's updated dietary guidelines published in 2025.
  • A new method for measuring food prices. The price measurements are now conducted in more stores and locations, and over a longer period, to better account for regional price differences and various offers and discounts.
  • An expected reduction in the VAT (sales tax) on food, which could lead to lower food prices.

It is important to note that the decrease in the Consumer Agency's food cost calculations is primarily due to changes in methods and underlying data, rather than an expectation of a radical drop in food prices next year.

For a family with two children aged 5 and 9, the Consumer Agency estimates food costs to be 8440 SEK, which is a decrease of 2090 SEK (-20%) compared to the calculations for 2025.

**New Meal Plans:** The Consumer Agency's meal plans are a way for the agency to calculate food costs. The food included in the meal plan is nutritious, and the recipes are designed based on current recommendations for diet and health. The meal plan assumes a physical activity level close to the median in the population and is composed with consideration for environmental sustainability.

**Facts:** The calculations cover approximately 40 percent of household expenses, such as food, clothing, and internet subscriptions. Major expenses that are not included are housing and transportation. These are excluded because the differences within these costs are very large depending on your personal circumstances, such as where you live and how you live. The calculated household expenses can be used as reference values when planning a household budget. They are used by government agencies, individual households, consumer advisors, debt counselors, social services, banks, and schools. The calculations are also provided to the government as background information for decisions regarding the national standard within income support.
