The government has proposed a new law regarding signal intelligence. Signal intelligence involves listening to and analyzing digital information and radio signals to identify important information. This new law aims to help the Swedish Defense Radio Agency (FRA) operate effectively when Sweden is at war or when there is a risk of war.

The proposal also includes changes to existing laws. It will become possible to use signal intelligence even during military exercises. The goal is also to be able to conduct more effective surveillance to detect international terrorism and other serious crimes that could harm Sweden's interests. It will also become easier to handle information in emergency situations where certain types of signals could not previously be stored.

To achieve this, the government also proposes changes to the rules governing the Defense Intelligence Court, which is the court that oversees the surveillance activities. They also want to change the rules regarding how personal data can be handled by the FRA when carrying out its new tasks.

Full text

The proposal suggests a new law regarding signal intelligence activities in defense intelligence operations during wartime or a state of imminent war. The proposal aims to enable the Swedish Defence Radio Agency (FRA) to conduct effective and appropriate signal intelligence even under such special circumstances.

The proposal also includes amendments to the law on signal intelligence in defense intelligence operations, which include:

  • A new purpose that allows for signal intelligence activities during training exercises.
  • A revised purpose concerning the mapping of international terrorism and other serious cross-border crimes that may threaten essential national interests.
  • An exception for emergency situations from the existing prohibition against collecting signals between a sender and a receiver, both of which are located in Sweden, and from the obligation to destroy such signals.

The proposal also suggests certain amendments to the law on the Defense Intelligence Court, with the aim of enabling a more efficient organization and handling, and to the law on the processing of personal data at the Swedish Defence Radio Agency, due to the new signal intelligence purpose related to training exercises.

The new law and other legal amendments are proposed to come into force on August 1, 2026.
