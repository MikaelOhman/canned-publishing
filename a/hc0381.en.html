Ban on bottom trawling in marine protected areas.

Source Riksdagen Read the original at Riksdagen →2025-01-21
TL;DRThe government wants to ban bottom trawling in certain protected marine areas in order to protect nature. The proposal would prohibit the use of large nets dragged along the seabed in areas that are important for the environment.
In plain language

The government has proposed changes to the fishing regulations in Sweden. The proposal aims to prohibit bottom trawling, a method that involves dragging large nets along the seabed, in areas that are specially protected. This applies to locations designated as marine national parks and nature reserves, as well as areas protected by EU regulations for nature conservation, known as Natura 2000 areas.

The proposal could result in very strict regulations. Even though the ban would make it much more difficult for fishermen to catch fish, the needs of the environment will take precedence in these protected areas. The purpose is to give greater consideration to nature and protect marine life.

If the Parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new regulations will come into effect on June 1, 2025. The proposal also includes minor changes to other parts of the law to ensure everything works together.

Full text

The government's proposal suggests expanding the authorization in the Fisheries Act (1993:787) to issue regulations regarding the consideration of nature conservation interests in fishing. This expansion would allow for regulations prohibiting bottom trawling, even if these regulations significantly hinder fishing activities. This would apply to fishing in marine national parks, nature reserves, biotopes, or Natura 2000 areas. Certain consequential amendments are also proposed.

The proposed amendments are scheduled to come into effect on June 1, 2025.
