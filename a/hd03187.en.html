The government has proposed a plan to make it easier for individuals to rent out their homes. A new law for private rentals is being proposed, which would introduce clearer rules and make it easier to determine the rent when renting out a property to others. It should also be easier to sublet a condominium.

The proposal also aims to help companies offer housing to their employees and to facilitate so-called "shared living" arrangements. In a shared living arrangement, several people rent individual rooms but share a kitchen and bathroom. The government wants to introduce regulations to make this easier, while also creating a system that makes it difficult to abuse the rules.

There are also proposals to change how much rental agents are paid and how legal proceedings related to rent should function. The purpose of all these changes is to increase the number of properties available on the market, making it easier for people to find a place to live.

If the parliament votes in favor of the proposals, the new rules will come into effect on July 1, 2026. The proposal involves changes to several different laws, including the Condominium Act and the Act on Rental Boards.

Full text

The proposal includes a number of suggestions aimed at creating a more flexible rental market and increasing the availability of housing.

It is proposed that a new private rental law be introduced, which would provide more accessible and clearer rules for individuals renting out their homes. The proposals would expand opportunities for renting out properties and allow for more flexible rent setting.

The possibilities for subletting apartments should also be expanded. The fact that the apartment has been rented out before should only be taken into account when the apartment is rented out again if the previous rental period was significant. A clearer distinction is also proposed between subleases and rentals to lodgers.

The new private rental law and the expanded possibilities for subletting apartments are expected to lead to more housing becoming available on the rental market and a better utilization of the existing housing stock.

It is also proposed to introduce more appropriate rules to meet the needs of companies for housing for their employees (company housing) and to facilitate the increasingly common living arrangement where tenants rent individual rooms and share common areas (shared housing). The proposals would improve the conditions for providing these types of housing through so-called block rentals. The proposals also include a clear set of rules with mechanisms to prevent abuse of the system.

The compensation to rental agents will be increased and linked to the consumer price index. Furthermore, it is proposed to introduce regulations regarding, among other things, a permit requirement in cases of claims for the reimbursement of rent, in order to streamline the process.

The new law and the amendments to the law are proposed to come into force on July 1, 2026.
