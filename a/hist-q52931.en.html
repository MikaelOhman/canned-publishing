Adolf Frederick (German: Adolf Friedrich), born May 14th (according to the new calendar) 1710 at Gottorp Castle in Holstein-Gottorp (now Schleswig-Holstein) in Germany, and died on February 12th, 1771 at Stockholm Castle, was Prince-Bishop of Lübeck from 1727 to 1743 and King of Sweden from 1751. He was appointed Crown Prince of Sweden in 1743 after the Russo-Swedish War under pressure from Empress Elizabeth. The following year, he married Louisa Ulrika of Prussia, sister of Frederick the Great. He had very little personal power, although people around him tried to give him more. He was the son of Duke Christian August of Holstein-Gottorp (the Prince-Bishop of Lübeck) and Albertine Frederica of Baden-Durlach, making him the uncle of Catherine II of Russia. Adolf Frederick reintroduced the House of Oldenburg into Sweden and founded its Holstein-Gottorp branch as the Swedish royal dynasty.

== Childhood ==

Adolf Frederick was born on May 4th (according to the Swedish calendar; May 3rd according to the Gregorian calendar, and May 14th according to the New Style calendar) in 1710 at Gottorp Castle near the city of Schleswig, then part of the Duchy of Schleswig (now the German state of Schleswig-Holstein).

His father, Christian August, was a younger son of Duke Christian Albrecht of Holstein-Gottorp and the younger brother of King Charles XII's sister-in-law, Duchess Frederick IV of Holstein-Gottorp. The father had been the Protestant Prince-Bishop of Lübeck since 1705 and, during the Great Northern War, also acted as regent for his underage nephew, Duke Charles Frederick. His mother, Albertina Fredrika of Baden-Durlach, was the great-granddaughter of Queen Christina's daughter, Catherine. Among his siblings was Johanna Elisabeth, the mother of Empress Catherine II of Russia.

At his baptism, King Charles XII, who had been invited as a godfather, sent him an appointment to an officer position in the Swedish army. In 1727, when his older brother, who had succeeded their father as Prince-Bishop, died, his mother, whose financial situation was precarious, managed to secure that the Prince-Bishopric passed to the seventeen-year-old Adolf Frederick. Although his cousin, Charles Frederick (King Charles XII's nephew), wanted to overlook him in the selection of a guardian for his son, Karl Peter Ulrik, Adolf Frederick still received this guardianship upon Charles Frederick's death in 1739 and became administrator of Holstein-Gottorp for a few years.

==Adolf Frederick as Swedish Heir Apparent== When Empress Elizabeth of Russia seized the Russian throne through a palace coup in 1741, and needed her nephew's inheritance rights to support her own claim, Adolf Frederick supported her plans and sent his young ward to Russia, hoping thereby to inherit his prospects for the Swedish throne. From the turbulent political struggles of 1743, and with Russian influence, Adolf Frederick was elected as the Swedish heir apparent on June 23rd. He ascended to the government on March 25th, 1751, and was crowned on November 26th of the same year.

==Coronation and Royal Title==

The coronation took place on November 26, 1751, at the Stockholm Cathedral, and was one of the most lavish royal ceremonies of the Swedish 18th century. The intention of the Council of State was to project an image of national unity abroad. The event was organized by superintendent Carl Johan Cronstedt, with the help of Crown Princess Lovisa Ulrika, who managed to secure Karl Henrik Gräum, a court musician from Prussia, to compose the music. New fabrics, lace, luxury goods, and more were ordered from Paris and Berlin. Lovisa Ulrika's coronation gown was made of "a material in silver, with golden crowns and the royal coat of arms embroidered in purple velvet, adorned with pearls..." according to her own description.

Around 9:00 AM, the procession began. Two companies of the Royal Guard led the way from the Council Hall to the Stockholm Cathedral, accompanied by drums and trumpets. The procession included members of the Council of State, Marshal of the Court Löwen, the mayor, representatives of various social classes, chamberlains, nobles and their wives, and of course, Adolf Frederick and Lovisa Ulrika. Adolf Frederick rode a white horse, which unfortunately proved to be temperamental and attempted to unseat him on several occasions during the procession. Lovisa Ulrika traveled in a carriage drawn by her marshal Gustaf Jakob Horn af Rantzien. Prince Gustav, who was only 5 years old at the time, watched the procession from the Hessenstein House before being transported in a carriage to the Stockholm Cathedral, which was decorated with the Swedish coat of arms and the three crowns. The ground outside the church was covered with blue fabric adorned with three yellow crowns.

After the royal couple received their anointing, Adolf Frederick took his oath, placing three fingers on a Bible, and received the regalia. Archbishop Benzelius, assisted by Nils Adam Bielke, placed the crowns on the heads of the royal couple. Following the ceremony, food and drink were provided for the residents of Stockholm, and a large dinner was held for the royal couple.

Adolf Frederick's full title in Swedish was: Adolf Fredrik med Guds Nåde, Sveriges, Götes och Vendes Konung, etc.etc.etc. Arvinge till Norge, Hertig till Schleswig, Holstein, Stormarn och Dithmarschen, Greve till Oldenburg och Delmenhorst, etc.etc.
