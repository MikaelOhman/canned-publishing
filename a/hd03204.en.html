In plain language

The government has proposed a bill to the parliament to improve the lives of government employees who are sent to work in other countries. This involves amending several laws, including the income tax law, as well as laws regarding healthcare and dental care.

Firstly, the government wants to change the tax rules. If an employee needs to be vaccinated in order to work abroad, family members should also receive free vaccinations without this being considered a taxable benefit. This means that the additional cost of the vaccinations would not be subject to tax.

Secondly, the government wants to change the rules regarding healthcare and dental care. Individuals working for government agencies abroad should have access to quality care from the Swedish regions. This applies even if the individual does not reside in Sweden during this time. It is important that family members who accompany the employee also have access to the same healthcare and dental care.

If the parliament approves the proposal, the new rules are planned to come into effect on July 1, 2026.

Full text

The proposal suggests improvements to the conditions for deploying personnel in two areas. In the income tax law, a change is proposed that would make the benefit of vaccinations for accompanying family members during assignments abroad tax-free, provided that the vaccination is a condition of the stay abroad. Furthermore, changes are proposed to the health care law and the dental care law. These changes would mean that personnel employed by certain government agencies for direct overseas assignments would be covered by the regions' responsibility to provide good health care and dental care, even if they are not considered residents of Sweden. In these cases, the regions' responsibility would also extend to accompanying family members.

The proposed amendments to the laws are intended to come into force on July 1, 2026.
