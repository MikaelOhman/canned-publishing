In plain language

It is important that everyone working with money and finance has a license or is registered with the Swedish Financial Supervisory Authority (Finansinspektionen). The Financial Supervisory Authority is the agency that ensures that everyone follows the rules. If companies do not have a license, it is difficult to ensure that money is not used for crimes, such as money laundering.

An example of a risky activity is so-called "hawala," which is a system for sending money between countries without using traditional banks. These systems often lack the necessary licenses, making it difficult to track the origin of the money.

The government is therefore proposing a new law that makes it a crime to operate a financial business without a license. This applies if it is done intentionally or if it is due to gross negligence. The law should also make it easier for the police and prosecutors to cooperate with the Financial Supervisory Authority when investigating crimes.

If the proposal is approved by the Parliament, the new law will come into effect on March 1, 2026. The purpose is to increase safety and confidence in the economic system in Sweden.

Full text

Financial activities require authorization or registration with the Swedish Financial Supervisory Authority (Finansinspektionen). Authorization and registration are prerequisites for the Financial Supervisory Authority to exercise oversight to ensure that the activity is conducted in accordance with applicable legislation, and ultimately to prevent the activity from being used for criminal purposes, such as money laundering. An example of a financial activity where the risk of criminal use is significant is so-called "hawala" activity, which involves money transfers and often lacks the necessary authorizations or registrations.

To ensure effective oversight and maintain confidence in the financial system, it is important that the authorities can take action against anyone conducting financial activities without authorization or registration. Therefore, the government proposes a new law on penalties for unauthorized financial activities. The law includes, among other things:

  • Criminal provisions that stipulate that anyone who intentionally or through gross negligence conducts financial activities without the authorization or registration of the Financial Supervisory Authority will be convicted of the new crime of unauthorized financial activity.
  • Provisions on cooperation between prosecutors and the Financial Supervisory Authority in the investigation of unauthorized financial activities.

The new law is proposed to come into force on March 1, 2026.
