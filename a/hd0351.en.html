Full text

In this document, the government addresses the observations and recommendations made by the Swedish National Audit Office (Riksrevisionen) in its report "General State Grants as a Stabilizing Policy Instrument" (RiR 2025:19).

The National Audit Office reviewed whether the increases in general state grants during the COVID-19 pandemic were designed effectively as a stabilizing policy instrument. According to the National Audit Office, the increases could have been designed in a more effective way, for example, the communication and the allocation of the grants could have been more targeted. The National Audit Office also believes that the government's explanation lacks transparency. The National Audit Office provides several recommendations to the government for future increases in general state grants for stabilization purposes.

The government constantly strives to design appropriate measures. The National Audit Office's observations and recommendations will be useful in this work. In the event of a crisis, the government will consider various forms of stabilizing policy instruments to support municipalities and regions, if necessary. The government intends to develop the overall principles of stabilizing policy in its upcoming government communication on the fiscal framework.

With this document, the government considers the National Audit Office's report to be finalized.
