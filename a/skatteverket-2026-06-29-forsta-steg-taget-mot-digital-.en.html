The first steps have been taken towards digital estate inventories.

Source Skatteverket Read the original at Skatteverket →2026-06-29
TL;DRStarting July 1st, it will be legal to create inventories digitally, which is a first step towards simplifying the process for those managing estates.
In plain language

An inventory of assets is a document that shows what a deceased person owned and what debts they had. It also indicates who will inherit the estate. Currently, these documents must be submitted on paper, but a new law coming into effect on July 1st makes it possible to do this online in the future.

The Swedish Tax Agency (Skatteverket) is the authority responsible for verifying these documents. They are currently working on creating an online service to speed up the process and make it easier for people who are grieving. This new digital service could be available as early as 2027.

Until the new online service is available, all inventories of assets must still be submitted as physical mail. The Swedish Tax Agency advises that you double-check that all signatures and attachments are included in the documents before submitting them to avoid delays.

Listen The Swedish Tax Agency's press release 2026-06-29 First steps taken towards digital estate declarations. It will become easier to create estate declarations. A new law, effective from July 1st, makes this possible. The Swedish Tax Agency has been a driving force behind this change and is now working on a digital solution that could be implemented as early as 2027.

Approximately 90,000 estate inventories are processed each year. Currently, this is done exclusively on paper, and there have long been calls for a more modernized process. "An estate inventory is something people may only do once in their lifetime, and often it's during a time of grief. We want to make the process as easy as possible, and a digital service makes that possible," says Anders Koskinen, unit manager at the Swedish Tax Agency (Skatteverket).

Until now, creating estate inventories on paper has been a legal requirement. Therefore, in 2023, the Swedish Tax Agency submitted a proposal to the government to amend the law. With the new law in place, the Swedish Tax Agency can now begin developing a digital solution for estate inventories. "We are very pleased with the law; it's a first positive step towards a faster, simpler, and more efficient estate inventory process. However, until we have a digital solution in place, everything must still be sent as physical mail," says Anders Koskinen, unit manager at the Swedish Tax Agency.

Anders Koskinen believes that a future online service could help avoid many common errors and shorten processing times. Currently, many people need to supplement their estate inventories after the initial submission, which prolongs the process. "I would advise those completing an estate inventory to double-check everything carefully, for example, ensuring all attachments are included and that the inventory is signed," says Anders Koskinen.

Facts about Estate Inventories: An estate inventory is a written summary of a deceased person's assets and liabilities, and it identifies the beneficiaries. It must be submitted within four months of the death.
