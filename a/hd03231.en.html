Full text

Sweden has participated in the work to establish a tribunal with jurisdiction over Russia's acts of aggression against Ukraine. The tribunal was established in June 2024 through a bilateral agreement between Ukraine and the Council of Europe. For the tribunal to become operational, states wishing to become members must accede to an extended partial agreement regarding the tribunal's administrative committee, within the framework of the Council of Europe's institutional structure. The agreement regulates the tribunal's operation and entails an obligation for the acceding states to pay an annual fee through the Council of Europe. By acceding to the agreement, Sweden will become a member of the tribunal. This proposal suggests that the Swedish Parliament should approve Sweden's accession to the extended partial agreement.
