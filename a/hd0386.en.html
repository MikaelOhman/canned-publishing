TL;DRNew regulations are being proposed to make it easier for companies to raise capital in the market. It will be cheaper and less complicated to sell securities, such as stocks.
In plain language

The EU has developed a proposal called the "listing act." The purpose is to make it easier for small and medium-sized enterprises to access capital by selling securities on the market. This is intended to allow companies in Europe to compete more effectively.

A significant change concerns so-called prospectuses. A prospectus is an important document containing information that companies must prepare when they want to sell securities to the public. Previously, the threshold for when this was required was €2.5 million, but it is now proposed to raise the threshold to €12 million. This means that fewer companies will need to undertake the expensive and complex task of writing such a document.

It will also be easier to choose a language. Companies operating in Sweden should be able to choose to write their documents in either Swedish or English. There are also proposals to change how fines are calculated if companies make mistakes in the market.

Most of the new rules are planned to come into effect on June 5 and 6, 2026. The government has now submitted the proposals to the Parliament for them to be decided upon and become law.

Full text

The European Commission presented the so-called "listing act" in 2022, with the aim of promoting opportunities for companies to access capital market financing and making capital markets in the EU more competitive.

The proposal includes legislative measures necessary due to changes in EU directives on financial instruments markets, the EU market abuse regulation, and the EU prospectus regulation.

The Securities Market Act proposes, among other things, a new possibility to register a segment of multilateral trading platforms as growth markets for small and medium-sized enterprises, and an expanded right to object to the secondary listing of already listed securities.

The Act on Supplementary Provisions to the EU Market Abuse Regulation proposes, among other things, changes to the calculation of administrative penalty fees.

Furthermore, changes are proposed to the Act on Supplementary Provisions to the EU Prospectus Regulation, which would raise the threshold for the prospectus requirement from 2.5 million euros to twelve million euros. It is also proposed that issuers with Sweden as their country of incorporation, who list securities for trading on a regulated market in Sweden or make offers to the public in Sweden, should be able to choose to prepare a prospectus either in Swedish or English.

The amendments to the laws are proposed to enter into force on June 5 and 6, 2026, respectively. One of the proposed amendments is to enter into force on a date determined by the government.

Proposed Resolutions for the Parliament

The government's proposal:

  1. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Act (2007:528) on the securities market.
  2. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Act (2016:1306) on supplementary provisions to the EU market abuse regulation.
  3. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Act (2019:414) on supplementary provisions to the EU prospectus regulation.
