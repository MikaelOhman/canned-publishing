A more accurate and reliable housing allowance.

TL;DRThe government wants to change how housing benefits are calculated to reduce the risk of people receiving incorrect amounts and having to repay them later. The proposal involves looking at income on a monthly basis instead of annually.
In plain language

The government has proposed a change to housing benefits to make them more equitable. The goal is to ensure that fewer people receive excessive payments, which often leads to debt when the authorities demand repayment.

The new system will calculate benefits based on monthly earnings. It will consider income from employment during the first three months of a four-month period. This information will be obtained from data provided by employers to the government.

For individuals with income from self-employment or those who find it difficult to determine their monthly income, the previous system, which calculates based on the entire year, will still be used. Income from bank accounts or other assets will continue to be calculated based on the previous year, as this information cannot be updated monthly.

If your income changes during the year, your housing benefit will be recalculated immediately. If the proposal is approved, the new rules will come into effect on January 1, 2027.

Full text

The proposal includes suggestions for a more accurate and reliable housing allowance system. The proposals aim to reduce the risk of incorrect payments, claims for repayment, and debt accumulation within the housing allowance system.

When calculating the income used to determine eligibility for housing allowance, income from employment should be determined based on individual-level data in employer declarations and simplified employer declarations. The income used to determine eligibility should correspond to the insured person's average monthly income within a defined period. This period should be three months, specifically the first three months of a four-month period immediately preceding the month for which the housing allowance is being applied. The income used to determine eligibility in the new monthly system should be calculated based on the same types of income as in the current annual system, with the exception of income from business activities. Income from capital and assets should refer to the previous year, as this type of information cannot be obtained on a monthly basis.

The income used to determine eligibility should continue to be calculated on an annual basis if the insured person has income from employment but there is a lack of information in employer declarations, for example, if the insured person is engaged in business activities. In certain cases involving international connections, the income used to determine eligibility should also be calculated on an annual basis.

A housing allowance in the monthly system should be recalculated when there has been a change in the income used to determine eligibility that affects the amount of the allowance for the current month.

The proposed amendments to the law are scheduled to come into effect on January 1, 2027.
