The Swedish Migration Agency will participate in Almedalsveckan (Almedal Week) in Visby. During the week, the agency will host two of its own seminars, participate in several external events, and meet key stakeholders for discussions on current issues related to migration and societal development.

During Almedalsveckan, the Swedish Migration Agency will be involved in a total of 14 program segments. A particular focus this year is the implementation of the EU's migration and asylum pact. Other relevant topics on the agenda include: preparedness of authorities, labor market exploitation, artificial intelligence (AI), and LGBTQI+ issues.

The Swedish Migration Agency's programs have been developed jointly by the Executive Staff and the Communications Department. According to Anna Lindblad, Chief of Staff at the Executive Staff, participating in Almedalsveckan is an important part of the agency's mission.

"As a specialized authority, it is important that we participate in the public discussion about migration. There are extensive changes taking place in the field of migration, not least through the EU's migration and asylum pact. Therefore, we need to contribute our knowledge, experience, and perspective to the issues being discussed," said Anna Lindblad, Chief of Staff at the Executive Staff.
