In plain language

The Swedish National Audit Office is an agency that reviews how other government agencies operate. They conducted an investigation into how the Swedish Social Insurance Agency (Försäkringskassan) and the Swedish Pensions Agency control that the right people receive payments when moving between different countries. The National Audit Office has provided suggestions for improvements to several agencies, including the Swedish Migration Agency (Migrationsverket) and the Swedish Tax Agency (Skatteverket).

The government has now responded to the report. They believe the review is valuable because it highlights areas that need improvement. The goal is to reduce the number of people who receive incorrect payments from the state and to ensure that people have confidence in our welfare systems, such as health insurance and pensions.

The government states that they are already working to improve the agencies' controls. They have also been working to make it easier for agencies to share information with each other. Therefore, the government does not believe that they need to make any major changes at this time based on the report's recommendations. They therefore consider the matter closed.

Full text

In this document, the government presents its assessment of the observations made by the Swedish National Audit Office (Riksrevisionen) in the report "Social Security Coverage in the Context of International Mobility – Controls by the Swedish Social Insurance Agency and the Swedish Pensions Agency" (RiR 2025:14).

The Swedish National Audit Office provides a recommendation to the government and a number of recommendations to the Swedish Social Insurance Agency, the Swedish Pensions Agency, the Swedish Migration Agency, and the Swedish Tax Agency.

The government welcomes the Swedish National Audit Office's review and notes that it makes an important contribution to highlighting areas for improvement, so that the extent of incorrect payments can be reduced and the legitimacy of the welfare system can be strengthened. Given the measures that the government has taken and intends to take to strengthen the authorities' controls and their ability to collect and exchange information, the government believes that there is currently no reason to conduct a review in response to the recommendation made by the Swedish National Audit Office.

With this document, the government considers the review report to be finalized.
