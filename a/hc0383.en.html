Approvals and consents within the area of excise duties in the event of bankruptcy.

TL;DRThe government intends to amend the laws regarding taxes on energy, tobacco, and alcohol, so that important permits and rights will transfer to the bankruptcy estate when a company goes bankrupt. This also applies to certain regulations concerning taxes on chemicals used in electronics.
In plain language

When a company goes bankrupt, a bankruptcy estate is created to manage the company's remaining assets. The government is now proposing new rules that stipulate that certain permits and approvals should be transferred to this bankruptcy estate. This concerns permits to ship energy, tobacco, and alcohol without paying tax directly.

The change also applies to the right to receive a refund of paid electricity tax. If a company that is entitled to a refund of electricity tax goes bankrupt, this right will remain within the bankruptcy estate. Similarly, permits to ship tobacco and alcohol as a registered sender will be transferred to the bankruptcy estate.

The proposal also includes new rules on how to amend or appeal decisions regarding the refund of taxes on chemicals in certain electronic products. The purpose is to clarify what happens to these tax regulations when a company is no longer able to pay its debts.

If the parliament approves the proposal, the new rules will come into effect on July 1, 2025. This means that the rules regarding taxes on energy, tobacco, alcohol, and electronics will become more predictable in the event of a bankruptcy.

Full text

The proposal suggests amendments to the law (1994:1776) regarding taxes on energy, introducing provisions stating that approvals for registered dispatchers, tax-exempt consumers, and those voluntarily subject to tax, as well as approvals for special handling of provisions for ships at sea, will transfer to the bankruptcy estate if the holder of the approval becomes bankrupt. It also proposes a provision in the same law allowing for the possibility of applying for a refund of electricity tax for a shorter period than the standard period, which would also transfer to the bankruptcy estate if the recipient of the approval becomes bankrupt.

Furthermore, the proposal suggests adding a provision to the law (2022:155) on tobacco tax, stating that an approval as a registered dispatcher will transfer to the bankruptcy estate if the holder of the approval becomes bankrupt. The same is proposed for the law (2022:156) on alcohol tax, regarding approvals for registered dispatchers and tax-exempt consumers.

In addition, the proposal includes specific provisions regarding the review and appeal of certain decisions regarding refunds according to the law (2016:1067) on taxes on chemicals in certain electronics.

The proposed changes are intended to take effect on July 1, 2025.

Proposed Parliamentary Decisions

  2. The Parliament approves the government's proposal for an amendment to the law (2016:1067) regarding taxes on chemicals in certain electronics.
  3. The Parliament approves the government's proposal for an amendment to the law (2022:155) regarding tobacco tax.
  4. The Parliament approves the government's proposal for an amendment to the law (2022:156) regarding alcohol tax.
