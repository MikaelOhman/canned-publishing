In plain language

The Swedish National Audit Office, the agency responsible for overseeing how the state uses funds, has conducted an audit. They have identified issues related to the social security system potentially making incorrect payments to individuals with income from abroad. This is referred to as social security for foreign income.

To address the problem, the National Audit Office has proposed improvements. They have provided recommendations to the government and to three key agencies: the Swedish Social Insurance Agency (Försäkringskassan), the Swedish Pensions Agency (Pensionsmyndigheten), and the Swedish Tax Agency (Skatteverket).

The government believes that the audit is important for preventing social security fraud and incorrect payments. Therefore, the government has now assigned tasks to the three agencies. They are now required to work more closely together and to conduct more thorough checks on payments.

With this decision, the government believes that it has addressed the report and that the matter is now closed.

Full text

In this document, the government presents its assessment of the observations made by the Swedish National Audit Office (Riksrevisionen) in the report "Social Security for Foreign Income – Significant Risk of Incorrect Payments" (RiR 2024:16).

The Swedish National Audit Office has reviewed whether the state's efforts to prevent incorrect payments from social security for foreign income are effective. The Swedish National Audit Office provides two recommendations to the government, as well as a number of recommendations to the Swedish Social Insurance Agency (Försäkringskassan), the Swedish Pensions Agency (Pensionsmyndigheten), and the Swedish Tax Agency (Skatteverket).

The government welcomes the Swedish National Audit Office's review and notes that it makes an important contribution to the work of combating social security fraud and incorrect payments. In response to the recommendations made by the Swedish National Audit Office, the government has issued a mandate for strengthened cooperation and control to the Swedish Social Insurance Agency, the Swedish Pensions Agency, and the Swedish Tax Agency.

With this document, the government considers the review report to be finalized.
