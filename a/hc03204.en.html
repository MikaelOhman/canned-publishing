Rules on the suspension of state employees

Source Riksdagen Read the original at Riksdagen →2025-09-08
TL;DRNew regulations are being proposed that would allow government employees to be temporarily suspended from their jobs for a short period. This would be possible if the employee is at risk of being fired or laid off.
In plain language

The government wants to change the law to allow for the suspension of employees working in the public sector. A suspension may occur if an employee has done something that could lead to them being dismissed from their position. A suspension can last for a maximum of two months at a time and will take effect immediately.

The employee should continue to receive their salary and other benefits during the suspension, unless other provisions in the union agreement state otherwise. If the employee is a member of a trade union, the employer must inform the union about the suspension. The union also has the right to meet with the employer to discuss the decision.

If an authority makes a mistake in the process, for example, by failing to inform the union, they may be liable to pay damages. It should also be possible to request a court to quickly review whether the decision to suspend the employee is correct.

These new rules are intended to come into effect on January 1, 2026. The proposal involves changes to several laws that govern government agencies, such as the National Bank (Riksbanken) and the National Audit Office (Riksrevisionen).

Full text

The proposal suggests that regulations regarding the suspension of civil servants should be incorporated into the Act (1994:260) on Public Employment. It proposes that an employee may be suspended in certain situations that have led to, or may lead to, termination or dismissal. Furthermore, it is proposed that a decision to suspend an employee should take effect immediately and for a specified period, which may not exceed two months at a time. During the suspension period, the employee should retain their salary and other employment benefits, unless otherwise stipulated in collective agreements.

Certain provisions regarding employers' obligation to negotiate should not apply prior to a decision to suspend an employee. If the employee is a member of a trade union, the employer must notify the relevant trade union organization of the suspension. The trade union organization should also have the right to consult with the employer. A decision to suspend an employee may be subject to an interim review by a court, and there is a right to compensation if the authority, in a case involving suspension, violates certain procedural rules or the obligation to notify the trade union organization.

The new rules are proposed to come into effect on January 1, 2026.

  5. The Parliament adopts the Government's proposal for an amendment to the Act (2022:1568) on the Sveriges Riksbank (Sweden's Central Bank).
