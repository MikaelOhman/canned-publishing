Anna Vasa, also known as Anna of Sweden, was born on May 17, 1568, at Eskilstuna royal estate, and died on February 26, 1625, in Brodnica, Royal Prussia. She was a Swedish princess.

== Biography ==

=== Early Life === Anna was the daughter of King John III and Catherine Jagiellonica. She was born shortly after her parents were released from prison at Gripsholm Castle, and the following year, her father ascended to the throne. There is little information about her childhood. Anna was raised as a Catholic. According to Johannes Herbestus, she participated in her mother's Catholic devotions along with her brother, without any objections from her father. After her mother's death in 1583, Anna converted to Lutheranism. According to tradition, the reason for this conversion was: When Catherine Jagiellonica was near death, she was very concerned about the torments of purgatory. Her confessor, the Jesuit Warsewitz, comforted her and told her that there was no purgatory; it was just a fabricated story meant to warn simple people. The queen became upset and drove the Jesuit away. Princess Anna stood behind her mother's bed and witnessed everything, and from that moment on, according to the Lutheran account, she developed a lifelong aversion to the lies of the papists. In reality, there is nothing to confirm that she was offended by Stanislaw Warszewicki's religious discussions with her mother. However, Anna did convert after her mother's death in 1584.

Anna was the subject of several marriage proposals. In 1577, papal diplomacy proposed a marriage to an Austrian Archduke, Matthias or Maximilian. This plan was supported by her aunt, Queen Anna Jagiellonica of Poland, and after her mother's death in 1583, she suggested that Anna be sent to Poland for upbringing. However, John III refused. In 1585, her great-aunt Cecilia Vasa proposed arranging a marriage with a Catholic prince, but the father declined, citing that Anna had converted to Protestantism the year before. It is confirmed that Anna had her own court at least during this time: after her mother's death, she reportedly took over her mother's lady-in-waiting, Karin Göransdotter (Gyllenstierna), and in 1585, her chamberlain Johan Larsson Sparre and Axel Posse were mentioned; Sigrid Eriksdotter Vasa was among her ladies-in-waiting.

In 1587, Anna's brother, Sigismund, was elected King of Poland by his aunt, Queen Anna Jagiellonica. The aunt repeated her invitation to Anna, and this time, the father gave his consent, reportedly because Sigismund had a close relationship with his sister and he did not want to separate them, and also because "the time should not be too long for your dear brother." Anna left Sweden with Sigismund on September 12, 1587, and followed him to Kraków in Poland, where she attended his coronation and stayed until 1589. During her time at the Polish court, she and her Swedish ladies-in-waiting celebrated Lutheran services, which caused disapproval.

In 1589, Anna accompanied Sigismund to meet her father in Reval (Tallinn) in Swedish Estonia, and then followed her father back to Sweden. She attended the council meeting in Reval, where her father clashed with the council members, and she later acted as a mediator during the conflict: Erik Larsson Sparre appealed to her to intervene against the persecutions.

After the meeting in Reval, rumors circulated that John III was considering appointing Anna as his successor to the Swedish throne.

In 1592, Anna returned to Poland to attend the wedding of Sigismund and Anna of Austria. She was accompanied by her own court, led by Johan Larsson Sparre and Margareta Brahe. During this time, she became unpopular in Poland due to her religion and the political influence she was believed to have over Sigismund. She was suspected of supporting her father's previous plan for a marriage between Sigismund and the Protestant Kristina of Holstein-Gottorp. In 1592, Anna was attacked as part of Sigismund's "entourage," and Archbishop Stanislaw Karnkowski was so provoked by her Lutheran services that he threatened both Anna and Sigismund with excommunication. Her sister-in-law, Anna of Austria, prevented any open actions against her. In July 1593, she carried Sigismund's daughter, Anna Maria, to her christening. Anna was proposed as a bride by Cardinal Batori for the Prince of Siebenbürgen.

=== Anna's Role in the Conflict Between Sigismund and Duke Charles ===

Anna returned to Sweden with Sigismund and his wife in September 1593. During this time, she was viewed with suspicion by Malaspina due to her close relationship with her brother, combined with her Protestant beliefs. In November 1593, during the scandalous brawl between Catholics and Protestants at the funeral of the Polish court musician Sowka in Riddarholmskyrkan (Riddarholm Church), her court preacher, Olaus Simonis, participated on the Lutheran side. In February 1594, she visited Duke Charles in Uppsala. She was present during Ericus Schepperus's anti-Catholic sermon. When Sigismund returned to Poland in 1594, Anna remained in Sweden. Malaspina had persuaded Sigismund to leave her behind, citing the incident at Riddarholmskyrkan and reminding him of Karnkowski's threat of excommunication against "her."
