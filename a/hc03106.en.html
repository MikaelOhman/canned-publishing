In plain language

The government has decided that Sweden will join an international agreement. This agreement concerns the creation and operation of the Square Kilometre Array Observatory, a major project to study space using radio telescopes.

To ensure this collaboration functions effectively, the government proposes that the parliament amends an existing law. This law concerns so-called immunity and privileges. This means that certain individuals working on the project will have special protection and certain benefits while in Sweden.

These rights are not limited to the organization itself. Individuals working there, such as the director general, their family members, and experts appointed by the organization, are also covered by the new regulations.

The government signed the agreement in January 2025. Now, they are awaiting the parliament's approval of the amendment to the law so that Sweden can formally become part of this international collaboration.

Full text

On October 17, 2024, the government decided – subject to ratification – to sign the convention of March 12, 2019, establishing the Square Kilometre Array Observatory. This signing took place on January 21, 2025. This proposal suggests that the Swedish Parliament should approve Sweden's accession to the convention. According to the convention, the organization and certain individuals are to be granted certain immunities and privileges. Therefore, it is proposed that the Parliament decide on an amendment to the law (1976:661) concerning immunities and privileges in certain cases. The law amendment is proposed to come into force on the date determined by the government.
