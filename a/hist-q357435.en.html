Sverker the Elder was King of Sweden from the beginning of the 1130s until his death in 1156. He used marriages to strengthen his kingdom and gain support from other countries, such as Norway and Poland. Through these marriages, he established important connections with other powerful families.

During his reign, he was involved in both church construction and warfare. He had a church renovated and founded a monastery at Alvastra. In the 1150s, he participated in a war against Denmark, which resulted in victory for his side, leading to the selection of a new king in Denmark.

Sverker died as a result of being murdered by his own guards while on his way to church on Christmas Day in 1156. There are various historical sources that describe his family and children, but because these texts are very old, some details about his lineage are not entirely certain.

Sverker the Elder, who was murdered on December 25, 1156, was King of Sweden. According to the royal genealogy in the Westrogothic Law, compiled around 1240, his father's name was Cornube and he came from Östergötland. However, according to the Icelandic "Langfeðgatal," Sverker's father was instead named Erik Årsäll.

== Biography == Sverker the Elder is the Swedish king who gave his name to the Sverker dynasty. He was recognized as the king of Svealand in the early 12th century, and also as the king of Götaland from around 1134, ruling until his death in 1156. Sverker tended to marry individuals who would strengthen and stabilize his fragile kingdom. For example, there is a tradition that he had a previous wife who supposedly ran away from him and may have been the mother of his son, Kol.

His first known wife was Ulvhild Håkansdotter, a Norwegian noblewoman and widow of Inge the Younger. This marriage created strong ties between Sverker and Norway, and also connected him to the lineage of Inge the Younger. Later, he married Rikissa of Poland. This marriage ensured that he would be accepted as king in Västergötland.

Sverker and Ulvhild's names are associated with the founding of Sweden's oldest Cistercian monastery. This was established in 1143 at Alvastra, near Omberg, on land that Ulvhild received as a dowry from her husband. According to a tradition recorded in the 18th century, Sverker built the new Kaga church on the site where an older burial chapel had been built by either his grandfather or his father, Cornube.

In the 1150s, Sverker is said to have come into conflict with Denmark. Fortunately for Sverker, the people of Småland repelled the Danish king's attack, and this turned the conflict in such a way that he was able to secure the recognition of his stepson, the Danish heir apparent Knut, as the King of the Danes in 1154.

Sverker the Elder is said to have been murdered by his own retinue (according to the aforementioned royal genealogy, by his groom) on his way to Christmas Mass at Västra Tollstads church in 1156. He was subsequently buried at Alvastra monastery. The possible instigator of the murder was the Danish prince and claimant to the throne, Magnus Henriksson.

== Children == Different modern sources provide varying lists of Sverker's children; the only ones generally accepted are Johan and Karl. The identity of their mother also varies according to different accounts. However, the following children are mentioned in various sources:

  • Johan
  • A daughter with an unknown name, possibly Helena, who married King Knud V of Denmark in 1156.
  • Karl
  • Ingegerd
  • Sune Sik; this information comes from a medieval genealogy, but Natanael Beckman has suggested that it may be a fabricated tradition surrounding a benefactor to Vreta Monastery. Other historians consider him to be a historical figure.
  • Burislev. According to Valdemar Sejr's land register, he had a son with this name.
  • Kol. In the medieval sources available, he is only mentioned as the son of Johan, but later historians have still assumed that he was the son of Sverker.
