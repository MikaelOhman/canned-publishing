TL;DRStarting July 1, 2026, several new regulations will be implemented concerning taxes, electric vehicle charging, and family law. These changes include measures that will make it easier for the Swedish Tax Agency (Skatteverket) to prevent fraud, while certain taxes on fuel and alcohol will be reduced.
In plain language

Starting July 1, 2026, the Swedish Tax Agency (Skatteverket) will have improved tools to monitor businesses. It will become more difficult to commit fraud related to Value Added Tax (VAT), which is a tax applied to goods and services, as the Tax Agency can require owners to present identification documents and access more information digitally.

New regulations regarding electric vehicle charging are also being introduced. Starting in July 2026, it will be completely tax-free to charge an electric car or plug-in hybrid at one's workplace. Additionally, taxes on alcoholic beverages from small producers, as well as certain types of fuel for agriculture and boats, will be reduced during parts of the year.

New rules are also being implemented for individuals. Those living near wind turbines may, in some cases, receive compensation for disturbances, and the tax on attending dance events will be lowered. Furthermore, stricter regulations regarding marriage are being introduced; it will become illegal to marry cousins or other close relatives.

Finally, changes are being made to the rules for individuals convicted of imprisonment. Starting July 3rd, certain individuals may serve portions of their sentences abroad, but they must remain registered in the Swedish national registry (the register where people are officially registered as residents), even if they are located outside the country.

At the end of the first half of the year, several new laws will come into effect in the areas of taxation and population registration. The Swedish Tax Agency will, among other things, have better opportunities to combat VAT fraud, the benefit of charging an electric car at work will become permanently tax-free, and those who live near wind power plants may be eligible for compensation.

Starting July 1st, the Swedish Tax Agency (Skatteverket) will have greater authority to conduct checks when a company registers for VAT. In some cases, this may require the owner to visit a Skatteverket office and provide identification. The agency also gains increased power to deny or revoke VAT registrations in order to prevent fraud.

The ban on conducting audits via telecommunications networks is being lifted, meaning the Tax Agency can now perform audits remotely during inspections, evidence gathering, and visits related to excise duties. Furthermore, the Tax Agency will have new opportunities to search for and copy documents from readable information systems.

The carbon tax on gasoline and unmarked diesel will be temporarily reduced from July 1st to November 30th, 2026. Additionally, corresponding adjustments will be made to the reduction for taxes on diesel used in agricultural machinery, ships, and certain boats in professional agriculture, forestry, and aquaculture operations, lasting from July 1st to December 31st, 2026.

On July 1st, the alcohol tax will be reduced for alcoholic beverages produced by independent small producers.

According to a new law, individuals living near wind power plants may, in certain cases, receive compensation equivalent to a portion of the plant's annual revenue. If this compensation applies to a private residence, it is exempt from tax. The law takes effect on July 1st.

The benefit of charging an electric vehicle at work will become permanently tax-free. Individuals who have a plug-in hybrid as a company car and charge it with electricity at work should be entitled to the same fuel expense deduction for business trips as those who pay for all fuel themselves. These laws are effective from July 1st.
