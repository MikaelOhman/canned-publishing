TL;DRThe government wants to eliminate the system of special accounts at the Payment Agency. The purpose is for the agency to focus solely on preventing incorrect payments of money.
In plain language

The government has proposed changes to several laws. This proposal aims to abolish the system known as the "transaction account" within the government agency, the Payment Agency. This would require the agency to restructure its operations to focus solely on its core mission.

The Payment Agency's primary task is to ensure that no incorrect payments are made. They are responsible for preventing, deterring, and detecting instances where money is paid out improperly. To achieve this, the government proposes amendments to laws concerning the handling of information and the protection of personal data.

The proposal also includes changes to laws regarding the information that must be provided to the agency and how they are permitted to use personal data. If the parliament approves the proposal, the new regulations will come into effect on January 1, 2027.

Full text

This proposal suggests legislative changes to phase out the Payment Agency's system of transaction accounts. These changes aim to streamline the agency's operations, focusing on its core mission of preventing, deterring, and detecting improper payments. Furthermore, a number of legislative changes are proposed as a result of the system's discontinuation.

The proposed legislative changes are scheduled to take effect on January 1, 2027.

Prop. 2025/26:277

Government's proposal:

  1. The Parliament approves the government's proposal for a law repealing the law (2023:454) on transaction accounts at the Payment Agency.
  2. The Parliament approves the government's proposal for an amendment to the Public Access and Secrecy Act (2009:400).
  3. The Parliament approves the government's proposal for an amendment to the law (2023:456) on the obligation to provide information to the Payment Agency.
  4. The Parliament approves the government's proposal for an amendment to the law (2023:457) on the processing of personal data at the Payment Agency.
