Full text

In this document, the government provides an update on the progress made in combating organized crime since the national strategy "Resilience and Action – A National Strategy Against Organized Crime" (report 2023/24:67) was submitted to the Parliament in February 2024. The government has actively and systematically worked to strengthen protection against crime, in line with the strategy's objectives of stopping criminal careers, reducing the availability of illegal weapons and explosives, disrupting the criminal economy, building resilience against undue influence, and ensuring reliable identities and effective information sharing. This work has been carried out both nationally and within the EU, as well as in other international forums. Overall, the government believes that Sweden's ability to actively combat organized crime has significantly improved and will continue to improve through the work being carried out within the framework of the strategy. However, the societal threat posed by the prevalence of organized crime remains. Therefore, it is important to continue working along the established path in a long-term and sustainable manner.
