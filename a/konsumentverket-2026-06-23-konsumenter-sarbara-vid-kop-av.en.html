They have found that many companies are not complying with the law because they provide insufficient information about the risks associated with these procedures. This is particularly dangerous for young women, who often see a lot of advertising on social media.

The Consumer Agency is working with other authorities, such as the Swedish Medical Products Agency and the Swedish Inspectorate for Healthcare and Welfare (IVO), to address these problems. They believe it is important that customers receive clear information so they can make informed decisions about their health.

Consumers vulnerable when undergoing cosmetic procedures. News published: June 23, 2026.

The Swedish Consumer Agency's work shows that many companies marketing cosmetic surgery and aesthetic injection treatments are violating regulations. Young women are particularly vulnerable, as they encounter a great deal of advertising for these procedures on social media. The agency was tasked by the government before 2026 to follow up on previous investigations in this area. Now, the agency has submitted its report to the government, which reveals that the industry continues to be problematic. Many consumers are exposed to misleading advertisements and often receive necessary information about risks too late.

Young women as a target group: The agency notes that young women are particularly susceptible and vulnerable to advertising for cosmetic procedures on social media. At the same time, the Consumer Agency observes that marketing is frequently targeted at this demographic.

"Cosmetic procedures carry serious risks that can be difficult for consumers to understand," says lawyer Maja Rogemyr. "It is therefore very important that consumers receive clear information so they can make informed decisions about the procedure. Currently, many consumers risk being misled and unknowingly exposed to health risks."

The Consumer Agency has collaborated with several stakeholders, including the Swedish Medical Products Agency, the Swedish Inspectorate for Healthcare and Welfare (IVO), and the National Association of Aesthetic Medicine, in preparing the report.

Many companies violate the law: The agency states that many companies are violating marketing laws. However, it can be difficult to address the widespread problems within the industry solely through oversight of advertising. Some improvements were made when the so-called "beauty law" was introduced in 2021. This included setting a minimum age of 18 for these procedures.

The government will assess: The Consumer Agency is submitting this report to the government, which will assess whether an investigation is needed to analyze whether special regulations are necessary for the marketing of cosmetic procedures to ensure adequate protection for consumers' health.
