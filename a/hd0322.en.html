The rules stem from international cooperation between the OECD and G20 to prevent companies from avoiding taxes.

Full text

The government proposes further amendments to the regulations regarding the supplementary tax for companies in large groups. Law (2023:875) on the supplementary tax implements the directive on ensuring a global minimum tax level for multinational groups and large national groups within the European Union. The purpose of the directive is to implement the model rules on a global minimum tax developed within the framework of the OECD/G20 Inclusive Framework on Base Erosion and Profit Shifting (IF). The model rules are supplemented by explanations and examples in the commentary to the model rules. Furthermore, the IF has adopted administrative guidelines with supplements to the commentary, which aim to further clarify the interpretation and application of the rules. Additional administrative guidelines were adopted on May 24, 2024, and published in June 2024. To the extent that these are clarifications and examples, the guidelines are already covered by the provisions in the law on the supplementary tax. However, the law needs to be supplemented with additional or amended provisions.

The proposed amendments are intended to come into force on January 1, 2026. However, there is a possibility for the reporting entity to request that all or some of the provisions be applied for the first time to tax years beginning closest after December 31, 2023, or, in the case of Swedish partnerships, accounting periods beginning closest after December 31, 2023.
