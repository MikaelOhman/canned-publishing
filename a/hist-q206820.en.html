Anders Leonard Zorn (pronounced [so:rn]), born February 18, 1860, in the village of Utmelands in Mora, and died August 22, 1920, in Mora, Dalarna, was a Swedish artist.

He is considered a significant artist in Sweden and was also appreciated abroad, particularly in the United States, where he was commissioned as a portrait painter by presidents, business leaders, and members of high society.

== Biography ==

=== Origins and Parents ===

Anders Zorn was born at Grudd farm in Yvraden, Utmeland, Mora, following a brief romance between Anna Andersdotter of Grudd and the German brewmaster Leonard Zorn from Bavaria. The parents met when the mother, as a 20-year-old, worked as a bottle washer at the von Düben & Co brewery in Uppsala, owned by Baron Henrik von Düben. Leonard Zorn spent his last years in Helsinki, where he died on December 26, 1872. Zorn never met his father but later adopted his surname.

Because Zorn's mother lived in Uppsala, he spent his early years with his maternal grandparents. With the help of a small inheritance from his father, he was able to attend Enköping Upper Secondary School starting at the age of twelve. There, he learned to speak standard Swedish, as the local dialect of Mora was spoken at that time. He also received his first lessons in drawing, and his teachers soon recognized the young Zorn's artistic talent. Sketches of classmates, paintings of Enköping, and woodcuts of people and horses by Zorn are still preserved in the Zorn collections in Mora.

=== Education === In 1875, Zorn began studying at the Royal Academy of Fine Arts in Stockholm. This became possible thanks to the inheritance from his father, as well as a smaller sum of money from the German brewing association in Stockholm. Zorn initially worked with wood but soon switched to painting. In an exhibition in 1880, he displayed the watercolor "In Sorrow," which received considerable attention in artistic circles. Zorn also demonstrated great skill in portrait painting and received numerous commissions from wealthy Swedish families. During one of these assignments, he met his future wife, Emma Lamm.

=== Activities === After 1881, Zorn traveled extensively, primarily to England and Spain. He drew inspiration for his travels from paintings by his role model, Egron Lundgren. During these trips, he studied things like movement and reflections on water surfaces. In Madrid, in 1885, he created the work "Kärleksnymf" (Love Nymph), which marked a breakthrough for him. He also received significant commissions while there, including from Spanish and Portuguese nobles, which improved his financial situation.

Upon returning to Sweden, he had a civil marriage on October 16, 1885, with Emma Amalia Lamm. They settled in Lisselby, near Mora. During their honeymoon in Hungary and Turkey, Zorn contracted typhoid fever, and it took him three months to fully recover before they could continue their journey.

Even in later years, Zorn made several trips to Spain, North Africa, and England. During the 1890s in Paris, he spent a lot of time with the Finnish artist Albert Edelfelt. In 1893, Zorn traveled to the United States to paint portraits of leading politicians and business figures. The portrait of President Grover Cleveland became very well-known. Later, Zorn also painted portraits of Presidents Theodore Roosevelt (1905) and William Howard Taft (1911).

In the summer of 1897, Zorn traveled to Russia, where he was warmly received. He stayed in St. Petersburg and Moscow, where he became particularly good friends with the artists Ilya Repin and Konstantin Korovin, at whose home he mostly resided.

Back in Sweden, he mainly created landscape paintings and also made some nude studies. He modeled sculptures such as Nymph and Faun, Morning Bath, and a statue of Gustav Vasa. Anders Zorn died at Mora Hospital on August 22, 1920, from pyelonephritis that led to sepsis, with an infection in the abdominal wall, and emphysema. He is buried in the old cemetery in Mora. According to his will, his legacy was bequeathed to the Swedish state to establish a museum. His and his wife Emma's home, Zorngården, became a museum after Emma Zorn's death in 1942 and is part of the Zorn Collections.

=== Anders Zorn's photographs ===

During his career, Zorn took many photographs. These photographs could be used as models for other works. In 2015, Fotografiska held an exhibition featuring a selection of these images.
