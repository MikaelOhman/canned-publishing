In plain language

The Swedish National Audit Office is an agency that monitors how the state uses money and complies with regulations. They have written a report on how estates are managed, which refers to the assets and liabilities remaining after a person's death. The National Audit Office wants the rules to be changed so that the process is faster and more efficient for those involved.

An important part of the proposal is that estate inventories should be able to be submitted digitally. An estate inventory is a document that shows what the deceased owned and what they owed. Currently, the system is not modern enough, and the government wants to enable the use of electronic services to submit these documents.

The government agrees that much needs to be improved. They have already begun working on new legislative proposals that make it possible for both individuals and companies to submit information digitally. They are also working on reviewing the Inheritance Act, which is the law that determines how inheritances are distributed.

In addition to digitalization, the National Audit Office wants to have better control over the individuals who manage estates. They also want to make it easier to resolve conflicts and disputes about goods or services without having to go to court.

Full text

In this document, the government presents its assessment of the observations and recommendations made by the Swedish National Audit Office (Riksrevisionen) in the report "The State's Efforts in Handling Estates – Investigation, Administration, and Distribution" (RiR 2025:23).

The Swedish National Audit Office recommends that the government review the current regulations for the investigation, administration, and distribution of estates as a whole, with the aim of enabling a swift and efficient handling of estates while safeguarding the interests of those involved. According to the Swedish National Audit Office, this review should include how the control and supervision of estate administrators' management can be designed and organized to be more effective, how regulations for estates purchasing services that involve the investigation, administration, and distribution of estates can be strengthened, and how estates can be given greater opportunities for alternative dispute resolution in disputes regarding goods and services. The Swedish National Audit Office further recommends that the government take the necessary measures to enable the electronic submission of estate inventories.

The government welcomes the Swedish National Audit Office's review and agrees with several of its observations and assessments. The government shares the assessment that estate inventories should be able to be submitted electronically and has recently decided on a proposal with draft amendments to laws that enable individuals and professional actors to submit both estate inventories and estate notifications electronically. Within the Government Offices, proposals are being prepared for modernizing important parts of the Inheritance Act.

With this document, the government considers the Swedish National Audit Office's report to be finalized.
