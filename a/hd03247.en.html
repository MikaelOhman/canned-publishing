In plain language

The government has proposed changes to the regulations regarding the sale of medicines. The goal is to allow more medicines to be purchased without a prescription, but with an important requirement. When you purchase these medicines, the pharmacist at the pharmacy must provide you with specific advice on how to use the medicine safely.

This additional assistance is necessary to protect the health of people and animals. It is intended, among other things, to reduce the risk of medicines becoming ineffective, a phenomenon known as antimicrobial resistance. The Swedish Medical Products Agency, the authority responsible for medicines in Sweden, will be able to decide which medicines should be subject to this special counseling requirement.

It is important to know that these medicines can only be sold at regular pharmacies where staff are present. They cannot be sold through pharmacy franchises, which are locations that are not actual pharmacies but assist with sales. This is to ensure that you always receive the correct information when purchasing the medicine.

If the Parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new regulations will come into effect on January 1, 2027. The proposal is the result of an agreement between the government and the political party the Sweden Democrats.

Full text

This proposal includes suggestions aimed at promoting access to medicines while ensuring the safe, effective, and responsible use of drugs. The proposals would allow more medicines to be classified as over-the-counter (OTC) under the condition that pharmacists provide specific guidance during the sale. The medicines that could be considered for this classification are those where guidance is deemed necessary to ensure patient safety, the safety of animals, or the safety of the person administering the medicine, or to reduce the risk of negative effects on public health or animal health, particularly the development of drug resistance.

It is proposed that the Swedish Medical Products Agency (MPA) should be able to decide, upon application, that a medicine should be available without a prescription, but with a requirement for specific guidance. It is proposed that community pharmacies should be obligated to provide this guidance during the sale of such medicines. Over-the-counter medicines requiring specific guidance should not be sold through pharmacy intermediaries.

The proposed amendments to the law are expected to come into force on January 1, 2027.

This proposal is based on an agreement between the government and the Sweden Democrats party.
