In plain language

The government has proposed a change to the law regarding gambling. The new proposal states that those who are licensed to operate gambling services or sell gambling products, known as licensees and gaming agents, are not allowed to allow customers to gamble with money they have borrowed.

Those who offer gambling services must also do everything they can to ensure that no one uses credit to gamble. This applies to all types of legal gambling in Sweden, whether it is played online or in a physical location.

There is a possibility to obtain an exemption from this rule, but the authority, the Swedish Gambling Authority, must approve it. The Swedish Gambling Authority is the authority that ensures that everyone complies with the rules for gambling in Sweden.

If the Parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new rules will come into effect on April 1, 2026.

Full text

In this proposal, the government suggests that a ban be introduced in the Gambling Act (2018:1138) prohibiting licensees and gaming agents from allowing or participating in gambling financed with credit. Licensees and gaming agents must also take appropriate measures to prevent gambling from being financed with credit. This regulation expands the existing credit ban in the Gambling Act, which currently prohibits licensees and gaming agents from offering or providing credit for gambling stakes. The purpose of the new regulation is to prevent indebtedness resulting from gambling. The requirements will apply to all forms of gambling requiring a license, regardless of how the game is provided. However, the Gambling Authority may decide to grant exemptions from the prohibitions for certain licensed operators with public benefit purposes.

The proposed amendments are to come into force on April 1, 2026.
