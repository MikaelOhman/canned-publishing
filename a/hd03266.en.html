Full text

In this document, the government presents its work environment strategy for 2026–2031. The work environment strategy outlines the government's long-term policy direction for this area in the coming years. The document explains how the strategy will contribute to achieving established goals, how it will be implemented and monitored, and how it will be evaluated.

The government's work environment strategy aims to clarify and strengthen efforts to achieve the goal for work policy set by the Parliament, which is to promote good working conditions and opportunities for development in the workplace for both women and men, as well as the government's goal of a work environment that prevents ill health and accidents, that counteracts people being excluded from the workforce, that takes into account people's different circumstances, and that contributes to the development of both individuals and organizations (prop. 2020/21:1 utg.omr. 14, section 5.1).

The sub-goals of the work environment strategy are as follows:

  • A sustainable working life – everyone should be able, willing, and have the opportunity to work throughout their working life.
  • A healthy working life – the working environment should contribute to health, development, and well-being.
  • A safe working life – no one should risk their safety, life, or health due to their work.
  • An innovative working life – good working environments contribute to the change, development, and transformation of individuals, the working environment, and society.
