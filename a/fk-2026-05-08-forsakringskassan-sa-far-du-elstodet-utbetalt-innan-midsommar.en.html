Swedish Social Insurance Agency: How to get the electricity support paid out before Midsummer

Source Försäkringskassan Read the original at Försäkringskassan →2026-05-08
TL;DRMost people who are eligible for electricity support will receive the money in their bank accounts in time for Midsummer. To receive the money more quickly, you should register your account with Swedbank by May 25th.
In plain language

The Swedish Social Insurance Agency (Försäkringskassan), a government agency that manages various types of grants and support, will begin distributing electricity subsidies in early June. Most people who are eligible for the funds will receive them in their bank accounts before Midsummer.

If you want to ensure a quick payment, you can register your bank account with Swedbank's registry by May 25th. If you have already registered your account previously, no further action is required. However, if you delay registering your account, it may take a few extra days for the funds to arrive.

If you do not register an account with Swedbank, the Swedish Social Insurance Agency will attempt to locate your information through other government agencies or send you a payment voucher. A voucher is a document that indicates you have funds available for collection from the bank.

It is important to note that no one from the Swedish Social Insurance Agency or any bank will call or contact you to request your personal information. If someone contacts you claiming to be from the agency and asks for your details, it is likely a scam attempt, and you should not provide any information.

May 8, 2026

Försäkringskassan will begin making electricity support payments for January-February 2026 in early June, and a few banking days later, the money will start appearing in customers' accounts. By Midsummer, most electricity customers who are eligible for the support will have received the funds.

Försäkringskassan automatically pays the electricity support to customers who are entitled to it. To ensure that the payment is as quick and smooth as possible, you should register your bank account with Swedbank's registry by May 25th. Even Swedbank customers should check that they have an account registered in the registry.

If you have already registered an account with Swedbank for previous electricity support payments, you do not need to take any further action.

It is possible to register your account even after May 25th to speed up the process, but this may delay the payment by a few more days. If you do not register an account with Swedbank's registry, there is no need to worry. If we cannot find an account at Swedbank, Försäkringskassan will check if there is a bank account registered with Försäkringskassan or Pensionsmyndigheten and will pay the electricity support to that account. If we still cannot find an account, Försäkringskassan will send you a payment slip.

Neither the Swedish Social Insurance Agency (Försäkringskassan) nor any bank will contact customers and ask them to provide account details or log in anywhere. If you are contacted by someone claiming to be from Försäkringskassan, it is likely a scam attempt. Such contacts should be ended immediately and reported to the police.

  • The electricity support payment will be made to the person who was on the electricity grid agreement as of February 28, 2026.
  • The amount of support is based on the household's monthly electricity consumption in January and February.
  • A homeowner is estimated to receive approximately SEK 1,100–1,850, while an apartment owner is estimated to receive approximately SEK 105–217 (1).
  1. This calculation is based on a homeowner consuming 6,400 kWh–7,900 kWh and an apartment owner consuming 750 kWh during the months of January and February.
