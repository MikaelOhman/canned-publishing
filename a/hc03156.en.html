The government has proposed changes to the rules regarding the "rotavdrag," which is a type of tax deduction. This means that you pay less tax when you hire someone to do work in your home, such as renovations.

Currently, the deduction is 30 percent, but the proposal suggests a temporary increase to 50 percent. This would be in effect from May 12th to December 31st, 2025. The purpose of this change is to provide support to companies in the construction industry, as the economic situation in the country is challenging at the moment.

For this to become a reality, the parliament needs to vote in favor of the proposal. The proposal also includes changes to several laws that govern how taxes are calculated and how to apply for the deduction. If the proposal is approved, the new rules will come into effect in May 2025.

Full text

The proposal suggests temporarily increasing the subsidy rate for the home improvement tax deduction from 30 percent to 50 percent during the period of May 12 to December 31, 2025. This proposal aims to support the construction industry in the current economic climate.

The proposal entails changes to the Income Tax Act (1999:1229), the Act (2009:194) on the procedure for tax deductions for household services, and the Tax Procedure Act (2011:1244).

The proposed changes are intended to take effect on May 12, 2025, and to expire at the end of December 2025.
