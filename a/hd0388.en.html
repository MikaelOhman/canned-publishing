Full text

The government proposes that the current regulations governing the processing of personal data by the Swedish Tax Agency (Skatteverket), the Swedish Customs Agency (Tullverket), and the National Enforcement Agency (Kronofogdemyndigheten) should be replaced with a new law for each agency, specifically addressing the processing of personal data. These new laws aim to enable the relevant authorities to process personal data in a modern and efficient manner, while simultaneously protecting individuals from violations of their personal privacy. The new laws will largely have the same scope of application as the current laws and will complement the EU's General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).

The government also proposes that the Swedish Tax Agency, the Swedish Customs Agency, and the National Enforcement Agency should be explicitly granted the legal authority to use data analysis and selection methods for even more effective control activities.

The proposed amendments to the laws are scheduled to come into force on April 2, 2026.
