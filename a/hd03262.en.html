Full text

The government is implementing a paradigm shift in Swedish migration policy. The goal is to reduce asylum-related immigration to sustainable levels to create better conditions for integration and to reduce social exclusion. An important part of this work is to align Swedish legislation on the granting of international protection and asylum procedures with the EU's minimum legal standards. In the proposal, the government therefore suggests that the possibility for certain foreigners to be granted permanent residence permits should be removed from the Aliens Act (2005:716).

The proposal also includes suggestions for adapting Swedish law to the EU's migration and asylum pact. These proposals include the following:

  • The terminology and structure of the Swedish asylum procedure will be changed.
  • The so-called border procedure for asylum will be applied in more situations than those that are mandatory according to EU law.
  • The right to publicly funded legal assistance in the handling of cases concerning international protection at the Swedish Migration Agency will be limited.
  • The police authority, the Swedish Migration Agency, and the regions are responsible for carrying out the new screening procedure.
  • The age limit for taking fingerprints and photographs in certain cases during the migration process will be lowered to six years.

The legislative changes are proposed to come into force on July 12, 2026, with the exception of the changes in the new reception law, which is proposed to come into force on October 2, 2026.
