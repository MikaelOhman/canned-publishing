TL;DRThe government wants to change the rules regarding how the Swedish Enforcement Agency (Kronofogden) seizes assets from individuals with debts. The goal is to make it faster to pay off debts and to make it easier to sell houses and apartments in order to obtain funds.
In plain language

The government has proposed a plan to make it easier for the Swedish Enforcement Agency (Kronofogden) to operate. By allowing more government agencies to assist with remote asset seizure, meaning checking assets without being physically present, the hope is to identify more property that can be sold. This is intended to help individuals who are entitled to compensation and those who are waiting to be paid for their debts.

There are also proposals to change the way houses and apartments are sold when they are seized to pay off debts. The aim is to make the process more similar to a regular sale, for example, through online auctions or via real estate agents. The idea is that this will lead to the property being sold for a higher price, which means more money can be used to pay off the debts.

Because this represents a significant intrusion into people's lives, the government also wants to make the rules clearer. The proposal includes a provision that the Swedish Enforcement Agency must explicitly consider what is reasonable and always prioritize the best interests of the child. This is important to protect families who are affected by the authorities seizing their belongings.

If the Parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new rules will come into effect on July 1, 2026. The proposal also includes changes to several other laws related to taxes, customs, and the coast guard, to ensure that everything works together.

Full text

The government proposes several measures to streamline and modernize the enforcement process. These proposals aim to achieve higher sale prices for seized assets, ensure that more debts are paid more quickly, and increase confidence in the enforcement system.

Remote seizure is an effective tool for identifying and securing assets subject to seizure. The government proposes expanding the rules regarding assistance with remote seizure to include more law enforcement agencies. This strengthens the Swedish Enforcement Agency's ability to quickly and effectively secure assets, which means that more creditors can receive payment for their claims. It is also a step in the work to combat and prevent organized crime and can contribute to ensuring the possibility of compensation for victims of crime.

The proposal also includes a provision that real estate and apartments should be treated as equally as possible during seizure. It is also proposed that real estate can be sold in a modern way, for example, through online auctions. For the same reason, it is proposed that apartments should be able to be sold privately, for example, through real estate agents. These proposals will lead to a more efficient process and higher sale prices.

The enforcement process is intrusive for debtors and their families. It is therefore important that the regulations are easy to apply and clear for everyone involved. The government therefore proposes that the Swedish Enforcement Agency's obligation to consider the principle of proportionality and the principle of the best interests of the child should be explicitly stated in the Enforcement Act.

The proposed amendments to the law will come into force on July 1, 2026.
