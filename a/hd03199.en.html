The use of cash continues to decline, but it is important to safeguard the functionality of cash as a means of payment. For cash to remain a viable payment method, it must be possible to pay with cash, especially for goods and services that are of the most basic necessity. The proposal therefore primarily suggests the following:

  • Grocery stores and pharmacies should, as a general rule, be obligated to accept cash as payment for goods and services at physical sales locations with staffed cash registers.
  • This obligation should not apply if it compromises the safety of the business's staff at the sales location, if there is a risk that the sales location may have to be closed due to the cost of handling cash, or if there are reasons to refuse cash payment in a particular transaction.
  • Consumers should be able to make cash deposits to a sufficient extent throughout the country.
  • There should be need-adapted and otherwise suitable services for cash handling and daily cash deposit for businesses.

The proposed amendments to the law should come into force on July 1, 2026.
