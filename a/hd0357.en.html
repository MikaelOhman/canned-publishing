In plain language

The government has proposed changes to two important laws concerning the control of dogs and cats, and the protection of animals. The aim is to enable authorities to work faster and more effectively to ensure that dogs do not cause harm or problems for people in society.

Under the new regulations, the county administrative board, which is the authority responsible for control, will gain more power. They will be able to enter homes and other premises to inspect the animals. The county administrative board will also be able to decide that an animal should be taken into care immediately if necessary to protect people or the animal itself.

It is also proposed that the police authority should be able to decide that an animal should be euthanized in certain specific cases. This applies if the animal has been taken into care under the Animal Welfare Act. The proposal aims to provide the police and the county administrative board with better tools to handle dangerous situations.

If the parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new regulations will come into effect on May 1, 2026. This is part of the government's work to make it easier to comply with the laws regarding the supervision of dogs and cats.

Full text

Every year, a relatively large number of people are injured as a result of being bitten or attacked by dogs. It is important that the authorities can work effectively to protect the public from injuries and inconveniences that dogs can cause.

In this proposal, the government proposes amendments to the Act (2007:1150) on supervision of dogs and cats (the supervision act) and to the Animal Welfare Act (2018:1192), which aim to improve the effectiveness of work under both laws. The amendments concern, among other things, the possibility for county administrative boards to exercise supervision under the supervision act, to gain access to homes and other premises for the purpose of supervision, and to make decisions about immediate seizure of animals to a greater extent. It is also proposed that the Police Authority be given the possibility to decide on the euthanasia of animals that have been seized under the Animal Welfare Act in certain cases.

The amendments to the law are proposed to come into force on May 1, 2026.
