Changes to legislation resulting from amendments to the EU's design protection regulation.

TL;DRThe government intends to amend Swedish laws to align them with new EU regulations regarding design protection. The proposal concerns the protection of the appearance of products within the EU and is scheduled to come into effect on June 1, 2025.
In plain language

The EU has updated its regulations regarding the protection of product designs, i.e., how a product looks. The old term "Community design" is now being replaced with "EU design." Since these regulations apply throughout the EU, Swedish laws must also be amended to align with the new rules.

A key aspect of the proposal concerns penalties and fines. The government wants to ensure that individuals or companies cannot be penalized or have their goods confiscated if the products are merely transiting through Sweden to reach other EU countries. In other words, it should not be considered a violation of design protection if products only pass through the country without being sold there.

Furthermore, the government intends to eliminate a fee that the Swedish Intellectual Property Office (PRV), the agency responsible for handling such registrations, has been able to charge. This fee relates to forwarding an application to the EU level. The aim is to make the system more modern and user-friendly.

The new regulations are proposed to come into effect on June 1, 2025. If the Swedish Parliament approves the proposal, the Swedish laws on design protection and labor disputes will be amended accordingly.

Full text

The government proposes amendments to the Design Protection Act and the Act on Procedure in Labour Disputes, following the revision of the EU Regulation on Community Designs, which is now being renamed the EU Design Regulation.

The revision modernizes the system for registering designs valid throughout the EU. The revised regulation is directly applicable in Sweden, but there is a need for certain supplementary legislative changes. Therefore, it is proposed that the provisions regarding criminal liability in the Design Protection Act and the forfeiture of property in the Penal Code should not apply in cases of infringement of an EU Design if the products only pass through the EU without being released onto the internal market. Furthermore, it is proposed to remove the regulation allowing the Swedish Intellectual Property Office to charge a fee for forwarding an application for the registration of a design at the EU level. The references to "Community Design" in the laws are proposed to be changed to "EU Design."

The proposed legislative changes are intended to come into force on June 1, 2025.
