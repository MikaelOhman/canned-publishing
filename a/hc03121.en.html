The government has developed a new national cybersecurity strategy. This national cybersecurity strategy outlines the government's approach to issues crucial for Sweden's cybersecurity.

The national cybersecurity strategy is based on national needs and the NIS 2 directive, taking a comprehensive approach to address a wide range of challenges. The strategy details several threats and vulnerabilities that impact Sweden's cybersecurity. It is structured around three key pillars that guide Sweden's cybersecurity efforts:

  • Pillar A: Systematic and effective cybersecurity work.
  • Pillar B: Enhanced knowledge and skills development in cybersecurity.
  • Pillar C: The ability to prevent and manage cybersecurity incidents.

The strategy also includes goals aimed at achieving progress in several areas during the strategy's lifespan, addressing the threats and vulnerabilities outlined within it.

An action plan (Appendix 1) is associated with the strategy, outlining a number of activities that align with the government's approach and the requirements of the NIS 2 directive. This action plan will be updated regularly.
