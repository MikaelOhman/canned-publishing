Full text

The proposal suggests a change in the Social Security Code of Laws, which would eliminate the requirement to notify the authorities before applying for parental leave. Removing this notification requirement would make it easier and simpler for parents to plan their parental leave and apply for parental benefits. This change could also contribute to more efficient administration.

The proposal also includes a transitional provision related to a change in the Social Security Code of Laws that was made in conjunction with the repeal of the Act (1994:451) on intensive monitoring with electronic control, effective January 1, 2026. This provision concerns who is covered by state personal injury insurance.

The proposal also includes a correction in Chapter 13, Section 25 of the Social Security Code of Laws regarding the benefit period for temporary parental leave in certain situations.

The legislative change regarding the elimination of the notification requirement is proposed to take effect on July 1, 2026. The other legislative changes are proposed to take effect on May 31, 2026, but will apply retroactively from January 1, 2026.
