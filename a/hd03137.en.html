The Swedish National Audit Office is an agency that monitors whether the state is doing its job correctly. They have written a report showing that the system for detaining individuals is not working well, specifically when people are held in custody while awaiting a decision on their deportation. They argue that it is too expensive and that the authorities are not cooperating effectively enough.

The two most important agencies involved are the Swedish Migration Agency and the National Police. The National Audit Office believes that they are not good at determining who should be detained and that this process is not conducted in a fair and just manner. "Fair and just" means that all laws are followed and that no one is treated unfairly.

The government agrees that there are problems that need to be addressed. Therefore, they have tasked the Swedish Migration Agency with communicating more with both the National Police and the Swedish Prison and Probation Service, which is the agency responsible for prisons. The goal is for them to be able to plan better together so that the available spaces are used effectively.

The government also wants to improve the planning of travel arrangements and processing procedures. The most important goal is to minimize the amount of time a person spends in custody before being deported.

Full text

In this document, the government presents its assessment of the observations and recommendations made by the Swedish National Audit Office (Riksrevisionen) in the report "Detention in the Migration Process – An Expensive Tool Without Clear Governance" (RiR 2025:32). The National Audit Office's overall assessment is that the detention system is not functioning effectively. The National Audit Office assesses that the processes of the Swedish Migration Agency (Migrationsverket) and the Police Authority (Polismyndigheten) do not ensure effective decisions regarding who should be detained, that the cooperation between the authorities is insufficient, and that the Swedish Migration Agency does not ensure that the detention facilities are operated in a legally sound and cost-effective manner.

The government welcomes the National Audit Office's review, which provides valuable input for the continued work on an efficient and legally sound detention system. The government agrees with some of the National Audit Office's recommendations.

On several occasions, the government has tasked the Swedish Migration Agency with taking measures to develop cooperation with other authorities. Furthermore, the government has instructed the Swedish Migration Agency, in dialogue and cooperation with the Police Authority and the Swedish Prison and Probation Service (Kriminalvården), to use detention strategically and to jointly create good planning conditions in order to achieve better foresight, an efficient and appropriate use of detention places, and to ensure necessary transportation. The handling and travel planning should promote a short stay between registration at the detention facility and departure.

With this document, the government considers the review report to be finalized.
