Enhanced support for seniors and for those who care for or support family members.

Source Riksdagen Read the original at Riksdagen →2025-11-25
TL;DRNew legislative proposals aim to make it easier to access help and support for those caring for a sick relative or living in a nursing home. The proposal focuses on providing better information and contact persons to both adults and children affected by serious illness within the family.
In plain language

The government wants to amend several laws to provide better support for individuals who care for sick or elderly relatives. Residents of nursing homes for the elderly will be assigned a dedicated contact person to assist them with navigating the healthcare system. The social services department, which is the part of the municipality responsible for social support, will also provide more information and a contact person to help with planning the necessary care.

Children who are negatively affected when a parent or other adult becomes seriously ill or dies will receive clearer support. This also applies if a sibling is seriously ill. The responsibility for supporting these children does not solely rest with the municipal social services department; healthcare professionals also have a role to play in providing support.

There will also be a change in the law regarding support for people with disabilities. Individuals who use respite care services (i.e., those who receive assistance to take breaks from their daily lives) are no longer required to receive that assistance in their own homes. This provides greater flexibility in how the support can be delivered.

If the Parliament votes in favor of these proposals, the new rules will come into effect on July 1, 2026. The proposal clarifies the responsibilities of both the municipality and the healthcare system in supporting vulnerable families.

Full text

The proposal suggests changes to the Social Services Act (2025:400). Individuals residing in specialized housing for the elderly should be offered a dedicated care contact person, unless it is clearly determined that there is no need. The support provided to those who care for or support a family member is clarified by stipulating that the social services board must offer information and guidance. The social services board must also provide a support contact person to those who care for or support a family member. This support contact person should address the individual's need for information, guidance, and support in planning any necessary actions. The social services board's responsibility for a child who requires support due to a family member, for example, being seriously ill or unexpectedly passing away, is clarified.

The Health and Medical Care Act (2017:30) and the Patient Safety Act (2010:659) clarify the responsibility of healthcare providers and healthcare personnel to support children when a child's parent, or another adult living with the child, for example, has a serious physical illness or unexpectedly passes away. This responsibility should also extend to situations where a child's sibling is seriously ill.

The Act (1993:387) on support and services for certain individuals with disabilities is amended so that the respite care service no longer needs to be provided in the home.

The proposed amendments are scheduled to come into effect on July 1, 2026.

Proposed Resolution of the Parliament

Government Proposal:

  1. The Parliament adopts the government's proposed amendment to the Social Services Act (2025:400).
  2. The Parliament adopts the government's proposed amendment to the Health and Medical Care Act (2017:30).
  3. The Parliament adopts the government's proposed amendment to the Patient Safety Act (2010:659).
  4. The Parliament adopts the government's proposed amendment to the Act (1993:387) on support and services for certain individuals with disabilities.

4

The social services board shall offer support to:

  1. Individuals who care for or support a family member who is elderly or chronically ill, and
  2. Individuals who support a family member with a disability.

The support shall be provided in the form of information, guidance, or other assistance. The social services board shall offer a support contact person to individuals who care for or support a family member as described in the first paragraph. The support contact person shall address the individual's need for information and guidance, as well as support in planning actions that may be necessary.
