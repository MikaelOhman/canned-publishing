TL;DRThe Swedish Tax Agency (Skatteverket) wants to gain better control over fraud related to tax deductions for things like cleaning services and solar panels. The proposal would make it easier for the agency to audit companies and detect violations of labor laws.
In plain language

The government has proposed new regulations to make it more difficult to cheat on taxes. This concerns deductions known as ROT, RUT, and green technology, which are refunds received when purchasing services such as cleaning or the installation of solar panels.

Companies performing the work must now clearly state whether they have used other companies or staffing agencies to assist. The tax authority (Skatteverket) will also gain greater opportunities to conduct audits and visit companies to ensure everything is being done correctly. It will also become easier to send information digitally to the authorities.

If a company has received incorrect payments, the tax authority will be able to demand repayment and also calculate an additional cost in the form of interest. It will also be possible to review taxes retroactively if errors are discovered in the application for a tax deduction.

If all of these changes are approved by the parliament, the new regulations will come into effect on January 1, 2027. The purpose is to stop tax fraud and crime in the workplace by making it easier for the tax authority to monitor what is actually happening.

Full text

To facilitate and improve the Swedish Tax Agency's controls, and to streamline work against tax evasion, fraud, and labor market crime, the government proposes the following changes to the regulations regarding tax deductions for household services and the installation of green technology, as well as to the regulations for employee registers:

  • A service provider must, in a payment request, specify whether subcontractors or staffing agencies have been or have not been used for the household services or the installation of green technology.
  • The information provided in a payment request must be provided in good faith.
  • The Tax Agency must, under certain conditions, be allowed to use third-party payment orders when checking a payment or a payment request.
  • The Tax Agency must be allowed to conduct control visits for a new purpose.
  • Certain additional information must be registered in the employee register.
  • Information in an electronic employee register must be able to be transferred electronically to the Tax Agency.
  • A possibility for reassessment is introduced if it is triggered by a decision to refund in a case or legal proceeding according to the procedural laws for tax deductions for household services or the installation of green technology.
  • Interest on an amount that the Tax Agency has decided that the service provider must repay must be calculated up to and including the day the amount is repaid.

The proposals are proposed to come into force on January 1, 2027.

Proposed Parliamentary Decisions

The government's proposal:

  1. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Tax Procedure Act (2011:1244).
  2. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Act (2009:194) on the procedure for tax deductions for household services.
  3. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Act (2020:1066) on the procedure for tax deductions for the installation of green technology.
