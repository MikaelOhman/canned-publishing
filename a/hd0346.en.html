Electronic submission of estate inventories.

TL;DRIt will become easier to submit documents related to deceased estates digitally to the Swedish Tax Agency. The proposal aims to reduce paperwork and make it easier for individuals to use digital services.
In plain language

The government wants to amend the law to allow the submission of estate inventories and notifications regarding deceased estates digitally. An estate inventory is a document that shows the assets and liabilities of a deceased person. This will be done through a secure e-service created by the Swedish Tax Agency.

The purpose of the change is to make the work at the Swedish Tax Agency faster and easier. By using digital solutions instead of paper, the need to handle documents manually is reduced. This is part of a larger plan to make Sweden's government agencies more modern and efficient.

It is important to know that you do not have to use a computer if you do not want to. It will still be possible to submit your documents on paper if you prefer or cannot use the digital service. This ensures that everyone can be confident that their cases will be handled correctly.

If the Parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new rules will come into effect on July 1, 2026. The proposal will be submitted through what is called a "proposition," which is a proposal from the government to the Parliament.

Full text

One of the goals of the government's digitalization strategy is for Sweden to have a public administration that simplifies processes and contributes to reduced bureaucracy through user-friendly, secure, and reliable digital services, which are made more efficient through AI and data-driven development. In this proposal, the government suggests that inventories of estates and notifications of deceased persons should be able to be created electronically and submitted to the Swedish Tax Agency (Skatteverket) through an e-service provided by the agency. The option to submit the documents in paper form will remain available for those who do not wish to or cannot use the digital service.

These proposals will streamline the inventory process at the Swedish Tax Agency by minimizing manual handling and making it easier for individuals and others to create and submit the documents.

The proposed amendments to the law are scheduled to come into force on July 1, 2026.
