In plain language

The government proposes a change to the law regarding special income tax for individuals residing abroad. Currently, there are regulations that require individuals living in another country but working for a Swedish company to pay taxes in Sweden. The proposed change aims to clarify that this also applies when a person is temporarily assigned to an employer in Sweden for a shorter period.

This change is not significant for everyone. It primarily affects individuals residing in Denmark due to the specific regulations in place between Sweden and Denmark. It is also important for individuals residing in countries where Sweden has not established tax agreements.

If the proposal becomes law, the new regulations will take effect on July 1, 2025. This means that salaries or other benefits paid out after June 30, 2025, will be subject to the new regulations if the work was performed after that date.

Full text

According to the law (1991:586) concerning special income tax for individuals residing abroad, work performed abroad by a person residing abroad, if carried out due to employment or a contract with a Swedish company or at a foreign company's permanent establishment in Sweden, under certain conditions, is considered to have been performed in Sweden and may therefore be taxed in Sweden. The proposal suggests clarifying in the law that work performed abroad within the framework of temporary assignments to an employer in Sweden may, in certain cases, also be considered to have been performed in Sweden.

Due to the structure of the tax agreements that Sweden has entered into with other countries, this clarification is, in practice, only relevant for individuals residing in Denmark to whom the agreement between Sweden and Denmark regarding certain tax matters applies, or for individuals residing in countries with which Sweden has not entered into a tax agreement.

The proposed amendment to the law is intended to come into force on July 1, 2025, and will be applied for the first time to wages or other similar benefits for work performed after June 30, 2025, where the payment is made after that date.
