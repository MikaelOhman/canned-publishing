Criminalization of virginity tests, virginity certificates, and hymenoplasty.

Source Riksdagen Read the original at Riksdagen →2025-06-17

In Sweden, practices such as virginity checks, virginity certificates, and procedures aimed at simulating virginity occur both within and outside the healthcare system. These practices can lead to serious physical and psychological consequences for those affected. To strengthen the protection of girls and women living in a culture of honor, the government proposes that two new offenses, "virginity checks" and "certification of sexual virginity," be included in the criminal code, and that a punishable prohibition against "procedures aimed at simulating virginity" be included in the law (1982:316) prohibiting female genital mutilation. Furthermore, it is proposed that the failure to report or prevent forced marriage and child marriage be criminalized.

These proposals would make it punishable to:

* Examine another person's female genitalia with the intent, for one's own or another's benefit, to determine whether the person has had, or appears to have had, vaginal intercourse or participated in comparable penetrative sexual acts. * Provide written or oral certification that another person has had vaginal intercourse or participated in comparable penetrative sexual acts, or that they have not, taking into account the condition of the female genitalia. * Perform procedures on another person's female genitalia with the intent that it should appear as though the person has not had vaginal intercourse or participated in comparable penetrative sexual acts. * Fail to report or prevent forced marriage and child marriage.
