Karl Sverkersson, born in the 1130s and died on April 12, 1167, at Visingsö, was a Swedish king. He was recognized as king in Östergötland by 1158 and throughout Sweden from 1161. He was the son of Sverker the Elder and Ulvhild Håkansdotter (of the Thjotta clan), and in 1163 he married Kristina, daughter of the Skåne nobleman Stig Hvitaled and Queen Margaret, sister of King Valdemar the Great.

== Biography == Karl Sverkersson was the son of King Sverker the Elder, who was murdered in 1155 or 1156. After this, Karl likely immediately proclaimed himself king. After defeating and killing his rival for the throne, Prince Magnus Henriksson of Denmark, in the Battle of Örebro in 1161, he gained power over the entire kingdom and subsequently called himself "king of the Svear and Goths." During Karl's reign, Sweden received its first archbishop in 1164 when Stefan, a monk from Alvastra, took office, according to preserved papal letters. This was at the request of the king, Earl Ulf, and the Swedish bishops. Alvastra monastery had been founded by Karl's parents, but he also supported Vreta monastery and Nydala monastery with donations. According to Russian chronicles, Swedes undertook a failed military campaign to Russia in 1164. It is possible that it originated from Finland. In the spring of 1167, Karl was ambushed and murdered at Visingsö by Knut, son of Saint Eric, who subsequently took over the kingship. Karl was buried in Alvastra monastery. Karl Sverkersson's seal (see image) depicts the king seated on his throne and is the oldest preserved Swedish royal seal. During his reign, the oldest written document known to exist in Sweden was also issued by the aforementioned Archbishop Stefan.

== Marriage and Children == In 1163, Karl married Kristina Stigsdotter Hvide. They had two children: Sverker the Younger and Kristina, who became a nun.
