In plain language

The government has proposed a new law called the "Social Data Registry Act." This law aims to help the Swedish National Board of Health and Welfare (Socialstyrelsen) manage personal data effectively. By collecting information, it will be possible to learn more about how social services function and how to improve support for people.

The proposal also stipulates that those working in social services and providing support to people with disabilities must provide necessary information to Socialstyrelsen. This means that organizations providing services under the laws governing social services and support for people with disabilities will be obligated to submit data to the new registry.

It is important to note that the law also includes regulations to protect people's privacy. This means that care must be taken to ensure that personal data is not used in a way that violates privacy. The goal is to create a secure system for managing all collected information.

If the proposal is approved, the new law will come into effect on August 1, 2026. The requirement for organizations to provide data will take effect one year later, on August 1, 2027. There is also a change regarding fees that is proposed to take effect on June 1, 2026.

Full text

The proposal suggests a social data register law that complements the European Parliament and Council Regulation (EU) 2016/679. The purpose of the law is, on the one hand, to enable the Swedish Social Agency (Socialstyrelsen) to process personal data in a social data register in an efficient manner, and on the other hand, to protect individuals from having their personal privacy violated during such processing. The proposal expands the Swedish Social Agency's ability to process personal data in national social data registers. A social data register should contribute to increased knowledge for the development and improvement of social services and activities according to the Act (1993:387) on support and services for certain individuals with disabilities.

The proposal also includes a requirement for organizations operating under the Social Services Act (2025:400) and the Act on support and services for certain individuals with disabilities to provide the necessary data to the Swedish Social Agency for inclusion in a social data register.

The new law is proposed to come into force on August 1, 2026. The provisions regarding the obligation to provide data are proposed to come into force on August 1, 2027.

The proposal also includes a provision for a transitional clause, regarding charges for specific services, to be introduced into the Social Services Act. This change is proposed to come into force on June 1, 2026.
