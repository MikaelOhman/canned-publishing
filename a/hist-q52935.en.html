Karl XI, born November 24, 1655, and died April 5, 1697, was King of Sweden from 1660 until his death. He also ruled from 1681 as Duke Karl I over the Duchy of Palatinate-Zweibrücken. He was the son of Charles X Gustav and Hedvig Eleonora of Holstein-Gottorp, and married Ulrika Eleonora of Denmark (1656–1693). They had several children, including Hedwig Sofia, Karl XII, and Ulrika Eleonora.

Karl XI's reign began with the Scanian War against primarily Denmark, which, after great efforts, ended without any territorial concessions to the Danes. He pursued a cautious policy that stabilized Sweden's finances, reformed the defense system, and resulted in very limited territorial losses. He established the "Carolinian absolutism," and to finance a larger army, a comprehensive reduction was implemented, significantly diminishing the assets of the nobility. Subsequently, efforts were largely focused on preventing another war, leading to the creation of the "younger recruitment system" and the Carolinian army. Karl XI was succeeded by his only surviving son, Karl XII, who used his father's army in the Great Northern War.

== The Regency Period (1655–1671) ==

Karl XI, the only child of Karl X Gustav and Hedvig Eleonora of Holstein-Gottorp, was born on November 24, 1655, at Tre Kronor Castle, and inherited the Swedish crown on February 13, 1660. When Karl XI was born, his father was engaged in a military campaign in Poland. Upon receiving news of the birth of the heir apparent at his headquarters in Elbing, the overjoyed father ordered the artillery to fire celebratory shots. For his christening on December 9th, his mother received a gilded baptismal font as a gift from her parents, Duke Frederick III of Holstein-Gottorp and Marie Elisabeth of Saxony.

It wasn't until Christmas Eve 1659 that Karl would finally meet his father, during the Diet convened by Karl X Gustav in Gothenburg. The Diet was opened on January 3, 1660, and the following day, the little heir prince sat "quite respectfully" on a chair next to the king, who delivered a lengthy speech about his military achievements. Just over a week later, the king fell ill; he wrote his will on February 12th, and passed away the next day. On the same day, the four-year-old Karl XI was proclaimed king, and at that time, he wore a coat of purple silk brocade with gold trim. The coat is preserved in the Livrustkammaren (Royal Armoury), where it is on display along with a saber that the young king received from Queen Kristina during his father's funeral, which took place on November 4th of the same year in Stockholm. The now almost five-year-old king was alternately carried and led by the hand behind his father's coffin in the long procession that wound its way through the rain and wind from the castle, along Skeppsbron and Stora Nygatan, to Riddarholmskyrkan (Riddarholm Church).

In his will, Karl X Gustav appointed Professor Emund Gripenhielm as his son's tutor. The education included Latin, French, and German. However, the king never learned French, which was unusual for a member of royalty at that time. The king was also expected to have knowledge of history, constitutional law, geography, astronomy, architecture, and military science. The regency government carefully monitored his studies and soon discovered that the king was struggling due to dyslexia. The king was shy, had difficulty reading and writing, but was good at memorizing things. When the Italian writer and diplomat Lorenzo Magalotti visited Stockholm in 1674, he described the young Karl XI as "a person who is in distress and afraid of everything. It seems that he does not dare to look anyone in the face, and he moves as if he were walking on glass." Magalotti also described him as a good-natured man "who liked to eat and drink and possessed all the good qualities that ignorance can bring." Other qualities that Magalotti considered distinguished the king were his deep piety and his skill as a fencer and rider.

In 1671, the king began attending meetings of the Council of State. The regency government was led by Dowager Queen Hedvig Eleonora. Other members included Lord High Chancellor Per Brahe, Chancellor Magnus Gabriel De la Gardie, Superintendent of Finance Herman Fleming, and Field Marshal Carl Gustaf Wrangel. It has been said that this government had noticeable weaknesses; it struggled to address the poor state finances. The "quartering tax," decided in 1655, was not enforced.

== The First Reign (1672–1674) == In August 1672, the king participated in a military exercise on the parade ground in Stockholm. This is described as the first true maneuver held in Sweden. A fortification was built on the parade ground by soldiers from the Uppland regiment, and approximately 3,000 attacking troops practiced assault tactics against the defenders, who were led by Field Marshal Gustaf Baner. King Charles XI participated in the attack as a cavalry officer under Marshal Carl Gustaf Wrangel.
