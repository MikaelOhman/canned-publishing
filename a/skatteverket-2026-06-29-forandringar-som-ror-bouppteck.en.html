Changes related to estate inventories:

Source Skatteverket Read the original at Skatteverket →2026-06-29
TL;DRStarting July 1st, there will be new regulations regarding how to create an inventory list (bouppteckning), which is the document that details everything a deceased person owned. It will also become easier to submit the necessary documents digitally in the future.
In plain language

An inventory of assets and liabilities is an important document that shows what a deceased person owned and what debts they had. From July 1st, there will be new rules for how these documents should be written. It's crucial that all individuals involved in the estate are listed by name and personal identification number to ensure the information is accurate.

This document must be submitted to the Swedish Tax Agency (Skatteverket), which is the government agency responsible for this process, within four months of the death. A registered inventory is necessary to demonstrate who has the right to make decisions regarding the deceased person's assets.

Starting July 1st, 2026, the Swedish Tax Agency may begin using digital solutions to receive these documents online. Until then, all documents must still be submitted in paper form.
