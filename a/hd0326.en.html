TL;DRThe government wants to amend the laws regarding electricity and gas to comply with EU regulations. The aim is to ensure that the authority responsible for overseeing these companies is completely independent, and that the rules governing prices are fair to consumers.
In plain language

The government has proposed changes to the regulations governing the operation of electricity and gas networks in Sweden. This involves ensuring that the authority responsible for overseeing these companies is completely independent, a requirement from the EU. This authority is tasked with ensuring that companies comply with the law.

When companies that supply electricity and gas operate, there are regulations governing how much profit they can make. These regulations are called "revenue caps." The proposal would give the authority more power to determine regulations in this area, and would remove certain outdated regulations that are no longer necessary. This is important to ensure that customers receive fair prices.

If companies or customers are not satisfied with the authority's decisions, they can appeal to a court. The government wants the courts that review these decisions to include experts in economics. This is intended to help ensure that decisions related to money and finance are more accurate.

The new regulations for gas will come into effect on January 1, 2026. For the regulations that apply to electricity, the changes will come into effect on January 1, 2027.

Full text

This proposal includes draft legislation aimed at ensuring that the Swedish regulations regarding the independence of the supervisory authority when performing its duties under the Electricity Act (1997:857) and the Natural Gas Act (2005:403) comply with the requirements of EU law.

The conditions applied by electricity grid companies and natural gas companies within the framework of electricity grid operations and natural gas operations are subject to prior review by the supervisory authority, according to the Electricity Act and the Natural Gas Act. Most of the revenues related to such activities should fall within a revenue framework determined by the supervisory authority for a regulatory period. It is also of great importance for customers that the regulation of the size of the revenue frameworks and the overall prior review of the companies' conditions functions effectively. Certain provisions in the law that affect the design of the conditions are proposed to be repealed. It is proposed that the government's ability to authorize the supervisory authority to issue regulations in this area be expanded. It is also proposed that the composition of the administrative court and the chamber court in the review of decisions by the supervisory authority regarding revenue frameworks should include economic experts.

The amendments to the law related to the natural gas sector are proposed to enter into force on January 1, 2026. The other amendments relating to the electricity sector are proposed to enter into force on January 1, 2027.
