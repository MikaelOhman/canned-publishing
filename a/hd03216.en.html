In plain language

Municipalities are responsible for a significant portion of healthcare services for the elderly and people with disabilities, but currently have limited opportunities to employ doctors directly.

The government proposes an amendment to the Health and Medical Care Act that would allow municipalities to hire doctors for their healthcare services.

The government, or an agency, should also be able to decide on the structure of agreements for physician involvement. The change is proposed to take effect from August 1, 2026.

Full text

The proposal suggests an amendment to the Health and Medical Care Act (2017:30) that would allow municipalities to hire physicians for activities related to municipal health and medical care services. Furthermore, the proposal suggests that the Health and Medical Care Act should include a provision authorizing the government or a government agency to issue regulations regarding the design and content of agreements for physician involvement.

The proposed amendment is scheduled to come into effect on August 1, 2026.
