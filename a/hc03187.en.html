An effective criminal law framework for protecting government subsidies to businesses.

Source Riksdagen Read the original at Riksdagen →2025-06-24

The government's proposal suggests the introduction of a law specifically addressing offenses related to subsidies. This law would provide special criminal protection for state support provided to companies and other legal entities, as decided by government agencies and individual bodies. Anyone who intentionally or through gross negligence provides false information or fails to report changes in circumstances, thereby causing a risk that state support for businesses is incorrectly paid out, credited, or not recovered, will be prosecuted. This proposal expands the scope of criminal offenses compared to existing fraud regulations and also includes the current "restructuring support offense," which would be repealed. The provisions in the Penal Code regarding misuse of subsidies and gross misuse of subsidies, which currently apply to support funded by EU funds, will be transferred to the new subsidy offense law and expanded to include state support for businesses.

The proposal also includes a law on the obligation to report errors in the disbursement of state support for businesses. This reporting obligation applies to government agencies and individual bodies that make decisions regarding state support for businesses, as well as the Swedish Enforcement Agency. The regions are also partially subject to this reporting obligation. A report must be submitted to the entity making the payment if there is reason to believe that state support for businesses has been incorrectly paid out, may be incorrectly paid out, or has been used for a purpose other than the one for which it was granted.

The government further proposes to expand the law (2008:206) on the obligation to report errors in payments from social welfare systems to also include the regions, to a certain extent.

The new regulations are proposed to come into effect on January 1, 2026.
