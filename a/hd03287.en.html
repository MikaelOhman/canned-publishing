A new policy direction for a stronger film nation

Source Riksdagen Read the original at Riksdagen →2026-06-22

The government's proposal outlines new goals for national film policy. Based on the national cultural policy objectives, the national film policy should contribute to the creation and distribution of a wide range of high-quality films throughout the country, attract a large audience for Swedish films both domestically and internationally, and preserve and utilize film heritage.

The proposal also includes a number of assessments regarding the content and governance of the policy. A new film policy should, among other things, be designed to encourage more people to watch Swedish films, regardless of the viewing window, and to focus on where state support can be most effective. The future funding of Swedish films should involve increased collaboration between private and public funding. The Swedish Film Institute should remain a foundation, while there should be clear guidelines for state funding for film. State support should promote content in Swedish and the national minority languages. Furthermore, the Film Institute should be responsible for supporting the production of audiovisual works (production incentives), and a new, predictable system should be introduced for the allocation of this support.

The government also proposes the introduction of a new appeals provision in the law (1992:318) concerning the delegation of administrative tasks within the scope of the Ministry of Culture's activities. In addition, changes are proposed to update outdated information about individual bodies that perform administrative tasks in accordance with the law.

The proposed amendments to the law are scheduled to come into effect on January 1, 2027.
