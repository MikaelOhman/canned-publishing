Full text

In this document, the government presents its assessment of the observations and recommendations made by the Swedish National Audit Office (Riksrevisionen) in the report "The Swedish Migration Agency's Handling of Citizenship Cases" (RiR 2025:5). The National Audit Office's overall assessment is that the Swedish Migration Agency's handling of citizenship cases is not efficient. The National Audit Office assesses that the Agency's governance and organization have led to a lack of opportunity to make correct decisions without unnecessary delays. Deficiencies in procedures, decision-making documentation, and case management systems affect the assessment of identity, residence, and other obstacles to citizenship. The processing time is considered unreasonably long for citizenship cases requiring investigation. The National Audit Office also points out that the government has not taken sufficient measures to address the negative consequences of the introduction of the requirement for a decision request in the Administrative Law Act (2017:900).

The government welcomes the National Audit Office's review as a valuable basis for continued work on an efficient and legally sound handling of citizenship cases. The government agrees in part with the National Audit Office's recommendations. The government has instructed the Swedish Migration Agency to take decisive measures to ensure that the procedures and requirements established in the regulations for citizenship cases are maintained, in order to strengthen the security perspective.

With this document, the government considers the review report to be finalized.
