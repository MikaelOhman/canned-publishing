In plain language

The government wants to amend several laws in Sweden to stop honor-related violence and oppression. This includes making it easier to prosecute individuals who deceive others into traveling for the purpose of forcing them into marriage. A new crime, called "child marriage travel fraud," is also proposed.

The proposal also includes increasing the penalties for crimes such as forced marriage and child marriage. The police will be authorized to use covert methods, such as wiretapping, to better investigate these crimes. It will also become more difficult for authorities to approve foreign marriages that are not legal.

The new rules are scheduled to come into effect on July 2, 2026. For this to happen, the Swedish Parliament, which is Sweden's legislative body, must vote in favor of amendments to several different laws related to crime and marriage.

Full text

Honor-based violence and oppression are a serious social problem that must be combated decisively. This requires comprehensive and effective legislation that reflects the seriousness of the problem. The government therefore proposes, among other things:

  • to expand the scope of the offenses of "deceptive inducement to marriage," which will be renamed "marriage trip offense," and forced marriage;
  • to introduce a new offense, "marriage trip offense against children";
  • to criminalize attempts to commit marriage trip offenses and marriage trip offenses against children;
  • to increase the penalties for forced marriage, child marriage, and marriage trip offenses;
  • to allow the use of secret investigative measures in investigations of forced marriage and child marriage; and
  • to make the prohibition on recognizing unauthorized foreign marriages apply without exceptions.

The proposed legislative changes are scheduled to come into effect on July 2, 2026.

Proposed Parliamentary Decision

The government's proposal:

  1. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Code of Criminal Procedure.
  2. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Penal Code.
  3. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Marriage Act.
  4. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Act (1904:26 s. 1) on certain international legal issues relating to marriage and guardianship.
