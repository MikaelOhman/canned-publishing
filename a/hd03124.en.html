Full text

The proposal includes the legislative changes necessary to align Swedish law with the new regulations for a European Single Access Point (ESAP), which provides centralized access to publicly available information relevant to financial services, capital markets, and sustainability. This essentially means that companies will be required to provide information to be made available through the access point.

The access point will be established through a new EU regulation (the ESAP regulation). The European Securities and Markets Authority (ESMA) is responsible for establishing and operating the access point, which must be operational by July 10, 2027. The access point will provide access to information that companies and authorities are required to disclose under EU law. A new EU directive (the omnibus directive) and a new EU regulation (the omnibus regulation) supplement several EU legal acts with provisions stating that information that must be disclosed under these legal acts must also be made available through the access point.

The access point will also provide access to information that companies wish to make available on a voluntary basis.

The information in the access point will be made available in three phases. The proposed legislative changes are therefore intended to come into force on July 10, 2026, January 10, 2028, and January 10, 2030, respectively.
