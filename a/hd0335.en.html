TL;DRSweden will be amending its laws to comply with new regulations from the EU regarding the inspection of building products. These new regulations will come into effect on March 1, 2026.
In plain language

The government has proposed changes to several important laws in Sweden. This primarily concerns the planning and building code, which governs how we are allowed to construct buildings, but also regulations regarding when government agencies purchase goods and services.

The reason for these changes is that Sweden must comply with the EU's new regulation for construction products. A regulation is a rule from the EU that applies directly in all member states. The purpose is to ensure that the rules for materials used in buildings are the same throughout Europe.

The proposal also means that the laws regarding public procurement need to be amended. Procurement refers to the rules that the state or municipality must follow when purchasing something, to ensure fairness. These rules must now be adapted to the new EU regulations.

If the parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new laws will come into effect on March 1, 2026. This means that companies that sell building materials and those that construct buildings must prepare for the new requirements.

Full text

This proposal suggests amendments to the Planning and Building Act (2010:900) that are necessary to align Swedish law with the EU's new Construction Products Regulation. The proposed amendments are scheduled to come into effect on March 1, 2026.
