Full text

The possibilities of using covert and preventative coercive measures are often crucial for preventing, deterring, and investigating serious crime. This applies regardless of the age of the perpetrator. Serious crime is increasingly involving younger individuals, and children are playing central roles in risky organized crime. This trend must be reversed, and children must be protected from being involved in criminal activity. To strengthen the ability of law enforcement agencies to combat serious crime committed by children, the government proposes the following:

  • Covert coercive measures should be permitted to investigate certain serious crimes where the suspect is under 15 years old.
  • Preventative coercive measures should be permitted against children under 15 years old to prevent, deter, and detect certain particularly serious crimes.
  • Children should be able to be detained in more cases and for a slightly longer period than currently allowed for questioning or with the purpose of being handed over to, for example, parents or social services.

The proposed legislative changes are scheduled to come into effect on October 1, 2025.
