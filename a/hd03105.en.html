Enhanced security measures for the transfer of real estate.

Source Riksdagen Read the original at Riksdagen →2026-02-03
TL;DRThe government wants to amend the law to prevent critical properties from being sold to individuals or companies that could harm Sweden's security. Starting in July 2026, checks must be carried out before important buildings or land change ownership.
In plain language

The government has proposed an amendment to the security protection law. The purpose is to strengthen Sweden's security by controlling who purchases important properties, such as buildings or land that are vital to the country's defense.

If you own a business that is important to Sweden's security and want to sell a property, you must first conduct a special investigation. You must verify that the new owner is suitable and consult with the authority that oversees security. This authority can also reject a sale if it is deemed to be a risk to the country.

The proposal also applies when changes are made to how land is divided through a process called land consolidation. This means that the rules also apply when creating new plots of land or changing the boundaries of land in a way that alters who owns what.

If the proposal is adopted, the new rules will come into effect on July 1, 2026. The government may, in certain special cases, determine that there are exceptions to these rules.

Full text

To strengthen Sweden's security, the government proposes that transfers of real estate be subject to the security protection law's regulations regarding transfers. This means that any entity conducting security-sensitive activities that wishes to transfer real estate of importance to Sweden's security must undergo a special security assessment and suitability assessment, and consult with the supervisory authority before the transfer. It also means that the supervisory authority can prohibit unsuitable transfers. The transfer of ownership of real estate through land division is also proposed to be covered by these regulations. The purpose is to reduce the risk of real estate being acquired with the intention of harming Sweden's security.

The proposed amendments to the law are scheduled to come into force on July 1, 2026.

4. 13 § 1 A business operator shall conduct a special security protection assessment and suitability assessment according to section 14, and consult according to section 15, if the business operator intends to transfer:

  1. the entire or part of the security-sensitive activity, or
  2. property that is important for Sweden's security or an international commitment binding on Sweden regarding security protection.

The first paragraph does not apply to transfers. A transfer is equated with the transfer of ownership of real property through a decision on real property formation according to the Real Property Formation Act (1970:988).
