Several Swedish royal figures are known by the names Kristina of Sweden and Christina of Sweden:

  • Kristina, Queen and Head of State, 1632
  • Christina Björnsdotter, Queen consort from 1156 to King Erik the Holy, born as a Danish princess.
  • Christina Stigsdotter Hvide, Queen consort from 1163 to King Karl Sverkersson, born into the Danish noble family Hvide.
  • Christina Abrahamsdotter, Queen consort from 1470 to King Karl Knutsson (Bonde), likely born in Finland.
  • Christina, Queen consort from 1497 to King Hans, also Danish and Norwegian, born as a Duchess of Saxe-Wittenberg.
  • Christina, Queen consort from 1604 to King Karl IX, born as a Duchess of Holstein-Gottorp in the House of Oldenburg.
  • Christina Ingesdotter, Princess, died in 1122, daughter of King Inge the Elder, married and became a Grand Princess of Kiev.
  • Christina Karlsdotter, Princess, circa 1166, daughter of King Karl Sverkersson, a nun.
  • Christina, Princess, died in 1252, daughter of King Sverker the Younger, married and became a Duchess of Mecklenburg.
  • Christina Birgersdotter, Princess, circa 1242, considered de facto as the daughter of Princess Ingeborg Eriksdotter and Birger Jarl.
  • Christina Tyrgilsdotter, Princess, 1302-1305, married to Prince Valdemar Magnusson, daughter of Torgils Knutsson.
  • Christina Karlsdotter (Bonde), Princess, 1448 de facto, daughter of King Karl Knutsson (Bonde), married into the noble family Gyllenstierna.
  • Christina of Oldenburg, Princess, 1521, daughter of King Christian II, also a Danish & Norwegian princess, married and became a Duchess of Lorraine and Milan.
  • Christina Karlsdotter (Vasa), Princess, 1598-1599, daughter of King Karl IX.
  • Christina Augusta, Princess, born and died in 1623, daughter of King Gustav II Adolf.
  • Christina Magdalena of Palatinate-Zweibrücken, Princess, 1654, sister to King Charles X Gustav, married and became a Duchess of Baden-Durlach.
  • Christina, Princess, 1943, sister to King Carl XVI Gustaf, married Magnuson.
