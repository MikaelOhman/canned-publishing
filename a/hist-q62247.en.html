Full text

Johan Kasimir of Palatinate-Zweibrücken, born on April 12, 1589, in Zweibrücken, and died on June 8, 1652, at Stegeborg, was a German-Swedish nobleman, son of Johan I of Palatinate-Zweibrücken and Magdalena of Jülich, and married to Katarina of Sweden, the daughter of Swedish King Karl IX. Through his son, King Karl X Gustav, he became the ancestor of the so-called Palatine line on the Swedish throne, a branch of the House of Wittelsbach.

His titles included Count Palatine of the Rhine, Duke of Jülich, Cleves and Berg, Count of Veldenz, Sponheim and Ravensburg, and Lord of Ravenstein, but these titles did not grant him any influence in any of those territories.

== Biography == Johan Kasimir was raised in his father's humanist princely school in Zweibrücken. After his father's death in 1604, he was placed under the guardianship of Frederick IV of Palatinate in Heidelberg. He inherited the County of Kleeburg in Alsace from his father, consisting of two castles, a town, and about ten villages. It was in this capacity that he held the title of Count Palatine. In 1613, he was sent to Sweden to seek support for the Protestant Union. His Calvinist beliefs initially made it difficult for him to adapt. In 1615, he married his cousin, Princess Katarina of Sweden, daughter of Karl IX (the cousins were related through Philip the Noble of Hesse, who was Johan Kasimir's maternal grandfather and Katarina's paternal grandfather). After returning to his duchy in 1617, he was forced to flee due to the successes of the Imperial forces in the Palatinate territories. When he returned to Sweden in 1622 with his wife, he was granted Stegeborg in Östergötland as a pledge for her dowry. This was reaffirmed in 1651. He is buried in Strängnäs Cathedral.

== Children == Christina Magdalena (1616–1662), married to Frederick VI of Baden-Durlach. Johan Wolfgang Karl (1618–1621). Emilia Elisabeth (1619–1628). Charles X Gustav (1622–1660), King of Sweden from 1654 to 1660. Maria Eufrosyne (1625–1687), married to Magnus Gabriel De la Gardie. Eleonora Katarina (1626–1692), married to Count Frederick of Hesse-Eschwege. Christina (1628–1629). Adolf Johan (1629–1689), Lord Marshal and other titles.
