New regulations regarding geographical indications and international trademarks.

TL;DRThe government wants to amend the laws to better protect the names of Swedish crafts and industrial products. The new system aims to make it easier to ensure that names are not used incorrectly and to simplify the management of international trademarks.
In plain language

The government has proposed new laws to protect specific product names. These refer to geographical indications, which are names that indicate the origin of a product. To register such a name in Sweden, the application must be submitted directly to the EU's Intellectual Property Office.

In Sweden, the Swedish Intellectual Property Office (PRV) is responsible for ensuring that the rules are followed. If someone uses a protected name without permission, there should be clear penalties and opportunities for compensation, similar to how trademark infringements are handled. This also applies to names protected through trade agreements with countries such as New Zealand and Chile.

The proposal also includes changes to several other laws. The aim is to make it easier to handle names belonging to large international organizations and other global brands. The proposal also seeks to amend regulations regarding the protection of weapons and official names to simplify the process.

If the Swedish Parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new rules will come into effect on December 1, 2025. The proposal requires updates to several laws, including the Trademark Act and the Act on Patent and Market Courts.

Full text

In the proposal, the government suggests a new law to supplement the EU regulation concerning the protection of geographical indications for crafts and industrial products.

The proposal includes suggestions that applications for the registration of a geographical indication originating in Sweden should be submitted directly to the EU Intellectual Property Office, and that the Swedish Intellectual Property Office should monitor compliance with the protection of this type of geographical indication. The provisions of the Trademark Act regarding compensation and other sanctions should be applicable in cases of infringement.

The proposal also suggests that the provisions of the Trademark Act regarding sanctions should apply in cases of infringement of geographical indications protected by the EU's trade agreements with New Zealand and Chile.

Finally, certain amendments are proposed to the Act on the Protection of Weapons and Certain Other Official Designations, and to the Trademark Act, in order to simplify the handling of designations of international organizations and international trademark matters.

The new law and the amendments to existing laws are proposed to come into force on December 1, 2025.

Proposed Resolutions for the Parliament

The government's proposals:

  1. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for a law on the control of protected designations on crafts and industrial products.
  2. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Act (1970:498) on the protection of weapons and certain other official designations.
  3. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Trademark Act (2010:1877).
  4. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Act (2016:188) on patent and market courts.
  5. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Act (2018:1654) on the protection of designations on agricultural products and foodstuffs.
