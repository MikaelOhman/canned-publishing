Full text

Robert Bárány, born April 22, 1876, in Vienna, and died April 8, 1936, in Uppsala, was an Austro-Hungarian and Swedish Nobel laureate and otologist (ear specialist). His father was Ignác Bárány from Várpalota, his paternal grandfather was also named Ignác from Pápa, and his mother was Maria Hock. In 1914, he received the Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for his work on the inner ear's balance organs. He lived in Sweden from around 1917 and became a Swedish citizen in 1919.

Bárány earned his medical doctorate in Vienna in 1900 and subsequently worked as an assistant at psychiatric clinics in Heidelberg and Freiburg im Breisgau from 1901 to 1902. He then served as an assistant and demonstrator at the university ear clinic in Vienna from 1903 to 1911, and became a private lecturer in otolaryngology at the same university in 1909. For a time, he also collaborated with Sigmund Freud.

During World War I, Bárány served as an Austrian sanitation officer. He was captured and held as a Russian prisoner of war in Turkestan when he was awarded the Nobel Prize. With the help of Swedish diplomatic efforts, including those undertaken by Prince Carl, but especially by the physician and professor of otorhinolaryngology, Gunnar Holmgren, Bárány was released and able to receive his Nobel Prize in 1916. With the assistance of Holmgren and other colleagues, Bárány was reunited with his family in Uppsala after receiving the prize. In 1917, he was appointed as a docent at Uppsala University and simultaneously became head of its department of otolaryngology, receiving the title of professor that same year. He obtained Swedish citizenship in 1919 and remained active in Uppsala until his death.

Robert Bárány was of Hungarian origin, son of Ignáz Bárány, born in 1842 in Várpalota, Kingdom of Hungary. He married Ida Felicitas Berger on March 9, 1909; she was born on December 12, 1881. Bárány was the father of professors Ernst Bárány and Franz Bárány, and grandfather of Anders Bárány. It is said that he learned Esperanto sometime before 1916.
