The Swedish National Audit Office has reviewed whether the state's efforts to support municipalities in fulfilling their responsibilities for providing water services, as stipulated by the Act (2006:412) on Public Water Services (the Water Services Act), are effective. The National Audit Office's overall conclusion is that the state's efforts are lacking in several areas.

The government largely agrees with the National Audit Office's description of the problems and believes that the measures proposed by the National Audit Office are well-justified. The government agrees that the oversight conducted by the County Administrative Boards, as stipulated in section 51 of the Water Services Act, needs to be followed up, and that it needs to be clarified that the County Administrative Boards' oversight responsibility covers the municipalities' entire responsibility as defined in section 6 of the Water Services Act. The government agrees that a government agency should be responsible for providing guidance, but believes that it should not be the same agency that is responsible for monitoring the water service plans. The Swedish Agency for Marine and Water Management has been tasked with providing guidance to the County Administrative Boards in their oversight of section 6 of the Water Services Act, which, in turn, oversees the municipalities. The government is uncertain whether there is currently a need for expanded powers for the County Administrative Boards, but is open to the possibility that this may be necessary. The government believes that the cooperation between the relevant authorities in matters related to wastewater and stormwater may need to be formalized.

With this statement, the government considers the National Audit Office's report to be finalized.
