Full text

The proposal includes suggested amendments to the Education Act (2010:800) aimed at ensuring that students, teachers, principals, and other school staff have a safe and secure learning and working environment. The proposal also aims to ensure that students receive adequate support.

The proposal introduces a requirement for the school unit from which a student is transferring to provide information about the student to the receiving school unit when a student changes school type or school unit within a school type, if there is a risk, based on specific circumstances, that the student may commit or be subjected to a crime, and if the information is needed by the receiving school unit to prevent or avoid this. The proposal also stipulates that school units in more school types than currently are required to inform that they have admitted a student when a student changes school type or school unit within a school type. Furthermore, the proposal supplements the regulations in the Education Act regarding the processing of personal data, providing a legal basis for receiving school units with private management to process received personal data.

The amendments to the Education Act are proposed to come into force on June 30, 2026, and will be applied for the first time to education and other activities conducted after December 31, 2026.
