Full text

The proposal includes suggestions for changes to the Education Act (2010:800), aiming for a strong school system with a focus on knowledge and learning. It also proposes how the curricula for the different school types should be revised for the same purpose. The proposed changes include the following:

The Education Act will be amended to clarify that the purpose of education within the school system is to impart and instill knowledge and values in children and students, and that teaching is determined and led by teachers or preschool teachers. Parents should only be given limited opportunities to influence their child's education.

The emphasis is on the student's acquisition of knowledge, rather than simply meeting grading criteria. Comprehensive assessment should be applied for all grade levels, including the E grade.

The curricula for the compulsory school forms should include introductory sections, course plans, and lesson plans. The course plans should consist of purpose, the subject's contribution to the school's goals, objectives and content, teaching strategies, and grading and assessment criteria. The curricula should be clearly focused on knowledge and a central starting point should be to emphasize basic knowledge and skills, especially in the younger age groups.

New course plans will be introduced for students who are new to the Swedish language, where grades will not be assigned.

The majority of the changes to the Education Act are proposed to take effect on July 1, 2028. However, some changes related to municipal adult education are proposed to take effect on January 1, 2031. New curricula for primary school, special primary school, special school, and Sami school should be implemented starting in the fall semester of 2028.
