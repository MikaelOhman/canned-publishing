Reporting and exchange of information regarding crypto-assets and certain other changes concerning administrative cooperation in the field of taxation.

TL;DRThe government wants to amend the laws to give the Swedish Tax Agency (Skatteverket) more information about cryptocurrencies and other digital assets. This is being done to make it easier for countries to share information with each other and to prevent tax fraud.
In plain language

The government proposes new regulations for how information about money and digital assets should be shared between authorities. This primarily concerns cryptocurrencies, which are a type of digital money. The purpose is to comply with new rules from the EU, called DAC 8.

The proposal also includes the approval of a new international agreement, called CARF. This agreement concerns the automatic exchange of information between countries regarding the ownership of cryptocurrencies. This allows authorities to verify that the correct taxes have been paid.

There are also proposals to amend the rules for how information about regular bank accounts is shared. This will be done through an amendment to an existing system called CRS. This system is already used to ensure that information about financial accounts reaches the appropriate authorities.

Finally, new reporting requirements are proposed for the sale of certain types of securities. This applies to assets related to commodities or indexes. The purpose of all these changes is to create better cooperation between tax authorities in different countries.

Full text

The proposal includes suggestions for:

  • Legislative changes to implement Council Directive (EU) 2023/2226 of October 17, 2023, amending Directive 2011/16/EU on administrative cooperation in the area of taxation (DAC 8).
  • Approval of the multilateral agreement between competent authorities on the automatic exchange of information regarding crypto-assets, and legislative changes to implement the agreement and the underlying model rules for this information exchange (CARF).
  • Approval of the supplementary agreement to the multilateral agreement between competent authorities on the automatic exchange of information on financial accounts, and legislative changes aimed at implementing the agreement and changes to the OECD's common reporting standards regarding the automatic exchange of information on financial accounts (CRS).
  • A reporting obligation for the disposal of securities relating to commodities, crypto-assets, or indices covered by the provisions of Chapter 52 of the Income Tax Act.
