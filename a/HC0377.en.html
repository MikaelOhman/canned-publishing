Full text

In the document, the government presents a national strategy in the area of mental health and suicide prevention. This national strategy aims to provide a long-term direction for the work during the period 2025–2034, based on a cross-sectoral approach that creates the conditions for a common direction for the entire society in the work of mental health and suicide prevention.

The national strategy covers both mental health and suicide prevention in order to develop, consolidate, and strengthen the work. The national strategy also integrates the work of promoting mental well-being and preventing mental illness and suicide with the aim of improving the living conditions for people living with various psychiatric conditions. The starting point is a broad and collaborative effort that addresses both individual and structural factors related to mental health. The strategy focuses on the mental health of both current and future generations, and on the need to create socially sustainable societal structures.

The national strategy is based on the vision: "A society that promotes good and equitable mental health for the entire population, and where no one should find themselves in such a vulnerable situation that suicide is perceived as the only way out." The strategy includes four overall goals and seven sub-goals with priorities that should guide the work in this area. An implementation and monitoring system will be linked to the national strategy.
