Improved regulations for Swedish tonnage taxation.

TL;DRThe government wants to change the rules for how ships pay taxes in order to help the Swedish shipping industry compete better against foreign competitors. The proposal makes it easier for more types of ships to use this tax system.
In plain language

The government has proposed changes to the rules regarding tonnage tax. Tonnage tax is a special way of calculating taxes for ships, designed to allow Swedish companies in the maritime sector to compete with other countries. The proposal also aims to ensure that Swedish regulations are more in line with EU regulations.

The new rules will allow more types of ships, known as specialized shipping, to use this system. It will also be easier for companies to charter out ships without crew. The proposal also aims to change the requirements for which ships can participate in the system. Instead of the ship having to operate internationally, it will be sufficient for the ship to operate in a market where there is significant competition from other countries.

There are also proposals to allow companies to use a special fund to save money on depreciation in a more advantageous way. This is called a "super-depreciation fund" and is a way for companies to manage their costs for ships.

If the parliament approves the proposal, the new laws will come into effect on July 20, 2026. The new rules will begin to be used when the new tax year starts after December 31, 2026.

Full text

The proposal suggests improvements to the Swedish system of tonnage taxation, aiming to strengthen Sweden's competitiveness within the shipping industry. It also proposes certain clarifications and adjustments to the regulations to better align them with the European Commission's guidelines on state aid for maritime transport and the Commission's rulings on the tonnage taxation systems of other member states, which have been established since Sweden introduced the current regulations.

The proposals include expanding the Swedish tonnage taxation system to cover certain activities within specialized shipping. It is also proposed to expand the possibilities of leasing qualified ships without crew within the framework of tonnage-taxed operations. Furthermore, it is proposed to introduce certain changes to the conditions for a ship to be qualified. This includes removing the requirement for international voyages and replacing it with a requirement that the ship is used in traffic subject to international competition in the shipping market. A lower requirement for gross tonnage is also proposed for ships within specialized shipping. The proposal also suggests introducing more generous conditions for companies to use a so-called accelerated depreciation fund. Furthermore, certain clarifications and adjustments to the current rules are proposed.

The proposed amendments to the law are to come into force on July 20, 2026, and will be applied for the first time to tax years beginning after December 31, 2026.
