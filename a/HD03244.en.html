New requirements for interoperability in data sharing within the public sector.

TL;DRThe government wants to introduce a new law that will make it easier for government agencies and municipalities to share digital information with each other. The purpose is to reduce paperwork and to use new technologies, such as AI, in a more efficient way.
In plain language

The government has proposed a new law to govern how information is shared between different parts of the public sector in Sweden. The law concerns so-called interoperability, which is a complex term describing how different data systems should be able to communicate with each other and understand each other's information. By making this easier, the aim is to reduce administrative burden and use modern technology, such as AI, more effectively.

The organizations that must comply with the new rules include government agencies, municipalities, regions, and certain municipal companies. The rules apply when information is shared frequently or in large quantities. However, the government has the right to determine that certain groups do not need to comply with the law, and they can also specify the exact technical requirements that apply to the sharing of information.

There are important exceptions to protect citizens and the country. The new requirements do not need to be followed if it would threaten Sweden's security, or if it becomes too risky for personal data and cybersecurity. Furthermore, the requirements do not need to be followed if the cost is disproportionately high compared to the benefits gained from sharing the information.

If the Parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new law will come into effect on August 15, 2026.

Full text

The government's digitalization strategy for 2025–2030 includes the goal of Sweden having a public administration that simplifies processes and contributes to, among other things, reduced administrative burden through user-friendly, secure, and reliable digital services that are made more efficient through AI and data-driven development. It also states that the public administration should use data to drive innovation and improve societal benefit. The government's AI strategy includes the goal of using and developing AI to promote societal benefit. It also states that the administration needs to increase its ability to work data-driven and to share data. To achieve the political goals in the area of digital administration, data sharing within the public administration needs to be made more efficient.

Therefore, this proposal suggests a new law on interoperability requirements for data sharing within the public administration. The law will apply to government agencies, municipalities, regions, and municipal companies when data sharing occurs regularly or on a large scale. The government will be authorized to issue regulations excluding certain actors from the scope of the law. The government or the agency that the government designates will be authorized to issue regulations on interoperability requirements for data sharing. Such an interoperability requirement should not be followed if it is inappropriate with regard to Sweden's security, or if the protection of cybersecurity or personal data cannot be maintained. An interoperability requirement for data sharing should also not be followed if it is clear that the costs it would entail are not in reasonable proportion to the societal benefit of complying with the requirement.

The new law is proposed to come into force on August 15, 2026.

Proposed Parliamentary Decision

Prop. 2025/26:244

Government Proposal: The Parliament shall adopt the government's proposal for a law on interoperability requirements for data sharing within the public sector. 3

Prop. 2025/26:244 2 Draft Law on Interoperability Requirements for Data Sharing within the Public Sector

Hereby is prescribed the following. Content and Purpose of the Law 1 § This law contains provisions on interoperability requirements for data sharing. The law aims to achieve cost-effective data sharing within the public sector. Scope of Application of the Law 2 § This law applies to data sharing that occurs regularly or on a large scale within:

  1. State authorities under the government,
  2. Municipalities and regions, and
  3. Companies as referred to in Section 2, Paragraph 3 of the Public Access and Secrecy Act (2009:400).

The government may issue regulations stating that certain actors shall be excluded from the scope of application of this law. Interoperability Requirements for Data Sharing 3 § For the purposes of this law, interoperability requirements for data sharing refer to requirements aimed at achieving the ability to provide or receive data with maintained meaning and usability through information systems that interact with each other. Authorization 4 § The government or the authority that the government designates may issue regulations on interoperability requirements for data sharing. Interoperability Requirements for Data Sharing Do Not Always Apply 5 § An interoperability requirement for data sharing shall not apply if:

  1. It would be inappropriate to comply with the requirement, considering Sweden's security,
  2. The protection of cybersecurity or personal data cannot be maintained if the requirement is followed, or
  3. It is obvious that the costs associated with complying with the requirement would not be proportionate to the societal benefit of complying with the requirement.

This law shall enter into force on August 15, 2026. 4
