TL;DRThe Swedish Association of Local Authorities and Regions (SKR) is offering the municipality of Norrtälje a partnership to strengthen its protection against IT attacks, also known as cybersecurity. The municipality must respond to this offer by June 15, 2026.
In plain language

The organization SKR, which represents the municipalities and regions of Sweden, has sent an offer to collaborate on improving the security of digital systems. This initiative, called a "cybersecurity collaboration," aims to protect important information from digital threats.

The offer is part of a broader effort to promote digital development. It has been sent to the municipal leadership and those responsible for IT matters. There are also free online meetings where you can learn more about how the collaboration works.

To proceed with the offer, the municipality must respond by June 15, 2026. If the municipality does not wish to participate, they must also respond and explain their reasons for declining.

The first online meeting to present the offer will be held on May 13, 2026. There is also an opportunity to ask questions at an online meeting on May 19, 2026.
