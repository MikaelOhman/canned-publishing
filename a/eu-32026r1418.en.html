In plain language

The European Union has decided to register the geographical indication for the product "Brhlovské podlievané buchty." This means that the product will receive official recognition as a specialty originating from a specific region in Slovakia.

When a product receives this type of protected designation, it signifies that only products manufactured using a particular method and within a defined area are allowed to use the name. This is a way for the EU to protect local traditions and the quality of food and beverages.

This decision means that the product is now included in the EU's official register of geographical indications. This applies to all countries that are members of the EU and is regulated by the new regulation 2026/1418.

COMMISSION IMPLEMENTING REGULATION (EU) 2026/1418

on the registration of the geographical indication "Brhlovské podlievané buchty" (SGB) in the register of geographical indications maintained by the Union pursuant to Regulation (EU) 2024/1143 of the European Parliament and of the Council.

THE EUROPEAN COMMISSION HAS ADOPTED THIS REGULATION,

having regard to the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union, having regard to Regulation (EU) 2024/1143 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 11 April 2024 on geographical indications for wines, spirits drinks and agricultural products, traditional specialties guaranteed, and quality terms for agricultural products, amending Regulations (EU) No 1308/2013, (EU) 2019/787 and (EU) 2019/1753, and repealing Regulation (EU) No 1151/2012 ( 1 ), in particular Article 21(2), and having regard to Article 50(2)(a) of Regulation (EU) No 1151/2012 ( 2 ), the application lodged by Slovakia for registration of the geographical indication "Brhlovské podlievané buchty", which the Commission received before Regulation (EU) 2024/1143 entered into force, has been published in the Official Journal of the European Union ( 3 ).

No objections pursuant to Article 17 of Regulation (EU) 2024/1143, applicable to applications for registration pursuant to Article 90(2) thereof, have been lodged with the Commission.

The geographical indication "Brhlovské podlievané buchty" should therefore be entered in the register of geographical indications maintained by the Union.

The geographical indication "Brhlovské podlievané buchty" (SGB) shall be entered in the register of geographical indications referred to in Article 22 of Regulation (EU) 2024/1143.

This Regulation shall enter into force on the twentieth day following its publication in the Official Journal of the European Union.

This Regulation is binding in its entirety and directly applicable in all Member States.
