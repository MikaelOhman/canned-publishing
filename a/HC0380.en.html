Enhanced protection for certain elected officials, and clearer internal controls in municipalities and regions.

TL;DRThe government wants to amend the law so that municipalities and regions are obligated to protect their politicians from threats and violence. They also want municipalities and regions to improve their ability to detect errors and fraud in their operations.
In plain language

The government has proposed a measure to enhance the safety of politicians working in municipalities and regions, such as municipal council members. Those serving in municipal or regional councils will now receive stronger protection against threats and violence in their work. This aims to provide them with the same protection against violence as regular employees have under the occupational health and safety law.

The proposal also focuses on improving internal controls. This means that municipalities and regions must have better systems for detecting and correcting errors or irregularities, such as fraud or incorrect decisions. The goal is to make the work in the municipality and region more legally sound and to be able to stop improper practices more quickly.

If the parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new rules will come into effect on July 1, 2025. This means that both municipalities and regions will have a greater responsibility to ensure that their operations function correctly and that their elected officials are safe.

Full text

In this proposal, the government suggests a requirement for municipalities and regions to prevent elected officials, as specified in section 4, paragraph 2, first sentence of the Municipal Act (2017:725), including municipal and regional councilors, from experiencing health problems or accidents as a result of threats and violence. This new requirement aligns with the responsibility that municipalities and regions have for their employees, as stipulated in section 3, paragraph 2, first sentence, and section 3, first sentence, second clause of the Working Environment Act (1977:1160), regarding threats and violence. The proposal aims to strengthen the protection against threats and violence for elected officials.

The proposal also suggests that internal controls within municipalities and regions should prevent errors and irregularities in operations. The purpose of the proposal is to ensure that municipalities and regions more effectively prevent, detect, and address errors and irregularities in their operations.

The proposed amendments to the law are scheduled to come into effect on July 1, 2025.
