In plain language

The government has proposed a measure that would make it tax-free to charge electric vehicles at the workplace, even in the future. Previously, this has only been a temporary rule, but now the government wants it to be permanent to help companies and employees transition to fossil-free vehicles.

It will also be easier to claim reimbursements for expenses related to fuel when driving for business trips. If you have a company car that is a plug-in hybrid, you should be able to deduct the costs of fuel, even if you have charged the car with electricity at your workplace. The purpose is to simplify the rules and encourage people to drive as much as possible on electricity.

The proposal involves changes to the income tax law, which is the law that determines how much tax you pay on your salary. If the parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new rules will come into effect on July 1, 2026. The new rules for deducting fuel expenses apply to trips made after the new law comes into effect.

Full text

The proposal suggests that the temporary tax exemption for the benefit of charging at the workplace should become permanent. The proposal aims, among other things, to continue facilitating the transition to a fossil-free vehicle fleet and to encourage business travel using rechargeable vehicles rather than other, less sustainable, alternatives.

The proposal also suggests an expanded deduction right for expenses related to fuel for business trips using a company car in certain cases. The proposal means that taxpayers who have a plug-in hybrid as a company car are entitled to deduct expenses for fuel associated with business trips, even if the car was charged with electricity provided by the employer at the workplace. The proposal aims to simplify the regulations and encourage people who have a plug-in hybrid as a company car to use electric power as much as possible for business trips.

The proposed changes to the law are scheduled to come into effect on July 1, 2026. The new rules regarding the expanded deduction right are proposed to be applied for the first time to expenses for fuel associated with business trips after the effective date.
