Disclosure of information from the register of beneficial owners.

TL;DRSweden is going to change the rules regarding who has access to information about the actual owners of a company. The purpose is to comply with new EU regulations aimed at preventing money laundering.
In plain language

The government proposes new laws that will change how information can be accessed from the register of beneficial owners. Beneficial owners are the individuals who ultimately control or own a company. The change is being implemented to comply with new EU regulations aimed at preventing crime and money laundering.

Currently, the register is too openly accessible to anyone. Following a ruling by the EU Court of Justice, the rules must be changed so that the information is not completely accessible to everyone. This means that there will be stricter rules about which individuals or companies can access the data.

Those who have a legitimate reason, also known as a legitimate interest, should still be able to access the information digitally. There will also be new rules on how to prove that one has such a legitimate reason. An electronic system should be available for searching for the information.

The new rules are proposed to come into effect on July 1, 2026. The proposal involves changes to several laws, including the Act on the Registration of Beneficial Owners and the Act on Public Access and Secrecy.

Full text

The European Union's sixth anti-money laundering directive must be implemented into Swedish law by July 2027 at the latest. However, certain articles must be implemented by July 10, 2025, and July 10, 2026, respectively. This concerns regulations on how information from the register of beneficial owners should be disclosed and the confidentiality of certain information in the register. These regulations are, among other things, the result of adjustments made following a ruling from the EU Court of Justice, which invalidated the current provision regarding public access to information from the register of beneficial owners.

This proposal addresses these articles. It proposes the introduction of:

  • Stricter rules on how different categories of recipients can access information from the register of beneficial owners, and
  • A new provision that allows for the disclosure of confidential information.

Since the new EU regulations require that individuals with a legitimate interest in the information in the register of beneficial owners should be able to access it electronically, and also specify a procedure for determining whether a legitimate interest exists, it is proposed that similar provisions be incorporated into national law.

The proposed amendments to the law are intended to come into force on July 1, 2026.

Proposed Parliamentary Decision

The government's proposal:

  1. The Riksdag (Swedish Parliament) adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Act (2017:631) on the registration of beneficial owners.
  2. The Riksdag adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Public Access and Secrecy Act (2009:400).
  3. The Riksdag adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Act (2011:755) on electronic money.
