Full text

The government's proposal includes several measures aimed at creating a more just penal system, where punishments better reflect the severity of the crime, the perspective of victims is given greater weight, and societal security is improved. These proposals include the following:

  • Approximately fifty penalty ranges will be tightened.
  • A new regulation will be introduced for the common penalty scale in cases of multiple offenses, stipulating that the maximum sentence that can be imposed is double the highest maximum penalty.
  • A life sentence may be imposed as a common sentence for certain serious cases of repeated violent and sexual offenses.
  • A new aggravating factor will be introduced, applicable to all crimes linked to criminal networks. When this factor applies, the severity of the crime should initially be assessed as twice as high as it would otherwise be.
  • The presumption for pre-trial detention will be extended to include the crimes of aggravated domestic violence, aggravated harassment, and honor-based oppression. This presumption should also apply when the suspicion relates to an attempt, preparation, or incitement to a crime for which a penalty less severe than imprisonment for four years is not prescribed.

The proposed amendments to the law will come into effect on August 1, 2026.
