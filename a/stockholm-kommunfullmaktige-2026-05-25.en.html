TL;DRThe City of Stockholm has made numerous decisions regarding new construction projects, parks, and protected natural areas. These decisions include plans for new housing, new roads, and the preservation of nature in various parts of the city.
In plain language

The City of Stockholm has approved several major construction projects. New apartments will be built in Aspudden, and new care facilities will be constructed in Bagarmossen and Gröndal. There are also plans to build a new administrative office in Järva and to develop the waterfront promenade along the Karlberg Canal.

Regarding transportation and roads, significant work has been approved for the western part of the Liljeholms bridges. There are also plans to build a new subway depot in the Älvsjö industrial area. To facilitate access for visitors during the FIFA World Cup 2026, temporary regulations have been approved regarding the hours during which restaurants and bars can serve alcohol.

Nature will be protected in several areas of the city. Several new areas will be designated as "biotope protection areas," meaning that the natural environment there will be preserved. This applies to areas such as Hemskogen, Vikingaberget, Gökdalen, and Åkeslund. The city has also decided to create a new nature reserve in the Kyrkhamn-Riddersvik area.

The city has also entered into business deals and agreements. This includes purchasing shares in Kista Utveckling AB and buying emission reductions to help combat climate change. Agreements have also been signed to build the Nobel Center in Södermalm.
