Full text

The Swedish National Audit Office has reviewed whether temporary tax payment deferrals have been an effective measure. Temporary tax payment deferrals have been used as a crisis measure during both the financial crisis and the pandemic, as well as in the years that followed. This measure allows companies to postpone the payment of certain taxes, to be paid later with accrued interest and fees. The purpose has been to assist fundamentally sound and viable companies facing temporary liquidity problems.

The Swedish National Audit Office's overall conclusion is that the temporary deferrals have been a relatively effective measure. However, there have been some shortcomings. The Swedish National Audit Office provides five recommendations to the government to consider in future proposals regarding temporary tax payment deferrals. These include limiting the possibility of obtaining deferrals to periods when the credit market is not functioning normally, limiting the scope of deferrals to a few accounting periods, considering clarifying the reasons for which deferrals should not be granted, considering whether deferral fees and interest charges should be charged continuously, and improving the calculation of expected collection losses and accounting for the risks associated with tax credits.

The government sees no reason to implement any measures in the near future based on the Swedish National Audit Office's recommendations. However, the Swedish National Audit Office's recommendations, in the areas where the government shares the same assessment as the Swedish National Audit Office, may be part of the considerations made in the event of a future crisis.

With this report, the government considers the Swedish National Audit Office's report to be finalized.
