TL;DRThe municipality of Tranemo has changed the rules regarding who is authorized to make decisions in cases involving individuals requiring assistance (legal guardians). They have also decided not to provide funding to an organization that supports the work of legal guardians.
In plain language

The Guardianship Board, which is the municipal department responsible for ensuring that individuals who need assistance receive the appropriate support, has revised its delegation of authority. This means they have rewritten the rules regarding which employees are authorized to make decisions and when those decisions must be made by managers or politicians. The purpose is to comply with new laws and improve efficiency.

A key change is that the guardianship board now determines whether someone should be assigned a legal guardian, whereas previously this was decided in the district court. It has also become clearer which cases are so important or complex that they can never be handled by employees and must be decided by politicians.

In addition to these regulations, the board has made a decision regarding a financial request. The Sjuhärad Guardianship Association requested 15,000 Swedish krona from the municipality to cover costs for items such as educational materials, insurance, and meetings. However, the board decided to reject this application.
