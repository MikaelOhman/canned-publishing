Increased limit for deductible expenses related to travel to and from work.

TL;DRThe limit for the amount of money you can receive back in tax deductions for commuting expenses will be increased. This means that more people will be able to deduct their travel costs on their tax return starting in 2026.
In plain language

The government has proposed a change to the rules regarding the amount that can be deducted for travel expenses on income tax returns. Currently, there is a limit of 11,000 Swedish krona, but the proposal is to increase this limit to 15,000 Swedish krona.

This applies to travel to and from work, as well as travel to and from educational institutions. This refers to the costs you incur to get to your workplace or school.

If the proposal is approved, the new rule will come into effect on January 1, 2026. This means that the new, higher limit will apply to your travel expenses from that year.

The proposal is now being sent to the Parliament, which is the part of the government that decides on laws, so that they can vote yes or no on the change to the income tax law.

Full text

The proposal suggests increasing the limit for deductible expenses for work-related travel, travel to and from educational institutions, and travel due to canceled appointments, from 11,000 to 15,000 Swedish krona.

The proposed amendment to the law is scheduled to take effect on January 1, 2026.
