In plain language

The Swedish Consumer Agency, a government body responsible for protecting consumers, has begun investigating barbecue brushes with metal bristles sold in the Swedish market. This is because many people in Sweden and other countries have suffered serious injuries after accidentally swallowing small pieces of metal from these brushes.

When a person swallows a metal bristle, it can cause significant damage to the stomach, often requiring surgery. A study from the United States shows that thousands of people have needed medical attention due to these incidents. Because there is a risk of many accidents, the agency wants to check how safe the brushes sold in Sweden actually are.

During the summer, the Consumer Agency recommends taking extra precautions when grilling. You should feel the brush to ensure the metal bristles are securely attached, carefully inspect the grill grate to make sure no loose bristles remain, and check the food before eating it to ensure it is free from metal fragments.

Following several serious accidents, both in Sweden and other countries, the Swedish Consumer Agency has initiated an investigation into grill brushes with metal bristles. The agency is also urging consumers to be careful when using grill brushes to avoid accidentally swallowing metal bristles. In Sweden, there are a couple of known cases where brush bristles have detached from the grill brush, become lodged on the grill, transferred to food, and then been inadvertently swallowed. These injuries can be very serious, for example, causing intestinal damage that requires emergency surgery. There is also data from other countries indicating a high risk of injury. In an American study examining the years 2015-2023, the authors estimate that thousands of accidents involving grill brushes have occurred, requiring consumers to seek medical attention. These figures suggest that grill brushes with metal bristles may be dangerous for consumers, and that there may be unreported cases in Sweden. Therefore, the Swedish Consumer Agency has now launched a market surveillance program in Sweden. "We need to check the safety of these grill brushes," says Catrine Niiranen Tengqvist, a lawyer at the Swedish Consumer Agency. "The injuries that can occur are very serious." The results of the Consumer Agency's investigation will be available later this year. However, to reduce the risk of further injuries, Catrine Niiranen Tengqvist urges caution during summer barbecues. Here are three things to keep in mind to avoid injury:

  • Check the brush to ensure that the bristles are securely attached.
  • Inspect the grill grate to make sure no loose bristles are stuck on it.
  • Examine the food to ensure it is clean and free of any metal bristles.
