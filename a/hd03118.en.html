In plain language

The government has proposed a plan that includes changes to several important laws, such as the Environmental Code and the Electricity Act. The purpose is to streamline the process of obtaining permits for renewable energy projects. If the proposal is approved, the new regulations could come into effect on July 1, 2026.

A key element is that the county administrative boards, which are the authorities responsible for environmental issues in each county, will have a greater role early in the process. By allowing the county administrative boards to review the documentation before engaging with other parties, unnecessary errors can be avoided. It will also become easier to determine the scope of environmental assessments required and how to build in protected natural areas designated as Natura 2000.

The proposal also aims to reduce duplication of effort. If a thorough environmental assessment has already been conducted in accordance with the Environmental Code, a completely new assessment should not be required for other laws related to the same project. This is intended to save time and resources for those who want to invest in green energy.

Finally, the proposal suggests that there should be clear deadlines for how long authorities can take to make decisions on cases related to renewable energy under the planning and building law. This is to prevent important projects from being delayed by long waiting times.

Full text

The proposal includes legislative changes necessary to implement the provisions on permitting procedures in the EU's directive on renewable energy. The changes are made in the Environmental Code, the Planning and Building Act, and a number of sector-specific laws, including the Electricity Act.

The proposed changes to the Environmental Code include, among other things, that those intending to operate a business or take action for renewable energy should give the county administrative board an opportunity to assess the consultation document before consultations with other stakeholders begin. Furthermore, provisions are introduced that aim to give the county administrative board's assessment of the scope of an environmental impact assessment a greater importance in a later permitting process. A provision is also proposed to simplify the assessment of whether a business or action that affects a Natura 2000 area can be permitted.

The changes to the sector-specific laws include, among other things, that a new specific environmental assessment should not be required in a permitting process in accordance with any of these laws, if the case concerns a business or action for renewable energy and a specific environmental assessment has been carried out in a case or proceeding regarding a permit according to the Environmental Code that covers the business or action.

Finally, it is proposed to introduce provisions with deadlines for the handling of certain cases under the Planning and Building Act that relate to renewable energy.

The legislative changes are proposed to come into force on July 1, 2026.
