In plain language

The government has proposed new regulations for port security. This means they want to amend the current law from 2006 to make it easier to understand what the law covers and where it applies.

A key part of the proposal is to precisely define the boundaries of a port's protected area. This will be done through a special investigation called the "Port Security Investigation." The purpose is to clearly define the extent of the area that needs protection for everyone.

If the Parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new regulations will come into effect on July 1, 2025. The proposal is presented in a document called "Proposition 2024/25:148."

Full text

The proposal includes suggested amendments to the law (2006:1209) concerning port security. These changes aim to clarify the scope of the law and stipulate that the boundaries of the port, for the purposes of security, should be defined in the port security assessment.

The proposed amendments are scheduled to come into effect on July 1, 2025.
