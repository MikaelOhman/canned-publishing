TL;DRThe government wants to change the law so that companies and organizations need a permit to inherit agricultural land. The purpose is to ensure a good balance between private individuals and companies when it comes to land ownership.
In plain language

The government has proposed a change to the law regarding land acquisition. This means that legal entities, such as companies or associations, will no longer be able to acquire agricultural land solely through inheritance or a will without requiring a special permit.

If a company or organization acquires new land through a will or from the National Inheritance Fund, they must also relinquish other agricultural land. This is to ensure fairness and to prevent too much land from ending up in the hands of large organizations instead of private individuals.

The proposal aims to create a balance in land ownership. If one acquires new land, they must give up other land to maintain a fair distribution across the landscape.

If the parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new rules will come into effect on January 1, 2027.

Full text

The government's proposal suggests expanding the requirement for acquisition permits under the Land Acquisition Act to include acquisitions of agricultural land by legal entities, both through testamentary provisions and from the National Inheritance Fund. These changes mean that legal entities will no longer be able to acquire such land without also being required to relinquish other agricultural land. The proposals aim to ensure that the balance in land ownership between legal and natural persons can continue to be maintained.

The changes are proposed to come into effect on January 1, 2027.
