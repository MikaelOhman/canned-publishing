In plain language

The Swedish Prison and Probation Service urgently needs more places in prisons and detention centers. This proposal aims to accelerate, streamline, and improve the expansion process through amendments to the planning and building law.

The possibilities for granting temporary building permits for institutions and detention centers are being expanded, and the maximum duration for such permits is extended to twenty years.

The government is also proposed to be given the authority to act in cases of an acute and significant risk of severe overcrowding in the prison system.

Full text

The proposal suggests changes to the Planning and Building Act (2010:900) that are expected to provide better conditions for a faster, more flexible, and more efficient expansion of correctional facilities and detention centers.

The possibilities of granting temporary building permits for correctional facilities and detention centers are expanded. Temporary permits should, after an assessment by the municipality, be granted if the applicant requests it, if it is technically possible to restore the site or building, and if the measure meets a condition for a permanent building permit. The maximum duration for temporary permits is extended to twenty years.

Furthermore, the government is proposed to be authorized to, if necessary to address an acute and significant risk of severe overcrowding in correctional facilities and detention centers, issue regulations regarding necessary and temporary exemptions from provisions in the Planning and Building Act, such as from the requirement for a building permit, in order to arrange temporary correctional facilities and detention centers.

The amendments to the law are proposed to take effect on July 1, 2026.
