In plain language

The government wants to amend the law so that certain types of investment funds can become tax-transparent. This means that investors in these funds will pay taxes in the same way as if they directly owned the assets. These funds are only intended for use by large organizations, known as institutional investors, which manage money on behalf of others or for public purposes.

The purpose of this change is to make it cheaper and more efficient for these organizations to manage money. Since most of these organizations handle pension savings, the new law will benefit ordinary people who are saving for their future retirement. It will be easier for them to have their money invested in a good way.

This change is also intended to make the Swedish financial market more competitive. By giving Swedish fund companies the same opportunities as they have in other countries, more large investors may choose to invest their money in Sweden. The new rules are planned to come into effect on January 1, 2027.

Full text

The proposal includes suggested amendments to laws that would make it possible to establish tax-transparent funds in Sweden. Such a fund should be a special type of investment fund, accessible only to certain categories of institutional investors. These are institutional investors who manage pension assets or other capital for public purposes or on behalf of the general public. The purpose of this new type of fund is to ensure that the tax situation regarding investments in these funds is the same as with a direct investment.

For investors, a tax-transparent fund can be a more cost-effective and tax-efficient alternative compared to a regular investment fund or a direct investment. Since these entities manage pension assets or otherwise manage capital on behalf of the public, the benefits of this proposal will accrue to pension savers and the general public.

By making it possible to establish tax-transparent funds in Sweden, the competitiveness of the Swedish fund market is also strengthened, as fund companies are given the same opportunities to attract capital from institutional investors into Swedish funds as fund managers have in other countries.

The proposed amendments are scheduled to come into effect on January 1, 2027.
