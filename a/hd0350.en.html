TL;DRThe government wants to change the law so that hospitals can employ physicists who are training to become specialists. This is being done to ensure that there are enough experts in hospitals in the future.
In plain language

The government has proposed a change to the law regarding healthcare. The proposal would allow the different regions in Sweden, which are responsible for healthcare services, to employ hospital physicists who are undergoing a specialized training program. This means they would be able to work and study simultaneously.

The purpose of this change is to prepare the healthcare system for the future. The goal is to ensure that there are enough physicists with specialized expertise when the demand increases. By offering these positions, it is possible to build up the necessary expertise in hospitals.

If the Parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new law will come into effect on April 1, 2026. The proposal is outlined in a document called "proposition 2025/26:50."

Full text

The proposal suggests that regions should have the opportunity to employ hospital physicists who undertake specialized training, to a degree that corresponds to the planned future need for hospital physicists with specialist expertise. The proposed amendment to the law is scheduled to come into effect on April 1, 2026.
