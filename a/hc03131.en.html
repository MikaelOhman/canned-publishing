New regulations regarding data centers and sustainable fuels, as well as a new reduction obligation.

TL;DRThe government is proposing new laws to improve the environment and regulate energy consumption. These proposals include requirements for data centers to disclose their electricity usage and changes to regulations regarding fuel.
In plain language

The government intends to amend several laws to comply with new regulations from the EU. A key aspect is that data centers, which are large facilities that store digital information, will be required to report their energy consumption to a common database in Europe. This is being done to gain better insight into the amount of electricity used for digital technology.

There will also be stricter regulations regarding what qualifies as sustainable fuels. This means that new requirements will be imposed on fuels derived from, for example, recycled materials or other environmentally friendly sources, requiring them to meet certain environmental standards. Laws related to energy taxes and electricity certificates will also be amended to align with these new regulations.

Regarding fuels used in cars, proposals are being made for new levels for the biofuel blending mandate. The biofuel blending mandate is the requirement to blend more environmentally friendly fuels into gasoline and diesel. It is now proposed that this requirement can be met by using fossil-free electricity. At the same time, it is proposed that the requirement for fuel suppliers to reduce greenhouse gas emissions be removed.

Most of these new regulations are intended to come into effect on July 1, 2025. However, certain aspects related to environmental requirements for fuels will take effect later, either on January 1, 2026, or January 1, 2030.

Full text

The proposal includes legislative changes aimed at implementing parts of the revised directives on renewable energy, fuel quality, and the revised energy efficiency directive, as well as adapting Swedish law to the EU regulation ReFuelEU Aviation. It also includes changes to implement the proposed reductions in the fuel tax, as announced by the government in the 2025 budget proposal.

A new law is proposed regarding the disclosure of information on the energy performance of data centers. This law would require data centers to report information, including energy consumption, to a European database. Data centers used for or providing services for certain purposes would be exempt from this reporting requirement.

The proposal also includes suggestions for new and amended sustainability criteria for renewable fuels, renewable fuels of non-biological origin, and recycled carbon-based fuels. More fuels will be covered by the sustainability criteria. Amendments are also proposed to the law on energy taxes and the law on electricity certificates.

New levels are proposed for the fuel tax reduction requirement, along with the possibility of meeting this requirement through the supply of fossil-free electricity.

Furthermore, changes are proposed to the fuel law. These changes include the elimination of the requirement for fuel suppliers to reduce greenhouse gas emissions from the fuels they supply.

Most of the proposed legislative changes are scheduled to come into effect on July 1, 2025. Some of the legislative changes related to sustainability criteria are proposed to come into effect on January 1, 2026, and January 1, 2030, respectively.
