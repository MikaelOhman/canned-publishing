Obligation to pay for dental care – new rules for certain foreign nationals

Source Riksdagen Read the original at Riksdagen →2026-06-25
TL;DRNew regulations are being proposed that would require certain foreign nationals residing in Sweden to pay the full price for emergency dental care, instead of a reduced fee.
In plain language

The government has proposed new regulations regarding who is eligible for dental care assistance. For certain foreign nationals, such as those seeking asylum or residing in Sweden without permission, the usual low fee of 50 kronor will no longer apply if the treatment is urgent and cannot be postponed. Instead, these individuals will be required to pay a reasonable fee to the Swedish Migration Agency (Migrationsverket).

The new regulations apply to individuals who have received deportation orders that have been suspended, or those residing in Sweden without proper authorization. If these individuals have their own funds or income, they are expected to pay for their dental care according to the dentist's fees.

If an individual has no personal funds whatsoever, they may be eligible for financial assistance from the local social services department (socialnämnden), which is responsible for social welfare matters within the municipality. This means that even individuals with very limited resources can still receive help with their dental care.

There are also regulations concerning newly established private dental practices. If a practice was started before 2026 but has not yet obtained all necessary permits from the Swedish Inspectorate for Health and Welfare (Inspektionen för vård och omsorg), it may continue to operate without full authorization during a transitional period, provided that the application process is initiated in a timely manner.

Full text

The proposal suggests new regulations regarding dental care fees for certain foreign nationals. The proposals stipulate that individuals seeking international protection, foreign nationals held in detention facilities operated by the Swedish Migration Agency, and foreign nationals who have received a decision for rejection or deportation but where enforcement has been suspended, must pay a reasonable fee to the Swedish Migration Agency for necessary dental care if they are over 18 years old and have income or assets.

For foreign nationals over 18 years of age residing in Sweden without authorization from authorities or legal basis, whose stay is not intended to be temporary, including individuals avoiding enforcement of decisions regarding rejection or deportation, non-deferrable dental care should not be subsidized. This means that these foreign nationals must pay for dental care according to the criteria determined by the healthcare provider. The payment obligation should therefore no longer be limited to 50 Swedish krona. It is proposed that such foreign nationals have the right to receive financial assistance from the social services department for necessary dental care.

The proposal also suggests that a private dental care provider who, before January 1, 2026, had commenced operations but had not registered it with the Board of Health and Welfare (Inspektionen för vård och omsorg), should be allowed to continue operating without a license, even if an application for a license has not been submitted by March 2, 2026, in accordance with existing transitional provisions. Provided that the application is submitted before January 1, 2027, the operation may continue without a license until the application has been finally processed. However, this does not apply if a previous application for a license has been rejected by a legally binding decision.
