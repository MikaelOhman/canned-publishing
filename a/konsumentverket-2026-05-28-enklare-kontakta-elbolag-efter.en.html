In plain language

The Swedish Consumer Agency (Konsumentverket) investigated 35 different electricity companies to see how easy it is for customers to contact them. They found that nine of the companies did not have a clearly visible email address on their website, instead using chat or contact forms.

According to the law, companies that sell services to private individuals must provide both a telephone number and an email address that are easily accessible. The Consumer Agency believes that these two methods of contacting the company are important for ensuring that customers receive prompt assistance.

All electricity companies that were missing this information have now updated their websites to comply with the regulations. It is important for all companies to consolidate all contact information in one place so that it is easy for customers to find what they need.

Easier Contact with Electricity Companies After Review News Published: May 28, 2026

The Swedish Consumer Agency (Konsumentverket) reviewed 35 electricity companies during the spring. The purpose was to check if they displayed email addresses and phone numbers for customer service. Of the 35 companies reviewed, 26 had both an email address and a phone number on their website. The nine that lacked this information instead provided a form or a chat function. "While contact forms and chats can supplement an email address, they should never replace it," says Emelie Neidre, a lawyer at the Swedish Consumer Agency. "Both the Distance Contracts Act and the E-commerce Act require companies to have an email address that customers can easily find."

All Companies Complied During the agency's review, all nine companies corrected the deficiency. "Clear and readily available contact information is crucial for consumers to be able to get in touch with companies. It is best when companies gather contact details and any additional contact methods in one place."

Background: In 2023, the Swedish Consumer Agency conducted a survey about company customer service and contact information. The agency then noted a lack of contact information for companies in several industries. Furthermore, the survey showed that many consumers did not feel they received adequate help when they finally reached customer service. This survey was followed by a government assignment the following year, which resulted in the report "Deficiencies in Company Customer Services." This report stated, among other things, that it is not uncommon for phone numbers or email addresses to be missing or difficult to find.
