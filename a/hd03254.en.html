Full text

The government's proposal aims to create better conditions for Sweden to participate in operational military cooperation within NATO. The government proposes, among other things, that the government should be authorized to decide that NATO, or a state that is a member of that organization or the EU, may take military defensive measures on Swedish territory when carrying out military operations for deterrence and defense. Furthermore, a number of legislative changes are proposed to improve the conditions for foreign forces to conduct military operations in Sweden.

These legislative changes are proposed to come into force on August 1, 2026.

Proposed Resolutions for the Parliament

The government's proposal:

  1. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Act (1992:1300) on War Materiel.
  2. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Protection Act (2010:305).
  3. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Act (2018:1200) on Camera Surveillance.
  4. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Act (2020:782) on Operational Military Cooperation.
  5. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Weapons Act (2026:000).
