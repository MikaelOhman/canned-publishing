Full text

Blanka of Namur (Blanche), born around 1320 and died in 1363, was the Countess of what is now a province in Belgium, and became Queen of Sweden, Norway, and Scania from 1335 through her marriage to King Magnus Eriksson.

== Biography == Blanka was born at Namur Castle in present-day Belgium, as the daughter of Count Jean Dampierre of Namur and his Countess Marie of Artois, who descended from King Louis VIII of France. Her year of birth is unknown, but she was likely around the same age as her husband.

It is not known how the Swedish King Magnus Eriksson first learned about Blanka. In June 1334, he traveled from Sweden with a retinue to the County of Namur to request her hand in marriage. They became engaged there, and he returned to Sweden in the autumn of 1334. Blanka left Namur in the autumn of 1335, and the wedding took place in October or early November 1335, possibly at Bohus Fortress. As a dowry, she received Tønsberg manor and province in Norway, as well as Lödöse on the Göta River and Lindholmen fortress on Hisingen Island. The coronation took place in July 1336, possibly on July 21st in Stockholm Cathedral. In a second marriage contract in 1353, she also received Bohus Fortress and Marstrand with Älvsyssel (a district), excluding Orust. Contemporary sources indicate that Queen Blanka was both beautiful and intelligent, but there are no known portraits of her that can be definitively identified as being of her. The queen's own seal is known from a diploma issued in 1346. The seal depicts a woman dressed in a long gown without a belt, holding a scepter in her right hand, and wearing a crown on her head. On April 9, 1363, her then 23-year-old son, Håkan Magnusson, married Valdemar Atterdag's daughter, Margaret. Shortly after the wedding, Blanka died. The cause of her death is unknown, as is the location of her grave. The children's song "Rida, rida ranka, hästen heter Blanka" (Ride, ride, swift horse, the horse is named Blanka) is about Queen Blanka and was written in the late 19th century by Hans Henric Hallbäck.
