Full text

When a crime cannot be prevented, societal resources should focus on mitigating the consequences of the crime and assisting the victim. Those who are victims of crime can suffer various damages, and financial compensation for these damages is an important part of the victim's recovery. The legitimacy of the compensation system requires that victims not only have the right to compensation for their damages but also have good opportunities to actually receive that compensation. Therefore, the government proposes several legislative changes to strengthen the position of victims and reduce the consequences of crime. These proposals include:

  • Simplifying and shortening the process for victims to receive compensation for damages caused by crime.
  • Victims should be able to receive such compensation from the National Authority for Victims of Crime based on a court judgment for damages without first having to contact the perpetrator.
  • Increasing the liability of guardians for damages caused by their children through criminal acts.
  • Facilitating the voluntary payment of compensation for damages caused by crime.
  • Limiting the prohibitions on the seizure of certain payments to a greater extent, in order to make it more possible to seize assets to benefit victims.

The government also proposes legislative changes to achieve a more reasonable and efficient system for compensation in cases of detention.

These legislative changes are proposed to come into effect on September 1, 2026.
