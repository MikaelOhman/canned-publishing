TL;DRNew regulations are being proposed to protect farmers and food suppliers from buyers suddenly canceling orders. It will be prohibited to cancel an order if the supplier does not have time to find another customer for the goods.
In plain language

The government wants to amend the law regarding the trade of agricultural and food products. The purpose is to make the rules clearer and to comply with EU regulations to prevent unfair practices in the purchase of food and agricultural products.

Currently, there is a ban on canceling an order if there are less than 30 days remaining until delivery. The new proposal would also prohibit canceling an order with more time remaining, if it is impossible for the seller to find another buyer in time.

The proposal aims to create security for those who produce food. If a buyer cancels an order too late, it can lead to significant losses for the party delivering the goods.

If the proposal is approved by the parliament, the new law will come into effect on June 1, 2026.

Full text

The proposal suggests an adjustment to the rules regarding the prohibition of late cancellations when purchasing agricultural and food products. The proposal supplements the current prohibition against canceling an order with less than 30 days' notice, or a shorter period as stipulated by regulations, with a new provision that clarifies that cancellations with longer notice periods are also prohibited if the supplier cannot reasonably expect to find an alternative way to sell or use the products in question. The proposal aims to ensure proper implementation of the so-called UTP Directive.

The proposed amendment to the law is scheduled to come into effect on June 1, 2026.

Prop. 2025/26:135 2 Proposed Amendment to the Act

(2021:579) on the prohibition of unfair business practices in the purchase of agricultural and food products.

Hereby, it is stipulated that section 5 of the Act (2021:579) on the prohibition of unfair business practices in the purchase of agricultural and food products shall have the following wording.

Current Wording Proposed Wording 5 § The following practices by a purchaser of agricultural and food products are prohibited:

  1. paying later than 30 days after the time specified in sections 6 or 7,
  2. cancelling an order with less than 30 days' notice, unless otherwise provided for in regulations issued pursuant to section 10,
  3. in cases other than those specified in paragraph 2, cancelling an order with such short notice that the supplier cannot reasonably be expected to find an alternative way to market or use the products in question,
  4. unilaterally imposing a change to the terms of a contract regarding intervals, methods, locations, times, or volumes of delivery, quality requirements, payment, or price,
  5. unilaterally imposing a change to the terms of a contract regarding the services specified in section 12, paragraph 1, items 3-5, or paragraph 2,
  6. requiring the supplier to pay for items not related to the supplier's sales,
  7. requiring the supplier to pay for damage or loss that occurs in the buyer's premises or after the buyer's ownership of the goods has passed, where such damage or loss is not due to the supplier's negligence or fault,
  8. failing to accommodate the supplier's request for a written confirmation of the terms of a contract,
  9. taking or threatening to take commercial retaliatory measures against a supplier who exercises their contractual or legal rights, and
  10. requiring the supplier to pay for the cost of handling customer complaints related to the sale of the supplier's agricultural and food products when there has been no negligence or fault on the part of the supplier.

Provisions regarding unauthorized access to trade secrets are found in the Act (2018:558) on trade secrets. This Act shall enter into force on June 1, 2026.
