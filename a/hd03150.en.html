Full text

Serious violent crime, particularly shootings and bombings within criminal circles, is a significant societal problem. To reverse this trend and also to address other serious forms of crime, the police must have effective and appropriate tools. The government is therefore proposing a new law that would allow the police to use AI-powered facial recognition systems in real-time to combat crime.

The new law would authorize the police to use this AI technology only when it is absolutely necessary to locate or identify a specific person:

  • Who is suspected of being a victim of kidnapping, human trafficking, or human exploitation, or who is missing and suspected of being a victim of a crime;
  • If there is an imminent risk that the person will commit a serious crime that poses a danger to the life or physical safety of others;
  • Who is reasonably suspected of having committed a specific serious crime for which the penalty includes imprisonment for four years or more, or a similar penalty may apply for attempted, preparatory, or accessory offenses, in order to investigate or prosecute the crime; or
  • Who has been convicted of such a serious crime, in order to enforce the criminal penalty.

The use of this technology must be proportionate, and authorization from a prosecutor or court must be required. In urgent cases, the use of the technology may begin without authorization, but an application for authorization must be submitted within 24 hours.

The new law and other proposed amendments are scheduled to come into effect on July 1, 2026.
