Temporary housing for certain newly arrived immigrants – a new law regarding settlement.

Source Riksdagen Read the original at Riksdagen →2026-03-31
TL;DRThe government wants to change the rules regarding how newly arrived immigrants can reside in Sweden. The goal is to give municipalities more power over who moves to which area, and to help newly arrived immigrants find employment.
In plain language

The government has proposed a new law regarding settlement. This law will replace the old law from 2016. The proposal concerns how newly arrived individuals should be accommodated in different municipalities in Sweden.

Municipalities will have more influence over how they receive new residents. When deciding which municipality a person should move to, consideration will be given to the availability of jobs and housing in that municipality. It will also consider areas where there are significant problems with social exclusion, meaning where many people are marginalized from society. Municipalities will also be able to express their preferences if they have the necessary resources.

Individuals who move to a municipality after the state has determined their placement will be entitled to housing for up to 36 months. The municipality is responsible for arranging this housing. However, the right to remain may expire earlier if the person does not participate in programs to find employment or if they have done something that results in them losing their place in a job market program.

If the Parliament approves the proposal, the new rules will come into effect on January 1, 2027. The proposal also includes changes to other laws concerning, among other things, personal data and regulations for real estate.

Full text

The proposal includes a draft law on settlement for certain newly arrived immigrants, which would replace the law (2016:38) on the reception of certain newly arrived immigrants for settlement. The proposals aim, among other things, to increase the municipalities' influence over the reception process and to ensure that newly arrived immigrants receive more equitable support regarding housing. Furthermore, the proposals aim to strengthen incentives for newly arrived immigrants to actively contribute to their integration.

The proposals include, among other things, that municipalities will continue to be obligated to receive newly arrived immigrants after assignment, and that the distribution of assignments between municipalities should be based on the conditions for integration in the municipalities. In addition to the criteria for distribution that apply today, such as the labor market and population size, it is proposed that the conditions on the housing market and areas with high levels of social exclusion should also be taken into account. It is proposed that a municipality's wishes regarding the reception process should be taken into account under certain conditions when distributing assignments.

Furthermore, it is proposed, among other things, that a newly arrived immigrant who has been received by a municipality after assignment should have the right to housing for 36 months, and that the municipality should arrange this housing during that time. It is proposed that the right to housing may be terminated prematurely, for example, if the newly arrived immigrant is not assigned to a labor market program and lacks the qualifications for it, or if their assignment to such a program has been revoked due to circumstances that the newly arrived immigrant is responsible for.

The proposal also includes consequential amendments to other laws. The new law and the consequential amendments are proposed to come into force on January 1, 2027.

Proposal for a Parliamentary Decision

Government Proposal:

  1. The Parliament adopts the government's proposed law regarding housing for certain newly arrived immigrants.
  2. The Parliament adopts the government's proposed amendment to the Land Code.
  3. The Parliament adopts the government's proposed amendment to the Act (2002:546) on the processing of personal data in labor market policy activities.
  4. The Parliament adopts the government's proposed amendment to the Reception Act (2026:000).
