TL;DRThe government has presented proposals on how Sweden's funds will be used from 2026 to 2028. This involves determining how much the state can spend and how much debt can be incurred.
In plain language

The government has presented its plan for the state's economy for the years 2026, 2027, and 2028. The plan includes proposals for a limit on the amount of money the state can spend, known as a spending cap. For the year 2026, the proposed cap is 1,936 billion Swedish krona, and for 2027, it is 2,009 billion krona. A cap of 2,105 billion krona is also proposed for 2028.

The proposal also includes rules for how the state should save money. Starting in 2027, the goal is for the state to have savings that are balanced when the economy is fluctuating. The government also wants approval for the amount of money that will come into the state through taxes and other revenues in the coming years.

The government is asking the parliament to approve borrowing money to pay for important things. This includes borrowing up to 74.9 billion Swedish krona to purchase equipment and buildings that the state needs for its operations. It also includes borrowing money to cover the costs of the authorities' interest accounts.

Finally, there are many proposals to change various laws related to taxes. This includes taxes on energy, taxes on gasoline, and road traffic taxes. There are also proposals for changes to the rules for income tax and how taxes are handled for people who live abroad.

The budget proposal contains the government's proposal for the state budget for 2026, as well as the other proposals and assessments that follow from the Rules of Procedure of the Parliament and the Budget Act (2011:203).

Proposals for Parliamentary Decisions

Government Proposals

  1. The Riksdag (Swedish Parliament) adopts the proposed amendment to the Budget Act (2011:203) (sections 3.1 and 5.5).

Guidelines for Economic Policy and the Draft State Budget for 2026

  1. The Riksdag approves the guidelines for economic and budget policy (section 1).
  2. The Riksdag sets the expenditure ceiling for the state, including the pension system, outside the state budget, at SEK 1,936 billion for 2026 and SEK 2,009 billion for 2027, due to technical adjustments and fiscal reasons (section 5.2).
  3. The Riksdag sets the expenditure ceiling for the state, including the pension system, outside the state budget, at SEK 2,105 billion for 2028 (section 5.2).
  4. The Riksdag establishes the goal for the financial savings of the public sector to be a balanced saving over the business cycle, starting in 2027 (section 5.5).
  5. The Riksdag approves the calculation of state budget revenues for 2026 (section 6.1 and Appendix 1, section 2).
  6. The Riksdag approves the preliminary calculation of state budget revenues for 2027 and 2028 as a guideline for the government's budget work (section 6.1 and Table 6.1).
  7. The Riksdag decides on the allocation of expenditures to expenditure areas for 2026 (section 7.2 and Table 7.1).
  8. The Riksdag approves the calculation of changes in appropriations for 2026 (section 7.2 and Table 7.1).
  9. The Riksdag approves the calculation of expenditures for the pension system, outside the state budget, for 2026 (section 7.2 and Table 7.1).
  10. The Riksdag approves the preliminary allocation of expenditures to expenditure areas for 2027 and 2028 as a guideline for the government's budget work (section 7.3 and Table 7.3).
  11. The Riksdag authorizes the government to borrow funds according to Chapter 5 of the Budget Act (2011:203) during 2026 (section 8.2).
  12. The Riksdag approves the calculation of the National Debt Office's net lending for 2026 (section 8.2 and Table 8.1).
  13. The Riksdag approves the calculation of the cash correction for 2026 (section 8.2 and Table 8.1).
  14. The Riksdag authorizes the government to decide on loans from the National Debt Office for 2026 for investments in fixed assets used in state activities, including previous borrowings, up to a maximum of SEK 74,900,000,000 (section 10.2).
  15. The Riksdag authorizes the government to decide on credits for the interest accounts of government agencies at the National Debt Office for 2026, including previously utilized credits, up to a maximum of SEK 23,400,000,000 (section 10.2).
  16. The Riksdag authorizes the government to, during 2026, with the limitations specified in Section 8, Paragraph 2 of the Budget Act (2011:203), decide that an appropriation not intended for administrative purposes may be exceeded if a decision by the Riksdag to amend the appropriation cannot be awaited, and the exceedance falls within the established expenditure ceiling for the state (section 10.7).
