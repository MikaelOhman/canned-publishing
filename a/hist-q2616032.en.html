Kristina Ingedotter, born in an unknown year and died on January 18, 1122, was a Swedish princess and Grand Princess of Novgorod, daughter of King Inge the Elder and Queen Helena of Sweden, and wife of Prince Mstislav I Vladimirovich the Great.

== Biography == Kristina is believed to have been the eldest of Inge and Helena's three daughters, and she married before her sisters Margareta and Katarina. She married Prince Mstislav of Novgorod in 1095. Archaeologists have rediscovered Kristina's seal, which depicts a crowned woman with a halo and bears the name "Saint Christina." An image of Saint Christina in the Nereditsa Church is believed to depict Kristina, and it has been suggested that she may have been venerated as a local saint. Upon her father Inge the Elder's death, she was excluded from inheriting his property because she lived too far from Sweden and was considered to have forfeited her inheritance rights. However, her daughter Ingeborg, who married a Danish prince and settled in Scandinavia, received one-quarter of Kristina's father's inheritance from her aunt Margareta. Kristina died in 1122, a year before her husband became Grand Prince of Kiev.

Children:

  • Ingeborg, married to Duke Knut Lavard of Jutland and mother of King Valdemar the Great of Denmark.
  • Malmfrid, married to King Sigurd Jorsalafarare of Norway and later to King Erik Emune of Denmark.
  • Eupraxia, married to Alexios II Komnenos, son of John II Komnenos.
  • Vsevolod of Novgorod and Pskov.
  • Maria, married to Vsevolod II of Kiev.
  • Iziaslav II of Kiev.
  • Rostislav of Kiev.
  • Sviatopolk of Pskov.
  • Rogneda, married to Jaroslav of Volynien.
  • Xenia, married to Briatjislav of Iziaslavl.
