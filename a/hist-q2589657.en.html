Full text

Paul Justen, also known as Paulus Juusten, born around 1516 in the village of Juustila in the parish of Viborg, died on August 22, 1575, in Turku, was a Finnish bishop during the Swedish era.

== Biography == Juusten studied at the University of Wittenberg from 1543 to 1546 and served as rector of the cathedral school in Turku from 1548 to 1554. In the latter year, he was appointed the first bishop of the Diocese of Viborg, and a decade later, he was transferred to the bishopric of Turku, where he served as bishop from 1563 to 1575. In 1569, Juusten was sent by King John III as a Swedish envoy to Tsar Ivan in Moscow, whose displeasure the Swedish envoys inadvertently incurred. Juusten was subjected to mistreatment and imprisoned, where he remained for three years. Finally released, he returned to Finland with failing health and died in his hometown of Turku. In 1574, he translated Luther's Small Catechism into Finnish. During his time in Russia, he compiled an analysis of the year's sermon texts for the Finnish clergy, which remained unpublished. Professor Henrik Gabriel Porthan arranged for the publication of his account of his mission to Russia, "Acta legationis moscoviticæ," and his chronicle of the bishops of Finland, "Chronicon episcoporum Finlandensium." The bishop was ennobled in 1572 by King John III. He was one of the bishops who, at the king's command and with due observance of Catholic ceremonies, consecrated Laurentius Petri Gothus as Archbishop.
