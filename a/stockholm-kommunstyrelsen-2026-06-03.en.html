TL;DRThe City of Stockholm plans to implement digital personnel records for all of its departments and will invest 70 million Swedish krona in this initiative. Additionally, a building in Frihamnen, called Magasin 5, is set to change ownership for approximately 400 million Swedish krona.
In plain language

The City of Stockholm will begin using digital personnel records instead of paper files for all of its departments. This means that existing paper documents will be scanned and digitized to create a centralized digital archive. The project will be led by the Municipal Executive Board in collaboration with the City Archives and is estimated to cost 70 million Swedish krona between the years 2026 and 2031.

A significant deal involving a building in Frihamnen has also been approved. The building, named Magasin 5, will change ownership. The company St Erik Markutveckling AB will purchase all shares in the company Nya Lagerbolaget Y AB, which owns the building. The estimated price for the transaction is approximately 400 million Swedish krona.

To facilitate this acquisition, two new companies will be created. One is a holding company that will serve as the parent company, and the other is a subsidiary company that will own the warehouse building itself. The transaction involves Stockholm Hamn AB selling the building to St Erik Markutveckling AB.

These decisions were made by the Municipal Executive Board in Stockholm. They have presented the proposals to the Municipal Council, which is the highest decision-making body in the city, for their approval of the final plans.

City Council Minutes No. 10 2026-06-03 Item 3 Transition to Digital Personnel Records for the City of Stockholm's Committees KS 2026/153

Decision The City Council proposes that the City Council adopt the following:

  1. The committees of the City of Stockholm shall transition to digital personnel records in accordance with the attached report.
  2. The committees of the City of Stockholm shall scan existing paper personnel records to create a comprehensive digital personnel record.
  3. The City Council, in collaboration with the Culture Committee (the city archive), shall lead the work of transitioning to digital personnel records in accordance with the attached report.
  4. The City Council is responsible for system administration, contract management, and development.
  5. The City Council is granted funding of 70 million SEK for the development and implementation of digital personnel records for the years 2026-2031. Funding will be provided through the annually reserved funds for IT development costs in the central reserve, as detailed in the attached report.

The City Council, for its own part, and subject to the City Council's decision as stated above, resolves the following: The City Director shall implement the development, ongoing contract management, and implementation of digital personnel records, and shall be responsible for administration in accordance with the attached report.

Special Statements All members of the Moderate Party, the Liberal Party, the Center Party, and the Christian Democrats refer to the special statements of the Moderate Party, the Liberal Party, the Center Party, and the Christian Democrats, as detailed in the attached report.
