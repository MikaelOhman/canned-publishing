In plain language

The government has presented a proposal to the Swedish Parliament regarding Sweden's contribution of military assistance to NATO until the end of 2026. This involves deploying various types of forces, including ground troops, naval vessels, and aircraft to defend the North Atlantic area.

Sweden proposes that the contribution consist of up to 1,700 personnel for land-based operations, 700 personnel for maritime operations, and 600 personnel for air operations. Planned activities include deploying a group of soldiers to Latvia and participating in joint operations to protect airspace and maintain control of the seas.

It is important that these operations are conducted in accordance with international law, which are the laws that govern relations between countries. The exact dates and the number of personnel to be deployed on each occasion will be determined later, depending on the plans that NATO develops for its military operations.

In the proposal, the government also describes why it is important for Sweden to participate in this effort due to the changing security situation in the world. The government has also examined the financial cost of these military contributions for Sweden.

Full text

The proposal suggests that the Parliament should decide that the government, until December 31, 2026, may deploy Swedish armed forces consisting of ground units and special forces, up to a maximum of 1,700 personnel, naval units, up to a maximum of 700 personnel, and air and air defense units, up to a maximum of 600 personnel, to contribute, in accordance with international law, to NATO's collective deterrence and defense of the North Atlantic area.

The proposed Swedish contributions include a ground unit to NATO's multinational brigade in Latvia, naval units to NATO's standing naval forces in the North Atlantic area, air and air defense units to NATO's integrated air and missile defense in the North Atlantic area, as well as ground units, special forces, naval units, and air and air defense units for certain other security-enhancing operations and activities in the North Atlantic area.

The Swedish force contributions, within the aforementioned framework, will be provided during 2026 according to the specific timelines and scope as determined by NATO's operational planning or, for the relevant cooperation, the applicable operational planning.

In the proposal, the government describes the geopolitical developments and NATO's deterrence and defense. The government also explains the legal basis for the Swedish contributions, provides a description of the contributions, and outlines its considerations regarding the contributions. Finally, the proposal details the economic consequences of the contributions.
