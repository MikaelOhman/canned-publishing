Ingrid Bergman, born August 29, 1915, in the Hedvig Eleonora parish of Stockholm, and died August 29, 1982, in London, was a Swedish actress. Bergman starred in European and American films and received three Academy Awards. She won two Best Actress awards – for *Gaslight* in 1944 and for *Anastasia* in 1956 – plus one Best Supporting Actress award for *Murder on the Orient Express* in 1974.

Ingrid Bergman is best known for her roles as Ilsa Lund in *Casablanca* (1942), opposite Humphrey Bogart, and as Alicia Huberman in Alfred Hitchcock's thriller *Notorious!* (1946), opposite Cary Grant. The American Film Institute ranks her as the fourth greatest female star in American film history.

== Biography ==

=== Early Years === Ingrid Bergman was the daughter of artist and photographer Justus Bergman (1871–1929) and Friedel Adler (1884–1918). Her mother was born in Kiel, Germany, and her parents married on June 13, 1907, in Hamburg. In 1908, she gave birth to a stillborn son, and a few years later they had another child who died after a few weeks, making Ingrid Bergman their only surviving child. At the time of her birth, they lived at Strandvägen 3 – in the neighborhood closest to the Royal Dramatic Theatre – where Justus Bergman also ran a photography shop. In 1918, her mother died of jaundice. Her father's photography studio sparked her interest in photography and film – a hobby she pursued throughout her life – and as a child, she loved dressing up and posing for the camera. When Ingrid was 13 years old, in 1929, her life was completely turned upside down when her father also died, forcing her to move in with her unmarried aunt, Ellen Bergman. The following year, her aunt also passed away, and she moved in with an uncle, Otto Bergman, and his wife, Hulda. Ingrid Bergman's first experience with acting came at the age of 16 as an extra in Gunnar Skoglund's film *Landskamp* (1931). In 1933, she was accepted into the Dramaten school for actors.

=== Early Career in Sweden ===

In 1935, Bergman landed her first significant film role in Sigurd Wallén's "Munkbrogreven," co-starring with Edvin Adolphson. Her next role was as Astrid in Swedenhielm's production. Following this, she quickly appeared in Ivar Johansson's "Bränningar" and Gustaf Edgren's "Valborgsmässoafton," both featuring Lars Hanson and Victor Sjöström, all within the same year, 1935. However, the film that proved most pivotal for Ingrid Bergman's career was Gustaf Molander's "Intermezzo," released in 1936. Her collaboration with Molander continued with "På Solsidan" (also in 1936) and "En kvinnas ansikte" in 1938. The film "Dollar" was also made that year.

In 1937, Ingrid Bergman married Petter Lindström (1907-2000), who would later become a neurosurgeon. In 1938, they had their daughter, Pia.

In 1938, Bergman appeared in the German film "Kvartetten gifter sig" ("Die vier Gesellen").

After the Swedish film "Intermezzo" was shown in the United States, where Ingrid Bergman's Nordic beauty gained attention, she was contacted by Hollywood producer David O. Selznick, who offered her a film contract. Consequently, in 1939, Ingrid Bergman moved to California and began filming the American version of "Intermezzo," with Leslie Howard playing the role of Professor Brandt.

In the autumn of 1939, Bergman was contracted for Per Lindberg's film "Juninatten," so she returned to Sweden for her final commitment with Svensk Filmindustri.

=== A Star in Hollywood === Bergman quickly made a significant impression on both the American and international film audiences, who perceived her as an unusually natural, healthy, and unpretentious beauty. Her popularity grew rapidly, and with her role as Ilsa Lund in Michael Curtiz's film *Casablanca* in 1942, she became a major international movie star. In 1943, she played the role of Maria in Sam Wood's film *Now, Voyager*, starring Gary Cooper, and she received her first Oscar for her performance as Paula in George Cukor's film *Gaslight* from 1944. Ingrid Bergman then starred opposite Bing Crosby in *The Bells of St. Mary's* (1945), against Gregory Peck in Alfred Hitchcock's *Spellbound* the same year, and delivered a fine performance as Alicia in Hitchcock's *Notorious* in 1946, where she appeared with Cary Grant for the first time. Bergman began a secret love affair with photographer Robert Capa, which lasted for a couple of years after they met in Paris in 1945. At the end of the 1940s, Ingrid Bergman began to feel increasingly bored by the factory-like filmmaking process in Hollywood and sought contact with the Italian film director Roberto Rossellini, whose strong, neorealist films she was fascinated by, and she felt that it was the challenge she needed as an actress.

=== The Years in Italy and Later Career === Bergman and Rossellini began a relationship while she also took on a role in his film *Stromboli* (1950). Both were already married, and their affair became a major international media scandal, which caused her significant professional problems, as no one in Hollywood dared to work with her. In the United States, there were even calls for a "ban" on all of her films, and Senator Edwin C. Johnson gave a lengthy speech in the U.S. Congress on March 14, 1950, about the actress.
