(or), born around 1210 in Bjälbo, Östergötland, and died on October 21, 1266, at Jälbolung, was a Swedish Earl (Jarl) and nobleman. Birger Jarl is first mentioned in 1237 and served as a Swedish Earl from 1248 to 1266. Initially, he ruled Sweden informally as regent for his son, King Valdemar (from 1250 until Valdemar was crowned in 1251), and then jointly with his son (until Birger's death in 1266), holding the title "by the grace of God." It is also said that Birger founded Stockholm in 1252.

Due to linguistic changes that the names "Birghir" and "jarl" have undergone, there are many variations of the name found in older sources, such as "Birge jerl" and "Börje järl," but the form "Birger jarl" has become standard in modern Swedish.

== Biography ==

=== Childhood and Family ===

Birger was the son of Magnus Minnisköld and Ingrid Ylva. According to a source by Olaus Petri, Ingrid Ylva's father was Sune Sik, a prince. There has been much debate about whether such a person even existed. Birger jarl was probably born in a farmstead that has not yet been excavated, located not far from the Bjälbo church. After his father died around 1210, Birger grew up with his single mother and older brothers.

Magnus Minnisköld is said to have died either in 1208 or 1210. Investigations conducted in the early 2000s of what are likely the remains of Birger Magnusson in Varnhem church revealed that he was between 50 and 55 years old at the time of his death in 1266. Taken together, these pieces of information lead to the conclusion that Birger Magnusson was probably born around 1210.

Birger Magnusson married Ingeborg, sister of the Swedish king Erik, the last ruler of his lineage, sometime between 1235 and 1237, certainly before 1241. They had several children, and two of their sons became kings: Valdemar, for whom Birger jarl served as regent in 1250, and Magnus Ladulås, who became king when he deposed Valdemar in 1275.

One of Birger's earliest known public assignments was to mediate a conflict in Småland between the Cistercian monks of Nydala monastery and the farmers of Östbo district in 1238. He also led what is known as the second crusade against the Tavastians in Finland in either 1238 or 1239. On July 15, 1240, the Battle of Neva was fought against Russian troops under Prince Alexander of Novgorod (born 1220), who became known as Alexander Nevsky because of the Neva River.

Another diplomatic-like assignment occurred when Birger represented King Erik to King Håkon of Norway in 1241.

=== Birger Magnusson Becomes Earl ===

Birger Magnusson's predecessor in the office of Earl was his cousin, Ulf Fase. This man may have become Earl as early as 1220, during the reign of Johan Sverkersson. However, Johan Sverkersson died in 1222 on Visingsö, and with him, the Sverker dynasty came to an end. The only possible candidate for king was the six-year-old Erik Eriksson, from the Erician dynasty. During his minority, the kingdom was governed by a council of state consisting of nobles and bishops. One of the members of this council was Knut Holmgersson (Knut långe), who, in the Battle of Olustra in 1229, deposed the now 13-year-old king and took the throne himself. Knut Holmgersson belonged to what were known as the "House of Folkung," a conservative group of nobles who opposed the centralization of the kingdom.

After Knut Holmgersson's death in 1234, King Erik returned, but the real power lay with Earl Ulf Fase and the "House of Folkung." Ulf Fase was likely a supporter of Knut Holmgersson and therefore in opposition to King Erik. However, sometime between 1247 and 1248, Ulf Fase disappeared from the scene, probably ousted by a Birger Magnusson who was becoming increasingly powerful. It is said that Ulf Fase sided with Holmger Knutsson and the "House of Folkung" in the Battle of Sparrsätra in 1247 and lost to the troops of King Erik and Birger Magnusson. It is likely that Ulf Fase either died in the battle or was executed on the orders of the king and Birger Magnusson, who then took over the office of Earl. Until his death in 1266, Birger was the true ruler of Sweden.

=== The Time as Earl === In February 1248, the so-called Skänninge assembly was held, where guidelines for the kingdom's relationship with the Catholic Church were established. In addition to Birger Jarl, those present at the meeting with Cardinal Bishop William of Sabina included Archbishop Jarler, the kingdom's other five bishops, monastery leaders, priests, the district judge of Östergötland, and a number of nobles. While Birger Jarl was on a crusade in central Finland, where he founded, among other things, Tavastehus Castle, King Eric the Learned and Lame died in 1250, and with him, the Erician dynasty came to an end. Birger Jarl's son, Valdemar, who was only about ten years old, was then elected king. On his mother's side, Valdemar descended from the Erician dynasty. The election of the king took place before Birger Jarl had returned from Finland. In the Battle of Herrevadsbro in 1251, Birger Jarl defeated a group of rebellious nobles (the Folkung faction), including Knut Magnusson (of the Bjälbo clan), Filip Knutsson, and Filip Larsson. After Ingeborg's death in 1254, Birger remarried in 1261 to Mechtild of Holstein, the widowed queen of King Abel of Denmark. In the summer of 1266, he met with his sons and a papal delegation.
