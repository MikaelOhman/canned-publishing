Ulrika Eleonora the Younger, born January 23, 1688, and died November 24, 1741, was the reigning Queen of Sweden from 1719 to 1720. She was the daughter of Charles XI and Ulrika Eleonora (the elder), sister to Charles XII, and cousin to Augustus the Strong, Frederick IV of Denmark, and Frederick IV of Holstein-Gottorp. She married Frederick of Hesse on March 24, 1715, who later became Frederick I, but they remained childless. As Frederick's consort, she was Queen of Sweden until her death.

== Royal Title ==

Ulrika Eleonora's full title in Swedish was: "Ulrika Eleonora, by the grace of God, Queen of Sweden, Goths and Vandals; Grand Duchess of Finland; Duchess in Scania, Estonia, Livonia, Karelia, Bremen, Verden, Stettin-Pomerania, Kashubia, and Venden; Princess of Rügen; Lady of Ingria and Wismar, also Countess Palatine of the Rhine in Bavaria, Duchess of Jülich, Cleves, and Berg; Landgravine and Hereditary Princess of Hesse; Princess of Hischfeld; Countess of Katzen-Ellenbogen, Dietz, Ziegenhem, Nidda, and Schaumburg, etc., etc."

== Biography ==

=== Princess Ulrika Eleonora ===

Ulrika Eleonora was born on January 23, 1688, at Tre Kronor Castle in Stockholm, as the daughter of King Karl XI and Ulrika Eleonora of Denmark. During her childhood, she was often overshadowed by her older, more lively and talented sister, Hedvig Sofia. Her elder siblings reportedly enjoyed games and dancing, sometimes looking down on her because she was prone to crying and hesitant to participate in their activities. She was described as kind, modest, and dignified, with good posture and beautiful hands, but she was shy and reserved, and considered neither beautiful nor intelligent. She was a skilled musician; while her sister Hedvig Sofia sang, Ulrika Eleonora played the harpsichord. Her grandmother described her as stubborn and willful, and she reportedly had a habit of showing her displeasure by going to bed.

As soon as she became eligible for marriage, she received many suitors. Because her brother, Karl XII, was unmarried and without heirs, she was considered a likely successor to the Swedish throne, which attracted numerous marriage proposals, including from the future George II of Great Britain and Prince Frederick of Hesse-Kassel. In 1698, a marriage was proposed between Ulrika Eleonora and Prince Carl of Denmark, and between her brother Karl XII and Sofia Hedvig of Denmark; this plan was reportedly abandoned in 1700. The future George II of Great Britain proposed to her when she was fourteen, but the plans were postponed and replaced with others. A proposal for Ulrika Eleonora to marry Frederick William I of Prussia nearly went through, but it was abruptly canceled without explanation by Karl XII. Duke Johann Wilhelm of Saxe-Gotha, who served in the Swedish army, was given permission to present himself to Ulrika Eleonora and court her, but marriage plans were abandoned after he dueled with Anders Lagercrona in the king's presence.

After reports of the defeat at the Battle of Poltava reached Sweden in late July 1709, two factions emerged within the court. One was a Holstein faction that wanted Karl Frederick of Holstein-Gottorp to be the king's successor, supported by Arvid Horn, Nils Gyllenstierna, the Piper family, and Queen Dowager Hedvig Eleonora. The other was a Swedish faction that wanted Ulrika Eleonora to be the king's successor, preferably in marriage with a Danish prince. Prince Carl of Denmark, son of Christian V, was proposed. Fabian Wrede, Gabriel Falkenberg, and Knut Posse, along with many members of the nobility, supported this faction. After receiving a letter from Karl XII in August informing him that he had escaped and was safe in Turkey, the proposal was abandoned.

As early as 1710, Frederick of Hesse requested her hand, but their engagement was not announced until January 23, 1714. Emerentia von Düben, whose relative Konrad Ranck was a lieutenant general in Hesse, facilitated the marriage proposal between Ulrika Eleonora and Frederick of Hesse in exchange for a cash payment of 30,000 German thalers from Landgrave Karl I of Hesse-Kassel, whose rent she would then collect. Her grandmother, Hedvig Eleonora, supported the marriage, hoping that Ulrika Eleonora would move with her husband to his homeland of Hesse, which would increase the chances of Ulrika Eleonora's nephew, Karl Frederick of Holstein-Gottorp, becoming the Swedish heir apparent.

The wedding was celebrated on March 24, 1715. At her wedding, her brother said: "Tonight, my sister will dance away the crown." During Karl XII's absence abroad, she was, after Hedvig Sofia's death (1708), the only adult member of the royal family within the kingdom, excluding her grandmother, Queen Hedvig Eleonora.

In late 1712 or early 1713, Karl XII considered making his sister Ulrika Eleonora a regent, but he did not follow through with this plan. The Royal Council, however, persuaded her to attend its meetings in order to gain her support. During her first appearance at the council on November 2, 1713, it was decided to convene a Diet.
