In plain language

The government has proposed changes to the Planning and Building Act, the law that governs how buildings can be constructed and modified in Sweden. The proposal aims to replace the term "renovation" with "major building alteration" to clarify the regulations when making changes to a building.

When making a significant alteration to a part of a building, only the new regulations applicable to that specific part will need to be followed. Previously, there were possibilities to require the entire building to comply with new regulations, but the proposal seeks to limit this. Changes to the entire building can only be required if there are significant risks to people's health or safety, or if it involves critical systems such as electricity, heating, and internet access.

It will also become slightly easier to comply with accessibility regulations. This aims to make it simpler to adapt buildings so that people with mobility or orientation difficulties can use them.

If the Parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new regulations will come into effect on January 1, 2027. The proposal also affects other laws related to energy performance certificates and the measurement of energy consumption in buildings.

Full text

The proposal suggests changes to the Planning and Building Act (2010:900) to clarify and simplify the regulations regarding renovations and other alterations to a building.

The regulations are clarified, among other things, by replacing the word "renovation" with the term "major building alteration," which will be defined in the Planning and Building Act. This change will also apply to the Act (2006:985) on energy declarations for buildings and the Act (2022:333) on energy metering in buildings.

Furthermore, the possibilities of imposing requirements during a major building alteration are limited. For the part of the building that is being altered, design requirements and technical performance requirements must be met. The possibility of imposing follow-up requirements on the entire building during a major building alteration is restricted. Such requirements can only be imposed to address deficiencies that pose significant risks to health and safety, and to comply with requirements regarding broadband connections, energy efficiency, insulation, and sustainable mobility. The proposal also includes certain relaxations of the requirements for accessibility and usability for people with impaired mobility or orientation skills when altering a building.

The proposed amendments to the law are scheduled to come into effect on January 1, 2027.

The government's proposal:

  2. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Act (2006:985) on energy declarations for buildings.
  3. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Act (2022:333) on energy metering in buildings.
