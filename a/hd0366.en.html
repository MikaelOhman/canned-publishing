TL;DRThe government wants to reduce taxes for companies that hire young adults between the ages of 18 and 22. This measure will be implemented for a limited time to make it easier for employers to hire young people.
In plain language

The government has proposed a measure that would lower employer contributions for certain young people. Employer contributions are the additional costs that an employer must pay to the state when paying wages to an employee. The proposal applies to individuals who have turned 18 but are not yet 23 years old at the beginning of the year.

For young people earning up to 25,000 Swedish krona per month, the company will pay lower contributions. They will only pay the portion that goes towards the old-age pension and half of the other contributions. This is a way to make it cheaper for companies to hire young adults.

If the proposal is approved, the new rules will come into effect on April 1, 2026. The lower contributions will then be in effect until September 30, 2027. This means that the government wants this reduction to be temporary and last for approximately one and a half years.

Full text

The proposal suggests introducing a temporary reduction in the total amount of employer contributions and general wage taxes on compensation paid to individuals who have reached the age of 18 but not 23 years at the beginning of the year. This reduction means that only the age pension contribution and half of the other contributions will be paid on compensation up to 25,000 Swedish krona per calendar month.

The provisions regarding the temporary reduction in employer contributions are proposed to come into effect on April 1, 2026. These provisions apply to compensation paid out during the period from April 1, 2026, to September 30, 2027.
