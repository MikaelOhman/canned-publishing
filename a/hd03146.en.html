Improved immigration regulations for researchers and doctoral students, along with measures to prevent abuse of residence permits for study purposes.

TL;DRThe government wants to make it easier for foreign researchers to work and live in Sweden, while simultaneously making it more difficult to abuse student visas. The proposals also include new regulations for people who have fled Ukraine.
In plain language

The government has proposed changes to the rules regarding who is eligible to enter Sweden. The goal is to provide researchers and doctoral students, i.e., individuals studying at a high level, with better opportunities to stay in the country. They should be able to obtain residence permits for their research more quickly and be allowed to stay longer to search for jobs after their studies.

It will also become easier for these researchers and their families to apply for permits while they are already in Sweden. At the same time, the government wants to prevent fraud related to student visas. This means that it will be more difficult to work with a student visa, and the requirements to actually study properly will increase. If someone does not fulfill their study obligations, they may lose their permit.

The proposal also includes new rules for individuals who have fled Ukraine and need temporary protection. The government wants to introduce a new law that determines how these individuals will be granted residence permits. This is part of a larger change to Swedish immigration law, which is the law that governs the rules for foreigners in the country.

If the parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new rules will come into effect on June 11, 2026. The proposal involves changes to several different laws related to both research and migration.

Full text

The proposal includes suggestions aimed at improving the conditions for foreign researchers and doctoral students to work in Sweden, as well as preventing abuse of residence permits for studies. This includes the following:

  • Doctoral students should be able to be granted residence permits for research.
  • Researchers and doctoral students should be able to be granted permanent residence permits more quickly.
  • Researchers and doctoral students should be able to be granted longer residence permits to seek employment after completing their research or studies.
  • The opportunities for researchers and doctoral students, as well as their family members, to apply for residence permits from within Sweden are expanded.
  • The opportunities to work with a residence permit for studies are limited.
  • The requirements for acceptable progress in studies and for switching from a residence permit for studies to, for example, a residence permit for work are tightened.
  • More opportunities to revoke residence permits for family members of students are introduced.

The proposal also includes a new law with special provisions regarding the granting of residence permits for so-called mass refugees from Ukraine.

The proposed changes to the law are scheduled to come into effect on June 11, 2026.
