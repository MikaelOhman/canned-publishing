TL;DRThe government wants to change the rules for social assistance to encourage more people to find employment. They propose a limit on the amount of money that can be received in benefits and stricter requirements for receiving support when sick.
In plain language

The government wants to make it more worthwhile to start working by introducing a benefit cap. This means that people should not be able to receive so much in benefits that it doesn't make financial sense to work. They also want to change how the amount of money a household needs for its most basic needs is calculated.

For people who are sick and unable to work, new requirements for medical certificates will be introduced. The social services department, which is the municipal authority responsible for financial assistance, will be able to require sick individuals to participate in programs to help them return to the workforce. Several agencies will also work together to create a personalized plan for those who need support.

It will also become more difficult to receive financial assistance for rent if you are renting a property as a subtenant. You will then need to provide proof that there is a permit or approval for renting that property. In addition, the government proposes that only individuals who have the legal right to reside in Sweden should be eligible for financial assistance.

The new rules regarding medical certificates are proposed to come into effect on July 1, 2026. The other changes to the law are expected to come into effect on January 1, 2027.

Full text

A reform of social assistance is needed to break the cycle of dependency and reduce social exclusion. This proposal includes draft legislation for a cap on social assistance benefits, aimed at increasing the financial advantages of transitioning from social assistance to employment. The government proposes, among other things, that the national standard rate should be calculated based on a reference value derived from price surveys of the basic consumption of different household types. Furthermore, certain changes are proposed to the cost items included in the national standard rate, limitations on the possibility of providing social assistance in addition to the national standard rate, and restrictions on social assistance for larger households.

The government also proposes that individuals with reduced work capacity due to illness must provide a medical certificate to receive social assistance. It is also proposed that social welfare committees should have the authority to require individuals with reduced work capacity due to illness to participate in work-related rehabilitation, and that certain authorities should be obligated to participate in developing an individual plan.

In addition, it is proposed that social assistance for housing costs should only be granted if the dwelling is rented as a sublet, and only if the necessary consent or permit for the sublet exists. Furthermore, the government proposes that financial assistance should only be provided to individuals who are legally residing in Sweden.

The legislative changes that introduce the requirement to provide medical certificates are proposed to come into effect on July 1, 2026. The other legislative changes are proposed to come into effect on January 1, 2027.
