Full text

The proposal includes suggested amendments to the Education Act (2010:800), aiming to create better conditions for safety and a conducive learning environment in schools through several collaborative measures. The proposals essentially entail the following:

The responsibilities of the governing body and the principal regarding safety and a conducive learning environment within the school unit should be clarified. In addition to preventive work, the governing body and the principal must work to maintain safety and a conducive learning environment. Schools should become mobile phone-free by collecting students' mobile phones at the beginning of the school day and returning them at the end of the school day in compulsory school forms, after-school care, and open leisure activities. Current rules of conduct should be renamed "school rules," become school-wide, and include a plan for consequences in cases of rule violations. Furthermore, the possibilities of using disciplinary measures such as expulsion from the classroom, temporary reassignment within the school unit, temporary placement outside the school unit, and suspension should be strengthened. In certain cases, it should also be possible to deny a student access to the school unit if the student poses a threat to the safety of other students and staff. The school's expectations of students and their guardians should be clarified through an expectations document.

In addition, it is proposed that it should be possible to operate an emergency school as an outsourced service, i.e., a facility where students are temporarily provided with education outside their own school unit.

The amendments to the Education Act are proposed to come into force on August 1, 2026.
