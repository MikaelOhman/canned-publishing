Full text

The Swedish National Audit Agency has reviewed whether the government is working effectively to prevent ethnic discrimination in decisions made by authorities that affect individuals. Each authority is responsible for striving for equal treatment and preventing discrimination in all its activities. However, there are indications that ethnic discrimination occurs in the decisions made by several state agencies regarding various types of support, controls, or interventions. The review covers the work being carried out at a selection of seven agencies: the Public Employment Service (Arbetsförmedlingen), the Swedish Social Insurance Agency (Försäkringskassan), the Swedish Prison and Probation Services (Kriminalvården), the National Enforcement Agency (Kronofogdemyndigheten), the Police Authority (Polismyndigheten), the Swedish Tax Agency (Skatteverket), and the Customs Agency (Tullverket). The review also examines whether the oversight and promotional work of the Discrimination Ombudsman (DO) contributes to preventing ethnic discrimination in decisions made by authorities. The National Audit Agency's overall conclusion is that the government's efforts to prevent ethnic discrimination in decisions affecting individuals are not effective. The government's work against such discrimination is primarily reactive. The shortcomings mainly lie in the agencies' preventative work. Furthermore, the DO's role in driving this issue forward is limited. These shortcomings pose a risk that the protection of individuals against discrimination is inadequate. Primarily, the authorities need to take greater responsibility for ensuring that their decisions do not lead to ethnic discrimination. The seven agencies included in the review have good preparedness to act based on reports concerning ethnic discrimination. However, the main shortcomings lie in the agencies' preventative work, which mainly consists of general equal treatment measures such as training on the government's core values. Such work can certainly contribute to preventing risks of ethnic discrimination in decisions. However, these measures are usually not well-integrated into any specific framework.
