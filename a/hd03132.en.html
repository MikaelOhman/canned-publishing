In plain language

Today, young people who commit serious crimes typically receive a form of care called closed youth care. This care is managed by the Swedish National Agency for Institutions and Welfare and takes place in special youth homes. In the current system, it is not possible to be released on parole, which means that the entire sentence must be served.

The government is now proposing a change to this. They want young people who commit serious crimes to instead be sentenced to prison. If this is approved, the Swedish Prison and Probation Service will take over responsibility for carrying out the sentences. This also means that the old type of youth care will be phased out.

To protect the rights of children, the government proposes that those in prison be placed in special units. These units should be adapted to the needs of children and young people. The new system also means that it will be possible to be released on parole, which means that individuals may be released early under supervision to receive support and monitoring when they return to society.

Full text

According to the current regulations, individuals who have committed a crime so serious before the age of 18 that a custodial sentence is warranted, are, as a general rule, sentenced to closed juvenile detention facilities instead of prison. Closed juvenile detention facilities are administered by the Swedish National Prison and Probation Service, and the convicted individual is placed in a designated juvenile facility. There is no possibility of parole from closed juvenile detention.

In the government proposal, it is suggested that children and young people, in cases where a custodial sentence cannot be avoided, should be sentenced to prison instead of closed juvenile detention. This proposal means that the Swedish Prison and Probation Service will be responsible for the execution of the sentence, and that closed juvenile detention will be phased out of the sentencing system.

Children sentenced to prison should, as a general rule, be placed in special units for children and young people, which should be specially adapted to meet the needs and rights of children.

By making the sentence a prison sentence, children and young people will also be subject to the system of parole. Upon release from prison, they will, as a starting point, be placed under supervision in order to provide them with the support and control they need. The proposal also suggests that the legislation governing the execution of sentences should be adapted in certain areas to meet the specific needs and rights of children.

The proposed amendments to the law are, in all essential respects, intended to come into force on July 1, 2026.
