In plain language

The government has proposed a set of changes to the rules governing how companies can compete in Sweden. By amending the Competition Act, the authority, the Swedish Competition Authority (Konkurrensverket), will gain more power to investigate and prevent practices that undermine fair competition. This is intended to promote growth and increase wealth in society.

A key component of the proposal is a completely new law regarding public sales activities. This law aims to ensure that private companies are not treated less favorably than publicly owned companies, such as municipalities or the state, when they sell goods in the same market. The goal is to create a level playing field for all companies selling goods or services.

The government also wants to change the rules regarding when large companies acquire other companies, a process known as corporate concentration. The purpose is to make it easier for the Swedish Competition Authority to detect and prevent acquisitions that risk eliminating competition. This will make it more difficult for a few large companies to dominate the entire market.

If the Parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new rules are planned to come into effect on August 1, 2026. Certain parts of the new law, which concern how results should be monitored and reported, are proposed to come into effect on January 1, 2027.

Full text

The government's proposal includes changes aimed at improving competition in both the public and private sectors. A well-functioning competitive environment is a crucial prerequisite for creating productivity, increased growth, economic prosperity, and competitiveness. Through amendments to the Competition Act, the Swedish Competition Authority will be given the opportunity to investigate and take action against companies to eliminate obstacles to effective competition in one or more markets. Furthermore, the government proposes a new law on public sales activities. This new regulation aims to protect private companies from the unequal conditions that arise when public and private actors operate in the same market. In addition, changes are proposed to the rules regarding the handling and prohibition of business concentrations. These changes aim to improve the Swedish Competition Authority's ability to detect and take action against concentrations that are likely to significantly hinder the existence or development of effective competition.

The new law and the amendments are proposed to come into force on August 1, 2026. However, the new law's provisions regarding evaluation and separate accounting are proposed to come into force on January 1, 2027.
