Full text

The economic report for the Royal Household consists of income statements and balance sheets, along with supplementary information and notes.

The report follows established accounting principles applied to the Royal Palaces. In addition, the Royal Marshal's Office has committed to increasing transparency regarding the allocation of funds by, from 2021, providing information on how the funds are allocated to the Head of State and other members of the royal family who perform official duties or are related to the Head of State's office (Prop. 2021/22:232 p. 4).

In 2024, the funds allocated to the Royal Household amounted to 86.2 million Swedish krona. These funds are used to finance costs related to official events, travel, transportation, personnel, and living expenses directly related to the Head of State's functions.

In 2024, the allocated funds represented 86 percent of the organization's revenue. Other revenue, primarily consisting of reimbursements for services provided to, among others, the Royal Palaces and the Royal National City Park, amounted to 13.8 million krona.

The Royal Household's expenses decreased from 105.8 million krona in 2023 to 99.1 million krona in 2024, primarily due to lower operating costs.

The Royal Household's financial performance for 2024 was positive, at 1 million krona, compared to a result of -8.9 million krona in 2023.

**Royal Palaces Finances**

The Royal Palaces have, as in previous years, submitted an annual report according to standard format. The Royal Palaces' operations are financed through appropriations, rental and lease income, revenue from tours of the royal palaces, and revenue from sales in the palace shops.

The Royal Palaces' revenue from fees and other reimbursements increased from 128.1 million krona in 2023 to 137.6 million krona in 2024, primarily due to increased entrance and tour revenue following price adjustments. The organization's expenses simultaneously decreased from 225.1 million krona in 2023 to 209.4 million krona in 2024. Both personnel costs and other operating expenses decreased. This decrease is partly due to cost-saving measures and partly because 2023 was a jubilee year with expenses for special exhibitions and activities.

The Royal Palaces' financial performance for 2024 was positive, at 12.1 million krona, compared to a negative result of 10.8 million krona in 2023.

**Employees in the Royal Household and Royal Palaces**

As of 2024, the number of permanently employed staff, converted to full-time equivalents, was 275, which was a decrease compared to 2023, when the number of employees was equivalent to 279 full-time positions. The number of temporary employees was equivalent to 71 full-time positions, which was a decrease compared to 2023, when it was equivalent to 76 full-time positions.

**Government's Assessment of Goal Achievement**

The Royal Household's program activities continued to be extensive in 2024. The number of official events and programs increased slightly compared to 2023. More state visits were conducted, and a new state ceremony was established for the awarding of honors within the royal orders of chivalry. The increase in appropriations in 2024 and the cost-saving measures implemented in the organization resulted in a positive change in capital. Demand for royal participation is expected to remain high in the future.

During 2024, a number of the royal visitor attractions were closed, but otherwise, the Royal Palaces continued to operate an extensive exhibition and tour program. More investments have been made in security measures. Reduced costs and increased entrance and tour revenue resulted in a positive financial result. Interest in visiting the royal attractions and palace parks was high in 2024 and is expected to remain high in the coming years.
