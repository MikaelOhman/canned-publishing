The Swedish National Audit Office is an agency that reviews how other government agencies operate. They have investigated whether the Swedish Migration Agency's controls on work permits are effective. The results show that the controls are not sufficient to prevent fraud and abuse.

The problems exist both when someone is applying for a new work permit and when checking those who already have a permit. The National Audit Office believes that this can lead to people being exploited in the workplace and that it creates unfair competition between companies. There is also a risk that labor-related crime will increase.

A major shortcoming is that the Swedish Migration Agency grants work permits to employers who the agency has previously identified as unreliable. Insufficient in-depth investigations are conducted in the new applications. In addition, follow-up checks, i.e., the controls that take place after a permit has been granted, are too weak to detect errors.

In the letter, the government presents its assessment of the observations and recommendations made by the Swedish National Audit Office (Riksrevisionen) in the report "The Swedish Migration Agency's Controls of Work Permits – Significant Risk of Errors and Abuse (RiR 2026:5)." The National Audit Office reviewed whether the Swedish Migration Agency's controls in work permit cases are effective in detecting and preventing errors and abuse. The National Audit Office's overall conclusion is that the controls are not effective. This applies to both controls during the application process for work permits and follow-up checks of ongoing work permits. According to the National Audit Office, these shortcomings risk contributing to increased labor exploitation, unfair competition, and labor market crime. The National Audit Office points out, among other things, that the Swedish Migration Agency grants work permits to employers that the agency has previously assessed as unreliable, without conducting any in-depth investigations into the new applications. Insufficient follow-up checks mean that errors and abuse are not detected.

The government welcomes the National Audit Office's review as a valuable basis for the ongoing work to prevent abuse of the rules for labor immigration. The government agrees with the National Audit Office's recommendations. The Swedish Migration Agency has received goals and reporting requirements from the government regarding strengthened control activities to prevent fraud and abuse. The Swedish Migration Agency has also been tasked with taking measures to strengthen the work of detecting and preventing.
