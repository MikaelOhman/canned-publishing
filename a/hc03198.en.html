TL;DRThe EU requires that so-called minor forms of support provided to companies be registered in a central database. Sweden is amending its legislation to ensure that this type of support can continue to be provided in a manner that complies with EU state aid regulations. The change is proposed to take effect from January 1, 2026.
In plain language

The EU has regulations regarding state aid to prevent such aid from distorting competition. For smaller amounts of aid – known as "de minimis" aid – the EU has introduced a requirement that the aid be registered in a central registry.

The government therefore proposes amendments to the Swedish law on the application of EU state aid rules.

The purpose is to ensure that such aid can continue to be granted in a manner that is consistent with EU law. The amendment is proposed to come into force on January 1, 2026.

Full text

The proposal suggests changes to the law concerning the application of the European Union's state aid rules, due to the introduction of a requirement in EU regulations regarding de minimis aid, which stipulates that such aid must be registered in a central register. The proposal aims to ensure that de minimis aid can continue to be provided in a manner consistent with EU law.

The proposed amendment is scheduled to come into force on January 1, 2026.

Proposed Parliamentary Decision

Prop. 2024/25:198

Government's proposal: The Parliament shall approve the government's proposal for an amendment to the Act (2013:388) on the application of the European Union's state aid rules.

Prop. 2024/25:198 2 Proposed Act amending the Act (2013:388) on the application of the European Union's state aid rules.

Hereby, it is prescribed that section 12a of the Act (2013:388) on the application of the European Union's state aid rules shall have the following wording.

Current wording Proposed wording

12 a § 1 Anyone who implements a support measure approved by the European Commission, or who is exempted from the notification requirement in Article 108.3 of the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union by a regulation or decision of the Commission, shall, to the extent determined by the Commission:

  1. provide information for registration, publication, and reporting of the support measure, and
  2. maintain a register of the support measure.

The government or the authority that the government determines may, with the support of section 8, paragraph 7 of the Instrument of Government, issue more detailed regulations regarding the obligation to provide information and maintain a register as stated in the first paragraph.

This law shall enter into force on January 1, 2026.
