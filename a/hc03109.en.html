Changes to tax subsidies for solar panels and small-scale electricity generation.

Source Riksdagen Read the original at Riksdagen →2025-03-18
TL;DRThe government intends to reduce subsidies for solar panels and eliminate the tax deduction for self-generated electricity. This means that installing solar panels will become more expensive, and individuals will receive less money back for the electricity they sell.
In plain language

The government has proposed changes to the rules regarding so-called "green technology." Currently, homeowners can deduct 20 percent of the cost when installing solar panels, but the proposal suggests reducing this to 15 percent. If the proposal is approved, the new, lower rate will take effect from July 1, 2025.

There is also a proposal to eliminate the tax deduction for small-scale renewable energy production. Small-scale production refers to generating electricity yourself, for example, using solar panels, and selling it to the electricity grid. The proposal means that you will no longer be able to claim a tax deduction for the electricity you produce yourself.

If the parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new rules for electricity production will come into effect on January 1, 2026. The proposal involves changes to several different laws that govern how taxes and tax deductions work in Sweden.

Full text

The proposal suggests reducing the subsidy rate for the installation of solar panels, within the framework of the tax reduction for green technology, from 20 to 15 percent. This is the subsidy rate that was in effect before 2023. The proposal also suggests abolishing the tax reduction for small-scale production of renewable energy.

The proposed reduction in the subsidy rate for solar panel installations is suggested to take effect on July 1, 2025, and the proposal to abolish the tax reduction for small-scale production of renewable energy is suggested to take effect on January 1, 2026.
