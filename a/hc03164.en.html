The government has proposed changes to the law governing radio and television. Currently, there are different regulations for broadcasting on traditional analog radio and on digital radio. The proposal aims to make the rules the same for both methods of broadcasting.

When awarding licenses, which are permits to broadcast, the authorities should strive to ensure a diverse range of programming and that different individuals or companies own the radio stations. They should also consider whether the applicants have experience in radio or other audio media, and whether they are capable of operating their business for an extended period.

Those who are granted licenses to broadcast on traditional radio will also be required to pay an annual fee. The amount of the fee will depend on the value of the radio market. There are also proposals for changes to the regulations for those who wish to broadcast radio to foreign countries or retransmit radio programs.

If the parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new regulations will come into effect on November 15, 2025.

Full text

The proposal suggests amendments to the Radio and Television Act (2010:696) that would unify the regulations regarding licenses and conditions for operating both analog and digital commercial radio stations. The proposals aim to create favorable conditions for a long-term, sustainable commercial radio sector with a wide range of content catering to diverse interests and preferences.

The proposals include the following:

  • The current bidding process for awarding licenses to operate analog commercial radio stations will be replaced with the same selection process used for digital commercial radio stations.
  • When awarding licenses, efforts should be made to promote diversity in both content and ownership. Consideration may also be given to whether an applicant has prior experience in commercial radio or other audio media and possesses the capacity to operate a long-term commercial radio business.
  • A license to operate an analog commercial radio station, similar to digital commercial radio licenses, may be subject to conditions, including the requirement to broadcast a diverse program offering.
  • License holders for analog commercial radio stations will be required to pay an annual broadcasting fee. This fee will be determined based on the market value of the license in the commercial radio market.

In addition, changes are proposed to the regulations governing the granting of licenses to broadcast radio to foreign countries and to retransmit radio broadcasts.

The proposed amendments to the law are scheduled to take effect on November 15, 2025.
