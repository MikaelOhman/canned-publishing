In plain language

The government has proposed changes to the travel guarantee law. The aim is to strengthen the protection for consumers who purchase travel packages, while also reducing complexity and costs for companies that sell travel.

A major change is the creation of a common fund, which is a large pool of money to which all travel companies contribute. Instead of each individual travel company having its own large guarantees, this common fund will be used. This is proposed to come into effect on April 1, 2026.

The proposal also means that more types of refund claims can be covered by the system. This makes it easier to get your money back if something goes wrong with your trip. The government also wants to introduce a rule that allows companies, such as credit card companies, to reclaim money from the guarantee fund if they have paid out compensation to a customer who was actually entitled to compensation under the law.

It is important to know that anyone selling a package tour must always ensure that the trip has a valid travel guarantee. If the tour operator is located in a country outside the EU or EEA, it is the reseller located within the EU or EEA that is responsible for ensuring that the guarantee is in place before the trip is sold.

Full text

The government proposes changes to the travel guarantee law, aiming to strengthen consumer protection and reduce costs and administrative burdens for travel industry operators. The proposal includes the following:

  • The travel guarantee system will be modified by establishing a collective fund, and the individual guarantees provided by each travel operator will be reduced in size.
  • The scope of the travel guarantee system will be expanded to allow for the replacement of more refund claims and certain types of vouchers.
  • A right of recourse will be introduced for legal entities that have compensated a traveler for a claim that the traveler would otherwise have been entitled to under the travel guarantee law. This means that, for example, credit card companies will have the right to claim compensation from the travel guarantee fund for amounts paid to the traveler.

The changes involving the establishment of a collective fund are proposed to take effect on April 1, 2026. The remaining changes are proposed to take effect on a date determined by the government.
