This means that authorities will no longer use the fisheries law to determine the permits required for cultivating fish, crustaceans, or shellfish. Full text

In the proposal, the government suggests amendments to the Fisheries Act (1993:787) and the Environmental Code, aiming to ensure that the environmental impact of aquaculture is assessed solely according to the Environmental Code's regulations regarding environmentally hazardous activities.

The proposed changes to the Fisheries Act include, among other things, the removal of the authorization to issue regulations regarding requirements for permits and other conditions for the cultivation of fish, aquatic mollusks, and aquatic crustaceans. Consequently, it is also proposed that cultivating these species without a permit no longer be punishable under the Fisheries Act.

In the Environmental Code, a proposed amendment would exempt the construction of aquaculture facilities from the requirement for a permit for water-related activities. Furthermore, the government proposes granting the government or a designated authority the power to issue regulations regarding environmental considerations in aquaculture.

These legislative changes are proposed to come into effect on July 1, 2026.
