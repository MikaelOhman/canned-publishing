Full text

Katarina of Vadstena, Katarina Ulfsdotter, born in 1331 and died on March 24, 1381, in Vadstena, was a Bridgettine nun and virgin. She was the daughter of Saint Birgitta and Ulf Gudmarsson.

== Biography == At a young age, Katarina was married to the nobleman Eggard van Kyren. Inspired by the ideals of female purity prevalent at the time, she persuaded her husband to respect her virginity, which he did. In 1349, Birgitta embarked on her long-awaited pilgrimage to Rome, and Katarina followed her a year later. Shortly thereafter, news arrived of Eggard's death. Birgitta and Katarina lived in Rome, leading a monastic life characterized by poverty and communal prayer. After Birgitta's death in 1373, Katarina brought her mother's remains back to Sweden. In 1375, Katarina returned to Rome to have the Bridgettine order's rule confirmed and to seek canonization for her mother. Like her mother, Katarina is said to have received heavenly revelations. In her final years in Vadstena, Katarina was seriously ill; she died on March 24, 1381, according to the Vadstena monastery's records. Three bishops officiated at her funeral, and many supernatural signs were witnessed at her grave. In 1484, Pope Innocentius VIII allowed the veneration of Katarina in Sweden, which is equivalent to today's beatification. The formal placement of her relics took place in Vadstena on August 1, 1489. She is celebrated on March 24 within the Roman Catholic Church and on August 2 in Sweden.

== Legacy == The Katarina Pilgrimage Trail is a pilgrimage route named after the saint. It runs from Närkes Kil via Riseberga monastery to Vadstena.
