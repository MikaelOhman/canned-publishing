Cecilia Vasa, born November 6, 1540, and died January 27, 1627, in Brussels, was a Swedish princess and Margravine of Baden-Rodemachern through her marriage to Margrave Christopher II of Baden-Rodemachern. She was the daughter of King Gustav Vasa and Queen Margaret Leijonhufvud. Cecilia is described as the most well-known of Gustav Vasa's daughters, and she was at the center of the infamous Vadstena scandal.

== Biography ==

=== Upbringing and Marriage === Like her sisters, Cecilia was likely taught German as a child, in addition to household chores and public performances. After her mother's death in 1551, she was placed under the care of Kristina Gyllenstierna and then her aunts, Queen Märta and Brita, before her father remarried to Katarina Stenbock. In 1556, Cecilia, like her sisters, had her portrait painted, poems praising her virtues were written by the court poet Henricus Mollerus, and she was provided with a dowry of 100,000 daler to be presented on the political marriage market. Cecilia was described as the most beautiful of the parents' daughters.

Like her sister Katarina, Cecilia was a candidate for marriage to Edzard II of East Frisia. When Edzard visited Sweden in 1558, he met both Cecilia and Katarina, and chose the latter. The wedding between Katarina and Edzard took place in Stockholm in 1559. When Katarina was about to leave Sweden, Cecilia was allowed to accompany the bridal couple on their journey. The entourage, which also included the bride's brother, Prince Erik, and the groom's brother, Johan of East Frisia, left Stockholm on November 8th. In December, the group visited Prince Magnus at Vadstena Castle. Magnus had been given the king's mission to try to persuade the couple to stay in Sweden for the winter and hinder their departure, partly because the king feared that they would be exposed to discomfort in Denmark. He also unsuccessfully requested that Cecilia return to Stockholm, especially since negotiations were underway for a marriage between her and Georg Johan of Palatinate-Veldenz.

On the night of December 13–14, Johan of East Frisia was discovered in Cecilia's bedroom at the castle. The guards had observed how Johan had entered Cecilia's chamber through the window. After this happened four times, they informed Erik. A guard told Duke Erik that he had seen Johan crawling through Cecilia's window. Erik consulted with Magnus and his courtiers, and decided to catch Johan in the act so that he could not deny it. They watched Cecilia's bedroom window, and the next time they saw Johan enter through it, Erik sent Charles de Mornay, along with some guards, who caught the couple "red-handed": "They immediately went into our sister's chamber and found him there in his shirt, barely wearing any trousers." The incident became a major scandal known as the Vadstena Affair. Erik held interrogations, imprisoned Johan in one of the castle towers, and then sent him to the king, who imprisoned him at Örbyhus Castle. On December 17th, the entire group returned to Stockholm: Katarina and Edzard were allowed to stay on their way and were placed under house arrest at Västerås Castle.

King Gustav Vasa took the scandal very hard and is said to have cried as he complained to Katarina Stenbock and blamed Erik and Katarina for failing in their responsibility not to have watched over Cecilia more closely, despite the fact that "they knew more and had been involved with her, than we did, and had seen her faults, so you should not have neglected this, as you promised." Cecilia later complained to Erik that her father, Gustav Vasa, had hit her and pulled out all her hair, and Erik accused the king of wanting to "slit her throat." Gustav Vasa defended himself by saying: "but her hair fell out anyway."

In January 1560, Katarina met with the king and tried to negotiate for permission to travel with Edzard, to have Johan released, and to prevent her father from confiscating Cecilia's jewelry. She asked Katarina Stenbock to mediate, and she seems to have also tried to intervene on her behalf. Gustav Vasa had Charles de Mornay arrested and scolded Erik for portraying Cecilia "as an obvious harlot" by not keeping the matter quiet but instead causing a public scandal. Johan's mother, the Regent of East Frisia, also had German nobles request his release through their envoys. Erik suggested that Cecilia's reputation could be saved through a forced marriage between her and Johan, but Gustav Vasa seems to have dismissed the idea, and it is not known whether the proposal was put to Johan. In the summer of 1560, Johan was brought to Stockholm, where he swore an oath before the Riksdag (national assembly) and the king on his and Cecilia's innocence, after which he was allowed to leave the country.

After ascending to the throne in 1560, Erik XIV took responsibility for his sisters and issued a court ordinance outlining the rules that applied to women at court, especially members of the royal family.
