In the letter, the government outlines its assessment of the observations and recommendations made by the Swedish National Audit Office (Riksrevisionen) in the report "Selection for Higher Education – Selection Criteria, Distribution of Places, and Implementation" (RiR 2025:10). The Swedish National Audit Office's overall conclusion is that the current selection system generally functions well, but that it could be more efficient in certain areas. The Swedish National Audit Office recommends that the government ensure that any changes to the selection regulations are implemented based on comprehensive impact assessments, that an appropriate authority is tasked with regularly evaluating the selection regulations, and that data on the selection group and merit score of university students is preserved for research and statistical purposes.

The government welcomes the review and largely agrees with the Swedish National Audit Office's observations and recommendations. Regarding the recommendation on impact assessments, the government emphasizes that great importance is attached to investigating potential consequences for the overall system when parts of it are changed, and that this will continue to be the case.

With this letter, the government considers the Swedish National Audit Office's report to be finalized.
