In plain language

The new regulations come into effect on June 11th and apply to individuals who have residence permits for studying at a university, or for working as doctoral students and researchers. This means changes in how these individuals can live and work in Sweden.

The rules also affect people who have been granted temporary protection under the mass migration directive, which is special protection for people fleeing war or difficult situations. For these individuals, it will be easier to apply for other types of residence permits without having to leave Sweden first.

The purpose of these changes is to clarify the regulations for different groups who are in Sweden on a temporary basis. If you belong to one of these groups, your rights or the way you apply for a permit may change starting from this date.
