Efficient and safe construction process.

TL;DRNew regulations for construction are proposed to come into effect on July 1, 2026. The purpose is to make the construction process safer and more transparent by changing roles and requirements for the inspection of buildings.
In plain language

The government wants to amend the law regarding planning and construction. A significant change is that companies previously known as certified building design firms will now be called "building assessors." These building assessors will not only be responsible for designing buildings but also for ensuring that the work on the construction site is carried out correctly.

It will also become clearer what needs to be checked during a construction project. Previously, there was a comprehensive control plan, but this will now be divided into two separate plans. One plan will focus on technical checks, and the other will address waste management. The requirements for experts to inspect certain parts of the construction will also increase.

For those who own or use a building, it will become more important to maintain essential components, such as those that ensure the building's stability and safety. If this is not done, the building authority, which is the local government body responsible for building permits, may require inspections to be carried out.

The proposal also clarifies the rules regarding what must be included in a technical consultation. It will also become easier to understand the responsibilities of the building owner (the developer) to ensure that everything is done correctly from start to finish. All of these changes are planned to come into effect on July 1, 2026.

Full text

The role of a certified building design company is expanded in the planning and building law to also include oversight during the execution of building projects.

The term "certified building design company" is changed to "building assessor."

If a building assessor is used, the building authority should not review matters that fall within the building assessor's responsibility. Which new constructions can be covered by a building assessor's responsibility will be specified in regulations issued under the authority of the law. The role of the expert is strengthened. For certain building projects, expert oversight will be mandatory. The requirements for the control plan are expanded to include what the controls should cover and how they should be carried out.

The control plan is divided into two plans: one for control and one for waste management.

A control supervisor may not oversee the execution of work that they themselves have designed. The rules regarding technical consultation are clarified regarding the documents required for the consultation and what it should include. The responsibility of the building owner for design and execution is clarified. The meaning of workplace safety is strengthened. It is clarified that a final inspection does not preclude later interventions.

Installations that are essential for load-bearing capacity, stability, and durability must be maintained so that they always fulfill their purpose. The building authority may, through a control order, require that the owner or user of a building ensure that such maintenance requirements are met.

It is clarified that certification activities may involve official duties, and that such decisions may be appealed. The proposal in this regard is that administrative tasks involving official duties may also be delegated to foreign organizations. Therefore, the decision of the parliament should be made in accordance with the order prescribed in Chapter 10, Section 6, paragraph 2 of the government form.

The proposed amendments to the law will come into force on July 1, 2026.
