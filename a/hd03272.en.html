Full text

One of the government's top priorities is to combat and prevent crime and increase safety in our country. Developing society's crime prevention measures and adapting them to today's challenges is a central part of this work. In March 2024, the government presented the report "Barriers Against Crime - A Social Prevention Strategy Against Criminal Networks and Other Crime" (report 2023/24:68). The purpose of this strategy is to deepen and develop social crime prevention efforts and to promote more actors implementing evidence-based measures to prevent crime. The strategy has a particular focus on breaking the recruitment and socialization into criminal networks, preventing children and young people from becoming involved in crime, and helping individuals who commit crimes to leave that life behind. This follow-up report outlines the measures that the government has taken in accordance with the strategy. The government's overall assessment is that the large number of measures implemented and initiated have contributed to developing and improving social crime prevention efforts. In particular, the conditions for work to break the recruitment into criminal networks have been improved.
