Full text

In this document, the government presents its assessment of the observations, conclusions, and recommendations made by the Swedish National Audit Office (Riksrevisionen) in its report "Equity in Preschool: State Support, Follow-up, and Supervision" (RiR 2025:15).

The Swedish National Audit Office reviewed whether the state is working effectively to promote equity in preschool. The overall conclusion of the Swedish National Audit Office is that the Swedish National Agency for Education (Skolverket) and the Swedish Schools Inspectorate (Skolinspektionen) are not working effectively enough to promote the goal of equity in preschool. The Swedish National Audit Office also assesses that the current supervision system for preschool, where supervision is divided between municipalities and the Swedish Schools Inspectorate, does not promote a uniform and legally sound supervision.

The Swedish National Audit Office's recommendation to the government is to ensure conditions for a legally sound and uniform licensing and supervision of all preschools at the operational level. For example, this could be achieved by giving the Swedish Schools Inspectorate responsibility for licensing and supervision of all preschools, or by assigning a government agency the responsibility for providing guidance on supervision to municipalities, while the Swedish Schools Inspectorate is responsible for supervising municipal preschools at the operational level. The government, during the period the review was ongoing, established the Inquiry on an equitable preschool of high quality with mandatory language-focused preschool (U 2024:04) and assesses that there are currently no grounds for taking further action in response to the Swedish National Audit Office's recommendation.

With this document, the government considers the Swedish National Audit Office's report to be finalized.
