Full text

A functioning return system is a prerequisite for a responsible and restrictive migration policy. In order to streamline the return process, the government is proposing in this proposal measures that will give the Police Authority and the Swedish Migration Agency better tools to enforce deportation and expulsion decisions. The proposals include the following:

  • A new obligation is introduced for the Public Employment Service, the Swedish Social Insurance Agency, the Swedish Prison and Probation Service, the Swedish Enforcement Agency, the Swedish Pensions Agency, and the Swedish Tax Agency to proactively provide information about a foreign national to the Police Authority if there is reason to believe that he or she does not have the right to reside in Sweden.
  • Certain fundamental rules regarding internal immigration control are being modernized and strengthened in order to enable more internal immigration controls than today.
  • The authorities are given additional tools to clarify a foreign national's identity. This includes introducing the possibility of seizing and searching a foreign national's mobile phone if it is suspected of containing information about the foreign national's identity.
  • Fingerprints and photographs should be used to a greater extent and more efficiently in immigration cases than today.

The proposed amendments to the law will come into force on July 13, 2026.
