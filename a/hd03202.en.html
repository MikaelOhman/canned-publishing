TL;DRThe government wants to change the law to make it easier to use hydropower to generate electricity in Sweden. This could mean lowering the requirements for how much hydropower plants must be adapted to protect the environment.
In plain language

The government has proposed an amendment to the Environmental Code, the law that governs how we protect our natural environment. The proposal concerns how to balance the need for clean electricity from hydropower with the need to protect animals and plants in the water.

Currently, there are strict regulations in place to protect nature, known as species and habitat directives. The government wants to make it easier to make exceptions to these regulations if it is deemed important for the entire country. This means that, in a new review of hydropower plants, it may be possible to choose to have lower requirements for environmental adaptation.

The proposal also means that the government will have more power to decide when exceptions can be made to prohibitions that protect the aquatic environment. This applies to both existing hydropower plants that need to have their permits reviewed and to completely new hydropower plants that are to be built.

If the Parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new rules will come into effect on July 1, 2026. The purpose is to ensure that Sweden has a good and reliable supply of electricity from hydropower.

Full text

The proposal suggests changes to regulations that are significant for balancing, on the one hand, the need for measures to improve the aquatic environment, and on the other hand, the need for a nationally efficient supply of hydropower when establishing modern environmental conditions for hydropower plants and dams. The changes aim to ensure that the scope provided by the Habitats Directive to give special consideration to activities that serve a significant public interest is fully utilized. The changes mean that it will be possible to reduce the requirements for environmental adaptation for these activities when reviewing modern environmental conditions.

The proposal also includes a suggestion to expand the government's authorization to issue regulations regarding exemptions from prohibitions on degrading the aquatic environment and jeopardizing the possibility of achieving the status or potential that the water should have according to an environmental quality standard. The authorization is proposed to also include the review of permits for existing activities and the issuance of new permits for such activities.

The proposed amendments to the law are scheduled to come into force on July 1, 2026.
