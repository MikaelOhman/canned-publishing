Hedvig Sofia of Sweden, born June 26, 1681, and died December 12, 1708, was a Swedish princess, duchess, and later regent of Holstein-Gottorp. She was the eldest daughter of King Charles XI of Sweden and Ulrika Eleonora the Elder, sister to Charles XII and Ulrika Eleonora the Younger, and cousin to Augustus the Strong and Frederick IV of Denmark. In 1698, she married her cousin, Frederick IV of Holstein-Gottorp.

== Biography ==

=== Childhood ===

In 1681, Hedvig Sofia was placed under the care of her lady-in-waiting, Hedvig Margareta von Parr (1620–1686). During her childhood, she had a close relationship with her brother, Charles. A significant part of her early years was spent at Karlberg Palace, where she had her own garden, "Princess Hedwig Sophia's Little Garden," filled with herbs and flowers. After her mother's death in 1693, she was cared for by her grandmother, Dowager Queen Hedvig Eleonora, who became her favorite grandchild and remained so. However, Juliana Schierberg was perhaps her most important maternal figure, continuing to exert a significant influence on her. Hedvig Sofia was described as healthy and intelligent, cheerful, and happy. It is noted that her father broke with his usual strict financial policies and spent a great deal of money on her. She accompanied him on hunting trips from an early age. She was said to be skilled in her studies and painting. She received instruction from the secretary Coelestin Friedrick Gutermuth in Christianity, Swedish, German, French, history, and geography. She was described as beautiful, but it is also said that she had split thumbs (two thumbs on each hand). However, the princess reportedly concealed this very skillfully. As a person, she was described as romantic and passionate, yet dignified.

=== Duchess === Hedwig Sophia married her cousin, Frederick IV of Holstein-Gottorp, at Karlberg Castle in 1698. The marriage was an arranged union with political motivations. Frederick had frequently visited Sweden during Hedwig Sophia's upbringing since 1687, and their engagement was decided very early on; the marriage was essentially planned as far back as 1694, but it was postponed due to her age. At the time of their wedding, Frederick had already gained a reputation for scandalous behavior, which Hedwig Sophia disapproved of, and the marriage never brought them happiness.

In connection with the wedding, a series of lavish celebrations were held at the Swedish royal court, events that became known as the "Gottorpian" or "Holsteinian festivities." After the wedding in 1698, she accompanied her husband to his home in Kiel, Holstein-Gottorp. The couple returned to Sweden in 1699, where she spent the rest of her life. They were invited by Charles XII to reside temporarily in Sweden, leaving an area that was then threatened by a state of war with Denmark, and her husband brought with him the entire chancellery and government of Holstein-Gottorp. To celebrate their arrival, a new period of lively entertainment began at the Swedish royal court – at the beginning of 1700, three large masked balls were held at great expense.

Hedwig Sophia was described as a beautiful woman interested in fashion, and she was known at the Swedish royal court as "The Happy Princess." She played a central role in the social life of the Swedish royal court, and after her death, the atmosphere at the court is said to have become more somber, and entertainment largely disappeared from it. Like her sister Ulrika Eleonora, she had a beautiful singing voice and musical talent that she also developed well: while her sister played the harpsichord, Hedwig Sophia focused on singing, and she often performed at the musical evenings held at the royal court in Kungshuset. As a singer, she was once praised by a poet with the words: "When your sound is nature's masterpiece/what voice can satisfy you?" Like her sister, she repeatedly tried to obtain permission from Charles XII to visit him in the war camps, as did other women of the same social class, but he refused each time to grant permission for such visits.

=== Regent ===

In 1702, she became a widow and served as regent in Holstein-Gottorp for her underage son. However, she delegated the governance to administrators and continued to reside at the Swedish court. As regent, she ensured that Holstein-Gottorp maintained its old alliance with Sweden against Denmark. This eventually led to tensions with the administrators in Holstein-Gottorp, but her policy remained in place until her death, and there was never an open conflict. In 1705, she expressed a desire to visit Holstein-Gottorp, but Charles XII asked her to remain in Sweden. She received several marriage proposals from various German princes and from the future George I of Great Britain, but she refused to enter into another arranged marriage. At this time, she was having a relationship with Count Olof Gyllenborg, a chamberlain whom she had known even before her marriage. He reportedly confessed his feelings for her after she became a widow and wrote several poems to her, in which he described how a secret love must be revealed rather than endured in silence.
