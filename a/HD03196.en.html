Time for the teaching assignment.

TL;DRThe government wants to amend the education law to give teachers more time for actual teaching and reduce administrative burdens. The proposal also aims to strengthen efforts to combat bullying and harassment in schools.
In plain language

The government has proposed a change to the rules in the Education Act. The goal is to make the professions of teacher and preschool teacher more attractive by clarifying that their jobs also include planning and following up on lessons. This is intended to provide better conditions for carrying out a good teaching assignment.

To reduce the amount of paperwork, it is proposed that parent-teacher conferences and student conferences only need to take place at least once per academic year, instead of every term. Information about how children develop should also be provided at specific times, rather than requiring staff to provide information constantly. Written plans should also focus more on how students learn.

The new proposal also makes it clearer that all staff in schools and preschools have a responsibility to prevent bullying, harassment, and sexual harassment. Staff must actively ensure that such incidents are addressed. If a student or child reports that they have been mistreated, or if they feel that a member of staff has offended them, the staff must inform the principal.

The new rules regarding the prevention of abuse are proposed to come into effect on August 1, 2026. The changes relating to the teacher's duties and teaching are proposed to come into effect on July 1, 2027. The remaining changes to the law are proposed to come into effect on July 1, 2028.

Full text

The proposal includes suggested amendments to the Education Act (2010:800) that aim to create better conditions for teachers and preschool teachers to fulfill their teaching duties and to increase the attractiveness of the teaching and preschool teaching professions.

It should be stipulated in the Education Act that the teaching duties of teachers and preschool teachers include teaching, as well as planning and evaluation of the teaching. The government is authorized to issue regulations regarding the scope of time allocated to the various components of the teaching duties.

Information about a child's or student's development should be provided at times determined by staff at the preschool, after-school center, or school, rather than being provided continuously. Development meetings should take place at least once per academic year, instead of once per term, and the written plans should focus on students' knowledge development.

Everyone working within the education system should promote fundamental democratic values and human rights. It should be clarified that all staff in activities governed by the Education Act are obligated to actively identify and prevent bullying, harassment, and sexual harassment within the activities, and to ensure that such incidents are addressed. The staff's obligation to report bullying to the principal is being changed to an obligation to inform that a child or student believes they have been subjected to serious or repeated bullying. This obligation should also apply to all cases where a child or student believes they have been subjected to bullying by a member of the staff.

The amendments to the law regarding bullying are proposed to come into effect on August 1, 2026, and the amendments regarding the teaching duties on July 1, 2027. Other amendments are proposed to come into effect on July 1, 2028.
