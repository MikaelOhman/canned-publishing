Improved conditions for the tourism industry regarding the small-scale production of alcoholic beverages.

TL;DRThe government wants to make it easier for small producers to sell alcohol directly on their farms. The aim is to help tourism and local businesses grow.
In plain language

The government has proposed a measure that would allow small businesses that produce their own wine, beer, or spirits to sell their products directly to customers on their premises. This is known as on-site sales. The idea is that visitors should come to learn more about the product, not just to buy alcohol to take home.

To comply with EU regulations and to avoid undermining the Systembolaget's role as the sole retailer, there are strict rules in place. Only small, independent producers are eligible to apply for a permit. They are only allowed to sell from a single location, and the products must be produced on-site. This means that they cannot purchase finished products from other companies to resell.

There are also limits on the amount that can be purchased during a single visit. For example, customers can purchase a maximum of three liters of wine or three liters of strong beer. The limit for spirits is lower, at a maximum of 0.7 liters. This is to ensure that sales remain small and controlled.

If the Parliament votes in favor of the proposal, Prop. 2024/25:86, the new regulations are expected to come into effect on June 1, 2025. The plan is to evaluate how the new regulations are working after six years.

Full text

The proposal suggests introducing the possibility of small-scale on-farm sales of alcoholic beverages, with the aim of improving the conditions for local tourism.

The government believes that it is compatible with EU law to introduce small-scale on-farm sales while also safeguarding the retail monopoly. A prerequisite is that restrictions are introduced to ensure that sales are limited and that the focus is on the experience rather than the sales itself. Therefore, it is proposed, among other things, that licenses are required for on-farm sales and that the alcoholic beverages must be produced on the farm. Only small-scale, independent producers should be granted on-farm sales licenses. On-farm sales should only be permitted to consumers who participate in an educational visit organized by the producer. The license holder should only be allowed to operate on-farm sales from one location. Sales to each individual consumer should not exceed 0.7 liters of spirits, three liters of wine, three liters of strong beer, and three liters of other fermented alcoholic beverages per visit.

The new rules are proposed to come into effect on June 1, 2025. The reform should be evaluated, and the new regulations regarding on-farm sales are proposed to be valid for six years from the date of entry into force.
