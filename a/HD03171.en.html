A more effective assessment of nuclear facilities.

TL;DRThe government wants to change the rules regarding the construction of nuclear power plants in Sweden. The new proposal makes it easier to obtain approval if there is a ready-made plan and the municipality agrees.
In plain language

The government has proposed new laws that will come into effect on June 17, 2026. The goal is to change the way nuclear facilities are inspected and approved, i.e., locations where nuclear power is used.

The new approach allows the government to directly approve a facility if a plan for the area already exists. For this plan to be valid, the municipalities affected by the plan must have approved the proposal. If this new approach is not used, the old rules in the Environmental Code still apply, which is the law that governs how we protect our environment.

The proposal also stipulates that the government must assess whether the benefits of a facility outweigh the drawbacks. It will also be possible to obtain a preliminary assessment, which is an early response as to whether a project can become a reality. The proposal involves changes to several different laws, including laws on radiation protection and how to pay for nuclear waste.

Full text

The proposal suggests a new process where the government, upon application, can approve a nuclear facility. Such an approval replaces the permissibility assessment that the government would otherwise have to conduct according to Chapter 17 of the Environmental Code. The government should be able to approve a nuclear facility if it has previously adopted a plan for nuclear facilities that covers the area and facilities to which the application relates. The plan assesses whether it is compatible with the environmental regulations in the Environmental Code to construct a particular type of nuclear facility within the area covered by the plan. A plan may only be adopted if an application has been submitted for the government's approval, and the municipality or municipalities within the areas to be covered by the plan have approved the application or the plan.

The current procedure for permissibility assessment according to the Environmental Code will continue to apply to those who do not apply for the government's approval.

Changes are also proposed regarding the assessment of nuclear facilities under the Nuclear Activities Act. A possibility for preliminary assessments is introduced. Changes are also proposed in the Environmental Code, which stipulate that the government, in a permissibility assessment of a nuclear facility, must assess whether the benefits of the facility outweigh the disadvantages it may entail.

The proposed amendments to the law will come into force on June 17, 2026.

  4. The Parliament approves the government's proposal for an amendment to the Act (2006:647) on the financing of nuclear waste.
