Full text

In the report, the government evaluates the state's borrowing and debt management for the period 2021–2025. The basis for the evaluation is the goal that the Parliament has established for state debt policy:

  • The state's debt should be managed in a way that minimizes the cost of the debt in the long term, while also considering the risks involved in the management.
  • The management should be carried out within the framework of the requirements set by monetary policy.

The evaluation covers both the government's governance and the National Debt Office's management of the state debt. The goal of state debt policy is long-term and is therefore evaluated over rolling five-year periods. The report must be submitted to the Parliament no later than April 25th every other year.

Regarding the current evaluation period, the following can be noted:

  • The gross state debt decreased from 22 percent to 19 percent of GDP over the entire evaluation period. The debt decreased to 17 percent of GDP at the midpoint of the evaluation period, before increasing slightly.
  • The borrowing need increased towards the end of the evaluation period, and the state moved from several years of surpluses to deficits.
  • The cost of the state debt has increased during the evaluation period from historically low levels, with a sharp increase in 2022, mainly due to high inflation and a weaker Swedish krona, to then decrease slightly in the later years of the evaluation period.
