In plain language

The government has proposed changes to several laws governing how the Swedish Prison and Probation Service (Kriminalvården) handles information about individuals. The goal is to modernize the regulations to better suit current needs, while also protecting people's privacy.

The new regulations will make it easier for the Prison and Probation Service to use the information necessary to carry out sentences safely. This includes giving the agency greater opportunities to use technology, such as facial recognition, to facilitate its work.

The proposal covers changes to several different laws, including the Prison Act and regulations for electronic monitoring, also known as ankle bracelets. If the Parliament approves the proposal, the new regulations are expected to come into effect on April 1, 2026.

Full text

The government proposes amendments to the law regarding the Swedish Prison and Probation Service's handling of personal data within the scope of the criminal data act, and to three other laws.

The purpose of the amendments is, among other things, to create a more modern, general, and appropriate framework for the processing of personal data, while maintaining a high level of data protection. The amendments generally mean that the Swedish Prison and Probation Service will have greater opportunities to process the personal data needed for the agency to effectively and safely execute sentences. At the same time, the protection of personal privacy is strengthened. The Swedish Prison and Probation Service will also have expanded opportunities to use facial recognition technology, which may, to some extent, facilitate the agency's expansion.

The amendments are proposed to come into force on April 1, 2026.

Proposed Parliamentary Decision

The government's proposal:

  1. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Act (2018:1699) on the Swedish Prison and Probation Service's handling of personal data within the scope of the criminal data act.
  2. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Prison Act (2010:610).
  3. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Criminal Data Act (2018:1235).
  4. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Act (2025:000) on the execution of prison sentences with electronic monitoring.
