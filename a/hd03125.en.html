TL;DRThe government wants to change the laws to make it easier for smaller companies to sell their shares on smaller trading platforms. This is intended to make it easier for companies to raise new capital for growth.
In plain language

The government has proposed a set of regulations designed to comply with new rules from the EU. The goal is to make it more attractive for small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) to use so-called MTF (Multilateral Trading Facility) platforms. An MTF platform is a smaller market for trading stocks that is not the main stock exchange.

A key part of the proposal is that companies should be allowed to have shares with different voting rights. This means that some shares can give more power in decision-making than others. The operators of the trading platforms must not refuse companies the right to trade there simply because they have such different voting rights. There should also be special rules for how decisions about voting rights are made at shareholder meetings.

The proposal also means that certain rules for how shares are handled should apply to these smaller trading platforms. This includes how shares are handled when companies are split up or merged, as well as how companies are allowed to buy and sell their own shares. The government wants to amend several different laws, such as the Companies Act and the Financial Instruments Trading Act.

If the parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the intention is that the new rules should come into effect on December 5, 2026.

Full text

The government proposes regulations necessary to implement the EU directive on shares with different voting rights into Swedish law. The directive aims to make it more attractive for small and medium-sized companies to list shares for trading on a multilateral trading facility (MTF) platform, thereby improving companies' opportunities to obtain venture capital. The proposals in the government's proposal mean that a stock exchange or a financial institution that operates an MTF platform must not refuse a company the opportunity to list shares for trading on the platform solely on the grounds that the company has shares with different voting rights. A special majority rule is also proposed, which should apply to shareholder resolutions regarding the voting rights of shares, taken in connection with the listing of shares on an MTF platform and as long as they are traded on the platform.

The government further proposes that the rules in the Companies Act regarding the sale of excess shares in the event of a merger or split should also apply to shares that are subject to trading on an MTF platform. In addition, it is proposed that the possibility for public limited companies to acquire and transfer their own shares should also apply to companies whose shares are listed for trading on an MTF platform.

The proposed amendments to the law are scheduled to come into force on December 5, 2026.
