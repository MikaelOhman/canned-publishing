The government has proposed a plan to reduce taxes on various types of income. For those who work full-time and have low to medium incomes, the proposed reduction would, on average, result in an extra 3,800 Swedish krona per year. This is achieved by modifying what is known as the "workplace tax deduction," which is a portion of the tax deducted from your salary.

The proposal also aims to make it more affordable for older people to pay taxes. For those who have reached the age of 66 at the beginning of the year, the proposed reduction would, on average, provide an extra 2,300 Swedish krona per person per year. This is achieved by increasing the basic allowance for older individuals, meaning that a larger portion of their income will not be taxed.

The proposal also includes tax reductions for individuals who receive disability benefits or activity benefits. The goal is to ensure that the tax on these benefits is equivalent to the tax on regular salaries for certain income levels. Those with benefits exceeding approximately 54,000 Swedish krona per year may receive an additional 1,800 Swedish krona per year.

If the parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new rules will come into effect on January 1, 2026. The proposal is a so-called "proposition," which is an official proposal from the government to the parliament.
