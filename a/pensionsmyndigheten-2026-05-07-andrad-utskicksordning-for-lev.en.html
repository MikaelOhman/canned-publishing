In plain language

The Swedish Social Insurance Agency (Försäkringskassan) sends out letters containing a "certificate of life" to individuals living in other countries who receive payments from Sweden. This certificate confirms that you still reside at your stated address, allowing Försäkringskassan to continue paying your pension or benefits.

In 2026, the schedule for sending these letters will be changed for certain countries. The date on which you receive your letter depends entirely on the country in which you live. It is important that you keep track of this so that your payments are not interrupted.

If you have an electronic identification method, such as BankID, you can approve your certificate of life directly online. If you do this, you will not need to visit any government agency or other organization to have the document stamped.

May 7, 2026 | News

Starting in May, we will be sending out confirmation of life certificates to those who receive pensions or benefits from Försäkringskassan (the Swedish Social Insurance Agency) and live abroad. The country you reside in determines the date on which your certificate is sent. This year, some countries have been reordered in the mailing schedule.

Each year, we require a confirmation of life from individuals living abroad to ensure they continue to receive the payments they are entitled to. If you live in a country without digital exchange agreements with Sweden, you will receive the confirmation of life certificate by mail from us.
