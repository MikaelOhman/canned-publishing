Full text

Brita Olofsdotter, who died in 1569, was a Swedish cavalry soldier in the Swedish army who served in the Livonian War. She is perhaps the oldest historically documented example in Sweden of women disguising themselves as men to serve in professions reserved for men in Europe; she can also be considered Sweden's first female soldier.

Olofsdotter came from Finland and was a widow, having been married to Nils Simonsson. She disguised herself as a man and enlisted in the army during the Livonian War, where she served in the cavalry and died in battle. On June 16, 1569, Johan III informed Gabriel Christensson about the matter, ordered an investigation, and instructed that her remaining salary should be paid to her relatives.
