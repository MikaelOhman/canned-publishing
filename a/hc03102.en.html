In plain language

The government has proposed new laws that will change the regulations regarding coastal protection. Coastal protection refers to the rules in place to protect nature and access to water. The proposal suggests that this protection should no longer apply to small lakes, narrow sections of waterways, or bodies of water created by humans after June 1975.

It will also become easier for agriculture and fishing to use land near water. The proposal aims to give companies the opportunity to obtain permits to build in areas where there are already very few houses and buildings. However, if an area is particularly important for nature, it is still possible to maintain a protected zone up to 100 meters from the shoreline.

Municipalities will also be given more authority to make decisions about their areas. It will become easier for municipalities to ensure that a protection is not automatically reinstated when they create new plans for how land should be used. These plans are called detailed plans and general plans.

If the parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new regulations will come into effect on July 1, 2025. The proposal involves changes to several important laws, including the Environmental Code and the Planning and Building Act.

Full text

The proposal includes suggestions aimed at creating a clearer and more effective coastal protection system, with better conditions for building homes and developing and operating businesses in coastal areas.

The proposed changes mean that the general coastal protection regulations will no longer apply to small lakes and narrow stretches of waterways, nor to bodies of water that were created after June 30, 1975. However, it should be possible to introduce coastal protection in individual cases within an area up to 100 meters from the shoreline of small lakes and narrow stretches of waterways if it is of particular importance to ensure one or more of the purposes of coastal protection.

Furthermore, it is proposed to introduce simplifications and clarifications to the exception from coastal protection for needs within the agricultural sector, and that this exception should also include aquaculture. A new possibility for exemptions from coastal protection for business activities with limited impact on the purposes of coastal protection is also proposed for areas with low building density and low pressure for development.

It is also proposed that municipalities should be able to easily decide that a lifted coastal protection does not automatically come back into effect when a new detailed development plan is adopted, and that clarifications are introduced regarding the fact that coastal protection may not come back into effect in certain cases, for example, when a general plan is repealed.

The changes are proposed to come into effect on July 1, 2025.
