In plain language

The security situation in Sweden has deteriorated, and therefore, the government is working to strengthen civil defense. This means we need better shelters and other safe places to be in, in the event of a war or a major crisis in the country. This is referred to as heightened readiness.

The government has submitted a proposal to the Parliament to amend several laws. They want to introduce a new law regarding shelters and change the laws that determine how people should be evacuated and provided with housing during crises. They also want to amend the planning and building code, which is the law that governs how buildings can be constructed in Sweden.

The purpose of these changes is to ensure that there are clear plans for who is responsible for what when people need to be evacuated. It should also be clear how housing will be arranged for those who cannot remain in their homes.

If the Parliament approves the proposal, the new rules will come into effect on June 1, 2026.

Full text

The security situation has deteriorated over time, and there is a rebuilding of the civil defense system. To achieve stronger protection for the civilian population in the event of heightened alert, modern and appropriate shelters are needed, as well as other types of effective protective spaces. It is also crucial to have clear planning and a division of responsibilities regarding evacuation and subsequent accommodation in the event of heightened alert.

The government therefore proposes a new law on shelters and protected spaces, as well as amendments to the law governing evacuation and accommodation during heightened alert. In addition, certain amendments are proposed to the planning and building code.

The legislative changes are proposed to come into force on June 1, 2026.

Proposed Parliamentary Decision

Prop. 2025/26:142

Government's proposal:

  1. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for a law on shelters and protected spaces.
  2. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for a law amending the law (2006:546) on evacuation and accommodation, etc., during heightened alert.
  3. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for a law amending the Planning and Building Act (2010:900).
