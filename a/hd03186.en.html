In plain language

The government has proposed changes to several laws regarding investment funds and savings. The aim is to strengthen the Swedish fund market and make it better prepared for the future. The proposal also includes changes to comply with new regulations from the EU.

For those who invest in funds, the rules regarding how they can sell their shares may become more flexible. It will also be easier to trade portions of funds on certain types of trading platforms. At the same time, new tools are being introduced for those who manage the funds, known as fund managers, to help them manage risks and money effectively.

For certain types of funds, known as alternative investment funds, stricter rules are being introduced. This includes limitations on the amount of borrowing that funds can use and a ban on lending money to certain individuals or companies. This is being done to reduce the risks associated with lending.

The new rules apply to everything from pension funds to investment savings accounts. If the parliament votes in favor of the proposal, most of these changes will come into effect on July 1, 2026.

Full text

The proposal includes draft amendments to laws aimed at strengthening the competitiveness and resilience of the Swedish investment fund market. Several of the proposals are necessary to align Swedish law with changes in the two key EU directives in the area of investment funds. The proposals essentially entail the following:

  • The rules for redeeming shares in investment funds will become more flexible. It will also be possible to apply a notice period for investment funds and special funds.
  • Rules are introduced regarding liquidity management tools for investment funds and open-ended alternative investment funds, requiring fund managers to select at least two such tools, e.g., swing pricing.
  • The possibility of allowing fund shares to be traded on a trading venue is expanded to include not only regulated markets but also MTF platforms. It will also be possible to create a share class that is traded on such a trading venue.
  • Harmonized rules for lending by alternative investment funds are introduced, including requirements for limitations on financial leverage, a ban on lending to certain entities, and rules designed to mitigate certain risks associated with lending.
  • Rules are introduced to enable fund managers to offer a wider range of services, such as credit management and administration of benchmarks.

The amendments to the law are proposed to come into force on July 1, 2026.
