In plain language

Many people traveling to countries like Spain, Germany, and Poland forget to bring their European Health Insurance Card (EHIC). Without this card, you may often have to pay the full cost of medical care yourself if you become ill or injured during your vacation. For some, these costs can be very high, sometimes exceeding one hundred thousand Swedish krona.

If you don't have the card with you, you may be able to get reimbursed by the Swedish Social Insurance Agency (Försäkringskassan) later, but it takes time to receive the money. With the EHIC, you only pay a small co-payment, just like residents of the country you are visiting. Therefore, it is much safer to always carry the card with you.

The EHIC is free to obtain from the Swedish Social Insurance Agency's website and is valid for three years within the EU, as well as in Norway, Iceland, Liechtenstein, and the United Kingdom. Older people tend to forget the card more often than younger people. It is important to bring it along to avoid large, unexpected expenses in case of illness.

June 4, 2026

The need for urgent medical care during an overseas vacation is most common among those traveling to Spain and aged 60-79. However, many forget to bring their European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) and risk incurring significant expenses. This is revealed in a new analysis of the use of the EHIC by the Swedish Social Insurance Agency.

With the EHIC, individuals vacationing or temporarily staying in Europe can receive necessary medical and dental care on the same terms and at the same cost as local residents. They only need to pay a patient co-payment.

In 2023, over 40,000 people received necessary medical care during their trips abroad. This was most common among those aged 60-79.

Half of all individuals who received necessary medical care abroad were in Spain; Germany and Poland were the next most frequent destinations.

Of those who needed necessary medical care abroad, four out of ten did not have an EHIC when seeking treatment. Younger people are more likely than older people to remember to bring their EHIC. Three out of four individuals aged 20-39 used their EHIC, while only half of those over 60 used the card.

Those who do not have an EHIC must pay for the medical costs themselves and can then apply for reimbursement from the Swedish Social Insurance Agency. For most people, the cost was less than 10,000 SEK, but for 300 individuals, the costs exceeded 100,000 SEK.

"If you don't have your card with you and need medical care, it can quickly become very expensive. While it is possible to get the money back later from the Swedish Social Insurance Agency (Försäkringskassan), it can take time. Therefore, it is highly recommended to carry your card," says Cecilia Eek, an analyst at Försäkringskassan.

The European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) can be ordered on the Försäkringskassan website. The card is free and valid for three years within the EU/EEA, Switzerland, and Great Britain. It primarily applies to public healthcare facilities but also to some private ones; always inquire beforehand whether the EHIC is accepted.
