Full text

The proposal suggests a new reception law to replace the law (1994:137) regarding the reception of asylum seekers and others. The purpose of the new law is to improve the conditions for orderly reception, a more efficient return process, and to reduce social exclusion.

The law will apply to asylum seekers and foreign nationals covered by the mass migration directive. Foreign nationals covered by the law will continue to have the right to assistance in the form of accommodation, daily allowances, and special grants. The conditions for the right to daily allowances will be tightened, and the daily allowance may be reduced for foreign nationals who do not fulfill their obligations. Asylum seekers will, among other things, be obliged to reside within a limited geographical area and to participate in attendance checks at the asylum accommodation. A reporting obligation is introduced for foreign nationals who have been issued a deportation order.

Amendments to other laws are also proposed. The new law and the amendments will come into force on October 1, 2026.
