Obligations to report data for certain e-identification providers.

TL;DRThe police and other authorities should be granted the right to obtain information from companies that offer digital identification documents. This is being done to more easily combat crimes related to banking services and cryptocurrencies.
In plain language

The government has proposed a measure that will make it easier for law enforcement agencies to obtain information. Currently, banks and financial institutions are obligated to disclose customer information during criminal investigations, even if they are bound by confidentiality agreements. The proposal now seeks to extend this obligation to companies that provide electronic identification (e-ID), or digital identification documents.

This is particularly relevant when the e-ID is used to log in to banking services or to authorize payments. With this change, the police and other authorities will be able to directly contact e-ID providers to determine how an individual has used their digital identification with financial institutions. The aim is to more effectively track and prevent criminal activity.

The proposal also includes changes to regulations concerning cryptocurrencies and other crypto-related services. Companies that work with crypto assets may now be required to disclose information to government intelligence agencies. This is part of the effort to control and monitor the digital economy.

If the parliament approves the proposal, the new regulations will come into effect on May 1, 2026. The proposal involves changes to several laws related to, among other things, international legal assistance and regulations for payments and crypto markets.

Full text

Banks and other financial institutions are subject to reporting obligations. These obligations mean that law enforcement agencies, in their crime-fighting activities, have the right to access information about individuals held by these companies, information that would otherwise be protected by confidentiality.

In order for customers of financial institutions to utilize services offered by these companies, they often need to use electronic identification (e-identification), for example, to log in to an online service or to approve transactions. This proposal introduces a reporting obligation for companies that provide e-identification services used by financial institutions. This proposal allows law enforcement agencies to directly contact an e-identification company to obtain information about an individual's use of e-identification in relation to financial institutions.

The proposal also suggests expanding the reporting obligation for companies that provide cryptocurrencies and related services to include their use in the law enforcement agencies' intelligence activities.

The proposed amendments to the law are scheduled to come into effect on May 1, 2026.

Proposed Parliamentary Decisions

The Government's proposal:

  1. The Parliament adopts the Government's proposal for a law on reporting obligations for certain e-identification companies.
  2. The Parliament adopts the Government's proposal for an amendment to the Act (2000:562) on international legal assistance in criminal matters.
  3. The Parliament adopts the Government's proposal for an amendment to the Act (2024:114) on clearing and settlement of payments.
  4. The Parliament adopts the Government's proposal for an amendment to the Act (2024:1159) containing supplementary provisions to the EU regulation on markets for crypto-assets.
