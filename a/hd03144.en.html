Nordic Enforcement in Criminal Cases

TL;DRThe government proposes a new law that will make it easier to transfer sentences between the Nordic countries. This means that individuals convicted of crimes will be able to serve their sentences more easily in Denmark, Finland, Iceland, or Norway.
In plain language

The government wants to change the rules for how countries in the Nordic region cooperate when someone has been sentenced to a punishment. The new law will replace the old law from 1963 and make it easier to transfer sentences between Sweden and our neighboring countries. This includes the possibility of transferring community service sentences between the countries.

To speed up the process, the government proposes that authorities such as the Swedish Prison and Probation Service and courts must work quickly on these cases. If the person who has been sentenced agrees to the transfer, the decision should take effect immediately without any waiting period. It should also be possible to sign a statement indicating that the person does not intend to appeal the decision.

The proposal also includes changes to many other laws that cover everything from witnesses to criminal records and police cooperation. The purpose is to improve the entire system for cooperation regarding crime and punishment throughout the Nordic region.

If the Swedish Parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new rules are intended to come into effect on July 1, 2026.

Full text

The proposal includes a draft law on Nordic enforcement in criminal cases, which will replace the law (1963:193) on cooperation with Denmark, Finland, Iceland, and Norway regarding the enforcement of sentences, etc. The proposal includes the following:

  • The scope of the regulation is expanded to include probationary sentences that include community service.
  • Several changes are made to improve the application of the regulations and increase the number of transfers, including the following:
  • A requirement for expediency shall apply to the handling of cases by the Swedish Prison and Probation Service and the courts regarding the transfer of the enforcement of prison sentences.
  • A decision to transfer the enforcement of a prison sentence may become final immediately if the convicted person consents to the transfer.
  • The convicted person may provide a statement waiving the right to appeal such a decision or to continue the appeal (a declaration of satisfaction).
  • Appeals will be reviewed by a district court.

The proposed amendments to the law are intended to come into force on July 1, 2026.
