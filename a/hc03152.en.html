TL;DRThe government wants to change the rules regarding how courts make decisions in cases involving pension funds. The proposal suggests that a single judge could make the decision instead of a panel of judges.
In plain language

The government has proposed changes to how the administrative court handles certain cases. These cases concern the procurement of funds for the investment options available within the supplementary pension system, which is the portion of your occupational pension that you can choose funds for yourself.

Currently, there are no clear rules regarding the number of judges required to hear these specific cases. Therefore, the government wants to amend the law governing administrative courts. The proposal states that a single judge, known as a legally qualified judge, should be able to decide the case alone unless there is a specific reason to include more judges in the decision-making process.

If the parliament approves the proposal, the new rule will come into effect on August 1, 2025. The purpose is to make the rules regarding the court's composition clear and consistent, similar to the rules applied in other similar procurement cases.

Full text

The proposal suggests that the composition of the court in cases of review under the Act (2022:760) on the procurement of funds for the premium pension fund market should be the same as in other procurement cases. The issue of the court's composition was not addressed when that law was introduced. The government therefore proposes an amendment to the Act (1971:289) on Administrative Courts, which would stipulate that in such cases, the administrative court should be able to hear cases with a single legally qualified judge, unless there is a specific reason for the case to be heard by a full panel of judges.

The proposed amendment is scheduled to come into effect on August 1, 2025.
