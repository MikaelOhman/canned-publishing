Offline card payments – we're expanding the possibilities now.

TL;DRStarting July 1, 2026, you will be able to pay with card even if the internet or other technical systems are down when purchasing essential items such as food, medicine, and gasoline.
In plain language

Starting July 1, 2026, it will be easier to make purchases even if technical problems arise. This means that many common payment cards will now work even when the internet or other communication channels are down. This is being done to ensure that we can buy essential items like food, medicine, and fuel, even during crises or major system disruptions.

To use this feature, you must use a physical card and enter your personal identification number (PIN). This functionality applies to most Visa and Mastercard cards owned by adults. This is an important security measure developed by the National Bank of Sweden (the authority responsible for Sweden's currency) in collaboration with banks and other companies.

In the future, there will also be investigations into whether the mobile payment service Swish can have similar capabilities to function without internet access. For now, however, it is primarily physical cards that are receiving this additional security feature to make the Swedish payment system more robust and secure.

Press Release

Starting today, July 1, 2026, expanded options for making card payments offline will be implemented when purchasing essential goods. This measure strengthens the resilience of the payment system and is based on an agreement between the National Bank and market participants.

"The Swedish payment market is undergoing rapid change, with a high degree of digitalization and low use of cash. Cards are currently the most common method of payment in stores. By ensuring that card payments continue to function even during disruptions and interruptions in data communication, we significantly increase preparedness," says Erik Thedéen, Governor of the National Bank.

The expanded possibility for offline payments is the result of an agreement reached in October 2025 between card issuers, card networks, payment processors, retailers, and the National Bank. Over the past year, these parties have implemented the technical and regulatory measures required to enable this change.

"A resilient payment system requires all actors within the system to take responsibility. In this effort, we would like to express our special thanks to the market for its active and constructive cooperation," says Erik Thedéen.

The expanded offline functionality covers most Visa and Mastercard cards issued to individuals over 18 years old by card issuers that have joined the agreement. These cards can be used starting today for purchases of essential goods, such as food, medicine, and fuel at staffed stations. This feature can be used when data communication is unavailable, for example, during major disruptions, cyberattacks, peacetime crises, or periods of heightened alert. The function only works with physical cards in conjunction with a PIN. The National Bank of Sweden encourages households to keep their physical cards and PINs readily accessible.

As the next step in efforts to increase the resilience of the payment system, the National Bank of Sweden has initiated a collaboration with Getswish and relevant companies to explore the feasibility of offline functionality for the Swish payment service during the year.
