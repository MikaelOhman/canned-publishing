A registry for all apartments.

TL;DRThe government wants to create a new registry of all apartments, which will be managed by the Swedish Mapping, Cadastre and Land Registry (Lantmäteriet). This is intended to make it easier and safer to obtain loans for apartments and to help the police combat crime.
In plain language

The government has proposed the creation of a new registry for all apartments (condominiums) in Sweden. The authority Lantmäteriet will be responsible for maintaining a registry of apartments, their residents, homeowners' associations, and loans associated with the apartments.

The purpose of the new registry is to make it easier for both associations and authorities to obtain accurate information. It will also enhance security when using an apartment as collateral for a loan, known as a mortgage. By having better information on ownership, the enforcement agency (Kronofogden) and the police can more easily work to detect and prevent crimes.

The proposal also involves changes to the rules for notifying homeowners' associations when an apartment is sold or when a loan is taken out. Instead of sending notifications, the information will be directly available in the new system. This is referred to as a unified system of property rights, meaning that the rules regarding rights in the apartment will be the same for everyone.

If the proposal is approved and the parliament votes in favor, the government plans for the new laws to come into effect on January 1, 2027. The government proposes amendments to several laws, including the Condominium Act and the Enforcement Act, to ensure the new system can function properly.

Full text

The government proposes the establishment of a condominium registry where all condominiums should be registered. The registry will be managed by the Swedish Mapping, Cadastre and Land Registry (Lantmäteriet).

The new condominium registry will include information about the condominium unit, the condominium owner, the condominium association, and encumbrances. The purpose of the registry is to:

  • make the handling of encumbrances more modern and legally secure,
  • simplify matters for condominium associations, authorities, and actors in the housing market,
  • provide society with better information about condominiums,
  • facilitate the Swedish Enforcement Agency's asset investigations,
  • contribute to increased opportunities to prevent and investigate crimes, and
  • contribute to more effective asset-based crime prevention.

Furthermore, a unified system of property law will be introduced for condominiums. Registration replaces the notification to the condominium association as a legal requirement for encumbrances and transfers of ownership of the condominium.

The legislative changes related to the establishment of the registry are proposed to come into force on January 1, 2027. The remaining changes are proposed to come into force on a date determined by the government.

Proposed Parliamentary Decisions

The government's proposal:

  1. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for a law on the condominium registry.
  2. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for a law on the implementation of the law (2026:000) on the condominium registry.
  3. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for a law amending the Condominium Act (1991:614).
  4. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for a law amending the Code of Civil Procedure.
  5. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for a law amending the law (1981:775) on the implementation of the Code of Civil Procedure.
  6. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for a law amending the law (2006:378) on the apartment registry.
