Supplementary provisions to the EU Battery Regulation.

Source Riksdagen Read the original at Riksdagen →2025-06-24

The proposal includes legislative changes that complement the EU regulation on batteries. These changes include, among other things, that a market surveillance authority may: * require economic operators to provide documents, specifications, data, or information; * carry out unannounced on-site inspections and physical checks of products; * require access to premises, land, or means of transport; * initiate investigations on its own initiative; * require economic operators to take appropriate measures to remedy any non-compliance with the requirements of the EU regulation on batteries or to eliminate a risk; * take appropriate measures itself to remedy any non-compliance with the requirements of the EU regulation on batteries and the EU regulation on market surveillance, or to eliminate a risk; * acquire, inspect, and dismantle product samples; and * require the removal of content from an online interface, such as a website, require a warning to be displayed on the online interface, or restrict access to the interface.

The proposal also includes that a duty of confidentiality shall apply to anyone involved in a case concerning market surveillance.

The proposal also includes the repeal of the Act (2007:162) on the car scrapping fund.

The legislative changes are proposed to come into force on January 1, 2026.
