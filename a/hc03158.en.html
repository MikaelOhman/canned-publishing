Repeal of the Law on Structural Fund Partnerships.

TL;DRThe government wants to repeal an old law concerning collaborations related to EU funding. The proposal stipulates that the law will cease to be in effect on August 1, 2025.
In plain language

The government has proposed to the Parliament that the law regarding structural fund partnerships be repealed. This law, which has been in effect since 2007, governs how collaborations related to EU financial support should operate.

If the Parliament approves the proposal, the law will no longer be in effect starting August 1, 2025. This means that the existing regulations for these collaborations will cease to apply on that date.

However, there is an exception for collaborations that have already been initiated. For groups that were formed before the law is repealed, certain aspects of the old regulations will remain in effect until the end of October 2025.

In the proposal, the government suggests that the law (2007:459) concerning structural fund partnerships should cease to be valid on August 1, 2025.

Proposed Parliamentary Decision

Prop. 2024/25:158

Government's proposal: The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for a law repealing the law (2007:459) concerning structural fund partnerships. 3

Prop. 2024/25:158 2 Draft law repealing the law (2007:459) concerning structural fund partnerships Hereby it is prescribed that the law (2007:459) concerning structural fund partnerships shall cease to be valid.

  1. This law shall enter into force on August 1, 2025.
  2. However, the provisions of paragraphs 1 and 2 of section 1, and sections 3 and 4 of the repealed law shall still apply until the end of October 2025 for structural fund partnerships that were established before the law came into force.
