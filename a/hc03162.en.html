The government has proposed changes to the unemployment insurance law. The most significant proposal is that individuals participating in special programs for people with disabilities should be eligible for unemployment benefits. Previously, the general rule was that individuals participating in so-called labor market programs (government initiatives to help the unemployed) were not entitled to benefits.

The proposal also clarifies the rules for those who have been members of an unemployment fund for at least twelve months. It should be clear that they are entitled to a benefit of 60 percent of their previous salary. The government also wants to clarify the rules regarding when individuals receive a reduced benefit if they are receiving funds from a source other than the unemployment fund.

There is also a rule regarding how benefits are calculated when a new period begins. The government wants to clarify that information from the previous period can be used to calculate the benefit, even if that period falls under the old law from 1997. This is to make it easier to understand how the benefits are calculated when switching to a new law.

Full text

The proposal suggests certain additions and clarifications to the new law (2024:506) regarding unemployment insurance.

It proposes an exception to the general rule that participants in labor market programs are not entitled to unemployment benefits. This exception applies to applicants participating in labor market programs specifically designed for individuals with disabilities that result in reduced work capacity.

It also proposes a clarification that applicants who have been members of or affiliated with an unemployment fund for twelve months or more should not be prevented from receiving a benefit level of 60 percent.

Furthermore, it proposes a clarification that the rule regarding the reduction of unemployment benefits when receiving benefits from a source other than the unemployment fund applies when the benefit received by the applicant pertains to the same month in which the applicant is applying for unemployment benefits.

Finally, a new transitional provision is proposed to clarify that the possibility of basing the unemployment benefit during a new benefit period on conditions from the applicant's most recently used benefit period should also apply when the most recently used benefit period was a period under the current law (1997:238) regarding unemployment insurance.

The proposed changes are suggested to come into effect on November 18, 2025. However, the provisions should apply retroactively from October 1, 2025.
