In plain language

The government proposes a new law that would establish a national investigative function to examine deaths related to suicide. If a child dies by suicide, an investigation will always be conducted. For adults, investigations will be carried out if it is deemed that they could help prevent future suicides, or if the cause of death is not clear.

A special agency, whose name will be determined by the government, will lead this work. This agency will investigate the circumstances surrounding the death and examine what actions were taken, or could have been taken, to save the person's life. Close family members will have the opportunity to provide information to the investigation if they feel comfortable doing so.

To ensure the quality of the investigations, other government agencies and experts will be obligated to provide assistance and the necessary information. Those working on the investigations will be subject to confidentiality, meaning they cannot disclose confidential information they have learned. Changes will also be made to laws regarding the handling of personal data and confidentiality.

If the Parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new rules will come into effect on July 2, 2026.

Full text

The proposal suggests a new law regarding investigations to prevent suicide. The purpose of the investigations conducted under this law is to prevent suicide. A national investigative function is needed to improve society's understanding of suicide.

The new law stipulates that an investigation must always be conducted when a child has died by suicide. An investigation may be conducted when an adult has died by suicide, or when a child or adult has died and the cause of death is unclear, if such an investigation is deemed to prevent suicide.

The investigations will be conducted by the authority designated by the government (the investigative authority). The investigative authority will, in an investigation, clarify and analyze circumstances that may have contributed to the death, and analyze measures that were taken or could have been taken to prevent the death. Relatives should be given the opportunity to provide information when an investigation is conducted, unless it is clearly inappropriate. The investigative authority will, based on the investigations, produce materials aimed at preventing suicide.

It is also proposed that certain authorities and professionals should provide the information needed for an investigation. Certain state administrative authorities will be obligated to assist the investigative authority with experts. A duty of confidentiality is proposed for those who have participated in an investigation. Furthermore, changes are proposed to other laws concerning the processing of personal data and confidentiality.

The new law and other legislative changes are proposed to come into force on July 2, 2026.

The government's proposal:

  2. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for a law amending the law (2001:454) on the processing of personal data within social services.
  3. The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for a law amending the Public Access and Secrecy Act (2009:400).
