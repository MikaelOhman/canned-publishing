In plain language

Many leaders in key government agencies, such as the Swedish Social Insurance Agency (Försäkringskassan) and the Swedish Tax Agency (Skatteverket), are now being trained to act quickly in difficult situations. The goal is for them to be able to take responsibility and make decisions even when faced with stressful or uncertain circumstances within society.

The training program is called "Decisive Leadership in Crisis and War." It is developed in collaboration with the Swedish Defence University (Försvarshögskolan), an institution for military and civil defense education. The 18-month program takes place at five different locations throughout Sweden.

By providing this training to these leaders, the aim is to ensure that essential societal functions continue to operate even if something goes wrong. It provides leaders with the tools they need to prioritize effectively and lead their teams when it truly matters.

2,000 managers from 10 government agencies are currently being trained in "Proactive Leadership in Crisis and War" (HLKK).

"Our daily operations are highly regulated, which is a strength. At the same time, as managers and leaders, we need to be able to make quick decisions, prioritize correctly, and take responsibility for the overall situation in stressful situations. This initiative provides us with practical tools and strengthens our ability to act when it really matters," says Malin Malm, one of the managers at the Swedish Social Insurance Agency who has completed the training.

One of the most important tasks of civil defense is to ensure that essential societal functions operate in all circumstances, even during crises and wars.

"This training aims to give managers an increased ability to act quickly, decisively, and with good judgment in uncertain situations, and hopefully also inspire more parts of civil society," says Monica Sjöstrand, a lecturer in leadership and management at the Swedish Defence University.

All 2,000 managers from the government agencies will complete the training at five locations in Sweden over a period of 18 months. The training includes two to three days of self-study and two days in group sessions. The Swedish Defence University has trained the 40 instructors from the various agencies who will lead the training programs during the fall.

"Our government agencies must function in all circumstances, regardless of what happens," emphasizes Nils Öberg, Director General of the Swedish Social Insurance Agency and one of the initiators of the training program.

This training program, the first of its kind, was launched at the beginning of the year and is a collaborative effort between the Swedish Social Insurance Agency (Försäkringskassan) and the Swedish Tax Agency (Skatteverket), along with the Swedish Defence University (Försvarshögskolan) and government agencies responsible for economic security and the provision of essential data.
