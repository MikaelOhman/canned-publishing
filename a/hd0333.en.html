The Swedish National Audit Office, which is the state's control agency, has reviewed how large digital projects within the government function. They found problems with the work not being efficient enough. They also believe that government agencies need better support when procuring IT services, and that it is difficult to follow up on goals because they are not clearly defined.

Full text

In this document, the government presents its assessment of the Swedish National Audit Office's (RiR) observations and recommendations in the audit report "State Strategic Digitalization Projects – Major Common Challenges" (RiR 2025:8).

The Swedish National Audit Office's overall conclusion is that there are several efficiency shortcomings in the government's work on strategic digitalization projects, that the support provided to agencies regarding IT procurement needs to be improved, and that the goals for the digitalization of the public sector need to be measurable in order to be systematically monitored more effectively.

The government welcomes the Swedish National Audit Office's review and agrees in part with the observations presented. The report makes an important contribution to the ongoing improvement efforts regarding the development of agencies' operations and the government's management of those agencies. As part of this work, the government has decided on a new digitalization strategy aimed at better leveraging the opportunities offered by digitalization. The government believes that the support provided to agencies in the procurement area needs to be coordinated and further developed. In the budget proposal for 2026, the government stated that certain tasks should be transferred from the National Agency for Public Procurement and the Agency for Digital Government to the Swedish Public Procurement Agency. On October 2, 2025, the government decided on a roadmap for public procurement. The Swedish Public Procurement Agency has been tasked with promoting and monitoring the implementation of the roadmap, and with, among other things, establishing a forum to coordinate procurement support related to IT.

With this document, the government considers the Swedish National Audit Office's report to be finalized.
