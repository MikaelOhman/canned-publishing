Hearing and temporary transfer in arrest warrant cases

TL;DRThe government wants to amend the rules on how Sweden surrenders wanted persons under a European or Nordic arrest warrant, in order to clarify them and align them with the case law of the EU Court of Justice. Among other things, a wanted person should be able to be heard or temporarily transferred for trial, and in certain cases the issuing country's consent will be required. The amendments are proposed to apply from 1 December 2026.
In plain language

An arrest warrant is a request from one country to have a wanted person surrendered – either to prosecute the person or to enforce a sentence. The government wants to amend the Swedish rules for European and Nordic arrest warrants, partly to make them clearer and partly to align them with how the EU Court of Justice has interpreted the framework.

Three changes are central:

  • If the warrant concerns prosecution, the wanted person should, on request, either be able to be heard in Sweden or be temporarily transferred to the country that wishes to prosecute.
  • If the person in Sweden has had special protection (privileges or immunity), the time limits start to run only when the court has been informed that the protection has ended.
  • For Sweden to be able to refuse to surrender someone to serve a sentence – and instead enforce the sentence here – the country that issued the warrant must consent.

The rules are proposed to enter into force on 1 December 2026.

Full text

The bill mainly contains proposals for amendments to the Act (2003:1156) on surrender from Sweden under a European arrest warrant and the Act (2011:1165) on surrender from Sweden under a Nordic arrest warrant. The purpose of the amendments is to clarify the Swedish implementation of the framework in certain respects and to adapt it to the case law of the EU Court of Justice. The proposals entail, among other things, the following:

  • If an arrest warrant concerns prosecution, the requested person shall, at the request of the issuing authority, either be able to be heard in the executing state or be temporarily transferred to the issuing state.
  • If the requested person has been protected by privileges or immunity in Sweden, the time limits applicable to a surrender case shall be counted from the day the district court has been informed that the privileges or immunity have ended.
  • The consent of the issuing state shall be required for a surrender for the enforcement of a custodial sentence to be refused and the sentence instead enforced in Sweden.

The amendments are proposed to enter into force on 1 December 2026.

Proposed decision by the Riksdag

The government proposes that the Riksdag adopt the proposals for:

  1. a law amending the Act (2003:1156) on surrender from Sweden under a European arrest warrant,
  2. a law amending the Act (2011:1165) on surrender from Sweden under a Nordic arrest warrant,
  3. a law amending the Act (2021:709) with supplementary provisions on criminal-law cooperation between the European Union and the United Kingdom.
