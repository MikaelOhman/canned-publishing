New rules for labor immigration.

TL;DRThe government wants to change the rules for working in Sweden. The goal is to make it more difficult to exploit undocumented labor, while making it easier for skilled professionals to come to the country.
In plain language

The government has proposed a plan that is scheduled to take effect on June 1, 2026. To obtain a work permit, it will be required to have health insurance and a certain salary. However, there are certain jobs that do not need to meet the salary requirement.

It will also become more difficult for companies to hire people from countries outside the EU if the company has made mistakes or is suspected of crimes. The government also wants to impose harsher penalties on companies that hire people who are not authorized to be in Sweden. Two new crimes will be introduced, related to exploiting foreign workers and selling work permits.

At the same time, the government wants to make it easier for people with higher education to move to Sweden. For those who hold an EU Blue Card, which is a permit for experts, or those who come for seasonal work, the length of time they are allowed to stay will be extended. The government also wants to allow experts to apply for their permit while they are already in Sweden.

The proposal concerns changes to the Immigration Act, which is the law that governs who is allowed to live and work in the country. Now, the parliament will decide whether the new rules will become law.

Full text

The proposal includes suggestions aimed at tightening the conditions for labor immigration while promoting the immigration of highly skilled workers. This includes the following:

  • A requirement for comprehensive health insurance and a salary requirement are introduced as conditions for a work permit. However, certain professions should be exempt from the salary requirement.
  • Certain professions may be excluded from the possibility of being granted a work permit.
  • A work permit may be denied if the employer has been subject to certain penalties or is suspected of or has been convicted of certain crimes.
  • The special fee for employing a foreign national who is not entitled to reside or work in Sweden is increased.
  • Two new offenses, exploitation of foreign labor and trafficking in work permits, are introduced.
  • The validity period for EU Blue Cards and permits for seasonal work is extended.
  • The opportunities for certain highly skilled foreign nationals to apply for a residence permit from within Sweden are expanded.

The proposed amendments to the law are scheduled to come into force on June 1, 2026.
