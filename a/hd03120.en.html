Regulations regarding the prohibition of the use and possession of certain veterinary drugs.

TL;DRThe government intends to introduce new regulations to prohibit certain medications and substances for use in animals. This is being done to protect human health and to comply with EU laws regarding food safety and animal welfare.
In plain language

The government has proposed that it, or another authority, should have the power to determine when certain medications and substances should no longer be used on animals. This primarily concerns substances that affect hormones or that may leave dangerous residues in meat and other food products.

The new regulations apply to everyone who keeps animals, such as farmers, and to those who work in animal healthcare. The purpose is for Sweden to comply with the regulations in place within the EU. The EU has stipulated that certain substances should not be administered to animals intended for food.

The proposal also includes a potential ban on the use of certain types of medications that affect the immune system in animals. There is also a desire to limit the use of medications that kill bacteria, known as antimicrobial agents, under certain circumstances.

If the parliament approves the proposal, the new laws will come into effect on July 1, 2026. This is part of an effort to make the rules surrounding animal medicine clearer and more comprehensive throughout Sweden.

Full text

The proposal suggests that the government or a government-appointed authority be authorized to issue regulations regarding restrictions or prohibitions on the use or possession of substances with hormonal effects and other substances that may leave harmful residues. These regulations should apply to animal keepers and personnel in animal health and veterinary care and are necessary to fulfill Sweden's obligations under EU law. The purpose of these authorizations is to enable a clearer and more comprehensive implementation of an EU directive prohibiting the addition of certain substances to food-producing animals.

The proposed amendments are scheduled to come into force on July 1, 2026.

Proposed Parliamentary Decision

Prop. 2025/26:120

Government's proposal:

  1. The Parliament approves the government's proposal for an amendment to the Act (2006:806) on sampling from animals, etc.
  2. The Parliament approves the government's proposal for an amendment to the Act (2009:302) on activities within animal health and veterinary care.

3.

  1. Restrictions or prohibitions on the use of antimicrobial agents by animal keepers in the treatment of animals, provided such restrictions or prohibitions are consistent with Article 107.7 and 107.8 of the European Parliament and Council Regulation (EU) 2019/6 of December 11, 2018, on veterinary medicinal products and on the repeal of Directive 2001/82/EC,
  2. Prohibitions on the use or possession of immunological veterinary medicinal products by animal keepers if any of the conditions in Article 110.1 of Regulation (EU) 2019/6 are met, and
  3. Restrictions or prohibitions that are necessary to fulfill Sweden's obligations under EU law regarding the use or possession of substances with hormonal effects and other substances that may leave harmful residues by animal keepers.
