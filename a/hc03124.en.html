TL;DRThe government is proposing new laws to provide stronger protection against child sexual abuse, fraud targeting the elderly, and hate crimes. These proposals include stricter penalties for certain offenses and are scheduled to come into effect on July 1, 2025.
In plain language

The government wants to amend the Penal Code, which is the law that defines crimes and their corresponding punishments. A key proposal is to better protect children up to the age of 18. If an adult exploits a child who is vulnerable and unable to defend themselves, for example, due to mental health issues or a disability, it should be classified as rape of a minor or sexual abuse of a minor.

It will also become illegal to purchase sexual acts, even if it occurs online without physical contact. Previously, the law primarily applied when the interaction was physical. Furthermore, the government wants to impose harsher penalties if a crime is committed due to hatred of someone's gender. This is known as a hate crime, and it means that the perpetrator is punished more severely when the motive is prejudice.

To protect elderly individuals, the government wants to increase the penalties for fraud. If a person commits fraud and exploits someone in a vulnerable position, or if the crime is part of an organized criminal group, it should be considered a serious offense, leading to a longer prison sentence.

All of these changes are proposals that the government is sending to the Parliament for consideration. If the Parliament approves them, the new rules will come into effect throughout Sweden on July 1, 2025.

Full text

In the proposal, the government suggests several amendments to the law aimed at strengthening the criminal protection against sexual offenses, fraud targeting the elderly, and crimes motivated by hatred based on gender. The proposals include the following:

  • Anyone who engages in sexual intercourse or another sexual act with a child under 18 years of age and improperly takes advantage of the fact that the child, due to factors such as mental health issues, disability, or the perpetrator's authority, has a diminished ability to protect their sexual integrity, should be convicted of rape of a minor and sexual abuse of a minor.
  • The term "purchase of sexual services" will be changed to "purchase of sexual acts," and the scope of the criminal offense and the related offense of prostitution will be expanded to include acts performed remotely, i.e., without physical contact.
  • Gender will be added to the provision concerning increased penalties for crimes committed with hate crime motives.
  • More cases of fraud will be classified as serious offenses and subject to harsher penalties, with particular consideration given to whether the perpetrator has callously exploited the victim's vulnerable or exposed position, or whether the act was part of criminal activity carried out in an organized or systematic manner.

The proposed amendments are scheduled to come into effect on July 1, 2025.
