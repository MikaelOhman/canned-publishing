Protecting children's rights and safety: A new law regarding child protection measures for young people.

Source Riksdagen Read the original at Riksdagen →2026-06-30
TL;DRA new law will replace the current childcare legislation to provide children with better rights and security. The new regulations aim to clarify how children are protected, especially those living in foster homes.
In plain language

The government wants to amend the laws governing how children and young people are taken into care (currently regulated by what is known as LVU). The aim is to create a more modern and clearer legal framework where the child's rights are at the center. The rules should be easier to understand, and it should be made explicitly clear that children have the right to assistance from a public guardian, which is a person who helps them with legal matters.

A key part of the new law concerns children living in foster homes. To create more security for these children, the rules are being changed to make it more difficult for parents to demand that a child be allowed to return home if it is not safe for the child. It should be clearer what situations within a home require authorities to intervene to protect the child.

The proposal also includes changes to many other laws, such as the education law and regulations on how personal data can be handled by social services. All of these new rules are planned to come into effect on January 1, 2027.

Full text

The proposal suggests replacing the current law (1990:52) concerning special provisions for care of young people, often referred to as LVU, with a new law on placement for the care of children and young people, and a new law on special powers for state child welfare services.

These proposals aim to create modern, clear, and comprehensive legislation that centers on the child's rights and addresses the protection and support needs of children and young people today. A stronger focus on children's rights is proposed, including consolidating children's and young people's rights into a dedicated chapter within the laws. Furthermore, it is suggested to clarify and expand the right to legal representation.

The new law on placement for the care of children and young people also proposes a new basis for placement due to connection with a foster home. This is proposed to increase security and stability for children placed in foster homes. To this end, it is also proposed that, in certain cases, a caregiver's request to terminate a placement may be denied. In order for placements to better meet the protection and care needs of children and young people, it is also proposed to adjust and supplement the conditions regarding circumstances within the home and behaviors that can constitute grounds for a placement.

The draft laws are proposed to come into force on January 1, 2027.
