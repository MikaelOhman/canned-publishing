Full text

Jakob Tordsson Bagge af Boo, born in 1502 and died in 1577, was a Swedish admiral and governor. The addition "af Boo" refers to the estate of Boo, located on Värmdön (now Nacka municipality), which Jakob Bagge received as a fief from Gustav Vasa. This addition was likely added later.

== Biography == Jakob Bagge was born in May 1502 in Halland or Norway, son of Thord Olofsson Bagge and Ingeborg Jakobsdotter, both of Norwegian noble descent. Both he and his father initially served the Danish crown but entered Swedish service under Gustav Vasa in 1522. Bagge first became known as a district judge (fogde), but by 1534 he was already a captain commanding a company of soldiers and participated in the Count's Feud, serving as a commander both on land and at sea. In 1535, he defeated Johan of Hoya in Halmstad. In 1541, he became vice admiral in the fleet. During the Dacke Rebellion, he served as a war colonel and played a significant role in the reprisals against the Smålanders after Nils Dacke's death.

During the war against Russia in the 1550s, Bagge participated as a field marshal and received a letter of nobility from Gustav Vasa, who also granted him extensive estates. During the Nordic Seven Years' War (1563–70), he commanded the Swedish fleet and on May 30, 1563, participated in the battle against the Danes off Bornholm, which led to the outbreak of the war. In this famous battle, he defeated the Danish fleet and captured its admiral, Jacob Brockenhuus, leading King Erik to organize a grand triumphal procession in Stockholm for the victor. He was considered a great military hero.

In the first battle off the northern tip of Öland in May 1564, the Danes boarded his flagship, "Mars," also known as "Jutehataren" or "Makalös," and captured Bagge. He was then taken to Copenhagen, where he was held prisoner for seven years.

Upon his return to Stockholm, he was appointed castle governor (kommendant) at Tre Kronor Castle in Stockholm in 1571 and died there on January 14, 1577. He also owned the estates of Boo, Lännersta, and Mems Castle. His wife's name was Anna Jönsdotter, and they had two sons: Jakob the Younger and Johan.

== Name == Several places and a ship have been named after him, including Baggensfjärden, Baggensstäket, Baggensgatan, and the torpedo cruiser HMS Jacob Bagge.
