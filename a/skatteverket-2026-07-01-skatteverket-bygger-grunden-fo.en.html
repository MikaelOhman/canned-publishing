TL;DRThe Swedish Tax Agency and the Social Insurance Agency are launching a new initiative called the "AI Workshop" to assist government agencies, municipalities, and regions in utilizing new technologies (artificial intelligence) safely and effectively.
In plain language

On July 1, 2026, a new initiative called the "AI Workshop" will launch. It is operated by the Swedish Tax Agency and the Social Insurance Agency. The purpose is to create a common foundation so that various parts of the public sector, such as municipalities and regions, can use artificial intelligence (AI) more effectively.

Initially, ten different organizations will test the technology together with the AI Workshop. These organizations vary in size and have different levels of technical expertise. By testing various solutions, it will be possible to learn what works best for different types of operations.

During the testing period, tools that can mask sensitive information, transcribe speech into text, and create digital assistants will be tested. At the same time, support is being developed to provide advice and assistance when government agencies want to develop their own solutions.

The AI Workshop will be expanded gradually until 2030. The goal is to make it easier for everyone working in the public sector to use modern technology in their daily work.

On July 1, 2026, the AI workshop will begin its trial operations. This is a first step towards building the foundation for scalable AI within the public sector. The AI workshop is operated by the Swedish Tax Agency and the Social Insurance Agency, and aims to make it easier for government agencies, municipalities, and regions to develop and use AI.

Ten pilot organizations are participating in the launch, including Region Halland and Mörbylånga municipality. The goal has been a diverse group of pilot organizations to capture the needs of organizations of different sizes and with varying levels of technical maturity.

The work involves organizations testing various types of solutions. The pilot projects include testing AI services for redaction, transcription, and digital assistants. These services are provided by both public and private entities. Simultaneously, support is being developed for connection, development, consulting, and technical assistance.

The AI workshop will be established gradually and is expected to be fully operational by 2030.
