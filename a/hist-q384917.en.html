Svante Edvin Nilsson, born June 10, 1869, in Stockholm, and died May 21, 1942, in Stockholm, was a Swedish medalist, lyricist, and lute player.

== Biography ==

Nilsson began studying medal engraving with Adolf Lindberg at the age of thirteen, and after ten years of apprenticeship, he mastered the art of creating the dies for medals. He was awarded a scholarship from the Board of Commerce in 1893, which he used to further his education in Paris and Rome. He remained in Paris and collaborated with the Swedish medalist Sven Kulle, who lived in France, while also studying at the École des Arts Décoratifs and the Académie Colarossi. Nilsson participated in numerous exhibitions, and during his time in Paris, he played a significant role within the Swedish community, where his home became a gathering place for many Swedish art students. He returned to Sweden at the outbreak of World War I in 1914. He is represented in the collections of the National Museum and the Royal Mint Cabinet. As a musician, he was a highly regarded lute player, specializing in Bellman songs and old French chansons.
