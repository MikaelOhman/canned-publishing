Runestone Sö Fv1986;218, made of red granite, is 1.8 meters high and 1.63 meters wide (east-west), tapering upwards, and 0.36 meters thick. The runic inscriptions begin 18 cm from the ground. Style group: Fp. The inscription faces north. The stone's inclination is 0° at the base, with an overhanging slope of 12° at the top. Runic text: × kaʀ : reþi sin : eti : bui : uiburk uaʀ : muþʀ : snuʀ : fatriþ : ---- Old Norse: Gæirr ræisti stæin æftiʀ Boa. Viborg vaʀ moðiʀ. [Vaʀ] snoʀ Fastrið(?) … Swedish translation: Ger raised the stone in memory of Bo. Viborg was his mother. (She was) his daughter-in-law, Fastridh… Signage: Information sign from the National Heritage Board (RAÄ). In good condition. The runestone was erected at its current location on October 15, 1986. The runestone, which was originally located in a field approximately 60 meters southeast of its current position (see L1984:4603 / Kjula 111:2), was discovered around 1975, although no inscription was initially observed. The stone was then moved to a sheep pasture, about 300 meters east.

Location: Sweden, Södermanland County, Eskilstuna Municipality, Kjula.

Runestone registration number: Sö Fv1986;218.
