A new consumer credit law.

Source Riksdagen Read the original at Riksdagen →2026-03-31

The government proposes measures to strengthen consumer protection regarding consumer credit. High standards of protection for consumers must be in place when dealing with businesses regarding credit. Consumers should be given good opportunities to understand the credit agreement and their rights. It is also important that consumer protection regulations apply broadly and that high standards are set for credit assessments. This can help prevent risky lending and over-indebtedness.

The government proposes a new consumer credit law aimed at strengthening consumer protection and adapting it to the developments of recent years. The new law will regulate many different aspects of lending to consumers, including information, right of withdrawal, marketing, and interest and cost limits. To prevent over-indebtedness, the government also proposes stricter rules for credit assessments. A new licensing requirement is being introduced, which means that more businesses that provide or broker credit must be subject to supervision and comply with requirements for sufficient knowledge and competence.

The proposals in the bill implement an EU directive on consumer credit.

The new consumer credit law and the amendments to the law are proposed to come into force on November 20, 2026.
