TL;DRThe EU has updated its list of individuals subject to sanctions due to the situation in South Sudan. This update aims to continue penalizing a specific person for obstructing peace and human rights.
In plain language

The Council of the European Union has decided to amend the sanctions regulations against South Sudan. The sanctions against Michael Makuei Lueth will remain in effect. He previously served as Minister of Information and is currently the Minister of Justice and Minister for Constitutional Affairs in South Sudan.

The reason for his inclusion on the list is that he has obstructed efforts to establish peace in the country. He has, among other things, used inflammatory language and hindered important peace agreements and monitoring groups from carrying out their work. He has also defended the arrest of political opponents.

The EU also believes that he is responsible for the shrinking political space in the country. He has supported censorship and threatened journalists, which undermines freedom of expression and democracy. This is why he continues to be subject to restrictive measures, which involve limitations on his activities.

The decision is effective immediately in all EU member states. New information regarding his current roles in the government has been added to the official documents to accurately describe his current position of power.

COUNCIL IMPLEMENTING REGULATION (EU) 2026/1402

implementing Regulation (EU) 2015/735 on restrictive measures in respect of the situation in South Sudan

THE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION HAS ADOPTED THIS REGULATION,

having regard to the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union,

having regard to Council Regulation (EU) 2015/735 of 7 May 2015 on restrictive measures in respect of the situation in South Sudan and repealing Regulation (EU) No 748/2014 ( 1 ), in particular Article 22.4,

having regard to the proposal from the Union High Representative for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy, and

WHEREAS, the Council adopted Regulation (EU) 2015/735 on 7 May 2015.

In accordance with Article 22.4 of Regulation (EU) 2015/735, the Council has reviewed the list of persons subject to restrictive measures in Annex II to that Regulation.

The Council has found that the restrictive measures against a person listed in Annex II to Regulation (EU) 2015/735 should be maintained and that the entry concerning that person should be updated.

Regulation (EU) 2015/735 should therefore be amended accordingly.

Annex II to Regulation (EU) 2015/735 is hereby amended in accordance with the Annex to this Regulation.

This Regulation shall enter into force on the day following its publication in the Official Journal of the European Union.

This Regulation is binding in its entirety and directly applicable in all Member States.

In Annex II to Regulation (EU) 2015/735, the entry numbered 1 shall be replaced with the following:

Michael Makuei Lueth held the position of Minister of Information in the Revitalized Government of National Unity of South Sudan from 2013 to November 2025. Since November 2025, he has been the Minister of Justice and Minister for Constitutional Affairs of South Sudan. He was also the spokesperson for the government delegation to the Intergovernmental Authority on Development (IGAD) peace talks from 2014 to 2015 and from 2016 to 2018.

Michael Makuei Lueth has, particularly through provocative public statements, obstructed the implementation of the Agreement on the Resolution of the Conflict in South Sudan (ARCSS) from August 2015 (which was replaced in September 2018 by the "revitalized ARCSS" (R-ARCSS)), the work of the ARCSS Joint Monitoring and Evaluation Commission (JMEC, which was renamed "the reconstituted JMEC" within the framework of R-ARCSS), the establishment of transitional justice institutions within the framework of ARCSS (also stipulated in R-ARCSS), and the efforts undertaken by the United Nations Regional Protection Force.

Furthermore, Michael Makuei Lueth is involved in the systematic restriction of the political and democratic space, as well as in media repression, including through regular threats against journalists and support for media censorship, which hinders democratization and a sustainable peace in South Sudan.

Since the beginning of 2025, Michael Makuei Lueth has publicly rejected calls for dialogue and has used a confrontational and provocative rhetoric in an increasingly tense situation. Michael Makuei Lueth has also defended the arrests of First Vice President Riek Machar and his allies, which hinders efforts to de-escalate the situation.

Michael Makuei Lueth has thus obstructed the political process in South Sudan and is responsible for serious violations of human rights, particularly freedom of expression.
