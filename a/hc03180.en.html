Increased information sharing between government agencies – a new provision that overrides confidentiality.

Source Riksdagen Read the original at Riksdagen →2025-05-28
TL;DRThe government wants to amend the law to make it easier for government agencies to share information with each other. This is intended to improve the ability to prevent crime and incorrect payments of money.
In plain language

Currently, regulations prevent government agencies from always sharing information with each other due to confidentiality rules. Confidentiality means that information must be kept secret to protect individuals. The government wants to change this to allow agencies to collaborate more effectively.

The new proposal would allow agencies to share information if it is necessary to prevent crime or detect fraud. This also includes identifying incorrect payments, for example, if money has been paid out by mistake or through fraudulent means. The purpose is to enable government agencies to perform their duties efficiently.

However, this does not mean that all information will be made public. Before an agency releases any information, it must verify that it is safe to do so. They must weigh the need to share information against the need to protect an individual's privacy. If there are strong reasons to protect a person, the information must not be disclosed.

If the parliament votes in favor of the proposal, the new law will come into effect on December 1, 2025. The proposal concerns an amendment to the law called the "Public Access and Confidentiality Act."

Full text

Confidentiality, intended to protect individuals, should not prevent the sharing of information between government agencies when necessary for specific purposes. To effectively fulfill their missions, government agencies need access to accurate, up-to-date, and relevant information. Information held by one agency may be crucial for another agency to carry out its duties. In the proposal, the government suggests that information should be shared when necessary for the following purposes:

  • Preventing, deterring, or detecting criminal activity.
  • Investigating crimes.
  • Preventing, deterring, detecting, or investigating improper payments.
  • Preventing, deterring, detecting, or investigating fraud and various types of violations.

Before any information is shared, an assessment of competing interests must be conducted. Information should not be shared if there are overriding reasons to believe that the interest protected by confidentiality outweighs the interest in disclosing the information. Certain types of confidential information are entirely excluded from the scope of this provision.

The proposed amendment to the law is scheduled to take effect on December 1, 2025.

Proposed Parliamentary Decision

The government's proposal: The Parliament adopts the government's proposal for an amendment to the Public Access and Secrecy Act (2009:400).
